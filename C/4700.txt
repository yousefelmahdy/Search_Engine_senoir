            <p>The <b>good article criteria</b> are the six standards or tests by which a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Good_article_nominations" title="Wikipedia:Good article nominations">good article nomination</a> (GAN) may be compared and judged to be a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Good_articles" title="Wikipedia:Good articles">good article</a> (GA). A good article that has met the good article criteria may not have met the criteria for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_articles" title="Wikipedia:Featured articles">featured articles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> </p>

            <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria">Criteria</span></h2> 
            <p>The six good article criteria are the only aspects that should be considered when assessing whether to pass or fail an article. All other comments designed to help improve the article are to be encouraged during the review process but should not be mandated as part of the assessment. </p> 
            <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Immediate_failures">Immediate failures</span></h3> 
            <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
            <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
             <span id="WP:GAFAIL"></span>
            </div>
            <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
             <div class="module-shortcutlist">
              <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
             </div>
             <ul>
              <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:GAFAIL&amp;redirect=no">WP:GAFAIL</a></span></li>
             </ul>
            </div> 
            <p>An article can, but by no means must, be failed without further review (known as a quick fail) if, prior to the review: </p> 
            <ol> 
             <li>It is a long way from meeting any one of the six good article criteria</li> 
             <li>It contains <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyright_violations" title="Wikipedia:Copyright violations">copyright violations</a></li> 
             <li>It has, or needs, cleanup banners that are unquestionably still valid. These include {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cleanup" title="Template:Cleanup">cleanup</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:POV" title="Template:POV">POV</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Unreferenced" title="Template:Unreferenced">unreferenced</a>}} or large numbers of {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Citation_needed" title="Template:Citation needed">citation needed</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Clarify" title="Template:Clarify">clarify</a>}}, or similar tags. (See also {{<a href="/wiki/Template:QF" title="Template:QF">QF</a>}})</li> 
             <li>It is not stable due to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_warring" title="Wikipedia:Edit warring">edit warring</a> on the page</li> 
             <li>A reviewer who has not previously reviewed the article determines that any issues from previous GA nominations have not been adequately considered</li> 
            </ol> 
            <p>In all other cases, the nominator deserves a full review against the six criteria from the reviewer. For most reviews, the nominator is given a chance to address any issues raised by the reviewer before the article is failed. Often the nomination is brought up to standard during the review. </p> 
            <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_six_good_article_criteria">The six good article criteria</span></h3> 
            <div class="floatright">
             <a href="/wiki/File:Symbol_support_vote.svg" class="image"><img alt="Symbol support vote.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/94/Symbol_support_vote.svg/75px-Symbol_support_vote.svg.png" decoding="async" width="75" height="77" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/94/Symbol_support_vote.svg/113px-Symbol_support_vote.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/94/Symbol_support_vote.svg/150px-Symbol_support_vote.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="185"></a>
            </div> 
            <p>A <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Good_articles" title="Wikipedia:Good articles">good article</a> is: </p> 
            <ol> 
             <li><b>Well written</b>: 
              <ol style="list-style-type: lower-alpha"> 
               <li>the prose is clear, concise, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Make_technical_articles_understandable" title="Wikipedia:Make technical articles understandable">understandable to an appropriately broad audience</a>; spelling and grammar are correct; and</li> 
               <li>it complies with the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style">manual of style</a> guidelines for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Lead_section" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Lead section">lead sections</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Layout" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Layout">layout</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">words to watch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Writing_about_fiction" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Writing about fiction">fiction</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Embedded_lists" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Embedded lists">list incorporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li> 
              </ol></li> 
             <li><b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">Verifiable</a></b> with <b>no original research</b>:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> 
              <ol style="list-style-type: lower-alpha"> 
               <li>it contains a list of all references (sources of information), presented in accordance with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FNNR" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FNNR">the layout style guideline</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li> 
               <li>all <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Inline_citation" title="Wikipedia:Inline citation">inline citations</a> are from <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources">reliable sources</a>, including those for direct quotations, statistics, published opinion, counter-intuitive or controversial statements that are challenged or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Likely_to_be_challenged" title="Wikipedia:Likely to be challenged">likely to be challenged</a>, and contentious material relating to living persons—science-based articles should follow the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Scientific_citation_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Scientific citation guidelines">scientific citation guidelines</a>;</li> 
               <li>it contains <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">no original research</a>; and</li> 
               <li>it contains no <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyright_violations" title="Wikipedia:Copyright violations">copyright violations</a> nor <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Plagiarism" title="Wikipedia:Plagiarism">plagiarism</a>.</li> 
              </ol></li> 
             <li><b>Broad in its coverage</b>: 
              <ol style="list-style-type: lower-alpha"> 
               <li>it addresses the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Out_of_scope" title="Wikipedia:Out of scope">main aspects</a> of the topic;<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and</li> 
               <li>it stays <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Article_size" title="Wikipedia:Article size">focused on the topic</a> without going into unnecessary detail (see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Summary_style" title="Wikipedia:Summary style">summary style</a>).</li> 
              </ol></li> 
             <li><b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Neutral_point_of_view" title="Wikipedia:Neutral point of view">Neutral</a></b>: it represents viewpoints fairly and without editorial bias, giving due weight to each.</li> 
             <li><b>Stable</b>: it does not change significantly from day to day because of an ongoing <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_warring" title="Wikipedia:Edit warring">edit war</a> or content dispute.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li> 
             <li><b>Illustrated</b>, if possible, by <b>media</b> such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Images" title="Wikipedia:Images">images</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Videos" title="Wikipedia:Videos">video</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Audio" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Audio">audio</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> 
              <ol style="list-style-type: lower-alpha"> 
               <li>media are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:File_copyright_tags" title="Wikipedia:File copyright tags">tagged</a> with their <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FAQ/Copyright" title="Wikipedia:FAQ/Copyright">copyright statuses</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Non-free_use_rationale_guideline" title="Wikipedia:Non-free use rationale guideline">valid fair use rationales</a> are provided for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Non-free_content" title="Wikipedia:Non-free content">non-free content</a>; and</li> 
               <li>media are <a href="/wiki/MOS:IMAGERELEVANCE" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:IMAGERELEVANCE">relevant</a> to the topic, and have <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Captions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Captions">suitable captions</a>.</li> 
              </ol></li> 
            </ol> 
            <h2><span id="What_cannot_be_a_good_article.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="What_cannot_be_a_good_article?">What cannot be a good article?</span></h2> 
            <ul>
             <li><b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Stand-alone_lists" title="Wikipedia:Stand-alone lists">Stand-alone lists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Portal" title="Wikipedia:Portal">portals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Creation_and_usage_of_media_files#Audio" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Creation and usage of media files">sounds</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Images" title="Wikipedia:Images">images</a></b>: these items should be nominated for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_lists" title="Wikipedia:Featured lists">featured list</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_pictures" title="Wikipedia:Featured pictures">featured picture</a> status, if applicable.</li> 
             <li><b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disambiguation" title="Wikipedia:Disambiguation">Disambiguation</a> pages and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Stub" title="Wikipedia:Stub">stubs</a></b>: these pages cannot meet the criteria.</li> 
             <li><b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_articles" title="Wikipedia:Featured articles">Featured articles</a></b>: a good article loses its status when promoted to a featured article. Accordingly, demoted featured articles are not automatically graded as good articles and must be reassessed for quality.</li>
            </ul>

            <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span></h2> 
            <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1011085734">.mw-parser-output .reflist{font-size:90%;margin-bottom:0.5em;list-style-type:decimal}.mw-parser-output .reflist .references{font-size:100%;margin-bottom:0;list-style-type:inherit}.mw-parser-output .reflist-columns-2{column-width:30em}.mw-parser-output .reflist-columns-3{column-width:25em}.mw-parser-output .reflist-columns{margin-top:0.3em}.mw-parser-output .reflist-columns ol{margin-top:0}.mw-parser-output .reflist-columns li{page-break-inside:avoid;break-inside:avoid-column}.mw-parser-output .reflist-upper-alpha{list-style-type:upper-alpha}.mw-parser-output .reflist-upper-roman{list-style-type:upper-roman}.mw-parser-output .reflist-lower-alpha{list-style-type:lower-alpha}.mw-parser-output .reflist-lower-greek{list-style-type:lower-greek}.mw-parser-output .reflist-lower-roman{list-style-type:lower-roman}</style>
            <div class="reflist reflist-columns references-column-width reflist-columns-2" style=""> 
             <ol class="references"> 
              <li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Good articles are only measured against the good article criteria. At the time of assessment, they may or may not meet <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_article_criteria" title="Wikipedia:Featured article criteria">featured article criteria</a>, which determine our <i>best</i> articles. The good article criteria measure <i>decent</i> articles; they are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Compare_criteria_Good_v._Featured_article" title="Wikipedia:Compare criteria Good v. Featured article">not as demanding</a> as the featured article criteria, which determine our <i>best</i> articles.</span> </li> 
              <li id="cite_note-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-2">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Compliance with other aspects of the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style">Manual of Style</a> or its subpages is <i>not</i> required for good articles.</span> </li> 
              <li id="cite_note-3"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-3">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reviewing_good_articles" title="Wikipedia:Reviewing good articles">Wikipedia:Reviewing good articles</a> says, "Ideally, a reviewer will have access to all of the source material, and sufficient expertise to verify that the article reflects the content of the sources; this ideal is not often attained. At a bare minimum, check that the sources used are reliable (for example, blogs are not usually reliable sources) and that those you can access support the content of the article (for example, inline citations lead to sources which agree with what the article says) and are not plagiarized (for example, close paraphrasing of source material should only be used where appropriate, with in text attribution if necessary)."</span> </li> 
              <li id="cite_note-4"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-4">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Dead links are considered verifiable only if the link is not a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Bare_URLs" title="Wikipedia:Bare URLs">bare url</a>. Using consistent formatting or including every element of the bibliographic material is <i>not</i> required, although, in practice, enough information must be supplied that the reviewer is able to identify the source.</span> </li> 
              <li id="cite_note-5"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-5">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The "broad in its coverage" criterion is significantly weaker than the "comprehensiveness" required of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_articles" title="Wikipedia:Featured articles">featured articles</a>. It allows shorter articles, articles that do not cover every major fact or detail, and overviews of large topics.</span> </li> 
              <li id="cite_note-6"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-6">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Reverted vandalism, proposals to split or merge content, good faith improvements to the page (such as <a href="/wiki/Copy_editing" title="Copy editing">copy editing</a>), and changes based on reviewers' suggestions do not apply to the "stable" criterion. Nominations for articles that are unstable because of disruptive editing may be failed or placed on hold.</span> </li> 
              <li id="cite_note-7"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-7">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The presence of media is <i>not</i>, in itself, a requirement. However, if media with acceptable copyright status is appropriate and readily available, then such media should be provided.</span> </li> 
             </ol>
