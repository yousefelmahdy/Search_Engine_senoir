       <h1 class="h2-mktg mb-4">Project management, made&nbsp;simple</h1> 
       <p class="f3-mktg color-text-secondary mb-5 col-md-9 mx-auto">On GitHub, project managers and developers coordinate, track, and update their work in one place, so projects stay transparent and on schedule.</p>

      <h2 class="h3-mktg mb-3 text-center">Start with an&nbsp;issue</h2> 
      <p class="f3-mktg text-normal color-text-secondary text-center mb-2 pb-md-4 col-md-8 mx-auto"> Create an issue to suggest a new idea or track a bug. Then organize and assign tasks to your team. And don’t spare any attachments—issues support most image and file types. </p> 
      <p class="f4 text-center mb-6"> <a class="Bump-link btn-mktg btn-outline-mktg btn-large-mktg mx-auto" href="https://docs.github.com/articles/managing-your-work-with-issues/">Learn the basics of issues&nbsp;<span class="Bump-link-symbol">?</span></a> </p> 
      <div class="d-md-flex flex-md-wrap gutter-md-spacious flex-md-justify-center"> 
       <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-5 mb-4"> <picture> 
         <source srcset="https://github.githubassets.com/images/modules/site/project-management/projects-tasks.webp" type="image/webp">

        </picture> 
        <h3 class="h6-mktg mb-3 mt-4">Tasks</h3> 
        <p class="color-text-secondary"> Issue, comment, and pull request descriptions support <a href="https://github.blog/2013-01-09-task-lists-in-gfm-issues-pulls-comments/" class="link-mktg">Markdown task lists</a>—checklists you can use to coordinate and track parts of a project. Reorder tasks as your priorities change, and check them off as you complete each one. </p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-5 mb-4"> <picture> 
         <source srcset="https://github.githubassets.com/images/modules/site/project-management/projects-mentions.webp" type="image/webp"> 
         <img src="https://github.githubassets.com/images/modules/site/project-management/projects-mentions.png" width="460" class="d-block width-fit my-3" alt=""> 
        </picture> 
        <h3 class="h6-mktg mb-3 mt-4">Assignees and mentions</h3> 
        <p class="color-text-secondary"> Assign up to ten teammates to an issue or pull request to make sure work has an owner. Mentioning other people or teams in the issue will notify them if something changes. They can also stay in the loop by opting to <a href="https://docs.github.com/articles/about-notifications/" class="link-mktg">receive notifications</a> whenever someone posts a comment. </p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-5 mb-4"> <picture> 
         <source srcset="https://github.githubassets.com/images/modules/site/project-management/projects-labels.webp" type="image/webp"> 
         <img src="https://github.githubassets.com/images/modules/site/project-management/projects-labels.png" width="460" class="d-block width-fit my-3" alt="" loading="lazy" decoding="async"> 
        </picture> 
        <h3 class="h6-mktg mb-3 mt-4">Labels</h3> 
        <p class="color-text-secondary"> Tagging issues and pull requests with <a href="https://docs.github.com/articles/applying-labels-to-issues-and-pull-requests/" class="link-mktg">labels</a> allows you to quickly search for them later. Filter your issues by label to find and reference all issues that are part of your project. </p> 
        <hr class="my-4" width="40"> 
        <p class="f5 color-text-secondary"> <span class="color-text-success text-bold">Pro-tip:</span> Templates help your team create issues without starting from scratch. </p> 
        <p class="f5"> <a href="https://docs.github.com/articles/creating-an-issue-template-for-your-repository/" class="Bump-link no-underline">Learn how to set up an issue <span class="no-wrap">template <span class="Bump-link-symbol">?</span></span></a> </p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="col-md-6 col-lg-5 mb-4"> <picture> 
         <source srcset="https://github.githubassets.com/images/modules/site/project-management/projects-milestones.webp" type="image/webp"> 
         <img src="https://github.githubassets.com/images/modules/site/project-management/projects-milestones.png" width="460" class="d-block width-fit my-3" alt="" loading="lazy" decoding="async"> 
        </picture> 
        <h3 class="h6-mktg mb-3 mt-4">Milestones</h3> 
        <p class="color-text-secondary"> Add a milestone to track a project as part of a larger goal, like a quarterly to-do list or a big feature. Then watch your milestone’s overall progress from <a href="https://docs.github.com/articles/viewing-your-milestone-s-progress/" class="link-mktg">the milestone page</a>. </p>
