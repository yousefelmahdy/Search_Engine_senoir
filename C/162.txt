      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="AGF">AGF</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: AGF">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Assume_good_faith" title="Wikipedia:Assume good faith">Wikipedia:Assume good faith</a>. At Did You Know, AGF means to assume that a referenced fact is actually in the reference, even though you haven't read the reference. This is acceptable if the reference is <a href="#offline">offline</a>, or if the reference is in a foreign language, or if you have to pay for a subscription before you can read it. </p> 
      <h4><span id="ALT.2C_ALT1.2C_ALT2_etc."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="ALT,_ALT1,_ALT2_etc.">ALT, ALT1, ALT2 etc.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: ALT, ALT1, ALT2 etc.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>means an alternate <a href="#hook">hook</a> for the same <a href="#article">article</a>. It could be a slight rewording of the same idea, or a completely different hook. ALTs are suggested at <a href="/wiki/T:TDYK" class="mw-redirect" title="T:TDYK">the suggestion page</a> after the original hook. Often an ALT is selected instead of the original version. </p>

      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="characters">characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>A <a href="/wiki/Character_(computing)" title="Character (computing)">character</a> is a letter, or a space between letters, or a punctuation mark, or anything else made with one keystroke (or one shifted keystroke). The next sentence is an example of how to count characters. This sentence has 32 characters. Count 4 for the four letters in "This", 1 for the space between "This" and "sentence", 8 for the 8 letters in "sentence", 1 for the space after "sentence", 3 for "has", 1 for the next space, 2 for the "3" and the "2" in "32", 1 for the space, 10 for "characters", and 1 for the period. 4 + 1 + 8 + 1 + 3 + 1 + 2 + 1 + 10 + 1 = 32. </p>
      <p>There are occasional debates on whether Wikipedia should count <a href="#article">article</a> size using "characters" or "bytes". Better terminology for that debate would be whether to use <a href="#prose">prose</a> or the article's history page, which counts the whole article. For ordinary text, one character occupies one byte, and only one byte, so the character count and the byte count are the same. </p>

      <p>An example of a hook is "... that <b><a href="/wiki/Jon_Olav_Alstad" title="Jon Olav Alstad">Jon Olav Alstad</a></b> was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Norwegian Parliament">Norwegian Parliament</a> at the age of 25?"       <p>at Did You Know means a reference that you can't get online, that is, on the Internet, usually because the source is a book or similar library resource. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="preparation_areas">preparation areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: preparation areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>are <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_1" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 1">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 1</a>, <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_2" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 2">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_3" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 3">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 3</a>, <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_4" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 4">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 4</a>, <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_5" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 5">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 5</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_6" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 6">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 6</a>. <a href="#approval">Approved</a> <a href="#hook">hooks</a> are moved from <a href="#T:TDYK">T:TDYK</a> to a preparation area and organized into a group of usually about eight. From there, an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrator" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Administrator">administrator</a> will eventually move each preparation area page into one of the <a href="#queue">queues</a>, after which <a href="#DYKADMINBOT">DYKADMINBOT</a> will move it to the <a href="/wiki/Main_Page" title="Main Page">Main Page</a>. See "update". </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="prose">prose</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: prose">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>At <a href="#Did_You_Know">Did You Know</a>, only <a href="#characters">characters</a> of "prose" are counted when measuring the length of an <a href="#article">article</a>, whether it's to meet the 1,500 character minimum, or to calculate whether an article has been expanded <a href="#fivefold">fivefold</a>. Characters of prose are counted by the <a href="/wiki/User:Shubinator/DYKcheck" title="User:Shubinator/DYKcheck">User:Shubinator/DYKcheck</a> <a href="/wiki/Scripting_language" title="Scripting language">script</a>, which excludes infoboxes, categories, references, lists, tables, block quotes, headers, images and captions, the "See also" section if any, Table of Contents, edit buttons, "citation needed" and similar superscripted text, and reference link numbers like [6]. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Prosesizebytes.js">Prosesizebytes.js</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: Prosesizebytes.js">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Scripts" title="Wikipedia:Scripts">script</a> that measures the <a href="#characters">characters</a> in the <a href="#prose">prose</a> portion of the <a href="#article">article</a>. See <a href="/wiki/User_talk:Dr_pda/prosesizebytes.js" title="User talk:Dr pda/prosesizebytes.js">User talk:Dr pda/prosesizebytes.js</a>. <a href="#DYKcheck">DYKcheck</a> is now preferred to prosesizebytes. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Prosesize.js">Prosesize.js</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Prosesize.js">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>is an older form of <a href="#Prosesizebytes.js">#Prosesizebytes.js</a>. <a href="#DYKcheck">DYKcheck</a> is now preferred to prosesize.js and to prosesizebytes.js. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="queue">queue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=38" title="Edit section: queue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>is what Americans call a "line", as in "line up and wait your turn". At <a href="#Did_You_Know">Did You Know</a>, it means <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Queue" title="Template:Did you know/Queue">Template:Did you know/Queue</a>, where Did You Know pages wait for their twelve-hour turn to go onto the <a href="/wiki/Main_Page" title="Main Page">Main Page</a>. There is room for six <a href="#update">updates</a> in the queue. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="reliable">reliable</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=39" title="Edit section: reliable">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>means <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources">Wikipedia:Reliable sources</a>. The word is often used in a way that sounds as if we are loosely calling people liars, but all it really means is that a source is on the list of what we consider to be reliable enough. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="run-on">run-on</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=40" title="Edit section: run-on">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>is described at <a href="/wiki/Run-on_sentence" class="mw-redirect" title="Run-on sentence">run-on sentence</a>. It does NOT mean a rambling sentence that runs on and on! If you don't understand the grammatical distinction in the <a href="/wiki/Run-on_sentence" class="mw-redirect" title="Run-on sentence">run-on sentence</a> article, then please do not use the term "run-on". </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="self-nom">self-nom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=41" title="Edit section: self-nom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>is a <a href="#nominate">nomination</a> of a <a href="#hook">hook</a> and an <a href="#article">article</a> you wrote yourself. This is permitted and encouraged. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="T:TDYK">T:TDYK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=42" title="Edit section: T:TDYK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>is both the shortcut to <a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Did_you_know" title="Template talk:Did you know">Template talk:Did you know</a> (the DYK suggestions page) and a shorthand way of referring to it. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="template">template</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=43" title="Edit section: template">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Template_namespace" title="Wikipedia:Template namespace">Wikipedia:Template namespace</a> </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="text">text</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=44" title="Edit section: text">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>See <a href="#prose">prose</a>. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="twofer">twofer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=45" title="Edit section: twofer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>Two <a href="#article">articles</a> introduced in the same <a href="#hook">hook</a>. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="update">update</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=46" title="Edit section: update">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>A single set of approximately eight <a href="#hook">hooks</a> to be displayed on the Main Page for about twelve hours. Each update is individually prepared in a <a href="#preparation_areas">preparation area</a> and moved into the <a href="#queue">queues</a> by an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrator" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Administrator">administrator</a>, then onto the Main Page by <a href="#DYKADMINBOT">DYKADMINBOT</a>. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="user_subpage">user subpage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=47" title="Edit section: user subpage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_subpage" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:User subpage">Wikipedia:User subpage</a>. You may write your article on a user subpage, so you will have time to study the Did You Know process before your article is disqualified for being too old. The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/New_article" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/New article">seven days (and the exception)</a> don't start until you move the <a href="#article">article</a> into <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Main_namespace" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Main namespace">Wikipedia:main namespace</a>. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="verify">verify</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=48" title="Edit section: verify">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>See <a href="#approval">approval</a>. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="x">x</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=49" title="Edit section: x">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>as in 5x or 1.3x, is a way of expressing how nearly an <a href="#article">article</a> has reached <a href="#fivefold">fivefold</a> <a href="#expansion">expansion</a>. From 2000 bytes of <a href="#prose">prose</a> to 3000 bytes is a 1.5x expansion. It would have to be expanded to 10,000 bytes of <a href="#prose">prose</a> (not the whole article, just prose) to be 5x and qualify as new. </p> <!--
