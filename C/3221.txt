      <p><b>Salar Kamangar</b> (; born 1977 in Tehran) is an Iranian-American senior executive at Google and former CEO of Google's YouTube brand. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_childhood_and_education">Early childhood and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salar_Kamangar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early childhood and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Salar Kamangar (born in <a href="/wiki/Tehran" title="Tehran">Tehran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pahlavi_dynasty" title="Pahlavi dynasty">Imperial State of Iran</a>) holds a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a> in Biological Sciences with honors from <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> and was the 9th employee to join Google.<sup id="cite_ref-tmn_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tmn-2">[2]</a></sup> He joined after graduating from Stanford in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-tmn_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tmn-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Google">Google</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salar_Kamangar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Google">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>On October 29, 2010, it was announced that Salar "SK" Kamangar, who was in charge of day-to-day activities, would replace <a href="/wiki/Chad_Hurley" title="Chad Hurley">Chad Hurley</a> as <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a> of <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tmn_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tmn-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was replaced as CEO of YouTube on February 5, 2014. His successor at <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> is <a href="/wiki/Susan_Wojcicki" title="Susan Wojcicki">Susan Wojcicki</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>Prior to that, Kamangar created the company's first <a href="/wiki/Business_plan" title="Business plan">business plan</a> and was responsible for its legal and finance functions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> From there, he became a founding member of Google's product team,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> where he worked on consumer projects including the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/DejaNews" class="mw-redirect" title="DejaNews">DejaNews</a> and the subsequent launch of <a href="/wiki/Google_Groups" title="Google Groups">Google Groups</a>. </p>
