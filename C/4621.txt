         <h2 class="margin-top0">Agile planning with GitLab</h2> 
         <p> Development teams continue to accelerate value delivery with iterative, incremental, and lean project methodologies, such as Scrum, Kanban, and Extreme Programming (XP). Large enterprises have adopted Agile at enterprise scale through a variety of frameworks, including Scaled Agile Framework (SAFe), Spotify, and Large Scale Scrum (LeSS). GitLab enables teams to apply Agile practices and principles to organize and manage their work, whatever their chosen methodology. </p> <a href="/demo/" class="btn cta-btn orange margin-top20">Learn more</a>

          <li> <strong>Seamless:</strong> GitLab supports collaboration and visibility for Agile teams — from planning to deployment and beyond — with a single user experience and a common set of tools </li> 
          <li> <strong>Integrated:</strong> Manage projects in the same system where you perform your work </li> 
          <li> <strong>Scalable:</strong> Organize multiple Agile teams to achieve enterprise Agile scalability </li> 
          <li> <strong>Flexible:</strong> Customize out-of-the-box functionality to the needs of your methodology, whether you're rolling your own flavor of Agile or adopting a formal framework </li>

         <h2>Features</h2> 
         <ul> 
          <li> <strong>Issues:</strong> Start with an <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/project/issues/"> issue </a> that captures a single feature that delivers business value for users. </li> 
          <li> <strong>Tasks:</strong> Often, a user story is further separated into individual tasks. You can create a task <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/markdown.html#task-lists"> list </a> within an issue's description in GitLab, to further identify those individual tasks. </li> 
          <li> <strong>Issue boards:</strong> Everything is in one place. <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/project/issues/#issues-per-project"> Track </a> issues and communicate progress without switching between products. One interface to follow your issues from backlog to done. </li> 
          <li> <strong>Epics:</strong> Manage your portfolio of projects more efficiently and with less effort by tracking groups of issues that share a theme, across projects and milestones with <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/group/epics/"> epics. </a> </li> 
          <li> <strong>Milestones:</strong> Track issues and merge requests created to achieve a broader goal in a certain period of time with GitLab <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/project/milestones/"> milestones. </a> </li> 
          <li> <strong>Roadmaps:</strong> Start date and/or due date can be visualized in a form of a timeline. The epics <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/group/roadmap/"> roadmap </a> page shows such a visualization for all the epics which are under a group and/or its subgroups. </li> 
          <li> <strong>Labels:</strong> Create and assigned to individual issues, which then allows you to filter the issue lists by a single label or multiple <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/project/labels.html#prioritize-labels"> labels. </a> </li> 
          <li> <strong>Burndown Chart:</strong> Track work in real time, and mitigate risks as they arise. <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/project/milestones/burndown_charts.html"> Burndown </a> charts allow teams to visualize the work scoped in a current sprint as they are being completed. </li> 
          <li> <strong>Points and Estimation:</strong> Indicate the estimated effort with issues by assigning <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/project/issues/issue_weight.html"> weight </a> attributes and indicate estimated effort </li> 
          <li> <strong>Collaboration:</strong> The ability to <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/discussions/"> contribute </a> conversationally is offered throughout GitLab in issues, epics, merge requests, commits, and more! </li> 
          <li> <strong>Traceability:</strong> Align your team's issues with subsequent <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/project/merge_requests/">merge</a> requests that give you complete traceability from issue creation to end once the related pipeline passes. </li> 
          <li> <strong>Wikis:</strong> A system for documentation called <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/user/project/wiki/"> Wiki, </a> if you are wanting to keep your documentation in the same project where your code resides. </li> 
          <li> <strong>Enterprise Agile Frameworks:</strong> Large enterprises have adopted Agile at enterprise scale using a variety of frameworks. GitLab can support <a href="https://www.scaledagileframework.com/"> SAFe, </a> Spotify, <a href="https://www.disciplinedagiledelivery.com/"> Disciplined Agile Delivery </a> and more. </li> 
         </ul>

         <h1>An Agile iteration with GitLab</h1> 
         <h2>User stories ? GitLab issues</h2> 
         <p> In Agile, you often start with a user story that captures a single feature that delivers business value for users. In GitLab, a single issue within a project serves this purpose. </p> 
        </div>

         <h2>Task ? GitLab task lists</h2> 
         <p> Often, a user story is further separated into individual tasks. You can create a task list within an issue's description in GitLab, to further identify those individual tasks. </p> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <hr class="divider"> 
       <div class="row"> 
        <div class="col-xs-12 col-md-6 col-md-offset-1"> 
         <h2>Epics ? GitLab epics</h2> 
         <p> In the other direction, some Agile practitioners specify an abstraction above user stories, often called an epic, that indicates a larger user flow consisting of multiple features. In GitLab, an epic also contains a title and description, much like an issue, but it allows you to attach multiple child issues to it to indicate that hierarchy. </p> 
        </div> 
        <div class="hidden-xs hidden-sm col-md-4">

        <div class="col-xs-12 col-md-6"> 
         <h2>Product backlog ? GitLab issue lists and prioritized labels</h2> 
         <p> The product or business owners typically create these user stories to reflect the needs of the business and customers. They are prioritized in a product backlog to capture urgency and desired order of development. The product owner communicates with stakeholders to determine the priorities and constantly refines the backlog. In GitLab, there are dynamically generated issue lists which users can view to track their backlog. Labels can be created and assigned to individual issues, which then allows you to filter the issue lists by a single label or multiple labels. This allows for further flexibility. Priority labels can even be used to also order the issues in those lists. </p>
