              <h6><span class="twtr-color--gray-700"><span class="twtr-color--gray-600"><span class="twtr-type--bold-200r">What will you release in the future?</span></span></span></h6> 
              <p><span class="twtr-type--roman-200r">If and when we identify additional attempted information operations on Twitter in the future,&nbsp; our first priority is to enforce our rules and remove accounts engaged in attempts to manipulate the public conversation.&nbsp; Following these enforcements, we carry out thorough investigations of the accounts and individuals involved. We only disclose datasets once we have determined attribution, and once all applicable investigations have concluded. We may also release incremental additions to existing datasets if we believe the additional information could materially impact research findings.</span></p> 
              <p>&nbsp;</p> 
              <h6><span class="twtr-color--gray-700"><span class="twtr-color--gray-600"><span class="twtr-type--bold-200r">What’s included?</span></span></span></h6> 
              <p><span class="twtr-type--roman-200r">Platform manipulation that we can reliably attribute to a government or state linked actor is considered an information operation and is prohibited by the <a href="https://help.twitter.com/en/rules-and-policies/platform-manipulation" rel="nofollow">Twitter Rules</a>.</span></p> 
              <p><span class="twtr-type--roman-200r">&nbsp;</span></p> 
              <p><span class="twtr-type--roman-200r">These datasets are of a size that a degree of capability for large dataset analysis is required. You can download the datasets <a href="#1.3" rel="nofollow">below</a>. While no content has been redacted, some account-specific information has been hashed to protect account privacy.</span></p> 
              <p><span class="twtr-type--roman-200r">&nbsp;</span></p> 
              <p><span class="twtr-type--roman-200r">These datasets include profile information, Tweets and media (e.g., images and videos) from accounts we believe are connected to state&nbsp;linked information operations. Tweets and media which were deleted are not included in the datasets. Note that not all of the accounts we identified as connected to these campaigns actively Tweeted, so the number of accounts represented in the datasets may be less than the total number of accounts attributed to the information operation and enforced against.</span></p> 
              <p>&nbsp;</p> 
              <h6><span class="twtr-color--gray-700"><span class="twtr-color--gray-600"><span class="twtr-type--bold-200r">Why hash some of the information?</span></span></span></h6> 
              <p><span class="twtr-type--roman-200r">For accounts with fewer than 5,000 followers, we have hashed certain identifying fields (such as user ID and screen name) in the publicly-available version of the datasets. While we’ve taken every possible precaution to ensure there are no false positives in these datasets, we’ve hashed these fields to reduce the potential negative impact on authentic or compromised accounts — while still enabling longitudinal research, network analysis, and assessment of the underlying content created by these accounts.&nbsp;</span></p> 
              <p><span class="twtr-type--roman-200r">&nbsp;</span></p> 
              <p><span class="twtr-type--roman-200r">Specialist researchers can apply <a href="#1.4" rel="nofollow">below</a> for research access to an unhashed version of these datasets. Access to the unhashed version is governed by a data license agreement limiting usage of the unhashed datasets to research purposes, with provisions to ensure the researcher may only use the data in a limited manner and with appropriate security measures in place.</span></p> 
              <p>&nbsp;</p> 
              <h6><span class="twtr-color--gray-700"><span class="twtr-color--gray-600"><span class="twtr-type--bold-200r">What can I do if I believe I've been included here in error?</span></span></span></h6> 
              <p><span class="twtr-type--roman-200r">If you believe your account has been included in error, please log into your account and file a suspension appeal <a href="https://help.twitter.com/forms/general?subtopic=suspended" rel="nofollow">here</a>. We carefully review these cases, and will help restore potentially compromised accounts, or accounts that may have been included in error, to their owners.</span></p>
