      <p><i><b>Counter-Strike</b></i> (<i><b>CS</b></i>) is a series of <a href="/wiki/Multiplayer" class="mw-redirect" title="Multiplayer">multiplayer</a> <a href="/wiki/First-person_shooter" title="First-person shooter">first-person shooter</a> video games in which teams of <a href="/wiki/Terrorism" title="Terrorism">terrorists</a> battle to perpetrate an act of terror (bombing, hostage-taking, assassination) while <a href="/wiki/Counter-terrorism" title="Counter-terrorism">counter-terrorists</a> try to prevent it (bomb defusal, hostage rescue). The series began on <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Windows</a> in 1999 with the release of the first game, <i><a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike_(video_game)" title="Counter-Strike (video game)">Counter-Strike</a></i>. It was initially released as a <a href="/wiki/Game_mod" class="mw-redirect" title="Game mod">modification</a> ("mod") for <i><a href="/wiki/Half-Life_(video_game)" title="Half-Life (video game)">Half-Life</a></i> that was designed by <a href="/wiki/Minh_%22Gooseman%22_Le" class="mw-redirect" title="Minh &quot;Gooseman&quot; Le">Minh "Gooseman" Le</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jess_%22Cliffe%22_Cliffe" class="mw-redirect" title="Jess &quot;Cliffe&quot; Cliffe">Jess "Cliffe" Cliffe</a> before the rights to the mod's <a href="/wiki/Intellectual_property" title="Intellectual property">intellectual property</a> were acquired by <a href="/wiki/Valve_Corporation" title="Valve Corporation">Valve</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">developers</a> of <i>Half-Life</i>, who then turned <i>Counter-Strike</i> into a retail product. </p>
      <p>The original <i>Counter-Strike</i> was followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Condition_Zero" title="Counter-Strike: Condition Zero">Counter-Strike: Condition Zero</a></i>, developed by <a href="/wiki/Turtle_Rock_Studios" title="Turtle Rock Studios">Turtle Rock Studios</a> and released in March 2004. A previous version of <i>Condition Zero</i> that was developed by <a href="/wiki/Ritual_Entertainment" title="Ritual Entertainment">Ritual Entertainment</a> was released alongside it as <i>Condition Zero: Deleted Scenes</i>. Eight months later, Valve released <i><a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Source" title="Counter-Strike: Source">Counter-Strike: Source</a></i>, a remake of the original <i>Counter-Strike</i> and the first in the series to run on Valve's newly created <a href="/wiki/Source_(engine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Source (engine)">Source engine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Counter-Strike:_Source_beta_begins_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Counter-Strike:_Source_beta_begins-4">[4]</a></sup> The fourth game in the main series, <i><a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Global_Offensive" title="Counter-Strike: Global Offensive">Counter-Strike: Global Offensive</a></i>, was released by Valve in 2012 for Windows, <a href="/wiki/OS_X" class="mw-redirect" title="OS X">OS X</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a>, and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a>. <a href="/wiki/Hidden_Path_Entertainment" title="Hidden Path Entertainment">Hidden Path Entertainment</a>, who worked on <i>Counter-Strike: Source</i> post-release, helped to develop the game alongside Valve.<sup id="cite_ref-CSGOannouncement_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSGOannouncement-5">[5]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>There have been several third-party spin-off titles created for Asian markets over the years. These include the <i>Counter-Strike Online</i> series, <i>Counter-Strike Neo</i>, and <i>Counter-Strike Nexon: Studio</i>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span></h2> 
      <p><i>Counter-Strike</i> is an objective-based, <a href="/wiki/Multiplayer_video_game" title="Multiplayer video game">multiplayer</a> <a href="/wiki/First-person_shooter" title="First-person shooter">first-person shooter</a>. Two opposing teams—the Terrorists and the Counter Terrorists—compete in game modes to complete objectives, such as securing a location to plant or defuse a bomb and rescuing or guarding hostages.<sup id="cite_ref-Destructoid_Review_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Destructoid_Review-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> At the end of each round, players are rewarded based on their individual performance with in-game currency to spend on more powerful weapons in subsequent rounds. Winning rounds results in more money than losing, and completing objectives such as killing enemy players gives cash bonuses.<sup id="cite_ref-Destructoid_Review_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Destructoid_Review-6">[6]</a></sup> Uncooperative actions, such as <a href="/wiki/Friendly_fire" title="Friendly fire">killing teammates</a>, results in a penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-navimoney_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-navimoney-8">[8]</a></sup> </p>

      <p>Originally a modification for <i>Half-Life</i>, the rights to <i>Counter-Strike</i>, as well as the developers working on it, were acquired by Valve in 2000. </p>
      <p>The game received a port to <a href="/wiki/Xbox_(console)" title="Xbox (console)">Xbox</a> in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-Counter-Strike_Xbox_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Counter-Strike_Xbox-9">[9]</a></sup> It was also ported to OS X and Linux in the form of a beta in January 2013. A full release was published in April 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-Counter-Strike_OS_X_Linux_Beta_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Counter-Strike_OS_X_Linux_Beta-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Counter-Strike_OS_X_Linux_Release_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Counter-Strike_OS_X_Linux_Release-11">[11]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Condition_Zero"><span class="anchor" id="Counter-Strike:_Condition_Zero"></span> <i>Condition Zero</i></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Condition_Zero" title="Counter-Strike: Condition Zero">Counter-Strike: Condition Zero</a>
      </div> 
      <p><i>Counter-Strike</i> was followed-up with <i>Counter-Strike: Condition Zero</i>, developed by Turtle Rock Studios and released in 2004. It used the <i>Half-Life</i> <a href="/wiki/GoldSrc" title="GoldSrc">GoldSrc</a> engine, similar to its predecessor. Besides the multiplayer mode, it also included a single-player mode with a "full" campaign and bonus levels. The game received mixed reviews in contrast to its predecessor and was quickly followed with a further entry to the series titled <i><a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Source" title="Counter-Strike: Source">Counter-Strike: Source</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Source"><span class="anchor" id="Counter-Strike:_Source"></span> <i>Source</i></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Source" title="Counter-Strike: Source">Counter-Strike: Source</a>
      </div> 
      <p><i>Counter-Strike: Source</i> was the first publicly released game by Valve to run on the Source engine. <i>Counter-Strike: Source</i> was initially released as a <a href="/wiki/Betaware" class="mw-redirect" title="Betaware">beta</a> to members of the Valve Cyber Café Program on August 11, 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-Counter-Strike:_Source_beta_begins_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Counter-Strike:_Source_beta_begins-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-updatehistory_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-updatehistory-13">[13]</a></sup> On August 18, 2004, the beta was released to owners of <i><a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Condition_Zero" title="Counter-Strike: Condition Zero">Counter-Strike: Condition Zero</a></i> and those who had received a <i><a href="/wiki/Half-Life_2" title="Half-Life 2">Half-Life 2</a></i> voucher bundled with some <a href="/wiki/ATI_Technologies" title="ATI Technologies">ATI</a> <a href="/wiki/Radeon" title="Radeon">Radeon</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_card" title="Video card">video cards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> While the original release only included a version for Microsoft Windows, the game eventually received a port to OS X on June 23, 2010 with a Linux port afterwards in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-CSS_Mac_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSS_Mac-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CSS_Linux_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSS_Linux-16">[16]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_Offensive"><span class="anchor" id="Counter-Strike:_Global_Offensive"></span> <i>Global Offensive</i></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Global_Offensive" title="Counter-Strike: Global Offensive">Counter-Strike: Global Offensive</a>
      </div> 
      <p><i>Counter-Strike: Global Offensive</i> was the fourth release in the main, Valve-developed <i>Counter-Strike</i> series in 2012. Much like <i>Counter-Strike: Source</i> the game runs on the Source engine. It is available on Microsoft Windows, OSX, and Linux, as well as the Xbox 360 and PlayStation 3 consoles, and is backwards compatible on the Xbox One console. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spin-offs">Spin-offs</span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Neo"><span class="anchor" id="Counter-Strike:_Neo"></span> <i>Neo</i></span></h3> 
      <p>A Japanese arcade adaptation of <i>Counter-Strike</i>, the original <i>Half-Life</i> multiplayer modification. It is published by Namco, and runs on a <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> system.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The game involves anime-designed characters in a futuristic designed version of <i>Counter-Strike</i>. A selection of single-player missions, mini-games, and seasonal events were added to prolong the game's interest with players.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_series"><span class="anchor" id="Counter-Strike:_Online_series"></span> <i>Online</i> series</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike_Online" title="Counter-Strike Online">Counter-Strike Online</a>
      </div> 
      <p><i>Counter-Strike Online</i> is a free-to-play spin-off available in much of eastern Asia. It was developed by <a href="/wiki/Nexon" title="Nexon">Nexon</a>, with oversight from <a href="/wiki/Valve_Corporation" title="Valve Corporation">Valve</a>. It uses a <a href="/wiki/Micropayment" title="Micropayment">micropayment</a> model that is managed by a custom version of the <a href="/wiki/Steam_(service)" title="Steam (service)">Steam</a> <a href="/wiki/Front_and_back_ends#Computer_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Front and back ends">back-end</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gamasutra2008_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gamasutra2008-19">[19]</a></sup> Announced in 2012 and aimed at the Asian gaming market, a sequel titled <i>Counter-Strike Online 2</i> was developed by <a href="/wiki/Nexon" title="Nexon">Nexon</a> on the Source game engine, and released in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-CSOnline2Anounced_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSOnline2Anounced-20">[20]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nexon:_Studio"><span class="anchor" id="Counter-Strike_Nexon:_Studio"></span> <i>Nexon: Studio</i></span></h3> 
      <p>In August 2014, Nexon announced <i>Counter-Strike Nexon: Zombies</i>, a free-to-play, zombie-themed spin-off,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> developed on the GoldSrc game engine.<sup id="cite_ref-gamestar_nexon_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gamestar_nexon-22">[22]</a></sup> On September 23, 2014, an open beta was released on Steam.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The game launched on October 7, 2014, featuring 50 maps and 20 game modes.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The game features both <a href="/wiki/Player_versus_player" title="Player versus player">player versus player</a> modes such as <a href="/wiki/Team_deathmatch" class="mw-redirect" title="Team deathmatch">team deathmatch</a>, hostage rescue, bomb defusal, and <a href="/wiki/Player_versus_environment" title="Player versus environment">player versus environment</a> modes such as cooperative campaign missions and base defending.<sup id="cite_ref-rps_nexon_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rps_nexon-25">[25]</a></sup> Reception from critics was generally negative with criticism aimed at the game's poor user interface, microtransactions,<sup id="cite_ref-rps_nexon_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rps_nexon-25">[25]</a></sup> and dated graphics.<sup id="cite_ref-gamestar_nexon_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gamestar_nexon-22">[22]</a></sup> On October 30, 2019, <i>Counter-Strike Nexon: Zombies</i> was renamed to <i>Counter-Strike Nexon: Studio</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-steamdb_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-steamdb-26">[26]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Competitive_play">Competitive play</span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main article: <a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike_in_esports" title="Counter-Strike in esports">Counter-Strike in esports</a>
      </div> 
      <p><i>Counter-Strike</i> has had nearly 20 years of competitive play beginning with the original <i>Counter-Strike</i>. The first major tournament was hosted in 2001 at the <a href="/wiki/Cyberathlete_Professional_League" title="Cyberathlete Professional League">Cyberathlete Professional League</a>. Cyberathlete Professional League, along with <a href="/wiki/World_Cyber_Games" title="World Cyber Games">World Cyber Games</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Sports_World_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic Sports World Cup">Electronic Sports World Cup</a> were among the largest tournaments for the <i>Counter-Strike</i> series until 2007. From 2013 to 2020, Valve have hosted <a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Global_Offensive_Major_Championships" title="Counter-Strike: Global Offensive Major Championships"><i>Counter-Strike: Global Offensive</i> Major Championships</a> with large prizes. These have become the most prestigious tournaments in CS:GO.<sup id="cite_ref-espo_Espo_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espo_Espo-27">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-blog_AneS_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blog_AneS-28">[28]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span></h2> 
      <p><i>Counter-Strike</i> is considered one of the most influential first person shooters in history. The series has a large competitive community and has become synonymous with first person shooters.<sup id="cite_ref-espo_Espo_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espo_Espo-27">[27]</a></sup> As of August&nbsp;2011<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Counter-Strike&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the <i>Counter-Strike</i> franchise has sold over 25 million units.<sup id="cite_ref-Eddie_Makuch_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eddie_Makuch-29">[29]</a></sup> </p>
