      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="How_a_DYK_suggestion_makes_its_way_to_the_Main_Page">How a DYK suggestion makes its way to the Main Page</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Lore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: How a DYK suggestion makes its way to the Main Page">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>A DYK suggestion goes through five steps from <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#nominate" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">nomination</a> through appearing in the Did you know section on the <a href="/wiki/Main_Page" title="Main Page">Main Page</a> to removal from the Main Page. </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>First, an editor <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Submission" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Submission">nominates an article</a> by creating the appropriate subpage and transcluding it on <a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Did_you_know" title="Template talk:Did you know">Template talk:Did you know</a>. Discussions about individual suggestions also appear on the subpage, such as suggested improvements of the proposed DYK text (the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hook</a>), or comments about the eligibility of the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article</a> under the requirements (see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Article" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Article">WP:Did you know/Article</a>).</li> 
       <li>If the suggested DYK meets the requirements, any uninvolved editor may add the suggestion to a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#preparation_areas" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">DYK preparation page</a> and close the nomination subpage. In practice, to ensure that all suggestions are given fair consideration, the oldest suggestions listed on the suggestions page are selected first, to ensure that they don't go stale before they are chosen. DYK entries listed on a preparation area are not final, and may be edited or rejected by any other editor. If they are rejected, the nomination subpage discussion is reopened.</li> 
       <li>When there are about eight hooks (the prescribed number may change every few weeks) at a preparation area, an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ADMIN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ADMIN">administrator</a> moves the page on to one of the protected <a href="/wiki/T:DYK/Q" class="mw-redirect" title="T:DYK/Q">queues</a>. The administrator moving the suggestions to the queue may edit or reject any DYK entries at their <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators#Administrator_conduct" title="Wikipedia:Administrators">discretion</a>.</li> 
       <li>Approximately every 12 hours (other frequencies may be specified), <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKADMINBOT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKADMINBOT">WP:DYKADMINBOT</a> moves a page from one of the queue files onto the Main Page. So DYK entries appear on the Main Page for about twelve hours, with about two updates per day. DYK entries listed on the Main Page are not final. Non-admins may report errors to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ERRORS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ERRORS">Main Page errors page</a> so they can be dealt with. Admins can amend, edit, replace, and remove entries in the DYK template while it appears on the Main Page. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators#Reinstating_a_reverted_action_.28.22Wheel_warring.22.29" title="Wikipedia:Administrators">Wheel warring</a> prohibits one admin from undoing one another's administrative actions. In particular, wheel warring prohibits one admin from reinserting a DYK hook onto the Main Page that was removed by another admin. In one example, aggressive wheel warring over a Main Page DYK hook led to <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Administrators%27_noticeboard/Incidents&amp;diff=prev&amp;oldid=228022930">desysopping by Jimbo Wales</a>.</li> 
       <li>The bot <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKA">archives</a> old DYK entries that are removed from the Main Page, and gives credits (notices on nominators' talk pages).</li>
      </ol> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Errors">Errors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Lore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Errors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Notification of DYK errors regarding what is currently on the main page may be posted at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ERRORS#Errors_in_the_current_Did_you_know..." class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ERRORS">the <i>Errors in the current </i>Did you know...<i> section of WP:ERRORS</i></a>. DYK errors on the main page may be addressed by an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ADMIN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ADMIN">admin</a> through changes to the <a href="/wiki/T:DYK" class="mw-redirect" title="T:DYK">DYK template.</a> </p> 
      <dl>
       <dt>
        Admins
       </dt> 
       <dd></dd>
      </dl> 
      <ul>
       <li>If a factual error is reported when the hooks are on the front page, try to replace the hook with another fact from the article, rather than just removing it.</li> 
       <li>In the case it has to be removed, try to replace it with another hook from the suggestions page.</li> 
       <li>If it is the first hook and hence has an associated picture, you <i>must</i> replace it with another hook with a picture.</li>
      </ul>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overlooked_rules">Overlooked rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Lore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Overlooked rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable relarticle mainarticle selfreference noprint">
       This section is <a href="/wiki/Help:Transclusion" title="Help:Transclusion">transcluded</a> from <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Supplementary_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Supplementary guidelines">Wikipedia:Did you know/Supplementary guidelines</a>. <span class="plainlinks" style="font-style: normal"><span style="font-size:85%;">(<a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Supplementary_guidelines&amp;action=edit">edit</a> | <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Supplementary_guidelines&amp;action=history">history</a>)</span></span>

      <p>These rules are listed elsewhere, but they are often overlooked at Did You Know: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>There must be a space after the <a href="/wiki/Ellipsis" title="Ellipsis">ellipsis</a>.</li> 
       <li>The link to your article should be in <b>bold</b> (e.g., '''[[Manx cat]]''').</li> 
       <li>The hook should end with a question mark.</li> 
       <li>For a hook with an accompanying picture, the string <i>(pictured)</i> is all in italics, <i>including</i> the parentheses.</li> 
       <li>For titles or words with dashes see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DASH" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DASH">WP:DASH</a>.</li> 
       <li>For hooks containing numbers see <a href="/wiki/MOS:NUM#Numbers_as_figures_or_words" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:NUM">MOS:NUM#Numbers as figures or words</a>.</li> 
       <li>Make sure your article title conforms with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style_(titles)" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style (titles)">Wikipedia:Manual of Style (titles)</a></li> 
       <li>A 'new' article is no more than seven days old. This does not include articles split from older articles, although an article sufficiently expanded from a section of an older article can be a fivefold expansion. The word "fork" is sometimes used to mean <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Splitting" title="Wikipedia:Splitting">Wikipedia:Splitting</a>.</li> 
       <li>Fix redirects in hooks&nbsp;– see <a href="/wiki/MOS:DYKPIPE" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:DYKPIPE">MOS:DYKPIPE</a>.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTADVERTISING" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTADVERTISING">WP:NOTADVERTISING</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Instruction_creep">Instruction creep</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Lore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Instruction creep">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Did You Know can be criticized for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Instruction_creep" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Instruction creep">instruction creep</a>. But it is important to distinguish instruction creep from <i>documentation</i> of that instruction creep. Instruction creep is bad because it takes longer and longer to understand all those instructions before you can actually do anything. But documentation of instruction creep eases the process of trying to learn all the unwritten rules before you can do anything. A ten-pound law book is bad, but a thousand-pound library of precedents one needs in order to guess what the unwritten laws are, would be worse. Did You Know nominations are approved by different reviewers, and there isn't an easy way to get them all to behave predictably. </p>
      <p>For instance, when determining if a Did You Know article is long enough or expanded enough, we don't count the whole article. "Did You Know defines 'prose' to exclude infoboxes, categories, references, lists, tables, block quotes, headers, images and captions, the "See also" section if any, Table of Contents, edit buttons, "Citation needed" and similar superscripted text, and reference link numbers like [6]." Wouldn't it be easier to just count the whole article, and use the length Wikipedia provides on each page's history page? That would allow shorter articles, so let's say the 1,500 character limit is raised to about 2,500, or to whatever number would eliminate lowering the bar as a reason to count characters the hard way. That simplification hasn't been done, because it has been argued that a short article can be padded to reach the minimum, just by adding categories and such. But prose can also be padded (<a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Template_talk:Did_you_know&amp;diff=269691211&amp;oldid=269691093">example</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Poetic_justice" title="Poetic justice">poetic justice</a> for anyone who disagrees would be to fill his user talk page with 100 different ways to tediously repeat that point. So it's unclear that complicated character counting rules are worth all the extra effort of computing and debating them. </p>
      <p>But imagine how much worse that computing and debating effort was before the process was documented. If this simplification is desirable, it will happen when everyone can agree to follow a new, simpler rule, not by ignoring the existing rules so that no one but regulars can know what they are, or requiring weeks of studying <a href="/wiki/T:TDYK" class="mw-redirect" title="T:TDYK">the suggestions page</a> to get any idea if a submission would be accepted or not. </p>
      <p>These rules aren't intended to cover everything, and we even have a rule than says so: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/You_knew_this" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/You knew this">R6</a>. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTBUREAUCRACY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTBUREAUCRACY">WP:NOTBUREAUCRACY</a> says "Written rules do not themselves set accepted practice, but rather document already existing community consensus ... " </p>
      <p>In conclusion, the only thing worse than a million written rules, is a million unwritten rules. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Daily_headings_on_the_suggestions_page">Daily headings on the suggestions page</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Lore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Daily headings on the suggestions page">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/T:TDYK" class="mw-redirect" title="T:TDYK">T:TDYK</a> has daily headings like "Articles created/expanded on February 18", with nominations as subheadings. We don't have an automated system to maintain the daily headings yet. So as <a href="/wiki/Universal_Time" title="Universal Time">Universal Time</a> midnight approaches, someone should add a heading for the next day, near the top. <a href="/wiki/T:TDYK#Older_nominations" class="mw-redirect" title="T:TDYK">T:TDYK#Older nominations</a> should be moved down a day, so it will always be seven days old. And if it's below the "Older nominations" header, the last daily heading near the bottom of the page can be removed when it contains no more nominations, except for nominations with unsatisfied objections. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recognition">Recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Lore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>The article, article's creator(s), and the DYK nominator may be recognized as contributing to DYK through the credit templates posted by DYK on user talk pages. When an article is first nominated for DYK, the hook may be followed by (i) 'article by XXX; nom by YYY' or (ii) 'self-nom.' These help DYK determine which user talk pages to post credits. For instructions on providing recognition, see <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_1#Credits" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 1">DYK credits.</a> </p>
      <p>The following templates are used to credit the article creator and the article nominator as well as give notice on the article talk page that the article appeared on the Main Page: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>Article creator's talk page:</b> <i>({{<a href="/wiki/Template:UpdatedDYK" title="Template:UpdatedDYK">UpdatedDYK</a>}})</i> <tt>{{subst:UpdatedDYK|5 June|2021|Article name}} --~~~~</tt></li> 
       <li><b>Nominator's talk page:</b> <i>({{<a href="/wiki/Template:UpdatedDYKNom" title="Template:UpdatedDYKNom">UpdatedDYKNom</a>}})</i> <tt>{{subst:UpdatedDYKNom|5 June|2021|Article name}} --~~~~<b></b></tt></li> 
       <li><b>Article talk page:</b> <i>({{<a href="/wiki/Template:Dyktalk" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:Dyktalk">dyktalk</a>}})</i> <tt>{{dyktalk|5 June|2021}}</tt> (check if small style templates in use, if so add small=yes parm)</li>
      </ul>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_Did_You_Know">History of Did You Know</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Lore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: History of Did You Know">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>DYK made its first Main Page appearance on <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Template:Did_you_know&amp;oldid=2500457">February 22, 2004</a>. The article, <a href="/wiki/Pencil_sharpener" title="Pencil sharpener">pencil sharpener</a>, was developed by <a href="/wiki/User:Raul654" title="User:Raul654">Raul654</a> who had been an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Editors" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Editors">editor</a> with Wikipedia for about six months at that time and now is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Bureaucrats" title="Wikipedia:Bureaucrats">bureaucrat</a>. An April 2004 <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://web.archive.org/web/20040403171050/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Main_Page">screen shot</a> shows DYK located in the space now occupied by In the News. Credit recognition for article creators started on <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Template:UpdatedDYK&amp;diff=prev&amp;oldid=8361278">November 24, 2004</a>, DYK began placing DYK notifications on article talk pages on <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Template:Dyktalk&amp;diff=prev&amp;oldid=35006381">January 13, 2006</a>, and nominators started receiving credit on <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Template:UpdatedDYKNom&amp;diff=prev&amp;oldid=52932552">May 13, 2006.</a> </p>
