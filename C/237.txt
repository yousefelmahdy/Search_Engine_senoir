       <h1 class="s-page-title--header"> What is "meta"? How does it work? </h1> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <div class="s-prose js-post-body w100"> 
      <div class="js-edit-hide col-section">
       <p>Meta Stack Exchange is the part of the site where users discuss the workings and policies of Stack Overflow the company and the software that runs the Stack Exchange Q&amp;A network. It is separated from the main Q&amp;A to reduce noise there while providing a legitimate space for people to ask how and why this site works the way it does. Meta is for...</p> 
       <ul> 
        <li><strong>...Stack Exchange users to communicate with each other</strong> about the Stack Exchange network (asking questions about how the websites work, or about policies and community decisions)</li> 
        <li><strong>...Stack Exchange users to communicate with Stack Overflow, the company</strong> (posting bugs, suggesting improvements, or proposing new features), and</li> 
        <li><strong>...Stack Overflow, the company, to communicate with the community</strong> (soliciting feedback on new ideas or features, or discussing policies that affect the whole network)</li> 
       </ul> 
       <p>Please <a href="https://meta.stackexchange.com/search" rel="nofollow noreferrer">look around</a> to see if your question has been asked before, and avoid asking questions that have nothing to do with the Stack Exchange network. This is not a random discussion area; rather, it's a place for improving our community and website, together.</p> 
       <h3>Voting is different on meta.</h3> 
       <p>Like normal Stack Exchange sites, Meta allows members to vote on questions and answers. For most posts, votes reflect the perceived usefulness: well-written, well-reasoned, well-researched posts tend to get more attention and more upvotes. Highly-voted and frequently-linked posts may become part of the <a href="https://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/tagged/faq" rel="nofollow noreferrer">community-curated FAQ</a> or codified as part of the site’s <a href="/helpcenter">Help</a> pages.</p> 
       <p><i>Unlike</i> normal Stack Exchange sites, Meta invites the community to discuss, debate and propose changes to the way the community itself behaves, as well as how the software itself works. <strong>On posts tagged <a href="https://meta.stackexchange.com/tags/feature-request" rel="nofollow noreferrer">feature-request</a>, voting indicates agreement or disagreement</strong> with the proposed change rather than just the quality or usefulness of the post itself.</p> 
       <h3>Meta has required tags.</h3> 
       <p>On the main site, we ask that you avoid using <a href="http://blog.stackoverflow.com/2010/08/the-death-of-meta-tags/">meta-tags</a>. </p> 
       <p>Because meta-discussion sites are different and serve multiple purposes, some meta-tags are okay, and even required. Each meta question is required to have one of the following tags: </p> 
       <ul> 
        <li><code>[support]</code>, which indicates a request for assistance with one of the site's features.</li> 
        <li><code>[bug]</code>, denoting a reproducible problem on the site that you believe is due to a mistake, malfunction, or programming error</li> 
        <li><code>[feature-request]</code>, for a proposal for a new feature on the site, or requests a change to an existing feature.</li> 
        <li><code>[discussion]</code>, for posts that may not have a clear-cut right or wrong answer and are often subjective. If it's not a bug or feature request, it is probably a discussion. </li> 
       </ul>
