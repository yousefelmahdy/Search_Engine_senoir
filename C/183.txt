      <h2 class="glue-headline glue-headline--headline-5">24/7 Clean Energy: Every hour. Everywhere.</h2> 
      <div class="glue-headline">
       <p>Our mission is to organize the world’s information. Every Google search you do requires a tiny amount of energy used by the servers in our data centers. With millions of searches per minute and trillions per year, it adds up to a whole lot of energy. Our mission won’t change, but we recognized early on that the energy we use to deliver our services must.</p> 
       <p>Why is energy important to Google? Reliable electricity supply from our data centers is the fuel that enables us to deliver your search results, emails, YouTube video plays, Cloud Platform services, and much more. And the emissions associated with our electricity consumption have comprised the majority of our carbon footprint.</p> 
       <p>We've worked hard to address our climate impacts. It began in 2007 when we became the first company to go carbon neutral -- offsetting as much carbon as our operations generated. In the process, we worked to make Google data centers some of the <a href="https://blog.google/outreach-initiatives/sustainability/data-centers-energy-efficient/#:~:text=The%20new%20study%20shows%20that,during%20the%20same%20time%20period." rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">most energy efficient in the world</a>. In 2017, we became the <a href="https://www.blog.google/outreach-initiatives/environment/meeting-our-match-buying-100-percent-renewable-energy/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">first company of our size to match 100%</a> of our annual electricity consumption with renewable energy. Today, we are the world's largest annual corporate purchaser of renewable energy.</p> 
       <p>But we're not stopping there. Our 100% renewable match was only an interim solution, and the urgency of the climate challenge demands a bigger and bolder vision. That’s why we’ve announced a first-of-its-kind target to achieve <a href="https://blog.google/outreach-initiatives/sustainability/our-third-decade-climate-action-realizing-carbon-free-future" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">24/7 Carbon-Free Energy by 2030</a>. We’re carving a path forward to fully decarbonize our electricity supply and operate on carbon-free energy <em>entirely</em>. Clean energy every hour, every day, everywhere. Read more about how we’re taking on this challenge in our <a href="https://www.gstatic.com/gumdrop/sustainability/247-carbon-free-energy.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">white paper</a>.</p>

      <h2 class="glue-headline glue-headline--headline-5">Accelerating the transition to carbon-free energy</h2> 
      <div class="glue-headline">
       <p>Round-the-clock clean energy is something that’s never been done before, but at Google we never shy away from a challenge. To make 24/7 clean energy a reality for us, we need to help revolutionize energy for everyone. How? In 2019, we made the<a href="https://blog.google/outreach-initiatives/sustainability/our-biggest-renewable-energy-purchase-ever/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"> biggest corporate purchase of renewable energy in history at the time</a>. We work with partners to help demonstrate and commercialize new technologies. We <a href="https://www.blog.google/outreach-initiatives/sustainability/let-sunshine-opening-market-more-renewable-energy-asia/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">develop and advocate for policies</a> to clean the electricity grid. We continuously apply our <a href="https://blog.google/inside-google/infrastructure/data-centers-work-harder-sun-shines-wind-blows" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">own software and machine learning to optimize clean energy</a> across the industry. And we share our learnings and techniques so other companies can follow suit. It’s going to take all hands on deck to get it done, and it starts with us.</p>
