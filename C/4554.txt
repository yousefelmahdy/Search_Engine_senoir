      <p><b>Conflict of interest</b> (<b>COI</b>) <b>editing</b> involves contributing to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a> about yourself, family, friends, clients, employers, or your financial and other relationships. Any <a href="#What_is_conflict_of_interest?">external relationship</a> can trigger a <a href="/wiki/Conflict_of_interest" title="Conflict of interest">conflict of interest</a>. That someone has a conflict of interest is a description of a situation, not a judgement about that person's opinions, integrity, or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GOODFAITH" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:GOODFAITH">good faith</a>. </p>
      <p>COI editing is strongly discouraged on Wikipedia. It undermines public confidence and risks causing <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedia_is_in_the_real_world" title="Wikipedia:Wikipedia is in the real world">public embarrassment</a> to the individuals and companies being promoted. Editors with a COI are sometimes <a href="#Why_is_conflict_of_interest_a_problem?">unaware of whether or how much</a> it has influenced their editing. If COI editing causes disruption, an administrator may opt to place blocks on the involved accounts. </p>
      <p>Editors with a COI, including <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PAY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PAY">paid editors</a>, are expected to <a href="#howtodisclose">disclose it</a> whenever they seek to change an affected article's content. Anyone editing for pay <a href="#COIPAYDISCLOSE">must disclose who is paying them</a>, who the client is, and any other relevant affiliation; this is a requirement of the <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Wikimedia Foundation">Wikimedia Foundation</a>. COI editors are strongly discouraged from editing affected articles directly, and can propose changes on article talk pages instead. However, our <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLP">policy on matters relating to living people</a> allows very obvious errors to be fixed quickly, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLPSELF" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLPSELF">including by the subject</a>. </p>
      <p>When investigating COI editing, do not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OUTING" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:OUTING">reveal the identity</a> of editors against their wishes. Wikipedia's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:HARASS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:HARASS">policy against harassment</a>, and in particular the prohibition against disclosing personal information, takes precedence over this guideline. To report COI editing, follow the advice at <a href="#How_to_handle_conflicts_of_interest">How to handle conflicts of interest</a>, below. Editors making or discussing changes to this guideline or related guidance shall disclose whether they have been paid to edit Wikipedia. </p>

      <h2><span id="Wikipedia.27s_position"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Wikipedia's_position">Wikipedia's position</span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose_of_Wikipedia">Purpose of Wikipedia</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_Wikipedia_is_not" title="Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not">Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not</a>
      </div> 
      <p>As an encyclopedia, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Five_pillars" title="Wikipedia:Five pillars">Wikipedia's mission</a> is to provide the public with articles that summarize accepted knowledge, written neutrally and sourced reliably. Readers expect to find neutral articles written independently of their subject, not corporate or personal webpages, or platforms for advertising and self-promotion. Articles should contain only material that complies with Wikipedia's content policies and best practices, and Wikipedians must place the interests of the encyclopedia and its readers above personal concerns. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="COI_editing">COI editing</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Plain_and_simple_conflict_of_interest_guide" title="Wikipedia:Plain and simple conflict of interest guide">Wikipedia:Plain and simple conflict of interest guide</a>
      </div> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:COIEDIT"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:COIEDIT&amp;redirect=no">WP:COIEDIT</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Editors with a COI should follow Wikipedia policies and best practices scrupulously: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>you should <b><a href="#howtodisclose">disclose</a> your COI</b> when involved with affected articles;</li> 
       <li>you are <b>strongly discouraged</b> from editing affected articles directly;</li> 
       <li>you may <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_requests" title="Wikipedia:Edit requests">propose changes</a></b> on talk pages (by using the <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Request_edit" title="Template:Request edit">request edit</a>}}</code> template), or by posting a note at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:COIN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:COIN">COI noticeboard</a>, so that they can be peer reviewed;</li> 
       <li>you should put new articles through the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AFC">Articles for Creation (AfC)</a> process instead of creating them directly;</li> 
       <li>you should <b>not act as a reviewer</b> of affected article(s) at AfC, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPP">new pages patrol</a> or elsewhere;</li> 
       <li>you should <b>respect other editors</b> by keeping discussions concise.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Note that no one on Wikipedia <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OWN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:OWN">controls articles</a>. If Wikipedia hosts an article about you or your organization, others may add information that would otherwise remain little known. They may also decide to delete the article or decide to keep it should you later request deletion. The media has several times drawn attention to companies that engage in COI editing on Wikipedia (see <a href="/wiki/Conflict-of-interest_editing_on_Wikipedia" title="Conflict-of-interest editing on Wikipedia">Conflict-of-interest editing on Wikipedia</a>), which has led to embarrassment for the organization concerned. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Paid_editing">Paid editing<span class="anchor" id="Paid_editing"></span><span class="anchor" id="Paid_advocacy"></span></span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:PE"></span><span id="WP:PAY"></span><span id="WP:NOPR"></span><span id="WP:NOPAY"></span><span id="WP:FCOI"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PE&amp;redirect=no">WP:PE</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PAY&amp;redirect=no">WP:PAY</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:NOPR&amp;redirect=no">WP:NOPR</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:NOPAY&amp;redirect=no">WP:NOPAY</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:FCOI&amp;redirect=no">WP:FCOI</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>An editor has a financial conflict of interest when they write about a topic with which they have a close financial relationship. This includes being an owner, employee, contractor, investor or other stakeholder. </p>
      <p>Being paid to contribute to Wikipedia is one form of financial COI; it places the paid editor in a conflict between their employer's goals and Wikipedia's goals. The kind of paid editing of most concern to the community involves using Wikipedia for public relations and marketing purposes. Sometimes called "paid advocacy", this is problematic because it invariably reflects the interests of the client or employer. </p>
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Wikimedia Foundation">Wikimedia Foundation</a> requires that all paid editing be disclosed. Additionally, <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Linking_to_external_advertising_accounts" class="extiw" title="meta:Linking to external advertising accounts">global policy</a> requires that (if applicable) you <b>must</b> provide links on your user-page to <b>all</b> active accounts on external websites through which you advertise, solicit or obtain paid editing. If you receive or expect to receive compensation (money, goods or services) for your contributions to Wikipedia, the policy on the English Wikipedia is: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>you must <b><a href="#COIPAYDISCLOSE">disclose</a></b> who is paying you, on whose behalf the edits are made, and any other relevant affiliation;</li> 
       <li>you should make the disclosure on your user page, <b>on affected talk pages</b>, and whenever you discuss the topic;</li> 
       <li>you are <b> strongly discouraged</b> from editing affected articles directly;</li> 
       <li>you may <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_requests" title="Wikipedia:Edit requests">propose changes</a></b> on talk pages by using the <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Request_edit" title="Template:Request edit">request edit</a>}}</code> template or by posting a note at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:COIN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:COIN">COI noticeboard</a>, so that they can be peer reviewed;</li> 
       <li>you should put new articles through the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AFC">Articles for Creation (AfC)</a> process instead of creating them directly;</li> 
       <li>you must <b>not act as a reviewer</b> of affected article(s) at AfC, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPP">new pages patrol</a> or elsewhere;</li> 
       <li>you should <b>respect volunteers</b> by keeping discussions concise (see <a href="#Paid_editors_on_talk_pages">PAYTALK</a>).</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Requested edits are subject to the same standards as any other, and editors may decline to act on them. To find an article's talk page, click the "talk" button at the top of the article. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TEAHOUSE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TEAHOUSE">WP:TEAHOUSE</a> if you have questions about these things. If you are an administrator, you <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TOOLMISUSE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TOOLMISUSE">must not use administrative tools for any paid-editing activity</a> (except when related to work as a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedian-in-residence" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Wikipedian-in-residence">Wikipedian-in-residence</a>, or as someone paid by the Wikimedia Foundation or an affiliate). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wikimedia_Foundation_terms_of_use">Wikimedia Foundation terms of use<span class="anchor" id="terms"></span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Paid-contribution_disclosure" title="Wikipedia:Paid-contribution disclosure">Wikipedia:Paid-contribution disclosure</a>
      </div> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Wikimedia Foundation">Wikimedia Foundation</a>'s <a href="https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Terms_of_Use#4._Refraining_from_Certain_Activities" class="extiw" title="wmf:Terms of Use">terms of use</a> require that editors who are being paid for their contributions disclose their <i>employer</i> (the person or organization who is paying for the edits); the <i>client</i> (the person or organization on whose behalf the edits are made); and any other relevant <i>affiliation</i>. This is the policy of the English Wikipedia. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="How_to_disclose_a_COI">How to disclose a COI<span class="anchor" id="howtodisclose"></span><span class="anchor" id="Declaring_an_interest"></span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="General_COI">General COI</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:DISCLOSE"></span><span id="WP:DCOI"></span>
      </div>

      <h2><span id="What_is_conflict_of_interest.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="What_is_conflict_of_interest?">What is conflict of interest?</span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="External_roles_and_relationships">External roles and relationships<span class="anchor" id="External_relationships"></span></span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:EXTERNALREL"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:EXTERNALREL&amp;redirect=no">WP:EXTERNALREL</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>While editing Wikipedia, an editor's primary role is to further the interests of the encyclopedia. When an external role or relationship could reasonably be said to undermine that primary role, the editor has a conflict of interest (similar to how a judge's primary role as an impartial adjudicator is undermined if they are married to the defendant.) </p>
      <p>Any external relationshippersonal, religious, political, academic, legal, or financial (including holding a <a href="/wiki/Cryptocurrency" title="Cryptocurrency">cryptocurrency</a>)can trigger a COI. How close the relationship needs to be before it becomes a concern on Wikipedia is governed by common sense. For example, an article about a band should not be written by the band's manager, and a biography should not be an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Autobiography" title="Wikipedia:Autobiography">autobiography</a> or written by the subject's spouse. There can be a COI when writing on behalf of a competitor or opponent of the page subject, just as there is when writing on behalf of the page subject. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Subject-matter_expert" title="Subject-matter expert">Subject-matter experts</a> (SMEs) are welcome on Wikipedia within their areas of expertise, subject to the guidance below on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FCOI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FCOI">financial conflict of interest</a> and on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SELFCITE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SELFCITE">citing your work</a>. SMEs are expected to make sure that their external roles and relationships in their field of expertise do not interfere with their primary role on Wikipedia. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="COI_is_not_simply_bias">COI is not simply bias<span class="anchor" id="notbias"></span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ADVOCACY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ADVOCACY">WP:ADVOCACY</a>
      </div> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:COINOTBIAS"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:COINOTBIAS&amp;redirect=no">WP:COINOTBIAS</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Determining that someone has a COI is a <b>description of a situation</b>. It is not a judgment about that person's state of mind or integrity. A COI can exist in the absence of bias, and bias regularly exists in the absence of a COI. Beliefs and desires may lead to biased editing, but they do not constitute a COI. COI emerges from an editor's roles and relationships, and the <i>tendency to bias</i> that we assume exists when those roles and relationships conflict. </p> 
      <h3><span id="Why_is_conflict_of_interest_a_problem.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Why_is_conflict_of_interest_a_problem?">Why is conflict of interest a problem?</span></h3> 
      <p>On Wikipedia, editors with a conflict of interest who unilaterally add material tend to violate Wikipedia's content and behavioral policies and guidelines. The content they add is typically unsourced or poorly sourced and often violates the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPOV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPOV">neutral point of view</a> policy by being <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PUFF" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PUFF">promotional</a> and omitting negative information. They may <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_warring" title="Wikipedia:Edit warring">edit war</a> to retain content that serves their external interest. They may overuse <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PRIMARY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PRIMARY">primary sources</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:INDEPENDENT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:INDEPENDENT">non-independent sources</a>, and they may give too much <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WEIGHT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:WEIGHT">weight</a> to certain ideas. </p> 
      <h3><span id="Actual.2C_potential_and_apparent_COI"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Actual,_potential_and_apparent_COI">Actual, potential and apparent COI</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:ACTUALCOI"></span><span id="WP:POTENTIALCOI"></span><span id="WP:APPARENTCOI"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:ACTUALCOI&amp;redirect=no">WP:ACTUALCOI</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:POTENTIALCOI&amp;redirect=no">WP:POTENTIALCOI</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:APPARENTCOI&amp;redirect=no">WP:APPARENTCOI</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="actualCOI"></span>An <b>actual COI</b> exists when an editor has a COI with respect to a certain judgment <i>and</i> is in a position where the judgment must be exercised. </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        Example: A business owner has an actual COI if they edit articles and engage in discussions about that business.
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="potentialCOI"></span>A <b>potential COI</b> exists when an editor has a COI with respect to a certain judgment <i>but is not</i> in a position where the judgment must be exercised. </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        Example: A business owner has a potential COI with respect to articles and discussions about that business, but they have no actual COI if they stay away from those pages.
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="apparentCOI"></span>An <b>apparent COI</b> exists when there is reason to believe that an editor has a COI. </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        Example: Editors have an apparent COI if they edit an article about a business, and for some reason they appear to be the business owner or in communication with the business owner, although they may actually have no such connection. Apparent COI raises concern within the community and should be resolved through discussion whenever possible.
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dealing_with_edit_requests_from_COI_or_paid_editors">Dealing with edit requests from COI or paid editors<span class="anchor" id="Responding"></span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_requests" title="Wikipedia:Edit requests">Wikipedia:Edit requests</a>
      </div> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Responding_to_requests">Responding to requests</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:COIRESPONSE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:COIRESPONSE&amp;redirect=no">WP:COIRESPONSE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Editors responding to edit requests from COI or paid editors are expected to do so carefully, particularly when commercial interests are involved. When large amounts of text are added to an article on behalf of the article subject, the article has, in effect, been <a href="/wiki/Ghostwriter" title="Ghostwriter">ghostwritten</a> by the subject without the readers' knowledge. Responding volunteers should therefore carefully check the proposed text and sources. That an article has been expanded does not mean that it is better. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Make sure the proposed paid text complies with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WEIGHT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:WEIGHT">WP:WEIGHT</a>.</li> 
       <li>Look for unnecessary detail that may have been added to overwhelm something negative.</li> 
       <li>Make sure nothing important is missing. Responding editors should do their own search for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:INDY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:INDY">independent sources</a>. <b>Do not rely on the sources offered by the paid editor.</b></li> 
       <li>Look for non-neutral language and unsourced or poorly sourced content.</li> 
       <li>Be cautious about accepting content based on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SPS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SPS">self-published sources</a> such as a personal website, or <a href="/wiki/Primary_source" title="Primary source">primary sources</a> such as a company website or press release.</li> 
       <li>If the paid text is added to the article, the edit summary should include full attribution; for example: "Text inserted on behalf of paid editor User:X; copied from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Draft:Paid_draft&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Draft:Paid draft (page does not exist)">Draft:Paid draft</a>." See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:COIATTRIBUTE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:COIATTRIBUTE">WP:COIATTRIBUTE</a> below.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Attribution_in_edit_summaries">Attribution in edit summaries<span class="anchor" id="Attribution"></span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copying_within_Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia:Copying within Wikipedia">Wikipedia:Copying within Wikipedia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copying_text_from_other_sources" title="Wikipedia:Copying text from other sources">Wikipedia:Copying text from other sources</a>
      </div> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:COIATTRIBUTE"></span><span id="WP:PAIDATTRIBUTE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:COIATTRIBUTE&amp;redirect=no">WP:COIATTRIBUTE</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PAIDATTRIBUTE&amp;redirect=no">WP:PAIDATTRIBUTE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>If editors choose to add material to an article on behalf of a COI or paid editor, they must provide <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ATTREQ" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ATTREQ">attribution</a> for the text in the <a href="/wiki/Help:Edit_summary" title="Help:Edit summary">edit summary</a>. The edit summary should include the name of the COI or paid editor, a link to the draft or edit request, and that the edit contains a COI or paid contribution. For example: "Text inserted on behalf of paid editor User:X; copied from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Draft:Paid_draft&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Draft:Paid draft (page does not exist)">Draft:Paid draft</a>." This transparency helps editors and readers to determine the extent of COI influence on the article. It also complies with copyright requirements. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Paid_editors_on_talk_pages">Paid editors on talk pages<span class="anchor" id="talk"></span></span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:COITALK"></span><span id="WP:PAYTALK"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:COITALK&amp;redirect=no">WP:COITALK</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PAYTALK&amp;redirect=no">WP:PAYTALK</a></span></li>

      </div> 
      <p>Paid editors must respect the volunteer nature of the project and keep discussions concise. When proposing changes to an article, they should describe the suggested modifications and explain why the changes should be made. Any changes that may be contentious, such as removal of negative text, should be highlighted. </p>
      <p>Before being drawn into long exchanges with paid editors, volunteers should be aware that paid editors may be submitting evidence of their talk-page posts to justify their salaries or fees. No editor should be expected to engage in long or repetitive discussions with someone who is being paid to argue with them. </p>
      <p>Editors who refuse to accept a consensus by arguing <i><a href="/wiki/Ad_nauseam" title="Ad nauseam">ad nauseam</a></i> may find themselves in violation of the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disruptive_editing" title="Wikipedia:Disruptive editing">disruptive-editing guideline</a>. </p>

      <p>Paid editors must ensure that they own the copyright of text they have been paid to add to Wikipedia; otherwise, they are unable to release it. A text's author is normally assumed to be the copyright holder. Companies sometimes provide paid editors with text written by someone else. Alternatively, a paid editor might write text for Wikipedia within the scope of their employment (a "<a href="/wiki/Work_for_hire" title="Work for hire">work for hire</a>"), in which case copyright resides with the employer. </p>
      <p>Where there is doubt that the paid editor owns the copyright, they (or the employer or author) are advised to forward a release from the copyright holder to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Volunteer_Response_Team" title="Wikipedia:Volunteer Response Team">Volunteer Response Team</a> (<kbd><span class="nowrap">permissions-en<img alt="@" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/15px-At_sign.svg.png" decoding="async" title="@" width="15" height="15" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/23px-At_sign.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/30px-At_sign.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="145" data-file-height="145">wikimedia.org</span></kbd>). See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PERMISSION" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PERMISSION">WP:PERMISSION</a> for how to do this and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Declaration_of_consent_for_all_enquiries" title="Wikipedia:Declaration of consent for all enquiries">Wikipedia:Declaration of consent for all enquiries</a> for a sample letter. </p>
      <p>If editors choose to add material to an article on behalf of a paid editor, they must provide <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ATTREQ" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ATTREQ">attribution</a> for the text in the <a href="/wiki/Help:Edit_summary" title="Help:Edit summary">edit summary</a>. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:COIATTRIBUTE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:COIATTRIBUTE">WP:COIATTRIBUTE</a> for how to do this. </p>

      <h3><span id="US:_Federal_Trade_Commission.2C_state_law.2C_and_native_advertising"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="US:_Federal_Trade_Commission,_state_law,_and_native_advertising">US: Federal Trade Commission, state law, and native advertising</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Native_advertising" title="Native advertising">Native advertising</a>, <a href="/wiki/Consumer_protection" title="Consumer protection">Consumer protection</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-consumer_advertising" title="Direct-to-consumer advertising">Direct-to-consumer advertising</a>
      </div> 
      <p>All editors are expected to follow United States law on undisclosed advertising, which is described by the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">Federal Trade Commission</a> (FTC) at <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ftc.gov/os/2009/10/091005revisedendorsementguides.pdf"><i>Endorsement Guidelines</i></a> and <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.ftc.gov/sites/default/files/attachments/press-releases/ftc-staff-revises-online-advertising-disclosure-guidelines/130312dotcomdisclosures.pdf">Dot Com Disclosures</a></i>. The FTC regards advertising as deceptive if it mimics a content format, such as a news report, that appears to come from an independent, impartial source: </p> 
      <div class="floatright">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Seal_of_the_United_States_Federal_Trade_Commission.svg" class="image"><img alt="Seal of the United States Federal Trade Commission.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Seal_of_the_United_States_Federal_Trade_Commission.svg/100px-Seal_of_the_United_States_Federal_Trade_Commission.svg.png" decoding="async" width="100" height="100" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Seal_of_the_United_States_Federal_Trade_Commission.svg/150px-Seal_of_the_United_States_Federal_Trade_Commission.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Seal_of_the_United_States_Federal_Trade_Commission.svg/200px-Seal_of_the_United_States_Federal_Trade_Commission.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="720" data-file-height="720"></a>
      </div> 
      <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>Marketers and publishers are using innovative methods to create, format, and deliver digital advertising. One form is "native advertising", content that bears a similarity to the news, feature articles, product reviews, entertainment, and other material that surrounds it online.&nbsp;...</p>
       <div class="paragraphbreak" style="margin-top:0.5em"></div>
       <p>In digital media, native ads often resemble the design, style, and functionality of the media in which they are disseminated.&nbsp;... The more a native ad is similar in format and topic to content on the publisher's site, the more likely that a disclosure will be necessary to prevent deception. Federal Trade Commission, 2015</p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>To judge whether an ad is deceptive under the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission_Act_of_1914" title="Federal Trade Commission Act of 1914">Federal Trade Commission Act of 1914</a>, the FTC considers "both what the ad says and the format it uses to convey that information&nbsp;... Advertisements or promotional messages are deceptive if they convey to consumers expressly or by implication that theyre independent, impartial, or from a source other than the sponsoring advertiser&nbsp;...". </p>
      <p>State law may have similar prohibitions. While the FTC law may apply only to interstate and foreign commerce, state law applies to intrastate commerce and must be obeyed. At least one state court case found liability for an ad disguised as editorial content. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_fair-trading_law">European fair-trading law</span></h3> 
      <div class="floatright">
       <a href="/wiki/File:No_ads.svg" class="image"><img alt="No ads.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/No_ads.svg/100px-No_ads.svg.png" decoding="async" width="100" height="100" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/No_ads.svg/150px-No_ads.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/No_ads.svg/200px-No_ads.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="3369" data-file-height="3369"></a>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Unfair_Commercial_Practices_Directive" class="mw-redirect" title="Unfair Commercial Practices Directive">Unfair Commercial Practices Directive</a>
      </div> 
      <p>In 2012 the Munich <a href="/wiki/Oberlandesgericht" title="Oberlandesgericht">Oberlandesgericht</a> court ruled that if a company or its agents edit Wikipedia with the aim of influencing customers, the edits constitute covert advertising, and as such are a violation of European fair-trading law. The ruling stated that readers cannot be expected to seek out user and talk pages to find editors' disclosures about their corporate affiliation. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UK_Advertising_Standards_Authority">UK Advertising Standards Authority</span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Advertising_Standards_Authority_(United_Kingdom)" title="Advertising Standards Authority (United Kingdom)">Advertising Standards Authority</a> (ASA) in the UK found in 2012 that the content of <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">tweets</a> from two footballers had been "agreed with the help of a member of the Nike marketing team". The tweets were not clearly identified as Nike marketing communications and were therefore in breach of the ASA's code. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Advertising_Standards_Canada">Advertising Standards Canada</span></h3> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Advertising_Standards_Canada" title="Advertising Standards Canada">Canadian Code of Advertising Standards</a>, administered by <a href="/wiki/Advertising_Standards_Canada" title="Advertising Standards Canada">Advertising Standards Canada</a>, states: "No advertisement shall be presented in a format or style that conceals the fact that it is an advertisement." </p>

      <h3><span id="Wikipedians_in_residence.2C_reward_board"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Wikipedians_in_residence,_reward_board">Wikipedians in residence, reward board</span></h3> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="Wikipedians_in_residence"></span> There are forms of paid editing that the Wikimedia community regards as acceptable. These include <a href="/wiki/Wikipedian_in_residence" title="Wikipedian in residence">Wikipedians in residence</a> (WiRs)Wikipedians who may be paid to collaborate with mission-aligned organizations, such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GLAM" title="Wikipedia:GLAM">galleries, libraries, archives, and museums</a>. WiRs must not engage in on-Wikipedia public relations or marketing for their organization, and they should operate within the bounds defined by <a href="https://outreach.wikimedia.org/wiki/Wikipedian_in_Residence#Core_characteristics_of_a_Wikipedian_in_Residence" class="extiw" title="outreach:Wikipedian in Residence">Core characteristics of a Wikipedian in Residence</a> at <a href="https://outreach.wikimedia.org/wiki/Main_Page" class="extiw" title="outreach:Main Page">Wikimedia Outreach</a>. They must work closely with a Wikipedia project or the general Wikipedia community, and are expected to identify their WiR status on their user page and on talk pages related to their organization when they post there. </p>
      <p>Another example of acceptable paid editing is the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:REWARD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:REWARD">reward board</a>, where editors can post incentives, usually to raise articles to featured-article or good-article status. If you participate in this, transparency and neutrality are key. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Miscellaneous">Miscellaneous</span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solicitations_by_paid_editors">Solicitations by paid editors</span></h3> 
      <p>In any solicitation sent to a prospective client, paid editors should disclose the following information: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Paid editors do not represent the Wikimedia Foundation nor the Wikipedia editing community, and they have no authority beyond that of any volunteer editor.</li> 
       <li>Paid editors must disclose their employer, client, and affiliations on Wikipedia. There is no confidentiality for the client.</li> 
       <li>Paid edits may be reviewed and revised in the normal course of work on Wikipedia. Neither the client nor the paid editor own the article.</li> 
       <li>Paid editors cannot guarantee any outcome for an article on Wikipedia. It can be revised or deleted by other editors at any time.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Providing a client with a link to this section is appropriate disclosure if it is done in a neutral and non-deceptive manner. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Paid editors must also provide a link to their user page which includes a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PAID" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PAID">declaration of their paid editing status</a>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>If you received a solicitation from a paid editor that does not include this information, we recommend that you not do business with them. They are not following our policies and guidelines. Some of these solicitations have been linked to fraud. See <a href="/wiki/Orangemoody_editing_of_Wikipedia" title="Orangemoody editing of Wikipedia">Orangemoody editing of Wikipedia</a>. If you think youve received a fraudulent solicitation, please forward it to <span class="nowrap">arbcom-en<img alt="@" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/15px-At_sign.svg.png" decoding="async" title="@" width="15" height="15" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/23px-At_sign.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/30px-At_sign.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="145" data-file-height="145">wikimedia.org</span> for investigation. </p>

      </div> 
      <p>Once an article is created about yourself, your group, or your company, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Ownership_of_articles" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Ownership of articles">you have no right</a> to control its content, or to delete it outside the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Articles_for_deletion" title="Wikipedia:Articles for deletion">normal channels</a>. If there is anything publicly available on a topic that you would <i>not</i> want to have included in an article, it will probably find its way there eventually. </p> 
      <h3><span id="No_shared_accounts.2C_no_company_accounts"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="No_shared_accounts,_no_company_accounts">No shared accounts, no company accounts</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOSHARE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOSHARE">WP:NOSHARE</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ORGNAME" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ORGNAME">WP:ORGNAME</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Do not create a shared organizational account, or use the name of an organization as the account name. The account is yours, not your employer's. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Making_uncontroversial_edits">Making uncontroversial edits<span class="anchor" id="COIADVICE"></span></span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:COIADVICE"></span><span id="WP:COIU"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:COIADVICE&amp;redirect=no">WP:COIADVICE</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:COIU&amp;redirect=no">WP:COIU</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Editors who have a general conflict of interest may make unambiguously uncontroversial edits (but see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FINANCIALCOI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FINANCIALCOI">WP:FINANCIALCOI</a>). They may: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>remove <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Spam" title="Wikipedia:Spam">spam</a> and unambiguous <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vandalism" title="Wikipedia:Vandalism">vandalism</a>,</li> 
       <li>remove unambiguous violations of the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Biographies_of_living_persons" title="Wikipedia:Biographies of living persons">biography of living persons</a> policy,</li> 
       <li>fix spelling and grammatical errors,</li> 
       <li>repair <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot">broken links</a>,</li> 
       <li>remove their own COI edits, and</li> 
       <li>add independent <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Identifying_reliable_sources" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources">reliable sources</a> when another editor has requested them, although it is better to supply them on the talk page for others to add.</li>
      </ol> 
      <p>If another editor objects for any reason, it is not an uncontroversial edit. Edits not covered by the above should be discussed on the article's talk page. If an article has few uninvolved editors, ask at the talk page of a related <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikiproject" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Wikiproject">Wikiproject</a> or at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:COIN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:COIN">the COI noticeboard</a>. Also see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:COITALK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:COITALK">WP:COITALK</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Supplying_photographs_and_media_files">Supplying photographs and media files</span></h3> 
      <p>Editors with a COI are encouraged to upload high-quality media files that are appropriately licensed for Wikipedia and that improve our coverage of a subject. For more information, follow the <a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:Welcome" class="extiw" title="commons:Commons:Welcome">instructions at Commons</a>. In some cases, the addition of media files to an article may be an uncontroversial edit that editors with a COI can make directly, but editors should exercise discretion and rely on talk pages when images may be controversial or promotional. If the addition of an image is challenged by another editor, it is controversial. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="How_to_handle_conflicts_of_interest">How to handle conflicts of interest</span></h2> 
      <h3><span id="Advocacy.2C_noticeboards"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Advocacy,_noticeboards">Advocacy, noticeboards</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main pages: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Neutral_point_of_view/Noticeboard" title="Wikipedia:Neutral point of view/Noticeboard">Wikipedia:Neutral point of view/Noticeboard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources/Noticeboard" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources/Noticeboard">Wikipedia:Reliable sources/Noticeboard</a>
      </div> 
      <p>If a user's edits lead you to believe that they might have a COI (that is, if they have an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:APPARENTCOI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:APPARENTCOI">"apparent COI"</a>), and there has been no COI disclosure, consider first whether the issue may be simple <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ADVOCACY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ADVOCACY">advocacy</a>. Most advocacy does not involve COI. Whether an editor is engaged in advocacy should first be addressed at the user's talk page, then at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPOVN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPOVN">WP:NPOVN</a>, the neutral-point-of-view noticeboard. The appropriate forum for concerns about sources is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RSN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RSN">WP:RSN</a>, the reliable-sources noticeboard. If there are concerns about <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Sock_puppetry" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Sock puppetry">sockpuppets</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Sock_puppetry#Meatpuppetry" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Sock puppetry">meatpuppets</a>, please bring that concern to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SPI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SPI">WP:SPI</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reporting_to_the_conflict_of_interest_noticeboard">Reporting to the conflict of interest noticeboard<span class="anchor" id="Posting_at_the_conflict_of_interest_noticeboard"></span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main page: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Conflict_of_interest/Noticeboard" title="Wikipedia:Conflict of interest/Noticeboard">Wikipedia:Conflict of interest/Noticeboard</a>
      </div> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:COICOIN"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:COICOIN&amp;redirect=no">WP:COICOIN</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>If you believe an editor has an undisclosed COI and is editing in violation of this guideline, raise the issue in a civil manner on the editor's talk page, which is the first step in resolving user-conduct issues, per the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Dispute_resolution#Resolving_user_conduct_disputes" title="Wikipedia:Dispute resolution">DR policy</a>, citing this guideline. If for some reason that is not advisable, or if it fails to resolve the issue, the next step is to open a discussion at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:COIN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:COIN">conflict of interest noticeboard (COIN)</a>. COIN is also the place to discuss <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DCOI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DCOI">disclosed COI</a> that is causing a problem: for example, an acknowledged BLP subject who is editing their own BLP. Similarly, if you're editing with a disclosed COI, you can ask for advice at COIN. </p>
      <p>During the COIN discussion, avoid making disparaging remarks about the user in question, their motives or the subject of the article(s). Post whatever public evidence you have to support that there is a COI, or that it is causing a problem, in the form of edits by that user or information the user has posted about themselves. Do not post private information; see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OUTING" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:OUTING">WP:OUTING</a>, which is policy, and the section below, "Avoid outing". If private information must be shared to resolve a COI issue, ask one or more uninvolved <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Functionaries" title="Wikipedia:Functionaries">functionaries</a> if they would be willing to examine the private details by email. Functionaries are editors with advanced permissions who have signed the Wikimedia Foundation's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Access_to_nonpublic_information" title="Wikipedia:Access to nonpublic information">access to nonpublic information agreement</a>. If they agree, follow the advice in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OUTING" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:OUTING">WP:OUTING</a>: "Only the minimum information necessary should be conveyed and the minimum number of people contacted." The priority should be to avoid unnecessary privacy violations. </p>
      <p>See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FUNC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FUNC">WP:FUNC</a> for a list of subscribers to the functionaries' mailing list. Alternatively, the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ARBCOM" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ARBCOM">arbitration committee</a> can be contacted by email. If the issue is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PAID" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PAID">undisclosed paid editing</a>, there is a dedicated <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:OTRS">OTRS</a>/functionaries email address: <i><span class="nowrap">paid-en-wp<img alt="@" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/15px-At_sign.svg.png" decoding="async" title="@" width="15" height="15" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/23px-At_sign.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/At_sign.svg/30px-At_sign.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="145" data-file-height="145">wikipedia.org</span></i>. Contact them before emailing private information and be guided by their advice. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Avoid_outing">Avoid outing</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Harassment#Posting_of_personal_information" title="Wikipedia:Harassment">Wikipedia:Harassment &nbsp;Posting of personal information</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikimedia_Foundation_statement_on_paid_editing_and_outing" title="Wikipedia:Wikimedia Foundation statement on paid editing and outing">Wikipedia:Wikimedia Foundation statement on paid editing and outing</a>
      </div> 
      <p>When investigating COI editing, the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:HARASS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:HARASS">policy against harassment</a> takes precedence. It requires that Wikipedians <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OUTING" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:OUTING">not reveal the identity of editors</a> against their wishes. Instead, examine editors' behavior and refer if necessary to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Checkuser" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Checkuser">Wikipedia:Checkuser</a>. Do not ask a user if they <i>are</i> somebody; instead one can ask if they have an undisclosed connection to that person. If revealing private information is needed to resolve COI editing, and if the issue is serious enough to warrant it, editors can seek the advice of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FUNCTIONARY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FUNCTIONARY">functionaries</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ARBCOM" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ARBCOM">arbitration committee</a> by email. Also see the section <a href="#Reporting_to_the_conflict_of_interest_noticeboard">"Reporting to the conflict of interest noticeboard"</a> above. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dealing_with_single-purpose_accounts">Dealing with single-purpose accounts</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Blocking_policy#Disruption-only" title="Wikipedia:Blocking policy">Wikipedia:Blocking policy &nbsp;Disruption-only</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Single-purpose_account" title="Wikipedia:Single-purpose account">Wikipedia:Single-purpose account</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Accounts that appear to be <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SPA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SPA">single-purpose</a>, existing for the sole or primary purpose of promotion or denigration of a person, company, product, service, website, organization, etc., and whose postings are in apparent violation of this guideline, should be made aware of this guideline and warned not to continue their problematic editing. If the same pattern of editing continues after the warning, the account may be blocked. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Templates">Templates</span></h3> 
      <p>Relevant article talk pages may be tagged with <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Connected_contributor" title="Template:Connected contributor">connected contributor</a>}}</code> or <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Connected_contributor_(paid)" title="Template:Connected contributor (paid)">connected contributor (paid)</a>}}</code>. The article itself may be tagged with <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:COI" title="Template:COI">COI</a>}}</code>. A section of an article can be tagged with <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:COI" title="Template:COI">COI</a>|section}}</code> </p>
      <p>Other templates include: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Uw-coi" title="Template:Uw-coi">uw-coi</a>}}</code> (to be placed on user Talk pages to warn editors that they may have a conflict of interest)</li> 
       <li><code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Uw-coi-username" title="Template:Uw-coi-username">uw-coi-username</a>}}</code> (another Talk page warning, this one for editors whose username appears to violate the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Usernames" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Usernames">WP:Usernames</a> policy)</li> 
       <li><code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:COI_editnotice" title="Template:COI editnotice">COI editnotice</a>}}</code> (this template goes on article talk pages and gives instructions to COI editors on how to submit edit requests to the article)</li> 
       <li><code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:UserboxCOI" title="Template:UserboxCOI">UserboxCOI</a>}}</code> (for users to self-declare on their own Userpages those articles with which they have a conflict of interest, one such template per article)</li>
      </ul>
