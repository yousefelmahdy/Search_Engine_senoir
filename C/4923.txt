    <h2 id="mission-statement">Mission Statement</h2> 
    <p>To deliver value to all customers by engaging in a consistent, repeatable, scalable way across defined segments so that customers see the value in their investment with GitLab, and we retain and drive growth within our enterprise customers.</p> 
    <ul> 
     <li>The mission of the Customer Success Department is to provide these customers with experience in order to: 
      <ul> 
       <li>Accelerate initial customer value</li> 
       <li>Maximize long-term, sustainable customer value</li> 
       <li>Improve overall customer satisfaction &amp; referenceability</li> 
       <li>Maximize the total value of the customer to GitLab</li> 
      </ul> </li> 
    </ul> 
    <h2 id="vision-page">Vision Page</h2> 
    <p>The <a href="/handbook/customer-success/vision">"Customer Success Vision Page"</a> provides the long-term view of company wide success to which the customer success will be building in partnership with the value centers of the GitLab organization (i.e., Marketing, Sales, Product/Engineering, Support, Finance and IT).</p> 
    <h2 id="department-initiatives">Department Initiatives</h2> 
    <p>Our large and strategic customers are in need of an ongoing partnership that combines expert guidance with flexibility and adaptability to support their adoption and continuous improvement initiatives.</p> 
    <p>These customers expect that partner to provide a streamlined, consistent and fully coordinated experience that encompasses the full span of their needs as well as the fully lifecycle of the relationship. Need to focus on 4 main areas in order to grow in our existing accounts as well as land large and strategic:</p> 
    <ol> 
     <li>Awareness</li> 
     <li>Adoption</li> 
     <li>Usage</li> 
     <li>Performance</li> 
    </ol> 
    <h3 id="initiative-awareness">Initiative: Awareness</h3> 
    <p>Opportunity to improve the overall awareness of GitLab in order to promote and evangelize our brand and solution in a meaningful way to provide big business impact to our customers so that they believe in our vision and strategy.</p> 
    <h3 id="initiative-adoption">Initiative: Adoption</h3> 
    <p>Ensuring paying customers are successful in their onboarding in order to gain adoption and get the most out of our platform and remain happy, paying GitLab team-members and brand advocates.</p> 
    <h3 id="initiative-usage">Initiative: Usage</h3> 
    <p>Collecting and making use of customer data and insights is key to customer success. It’s important to do more with data and when necessary share back with customers, which in turn helps and encourages our customers to improve and drive adoption.</p> 
    <h3 id="initiative-performance">Initiative: Performance</h3> 
    <p>Utilizing built-in monitoring instrumentation and <a href="https://docs.gitlab.com/ee/administration/reference_architectures/index.html">a scalable HA architecture</a>, The Customer Success team helps ensure implementations of GitLab are operating at peak performance as organization scale and adopt more features.</p>

    <h2 id="overlap-between-solution-architects-and-technical-account-managers">Overlap Between Solution Architects and Technical Account Managers</h2> 
    <p>SA owns 1) pre-sales technical evaluation and relationships prior to the initial sale and 2) tier upgrades and new business units (i.e., connected new) within an existing customer. TAM owns 1) post-sales customer relationship and 2) license upgrades within an existing customer.</p> 
    <p>TAM engagement prior to the sale should occur in the following situations:</p> 
    <ol> 
     <li>At the POV completion &amp; presentation meeting when the results are reviewed and the next steps agreed upon</li> 
     <li>A shared customer issue tracking project has been created that will affect the account long-term</li> 
     <li>As requested by the SA if the TAM has a specific subject matter expertise relevant to the conversation</li> 
    </ol> 
    <p>SA's will be brought back into a customer account in the following situations</p> 
    <ol> 
     <li>Development of expansion plans with the SAL and TAM leading to tier upgrades</li> 
     <li>A POV, product evaluation, RFP, or security begins for a "new" single team or an enterprise-wide tier upgrade not for renewal purposes</li> 
     <li>SAs may assist TAMs with an existing customer team on an <em>exception</em> basis. SA leadership approval is required. 
      <ol> 
       <li>Customer enablement assistance when specifically requested by the TAM team</li> 
       <li>Workshops assistance when specifically requested by the TAM team</li> 
       <li>ROI analysis for an existing customer team when requested by the TAM team</li> 
       <li>Unless it's for a tier upgrade, presales activities such as POVs and RFPs should be avoided for an existing customer team. Like with any presales opportunity, these activities should not be offered as a sales tools, but leveraged when a prospect/customer requires these activities as part of their evaluation.</li> 
      </ol> </li> 
     <li>Any Professional Services that are being discussed and may require an SOW</li> 
     <li>If a TAM is over-committed or unable to support a customer request, the SA may be requested to assist</li> 
     <li>Support of GitLab days or other on-site evangelism of GitLab at customer sites</li> 
    </ol> 
    <ul> 
     <li><a href="https://gitlab.com/gitlab-com/us-public-sector">us-public-sector</a> parent project for all public sector work</li> 
     <li><a href="https://gitlab.com/gitlab-com/account-management/">account-management</a> parent project for account-specific work and collaboration with the rest of the sales</li> 
     <li><a href="https://gitlab.com/gitlab-com/customer-success">customer-success</a> parent project for Customer Success employee, shared or triage work</li> 
    </ul>

    <h3 id="dogfooding">Dogfooding</h3> 
    <p>Outside of Engineering the Customer Success team has the largest concentration of tooling development capability. The team has unique needs that can't always be solved by GitLab's single <a href="/solutions/devops-platform/">DevOps platform</a>. However, it is important to <a href="/handbook/values/#dogfooding">dogfood</a> and avoid <a href="/handbook/engineering/#dogfooding-antipatterns">dogfooding anti-patterns</a>. As a result the <a href="/handbook/values/#dogfooding">Product organization heavily weights internal customers</a> when considering prioritization. If you are considering building tooling in support of Customer Success priorities outside of GitLab, please follow the <a href="/handbook/values/#dogfooding">dogfooding process</a>.</p> 
    <h3 id="customer-success-aws-test-account">Customer Success AWS Test Account</h3> 
    <p>In an effort to keep AWS spend down initiatives are being taken automatically clean up our AWS account. This account is primarily used as a proof of concept for IaC and creating demos for GitLab customers. An automated cleanup script is currently being tested that will tag, shutdown and delete old resources as they are no longer needed. The automation will:</p> 
    <ul> 
     <li>Turn off and Tag Un-named resources. When resources are created a "Name" tag should be created with a value that's meaningful and indicates who deployed the resource. Example: {initials}-GitLabRunner</li> 
     <li>New Resources will be automatically tagged with a Discovered and Expiration tag.</li> 
     <li>The Expiration tag is 14 days after the discovery. The script will only a tag an instance once. If you need additional time, please change the date to a reasonable date for cleanup (Add a month or two for prospective customers).</li> 
     <li>If a resource needs to be permanent please set termination protection on the instance. This should also include tagging the instance with an explanation on why it's permanent and what its for.</li> 
     <li>On expiration the resource will be shut off and left for 7 days.</li> 
     <li>In 7 days if the instance is still off a snapshot will be taken and it will be terminated.</li> 
     <li>If the instance is still on but the expiration has not been changed it will be terminated.</li> 
    </ul>

    <h3 id="communities-of-practice">Communities of Practice</h3> 
    <p><a href="/handbook/customer-success/initiatives/communities-of-practice.html">Community of Practice</a> are cross-functional groups of SME's (or aspiring to be!) within the CS organization dedicated to a topic within GitLab or the broader DevOps space. The goal is to build assets, best practices, demonstrations, and share experiences we learn from prospects and customers. In turn, CoP will build broader technical depth within our CS organization to better advise our customers and influence our product roadmap.</p> 
    <p>Anyone can establish a <a href="/handbook/customer-success/initiatives/communities-of-practice.html">Community of Practice</a>, and anyone can be a part of one. To be <a href="https://about.gitlab.com/handbook/values/?source=post_page---------------------------#efficiency">efficient</a> and <a href="https://about.gitlab.com/handbook/values/?source=post_page---------------------------#transparency">transparent</a>, we have set guidelines on how Communities of Practice should operate.</p>
