      <p>A <b>ban</b> is a formal prohibition from editing some or all Wikipedia pages, or a formal prohibition from making certain types of edits on Wikipedia pages. Bans can be imposed for a specified duration or an indefinite duration. </p>
      <p>Bans are a possible outcome of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Dispute_resolution" title="Wikipedia:Dispute resolution">dispute resolution</a>. They may be imposed by community <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a>, by the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee">Arbitration Committee</a>, the <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/WMF_Global_Ban_Policy" class="extiw" title="meta:WMF Global Ban Policy">Wikimedia Foundation</a>, or by <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators" title="Wikipedia:Administrators">administrators</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee/Discretionary_sanctions" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee/Discretionary sanctions">in certain topic areas</a>). A ban is normally a <b>site ban</b> (prohibiting all editing), but it may be limited to a <b>page ban</b>, a <b>topic ban</b> (prohibiting edits on pages relating to certain topic areas) or an <b>interaction ban</b> (prohibiting edits that interact with certain other editors). </p>
      <p>Bans are different from <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Blocking_policy" title="Wikipedia:Blocking policy">blocks</a>, which are used by administrators to technically prevent a user account or <a href="/wiki/IP_address" title="IP address">IP address</a> from editing Wikipedia. Blocks are used chiefly to deal with immediate problems such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vandalism" title="Wikipedia:Vandalism">vandalism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disruptive_editing" title="Wikipedia:Disruptive editing">disruptive editing</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_warring" title="Wikipedia:Edit warring">edit warring</a>. A ban, on the other hand, does not technically prevent editing; however, blocks may be used to <a href="#Blocks">enforce bans</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_bans">Types of bans</span></h2> 
      <p>The following are the common types of bans; other bans may be used when appropriate. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Site_ban">Site ban</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:SBAN"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:SBAN&amp;redirect=no">WP:SBAN</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Unless otherwise specified, a ban is a <b>site ban</b>. An editor who is site-banned is forbidden from making any edit, anywhere on Wikipedia, via any account or as an unregistered user, under any and all circumstances. The <i>only</i> exception is that editors with talk page access may appeal in accordance with <a href="#Appeals_of_bans_imposed_by_the_community">the provisions below</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Article_ban_or_page_ban"><span class="anchor" id="Article_ban"></span>Article ban or page ban</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:ABAN"></span><span id="WP:PBAN"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:ABAN&amp;redirect=no">WP:ABAN</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PBAN&amp;redirect=no">WP:PBAN</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>An article ban forbids an editor from editing a specific article or set of articles. The text of the ban should state whether the ban includes or excludes the article's talk page. Editors subject to an article ban are free to edit other related pages or discuss the topic elsewhere on Wikipedia. Article bans may be enforced using <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PBLOCK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PBLOCK">partial blocks</a> from the affected pages. </p>
      <p>When the word "page" is used in a ban it means <b>any page on Wikipedia</b>, including for example user, talk, discussion, file, category or template pages. The word "article" usually refers only to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Main_namespace" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Main namespace">mainspace</a> pages. If any other related pages (such as the page's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TP">talk page</a>) are to be covered it will usually be stated explicitly. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Topic_ban">Topic ban</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:TBAN"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:TBAN&amp;redirect=no">WP:TBAN</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>The purpose of a topic ban is to forbid editors from making edits related to a certain topic area where <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disruptive_editing" title="Wikipedia:Disruptive editing">their contributions have been disruptive</a>, but to allow them to edit the rest of Wikipedia. Unless clearly and unambiguously specified otherwise, a topic ban covers all <i>pages</i> (not only articles) broadly related to the topic, as well as the <i>parts of other pages</i> that are related to the topic, as encapsulated in the phrase "<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Broadly_construed" title="Wikipedia:Broadly construed">broadly construed</a>". For example, if an editor is banned from the topic "weather", this editor is forbidden from editing not only the article <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">Weather</a>, but also everything else that has to do with weather, such as: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>weather-related articles and <a href="/wiki/Help:List" title="Help:List">lists</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">Wind</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_weather_records" title="List of weather records">List of weather records</a>, and their talk pages;</li> 
       <li>weather-related <a href="/wiki/Help:Categories" title="Help:Categories">categories</a> such as all of the categories that are associated with <a href="/wiki/Category:Weather" title="Category:Weather">Category:Weather</a>;</li> 
       <li>weather-related project pages, such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Meteorology" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Meteorology">WikiProject Meteorology</a>;</li> 
       <li>weather-related parts of other pages, even if the pages as a whole have little or nothing to do with weather: the section entitled "Climate" in the article <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, for example, is covered by the topic ban, but the rest of the article is not;</li> 
       <li>discussions or suggestions about weather-related topics anywhere on Wikipedia, for instance a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AFD">deletion discussion</a> concerning an article about a <a href="/wiki/Meteorologist" title="Meteorologist">meteorologist</a>, but also including <a href="/wiki/Help:Edit_summary" title="Help:Edit summary">edit summaries</a> and the user's own user and talk pages (including sandboxes).</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Interaction_ban">Interaction ban</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:IBAN"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:IBAN&amp;redirect=no">WP:IBAN</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>The purpose of an interaction ban (IBAN) is to stop a conflict between individuals. A one-way interaction ban forbids one user from interacting with another user. A two-way interaction ban forbids both users from interacting with each other. Although the interaction-banned users are generally allowed to edit the same pages or discussions so long as they avoid each other, they are not allowed to interact with each other. </p>
      <p>Editors subject to an interaction ban are not permitted to: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>edit each other's user and user talk pages;</li> 
       <li>reply to each other in discussions;</li> 
       <li>make reference to or comment on each other anywhere on Wikipedia, directly or indirectly;</li> 
       <li>undo each other's edits to any page, whether by use of the revert function or by other means;</li> 
       <li>use the <a href="https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/Thanks" class="extiw" title="mw:Thanks">thanks</a> extension to respond to each other's edits.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>A no-fault two-way interaction ban is often a quick and painless way to prevent a dispute from causing further distress or wider disruption. </p>
      <p>Interaction bans are listed at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Editing_restrictions" title="Wikipedia:Editing restrictions">Wikipedia:Editing restrictions</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Exceptions_to_limited_bans">Exceptions to limited bans</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:BANEX"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:BANEX&amp;redirect=no">WP:BANEX</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Unless stated otherwise, article, page, topic, or interaction bans do not apply to the following: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>Reverting <b>obvious</b> <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vandalism" title="Wikipedia:Vandalism">vandalism</a> (such as page content being replaced by obscenities) or <b>obvious</b> violations of the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLP">policy about biographies of living persons</a>. The key word is "obvious" – that is, cases in which no reasonable person could disagree.</li> 
       <li>Engaging in legitimate and necessary dispute resolution, e.g. addressing a legitimate concern <i>about the ban itself</i> in an appropriate forum. Examples include: 
        <ul>
         <li>asking an administrator to take action against a violation of an interaction ban by another user (but normally not more than once, and only by mentioning the fact of the violation)</li> 
         <li>asking for necessary clarifications about the scope of the ban</li> 
         <li>appealing the ban</li>
        </ul></li>
      </ol> 
      <p>As a banned user, if you think your editing is excepted from the ban according to these rules, you should explain why that is so at the time of the edit, for example in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_summary" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Edit summary">edit summary</a>. When in doubt, do not make the edit. Instead, engage in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DR" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DR">dispute resolution</a> or ask whoever imposed the ban to clarify. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Decision_to_ban">Decision to ban</span></h2> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <i>See also: <a href="/wiki/Category:Banned_Wikipedia_users" title="Category:Banned Wikipedia users">Category:Banned Wikipedia users</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Long-term_abuse" title="Wikipedia:Long-term abuse">Wikipedia:Long-term abuse</a>. Note that the absence of editors from these lists does not necessarily mean that they are not banned.</i>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Authority_to_ban">Authority to ban</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:Ban_authority"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Ban_authority" title="Wikipedia:Ban authority">WP:Ban authority</a></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>The decision to ban an editor can be made by the following groups or persons: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>The Wikipedia community can impose a ban by <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a>, as described in <a href="#Community_bans_and_restrictions">§ Community bans and restrictions</a>.</li> 
       <li>The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee">Arbitration Committee</a> can use a ban as a remedy, usually following a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration/Requests" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration/Requests">request for arbitration</a>.</li> 
       <li>Both the Arbitration Committee and the Wikipedia community may delegate the authority to impose bans. They have authorized administrators to impose "discretionary sanctions" (including bans) in certain topic areas (see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee/Discretionary_sanctions" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee/Discretionary sanctions">Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee/Discretionary sanctions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:General_sanctions" title="Wikipedia:General sanctions">Wikipedia:General sanctions</a>).</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Role_of_Jimmy_Wales" title="Wikipedia:Role of Jimmy Wales">Jimbo Wales</a> retains the authority to ban editors.</li> 
       <li>Individual administrators may impose <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CONDUNBLOCK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CONDUNBLOCK">unblock conditions</a> (such as page, topic, and interaction bans) with the agreement of the blocked user.</li> 
       <li>The <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Wikimedia Foundation">Wikimedia Foundation</a> has the authority to ban editors (see <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/WMF_Global_Ban_Policy" class="extiw" title="meta:WMF Global Ban Policy">meta:WMF Global Ban Policy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedians_banned_by_the_Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Category:Wikipedians banned by the Wikimedia Foundation">Category:Wikipedians banned by the Wikimedia Foundation</a>), though it has rarely exercised this authority on the English Wikipedia individually.</li> 
       <li>Users may be <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/global_bans" class="extiw" title="m:global bans">globally banned</a> from the English Wikipedia and all other Wikimedia projects, either by the broader Wikimedia community or by the Wikimedia Foundation. In case of the former, English Wikipedia users will be explicitly invited to participate in the <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Requests_for_comment" class="extiw" title="m:Requests for comment">Meta-Wiki discussion</a> to ban the user in question.</li>
      </ol> 
      <p>Except as noted above, individual editors, including administrators, may not directly impose bans. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_bans_and_restrictions">Community bans and restrictions</span></h4> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:CBAN"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:CBAN&amp;redirect=no">WP:CBAN</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>The community may reach a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a> to impose various types of sanctions on editors: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>If an editor has proven to be repeatedly disruptive in one or more areas of Wikipedia, the community may impose a time-limited or indefinite topic ban, interaction ban, site ban, or other editing restriction(s) via a consensus of editors who are not involved in the underlying dispute. When determining consensus, the closing administrator will assess the strength and quality of the arguments made.</li> 
       <li>In some cases the community may review a block or an editor's unblock request and reach a consensus of uninvolved editors to endorse the block as a community sanction.</li> 
       <li>Editors who are indefinitely blocked by community consensus, or remain indefinitely blocked after due consideration by the community, are considered "banned by the Wikipedia community".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> 
        <ul>
         <li>Exception: A third party block review that results in a normal administrator block being endorsed is not converted into a community ban.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
        </ul></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Community sanctions may be discussed on the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators%27_noticeboard" title="Wikipedia:Administrators' noticeboard">Wikipedia:Administrators' noticeboard</a> (preferred) or on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators%27_noticeboard/Incidents" title="Wikipedia:Administrators' noticeboard/Incidents">Wikipedia:Administrators' noticeboard/Incidents</a>. Discussions may be organized via a template to distinguish comments by involved and uninvolved editors, and to allow the subject editor to post a response. Sanction discussions <i>must</i> be kept open for at least 24 hours before any sanction is implemented to allow time for comments from a broad selection of community members.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> For site bans, the discussion must be kept open for 72 hours except in cases where there is limited opposition and the outcome is obvious after 24 hours.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> If the discussion appears to have reached a consensus for a particular sanction, an uninvolved administrator <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CLOSURE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CLOSURE">closes</a> the discussion, notifies the subject accordingly, and enacts any blocks called for. Except for a site ban, the sanction should be logged at the appropriate venue if necessary, usually <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Editing_restrictions" title="Wikipedia:Editing restrictions">Wikipedia:Editing restrictions</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Long-term_abuse" title="Wikipedia:Long-term abuse">Wikipedia:Long-term abuse</a>. If a block is administered to enforce a community sanction, please include a link to the discussion and note that the block is enforcing a community sanction in the block log. </p>
      <p>Editors without usernames may be banned by the community (<a href="/wiki/Special:Diff/700172815" title="Special:Diff/700172815">example</a>), but bans of editors using only IP addresses are rare. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bans_for_repeated_block_evasion">Bans for repeated block evasion</span></h3>

       <span id="WP:THREESTRIKES"></span><span id="WP:3STRIKES"></span><span id="WP:3X"></span>

        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:3STRIKES&amp;redirect=no">WP:3STRIKES</a></span></li>

      <p>Editors who are confirmed by a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CheckUser" title="Wikipedia:CheckUser">CheckUser</a> to have engaged in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SOCK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SOCK">sockpuppetry</a> on at least two occasions after an initial indefinite block that is active, for any reason, are effectively site banned by the Wikipedia community.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> CheckUser findings<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> must be documented on Wikipedia before a user is considered banned. Users who have been banned in this way are subject to the same unban conditions as users banned by community discussion. </p>
      <p>Administrators or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SPI/C" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SPI/C">sockpuppet investigations clerks</a> will normally tag the master account's user page with {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Sockpuppeteer" title="Template:Sockpuppeteer">sockpuppeteer</a>|checked=yes|banned}}. If the user made substantial good faith contributions before being banned, a notice should be placed on the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators%27_noticeboard" title="Wikipedia:Administrators' noticeboard">administrators' noticeboard</a> alerting the community to the ban. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recidivism_may_lead_to_a_ban">Recidivism may lead to a ban</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">

      <p>In 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ARBCOM" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ARBCOM">Arbitration Committee</a> decided that "Users who have been sanctioned for improper conduct are expected to avoid repeating it should they continue to participate in the project. Failure to do so may lead to the imposition of increasingly severe sanctions."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Duration_of_bans">Duration of bans</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">

      <p>Bans are not intended as a short-term measure. Sometimes a ban may be for a fixed period of some months. More often no period is specified, because the ban is a decision that the editor may not edit or participate in the specified matters on this site. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Appeals_of_bans_imposed_by_the_community">Appeals of bans imposed by the community</span></h3> 
      <p>Bans imposed by the community may be appealed to the community or, where there are serious questions about the validity of the ban discussion or its closure, to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee">Arbitration Committee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-reluctantCommittee_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reluctantCommittee-8">[8]</a></sup> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>Editors who are banned from a topic area or certain pages but can otherwise edit</b>, may appeal (and comment in an appeal discussion) on-wiki, either at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AN">administrators' noticeboard</a>, or, if there are serious questions about the validity of the ban discussion or its closure, by <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration/Requests/Case" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration/Requests/Case">filing a case request</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-reluctantCommittee_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reluctantCommittee-8">[8]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><b>Editors who cannot edit any page except their talk page</b> may: 
        <ul>
         <li>Post an appeal {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Unblock" title="Template:Unblock">unblock</a>}} template or comment there, by email or other off-site means such as the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Unblock_Ticket_Request_System" title="Wikipedia:Unblock Ticket Request System">Unblock Ticket Request System</a> (UTRS), and ask for it to be reposted to the appropriate discussion board. This is a voluntary act and should not be abused or used to excess.</li> 
         <li>Submit an appeal to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:UTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:UTRS">UTRS</a> and ask an administrator to post it to the appropriate discussion board. This is a voluntary act and should not be abused or used to excess.</li> 
         <li>Where there are serious questions about the validity of the ban discussion or its closure, appeal by email to the Arbitration Committee. An email appeal must specify the banned editor's Wikipedia username and any other usernames he or she has used to edit Wikipedia in the past two years. (Using Wikipedia's email feature to email <a href="/wiki/Special:EmailUser/Arbitration_Committee" title="Special:EmailUser/Arbitration Committee">Arbitration Committee</a> automatically reveals the account used for sending it.) The appeal should clearly but succinctly explain the reasons the editor feels the ban should be overturned, such as what lessons the editor has learned since the ban or block was imposed, how the editor would conduct himself or herself differently in the future if they are allowed to resume editing, or why they believe the ban was unfair. The editor should also include links to any relevant on-wiki discussions and any other information necessary to understand the grounds for the appeal.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><b>Editors unable to edit any page (even their talk page)</b> should appeal through the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:UTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:UTRS">Unblock Ticket Request System</a> asking an administrator to post their appeal to the appropriate discussion board. This is a voluntary act, and should not be abused or used to excess.</li> 
       <li>In some cases, a banned editor may be unblocked for the purpose of filing an appeal. In such cases, editing of any unrelated page or other matter is grounds for immediate re-blocking. Editors banned by the Arbitration Committee must appeal to the Committee.</li>
      </ul>

The editor should also include links to any relevant on-wiki discussions and any other information necessary to understand the grounds for the appeal.</p></li>

      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Appeal_to_Jimbo_Wales">Appeal to Jimbo Wales</span></h4> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration/Policy#Appeal_of_decisions" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration/Policy">Appeal to Jimbo Wales</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Any arbitration decision may be appealed to Jimbo Wales. While it is not unusual for him to consider an appeal, it is exceedingly unusual for him to overturn such a decision. A topic-banned editor cannot discuss the topic ban or topic on Jimbo's talk page, but is allowed to appeal the topic ban to Jimbo Wales. An appeal should be lodged at <a href="/wiki/User_talk:Jimbo_Wales" title="User talk:Jimbo Wales">his user talk page</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Evasion_and_enforcement">Evasion and enforcement</span></h2> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:BANEVASION"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:BANEVASION&amp;redirect=no">WP:BANEVASION</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Wikipedia's approach to enforcing bans balances a number of competing concerns: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Maximizing the quality of the encyclopedia.</li> 
       <li>Avoiding inconvenience or aggravation to any victims of mistaken identity.</li> 
       <li>Maximizing the number of editors who can edit Wikipedia.</li> 
       <li>Avoiding conflict within the community over banned editors.</li> 
       <li>Dissuading or preventing banned editors from editing Wikipedia or the relevant area of the ban.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>As a result, enforcement has a number of aspects. While all editors are expected to respect the enforcement of policies by not undermining or sabotaging them, no editor is personally obligated to help enforce any ban. </p> 
      <h3><span id="Bans_apply_to_all_editing.2C_good_or_bad"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Bans_apply_to_all_editing,_good_or_bad">Bans apply to all editing, good or bad</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:BMB"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:BMB&amp;redirect=no">WP:BMB</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Editors are site-banned only as a last resort, usually for extreme or very persistent problems that have not been resolved by lesser sanctions and that often resulted in considerable disruption or stress to other editors. A site ban is not merely a request to avoid editing "unless they behave". The measure of a site ban is that even if the editor were to make good edits, permitting them to re-join the community is perceived to pose enough risk of disruption, issues, or harm, that they may not edit at all, <i>even if the edits seem good</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>A number of banned editors have used "good editing" (such as anti-vandalism edits) tactically, to try and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GAME" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:GAME">game</a> the banning system, "prove" they cannot be banned, or force editors into the paradox of either allowing banned editing or removing good content. Even if such editors make only good edits, they will be rebanned for evasion.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>On very rare occasions, a limited exception may be requested; for example, to participate in a particular discussion.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>If there is any doubt whether a limited ban prohibits any specific edit, the banned editor should assume that it does, unless whoever imposed the ban expressly clarifies that it does not. If clarification is not sought before making the edit, the banned editor assumes the risk that an administrator takes a broader view of the scope of the ban and enforces it with a block or other sanction. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blocks">Blocks</span></h3> 
      <p>In the case of project-wide bans, the primary account of any banned editor may be entirely blocked for the duration of the ban. Partial bans may be backed up by <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PB" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PB">partial blocks</a>, but note that the scope of a ban is defined by its wording and not by the presence of partial blocks. Users that violate the terms of a partial ban may be site-wide blocked to enforce the ban. </p>
      <p>If the banned editor creates <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Sock_puppet" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Sock puppet">sockpuppet</a> accounts to evade the ban, these usually will be blocked as well. When evasion is a problem, the <a href="/wiki/IP_address" title="IP address">IP address</a> of a banned editor who edits from a static IP address may also be blocked for the duration of the ban. If a banned editor evades the ban from a range of addresses, short-term IP blocks may be used. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reset_of_ban_following_evasion">Reset of ban following evasion</span></h3> 
      <p>It is customary for the "ban timer" to be reset or extended if a banned editor attempts to edit in spite of the ban. No formal consideration is typically necessary. For example, if someone is banned for ten months, but on the sixth month attempts to evade the ban, then the ban timer may be reset from "four months remaining" to "ten months remaining", so if the editor does not subsequently evade the ban again, their eventual total duration would be 16 months. Repeated evasion may lead to a longer or more serious sanction. </p>
      <p>An editor who has been banned or has had their account blocked, and tries to evade this by creating a new account, is known as a <b>reincarnation</b> of the old account. Obvious reincarnations are easily dealt with—the account is blocked and contributions are reverted or deleted, as discussed above. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Sock_puppetry" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Sock puppetry">sockpuppet</a> for policy on dealing with unclear cases. </p>
      <p><span class="anchor" id="revert"></span> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Edits_by_and_on_behalf_of_banned_editors">Edits by and on behalf of banned editors <span id="Edits_by_and_on_behalf_of_banned_users"></span><span id="Enforcement_by_reverting_edits"></span></span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:BRV"></span><span id="WP:BANREVERT"></span><span id="WP:REVERTBAN"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:BRV&amp;redirect=no">WP:BRV</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:BANREVERT&amp;redirect=no">WP:BANREVERT</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:REVERTBAN&amp;redirect=no">WP:REVERTBAN</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Anyone is free to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reverting" title="Wikipedia:Reverting">revert</a> any edits made in violation of a ban, without giving any further reason and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOT3RR" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOT3RR">without regard to the three-revert rule</a>. This does not mean that edits <i>must</i> be reverted just because they were made by a banned editor (changes that are obviously helpful, such as fixing typos or undoing vandalism, can be allowed to stand), but the presumption in ambiguous cases should be to revert. </p>
      <p>When reverting edits, care should be taken not to reinstate material that may be in violation of such core policies as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPOV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPOV">neutrality</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:V" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:V">verifiability</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLP">biographies of living persons</a>. </p>
      <p>Pages created by banned users in violation of their ban, and which have no substantial edits by others, are eligible for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CSD#G5" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CSD">speedy deletion under the G5 criterion</a>. If the edits by the good faith editors are substantial, G5 no longer applies. </p>
      <p>Since categorization can impact many pages, and deletion of a category without merging can leave pages orphaned, you should carefully consider what to do with categories created by a banned user. Blatantly useless categories can be speedy-deleted, as well as any categories which clearly violate existing category standards. Care should nonetheless be taken to see if articles need to be merged to a parent category before the speedy deletion. Categories created by a banned user which may be useful or fit into a larger category scheme should be tagged for discussion and possible merging using the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CFD">categories for discussion</a> process instead of deleting them outright. </p>

      </div>

      <p>Wikipedians in turn are not permitted to post or edit material at the <i>direction</i> of a banned or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLOCKEVASION" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLOCKEVASION">blocked</a> editor (sometimes called <b>proxy editing</b> or <b>proxying</b>) unless they are able to show that the changes are either verifiable or productive <b>and</b> they have independent reasons for making such edits. Editors who reinstate edits made by a banned or blocked editor take complete responsibility for the content. </p>
      <p>New accounts which engage in the same behavior as a banned editor or blocked account in the same context, and who appear to be editing Wikipedia solely for that purpose, are subject to the remedies applied to the editor whose behavior they are imitating.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> See also the policy on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SOCK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SOCK">sockpuppetry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:MEAT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:MEAT">meatpuppetry</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="User_pages">User pages</span></h3> 
      <p>Banned editors' <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_page" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:User page">user and user talk pages</a> should be updated with a notice of the ban, linking to any applicable discussion or decision-making pages. The purpose of this notice is to announce the ban to editors encountering the banned editor's edits. Indefinitely site-banned editors may be restricted from editing their user talk page or using email. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_enforcement_measures">Further enforcement measures</span></h3> 
      <p>Serious, ongoing ban evasion is sometimes dealt with by technical means or by making an abuse complaint with the operator of the network from which the edits originate. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Difference_between_bans_and_blocks">Difference between bans and blocks</span></h2> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:BLOCKBANDIFF"></span><span id="WP:BANBLOCKDIFF"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:BLOCKBANDIFF&amp;redirect=no">WP:BLOCKBANDIFF</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:BANBLOCKDIFF&amp;redirect=no">WP:BANBLOCKDIFF</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>The standard distinction is that a ban is a social decision about the right to edit; a block is a technically imposed enforcement setting. </p>
      <p>The Mediawiki software allows the ability to block editing of individual pages, known as a '<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Partial_block" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Partial block">partial block</a>', and sometimes this is used as a means of enforcing a specific set of ban conditions. However, bans such as topic bans or interaction bans still require human judgement to enforce and assess, and the presence or not of a partial block in furtherance of a topic ban or interaction ban should not be seen as a limitation on the scope of such a ban, which is defined by the wording of the ban and not of the presence or not of partial blocks. Editors who are banned from specific pages or topics must immediately cease editing these pages or topics. If they do not, <i>then</i> a block will be used to enforce the ban. Such a block will necessarily prevent their editing of the entire site, but they are not banned <i>from</i> the site and remain members of the community. </p>
      <p>An editor who is "sitebanned" (which may sometimes be described as a "full ban") has been completely ejected from the project. For the duration of their ban, their edits are subject to reversion, although <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPA">personal attacks</a> towards them remain unacceptable. </p>

         <td colspan="2">Edits by the editor or on their behalf may be reverted without question (<a href="#Enforcement_by_reverting_edits">exceptions</a>), and any pages where the blocked/banned editor is both the page's creator and the only substantial contributor may be speedily deleted under <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CSD#G5" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CSD">CSD#G5</a>. </td> 
         <td>Edits by the editor or on their behalf that are clearly within the topic area may be reverted without question (<a href="#Enforcement_by_reverting_edits">exceptions</a>), and any pages where the banned editor is both the page's creator and the only substantial contributor may be speedily deleted under <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CSD#G5" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CSD">CSD#G5</a>. If there is any reasonable doubt as to whether the page falls within the topic ban, discussion prior to deletion is generally warranted. </td>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conduct_towards_banned_editors">Conduct towards banned editors</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:CONDUCTTOBANNED"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:CONDUCTTOBANNED&amp;redirect=no">WP:CONDUCTTOBANNED</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Gravedancing" title="Wikipedia:Gravedancing">Wikipedia:Gravedancing</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Wikipedia's hope for banned editors is that they will leave Wikipedia or the affected area with their pride and dignity intact, whether permanently or for the duration of their ban. <b>It is unacceptable to take advantage of banned editors</b>, whether by mocking, baiting, or otherwise abusing them. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPA">Personal attacks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OUTING" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:OUTING">outing</a> and other behaviours remain unacceptable even if directed towards a banned editor. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scope_and_reciprocity">Scope and reciprocity</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:BANOP"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:BANOP&amp;redirect=no">WP:BANOP</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>The English-language Wikipedia does not have authority over the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Meta" title="Wikipedia:Meta">Meta-Wiki</a>, Wikimedia sister projects, or Wikipedias in languages other than English. As such, bans issued by the English Wikipedia community or Arbitration Committee have no effect on other projects. </p>
