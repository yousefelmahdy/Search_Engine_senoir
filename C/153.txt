        <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span></h2> 
        <p><b>Did you know</b>... is a section you can see on the Main Page. Beginners should consult <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Glossary" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Glossary">this Did You Know glossary</a> if they don't understand any of the terms used. Beginners may like to start by looking at a summary of our <a class="mw-selflink selflink">many rules</a>: this provides links to pages with more detailed explanations (which in turn may link to even more detailed pages). </p>
        <p>Here are the ways you can help. The ways are listed in increasing order of the Did You Know experience that is required to do these jobs well. So beginners should choose from the top of the list. Statistics show most people who read this page don't click anything, but that wasn't the intent. Please choose from the list and read on; there's more. </p> 
        <ul>
         <li>You can help <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Proofreading" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Proofreading">proofread nominations</a>.</li> 
         <li>You can <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Article" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Article">write a new article</a>. But you might want to study the nomination process first or write your article in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_space" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:User space">user space</a>, or your article might be too old before it's ready.</li> 
         <li>You can <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Nomination" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Nomination">nominate a new article and write a Did You Know hook for it</a>.</li> 
         <li>You can <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Approval" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Approval">approve or disapprove nominations</a>.</li> 
         <li>You can <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Preparation_areas" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Preparation areas">promote nominations</a> to the preparation areas.</li> 
         <li>You can help <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKADMINBOT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKADMINBOT">our software</a> to schedule regular moves from the preparation areas to the Main Page, or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Guide" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Guide">do it the old way</a> if the software isn't working.</li>
        </ul>

        <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nomination_rules">Nomination rules</span></h2> 
        <p>If you have never written a Wikipedia article, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Your_first_article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Your first article">Wikipedia:Your first article</a>. The following criteria are used to determine whether a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#nominate" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">nominated</a> <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article</a> is eligible for Did You Know: </p> 
        <ol>
         <li><b>New</b> – The first priority is to understand that the article must be new, because newness can't be fixed later. To make <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/New_article" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/New article">a long story</a> short: At least 80% of the article must be new (seven days old or less) when the article is nominated.</li> 
         <li><b>Long enough</b> – The article must have at least <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Article_length" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Article length">1,500 characters of prose</a> – not the whole article, just prose.</li> 
         <li><b>Citations</b> – The article must be cited <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Citation" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Citation">like this</a>.</li> 
         <li><b>Neutral</b> – <i>Articles on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLP">living individuals</a> are carefully checked</i> to ensure that no unsourced or poorly sourced negative material is included. Articles and hooks which focus unduly on negative aspects of living individuals <b>should be avoided</b>.</li> 
         <li><b>Free of copyvio or plagiarism</b> – Except for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NFC#Text" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NFC">brief, clearly marked quotations</a>, the article does not include material <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copy-paste" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Copy-paste">copied</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Close_paraphrasing" title="Wikipedia:Close paraphrasing">closely paraphrased</a> from copyrighted sources. Material taken from <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Public_domain" title="Wikipedia:Public domain">public domain</a> or <a href="/wiki/Copyright#Can_I_add_something_to_Wikipedia_that_I_got_from_somewhere_else.3F" title="Copyright">compatibly licensed</a> sources is properly attributed in line with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Plagiarism" title="Wikipedia:Plagiarism">Wikipedia:Plagiarism</a>.</li> 
         <li><b>You knew this</b> but – to settle arguments we wrote <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/You_knew_this" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/You knew this">this stuff</a> down.</li>
        </ol> 
        <p>The next step is nominating your article for Did You Know. You can ask a Did You Know regular or see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Admins" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Admins">this list of Did You Know administrators</a>, or you can nominate it yourself as described <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Nomination" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Nomination">here</a>. </p> 
        <div style="border:1px solid #a3bfb1; background:#DFFFDF; width:82%; padding:4px; margin-bottom:10px; align:left"> 
         <div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed" style="box-sizing:border-box;width:100%;font-size:95%;padding:4px;border:none;">

           =====Other, obvious rules=====
          </div>
          <div class="mw-collapsible-content" style="background:transparent;text-align:left;;">

           </div>
           <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
            <div class="module-shortcutlist">
             <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
            </div>
            <ul>
             <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:DYKRN&amp;redirect=no">WP:DYKRN</a></span></li>
            </ul>
           </div> 
           <p>R1: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">Articles</a> nominated for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AFD">deletion</a> won't be used unless/until they survive the deletion process. </p>
           <p>R2: The article is likely to be rejected for unresolved edit warring or having <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Template_messages/Disputes" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Template messages/Disputes">dispute tags</a>. (Removing the tags without consensus doesn't count.) </p>
           <p>R3: There is a reasonable expectation that an article which is to appear on the front page, even a short one, should appear to be complete and not some sort of work in progress. Therefore, articles which include unexpanded headers are likely to be rejected. Articles which fail to deal adequately with the topic are also likely to be rejected. For example, an article about a book that fails to summarize the book's contents, but contains only a bio of the author and some critics' views, is likely to be rejected as insufficiently comprehensive. </p>
           <p>R4: If your article contradicts an existing article, the contradiction should be resolved one way or the other before your article is approved. Don't expect Did You Know regulars to resolve the contradiction for you. </p>
           <p>R5: If there is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Stub" title="Wikipedia:Stub">stub</a> tag, it should ordinarily be removed if the article is long enough for DYK. </p>
           <p>R6: To some extent, Did You Know <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#approval" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">approval</a> is a subjective process. No amount of studying rules, almost-rules and precedents will guarantee approval, nor will violating any rule guarantee disapproval. Just because an unfamiliar criterion isn't listed, doesn't mean you can't be disqualified. The subjective decision might depend on an attempt to circumvent the details of the rules, especially if the attempt doesn't address the underlying purpose of improving the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hook</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article</a>. </p> R7: If the nominator is not one of the significant contributors to the article, then he or she should notify them of the nomination.

        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Article_length">Article length</span></h3> 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
        <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
         <span id="WP:DYKCN"></span>
        </div>
        <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
         <div class="module-shortcutlist">
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:DYKCN&amp;redirect=no">WP:DYKCN</a></span></li>
         </ul>
        </div> 
        <p>C1: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">Articles</a> must have a minimum of 1,500 <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#characters" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">characters</a> (including spaces) of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#prose" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">prose</a> (not the entire article, just prose) as counted by <a href="/wiki/User:Shubinator/DYKcheck" title="User:Shubinator/DYKcheck">this script</a>. Did You Know defines "prose" to exclude infoboxes, categories, references, lists, tables, block quotes, headers, images and captions, the "See also" section if any, Table of Contents, edit buttons, "citation needed" and similar superscripted text, and reference link numbers like [6]. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/DYKcheck" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/DYKcheck">More details here</a>. </p>
        <p>C2: Proposed lists need 1,500+ characters of prose as defined above. The listed items themselves are not counted as part of the 1,500 DYK qualifying characters. </p>
        <p>C3: In practice, articles longer than 1,500 characters may still be rejected as too short, at the discretion of the selecting reviewers and administrators. </p> 
        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Citation_requisites">Citation requisites</span></h3> 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
        <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
         <span id="WP:DYKDN"></span>
        </div>
        <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
         <div class="module-shortcutlist">
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:DYKDN&amp;redirect=no">WP:DYKDN</a></span></li>
         </ul>
        </div> 
        <p>D1: The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hook</a> may not be written yet, because that's part of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#nominate" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">nominating</a>. But when the hook is written, the hook fact <i>must</i> be <b>cited</b> in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article</a> with an <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Inline_citations" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Inline citations">inline citation</a></b>, since inline citations are used to support specific statements in an article. The hook fact must have an inline citation right after it since the fact is an extraordinary claim; citing the hook fact at the end of the paragraph is not acceptable. Once again, that rule is only for the hook fact, not for every sentence in the article. (Yes, we know featured articles don't have that rule, but that's because featured articles don't have hook facts.) </p>
        <p>D2: The article in general should use <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Inline_citations" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Inline citations">inline citations</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RS">reliable sources</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Rule_of_thumb" title="Rule of thumb">rule of thumb</a> is one inline citation per paragraph, excluding the intro, plot summaries, and paragraphs which summarize other cited content. Newly sourced BLPs are expected to be thoroughly sourced. </p>
        <p>D3: Sources should be <i>properly labelled</i>; that is, not under an "External links" header. References in the article must not be <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#bare_URLs" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">bare URLs</a> according to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Did_you_know/Archive_29#Reference_section" title="Wikipedia talk:Did you know/Archive 29">Wikipedia talk:Did you know/Archive 29#Reference section</a>. </p>
        <p>D4: Wikipedia, including Wikipedias in other languages, is not considered a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_source" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Reliable source">Wikipedia:Reliable source</a>. </p>
        <p>D5: Multiple sources are generally required, to ensure the article meets the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GNG" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:GNG">general notability guideline</a>. </p> 
        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Article_sources">Article sources</span></h3> 
        <p>Any editor may <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#nominate" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">nominate</a> new <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">articles</a> for Did You Know. Some new articles may be found at: </p> 
        <ul>

        <ul>
         <li>M1: For purposes of DYK, a "new" article is no more than <b>seven days old</b> when nominated (well, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Not_exactly" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Not exactly">not exactly</a>). This does not include articles split from older articles.</li> 
         <li>M2: For purposes of DYK, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Fivefold" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Fivefold">80% new</a> is enough to be considered "new". To add enough new material to make an article 80% new is called <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/fivefold" class="extiw" title="wikt:fivefold">fivefold</a> expansion, because four-fifths is 80%.</li> 
         <li>M3: Newly promoted <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:GA">good articles</a> (that weren't approved earlier) are also acceptable as new articles</li> 
         <li>M4: Try to pick articles that are <b>original</b> to Wikipedia (not inclusions of free data sources) and <b>interesting</b> to a wide audience. Working on existing unsourced BLPs, such as those listed at <a href="/wiki/CAT:BLP" class="mw-redirect" title="CAT:BLP">CAT:BLP</a>, is also most welcome – the community is working hard to ensure that all BLPs are sourced in the future, and assisting in eliminating the backlog is a valuable contribution to Wikipedia.</li> 
         <li>M5: Articles that have been featured (bold link) in blurbs on the main page's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ITN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ITN">In the News</a> section are not eligible, nor are items that have been on DYK before (pre-expansion, for example). Articles that have been linked to from the mainpage but were not featured links are still eligible.</li>

           <p>If you have never submitted a Did You Know <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#nominate" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">nomination</a> before, you don't have to master DYKcheck. But maybe you should; DYKcheck automates <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#prose" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">prose</a> <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#characters" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">character</a> counting and some of our other rules, so you don't have to learn them. If you routinely approve <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hooks</a> for others, you should install DYKcheck. If not, you can use DYKcheck without installing it. DYKcheck (or prosesizebytes, or prosesize, which give the same count) is the preferred method for counting characters in an article's prose, and usually carries the most weight at DYK, because it counts only the prose as defined by Did You Know rules, thus avoiding mistakes and providing an impartial settlement of disputed counting. </p> 
           <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Counting_prose_characters_without_DYKcheck">Counting prose characters without DYKcheck</span></h2> 
           <p>You'll probably make a mistake trying to count this way, but you'll be close. Use a free website like <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.javascriptkit.com/script/script2/charcount.shtml">this</a>, or an external <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a> <a href="/wiki/Computer_program" title="Computer program">program</a> that has a character-counting feature. For example, if you are using <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Word" title="Microsoft Word">Microsoft Word</a>, select the text from the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Articles" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Articles">article</a> page (or, in the case of "Did you know" nominations, the <a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Did_you_know" title="Template talk:Did you know">DYK talk page</a>) – not the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:How_to_edit_a_page" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:How to edit a page">edit</a> page containing <a href="/wiki/Wikitext" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikitext">Wikitext</a> – then <a href="/wiki/Cut,_copy_and_paste#Copy_and_paste" class="mw-redirect" title="Cut, copy and paste">copy and paste</a> it into a blank document. Delete everything DYKcheck doesn't count: infoboxes, categories, references, lists, tables, block quotes, headers, images and captions, the "See also" section if any, Table of Contents, edit buttons, "Citation needed" and similar superscripted text, and reference link numbers like [6]. Click "Tools" ("Review" in Office 2007), then "Word Count", and note the "Characters (with spaces)" figure. Other <a href="/wiki/Word_processing" class="mw-redirect" title="Word processing">word processing</a> programs may have a similar feature. For Mac users, <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a> has a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.apple.com/downloads/dashboard/calculate_convert/wordcounterwidget.html">Word counter widget</a> available for Mac OS X 10.4 or later. <b>Note</b>: The character counts indicated on "Revision history" pages are not accurate for DYK purposes as they include categories, infoboxes and similar text in articles, and comments and signatures in hooks on the suggestions page. </p> 
           <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Using_DYKcheck_if_you_have_a_username">Using DYKcheck if you have a username</span></h2> Copy the following phrase to your clipboard and then add the following phrase: 
           <div class="mw-highlight mw-highlight-lang-javascript mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr">
            <pre><span></span><span class="nx">importScript</span><span class="p">(</span><span class="s1">'User:Shubinator/DYKcheck.js'</span><span class="p">);</span> <span class="c1">//DYKcheck tool</span>
</pre>
           </div> to <b><a href="/wiki/Special:MyPage/common.js" title="Special:MyPage/common.js">your common.js</a></b> page and save it. If you don't have a common.js yet, it will be created when you click on the red link to the left. If you do have a common.js page, the link to the left will be blue. In that case, click on the blue link and add the phrase to your common.js page. If you only wish to add DYKCheck to one particular skin, then go to your <a href="/wiki/Special:MyPage/skin.js" title="Special:MyPage/skin.js">skin.js</a> page and put that line in it. There will be a scary warning about malware, but it hasn't hurt me any. 
           <p>After saving, you have to bypass your browser's cache to see the changes. Mozilla/Safari: hold down Shift while clicking Reload (or press Ctrl-Shift-R), Internet Explorer: press Ctrl-F5, Opera/Konqueror: press F5. For other browsers, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Bypass_your_cache" title="Wikipedia:Bypass your cache">Wikipedia:Bypass your cache</a>. </p>
           <p>If you are on a Wikipedia page that starts with Wikipedia:, User:, Talk:, or anything with ":", go to a normal page like <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">Dog</a>. You should then see the words "DYK check" on the left side of your screen, assuming you use the default <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Preferences" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Preferences">Wikipedia:Preferences</a>. Go to the page you want to check and click "DYK check". The "Prose size (text only)" figure is the figure used by Did You Know. The text actually counted is colored yellow. If DYKcheck finds errors, they will be listed at the top of the page and colored red. The DYKCheck can take several seconds to compile the conclusion so watch for that. </p> 
           <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Using_DYKcheck_without_installing_it">Using DYKcheck without installing it</span></h2> With or without a username, you can use the DYKcheck tool without installing it; just put this
           <blockquote>
            <p>javascript:importScript('User:Shubinator/DYKcheck.js'); dykCheck();</p>
           </blockquote>into your <a href="/wiki/URL" title="URL">URL</a> and hit Enter when you're viewing a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Mainspace" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Mainspace">mainspace</a> page. 
           <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prosesizebytes">Prosesizebytes</span></h2> 
           <p>Prosesizebytes is similar to DYKcheck, but it only counts characters of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#prose" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">prose</a> without checking anything else. Experienced users use prosesizebytes anyway because it's a few seconds faster. Installing prosesizebytes, and using it with or without installing it, is about the same as for DYKcheck, but substitute the file name User:Dr pda/prosesizebytes.js instead of User:Shubinator/DYKcheck.js, and the button is labeled "Page size" instead of "DYK check". Or use the instructions at <a href="/wiki/User_talk:Dr_pda/prosesizebytes.js" title="User talk:Dr pda/prosesizebytes.js">User talk:Dr pda/prosesizebytes.js</a>. </p>

        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fivefold_expansion">Fivefold expansion</span></h3> 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
        <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
         <span id="WP:DYKFN"></span><span id="WP:5X"></span>
        </div>
        <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
         <div class="module-shortcutlist">
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:DYKFN&amp;redirect=no">WP:DYKFN</a></span></li>
          <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:5X&amp;redirect=no">WP:5X</a></span></li>

        <p>F1: Former redirects, stubs, or other short articles in which the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#prose" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">prose</a> portion (not the whole <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article</a>) has been <b>expanded <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#fivefold" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">fivefold</a></b> or more within the last seven days since nomination (well, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Not_exactly" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Not exactly">not exactly</a>), are acceptable as "new" articles. The content with which the article has been expanded must be <i>new content</i>, not text copied from other articles. For step-by-step guide on how to calculate whether the expansion is big enough and recent enough, see <b><a href="/wiki/User:Rjanag/Calculating_fivefold_expansion_by_hand" title="User:Rjanag/Calculating fivefold expansion by hand">User:Rjanag/Calculating fivefold expansion by hand</a></b>. </p>
        <p>F2: Fivefold expansion means at least five times as much <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#prose" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">prose</a> as the previously existing article – no matter how bad it was (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyvio" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Copyvio">copyvios</a> are an exception), no matter whether you kept any of it, and no matter if it was <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AFD">up for deletion</a>. This may be a bad surprise, but we don't have enough time and volunteers to reach consensus on the quality of each previous article. </p>
        <p>F3: "Seven days old" means seven days old in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Article_space" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Article space">article space</a>. You may write your article on a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Subpage" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Subpage">user subpage</a> and perfect it for months. The seven days start when you move it into article space. Such moves are often overlooked when enforcing the seven day rule, so we may need a reminder. But if you move the edit history along with the article, we might not believe you moved it, because it isn't obvious in the edit history. </p>
        <p>F4: If some of the text was copied from another Wikipedia article, then it must be expanded fivefold as if the copied text had been a separate article. </p>
        <p>F5: The age of the previously existing article used to calculate fivefold expansion depends on the date of the <i>next</i> version, not the date that version was created. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Fivefold_day_explanation" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Fivefold day explanation">Explanation here</a>. </p>
        <p>F6: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Meanies" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Meanies">The fivefold rule is controversial.</a> </p>

        <p>G1: To calculate <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#fivefold" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">fivefold</a> <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#expansion" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">expansion</a> since a specific day, which I will call July 18, 2008, for definiteness: 1. Count the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#characters" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">characters</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#prose" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">prose</a>-only portion of the current version. 2. On the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article's</a> history screen, click the latest time stamp <i>before</i> July 18, not the first time stamp for July 18. 3. Divide by the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#prose" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">prose</a>-only characters on that screen. </p>
        <p>To explain the counter-intuitive step 2, I emphasize the difference between an edit's <i>change</i>, which you see by clicking "prev" on the history page, and an edit's <i>result</i>, which you see by clicking the time stamp on the history page. Although an edit's change and an edit's result are listed on the same line, the edit's change really comes between that edit's result and the previous edit's result. Similarly, an edit's result really comes between that edit's change and the next edit's change, even though an edit's change and an edit's result are shown on the same line. </p>
        <p>Example. On January 1, 2006, a 100 character stub is created. At 1:00 on July 18, 2008, the 100 characters are expanded to 1000 characters. An hour later at 2:00 July 18, 2008, the article is further expanded to 2000 characters. When I say it that way, the expansion is clearly 20x (or equivalently, 95% new) and qualifies for Did You Know. But to count the 100 characters, they wouldn't be listed as 1:00 July 18. The 100 characters existed on July 18 before 1:00, but the 100 characters were the <i>result</i> of the previous edit. So you would have to click the 2006 edit to count the 100 characters, even though 2006 is much too old for Did You Know. If you made the mistake of clicking the first edit for July 18, you would get the <i>result</i> of that first edit and therefore miss the <i>change</i> of that edit, and count 1000 characters, resulting in 2x expansion and an unjust disqualification. </p>

         <p>L1: A <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hook</a> introducing more than one <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article</a> is an exception to the hook length rule. If your hook introduces more than one article, you can do a basic calculation by subtracting the number of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#characters" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">characters</a> in the bolded character string for each additional new article beyond the first. If having done that the hook length is still 200 characters or less, it is probably an acceptable length. If it is over 200 characters after the subtractions, it may still be considered eligible if the hook is reasonably compact and readable, but such hooks will be considered on a case-by-case basis. </p>

        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hook_requirements">Hook requirements</span></h3>

        <p>A <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hook</a> is what you see, several times, in the <a href="/wiki/Main_Page" title="Main Page">Main Page</a>'s Did You Know section. Each hook begins with "...". </p>
        <p>H1: The hook should be formatted like the hooks you see on the Main Page. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Hook_format" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Hook format">Checklist here</a>. </p>
        <p>H2: The hook itself should be <b>concise</b> (fewer than about 200 characters, including spaces). <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Hook_length" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Hook length">More details here</a>. </p>
        <p>H3: The hook should refer to established facts that are not likely to change, and should be relevant for more than just novelty or newness. </p>
        <p>H4: The hook should be <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPOV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPOV">neutral</a></b>. </p>
        <p>H5: The "Did you know?" fact <b>must</b> be mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article</a> and <b>cited with an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#Inline_citations" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">inline citation</a></b> to a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RS">reliable source</a> since inline citations are used to support specific statements in an article. A lot of submissions are made which fail to meet one or both of these criteria. Nominators should ensure that their submissions meet both of these criteria or their submissions <b>will fail DYK eligibility</b>. </p>
        <p>H6: Articles and hooks which focus on negative aspects of living individuals <b>should be avoided</b>. </p>
        <p>H7: When you write the DYK item (or "hook") please make it "hooky", that is, short, punchy, catchy, and likely to draw the readers in to wanting to read the article. An interesting hook is more likely to draw in a variety of readers. Shorter hooks are preferred to longer ones, as long as they don't misstate the article content. </p>
        <p>H8: Once again, you need <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/New_article" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/New article">a new, qualifying article</a>, not just a hook, no matter how interesting the hook is. </p>
        <p>H9: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Pipe" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Pipe">Piping</a> the article link is sometimes discouraged, but many hooks are better when the link is piped, and show on the Main Page that way. Disambiguated article titles like <b><a href="/wiki/Gene_Green_(baseball)" title="Gene Green (baseball)">Gene Green (baseball)</a></b> are always piped like this: <code>'''[[Gene Green (baseball)|Gene Green]]'''</code>. </p>
        <p>H10: Don't falsely assume that everyone worldwide knows what country or sport you're talking about. </p>
        <p>H11: If the subject is a work of fiction or a fictional character, the hook must involve the real world in some way. </p>
        <p>H12: No links to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disambiguation" title="Wikipedia:Disambiguation">disambiguation</a> pages. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disambiguation#Links_to_disambiguation_pages" title="Wikipedia:Disambiguation">Here is the Wikipedia guideline.</a> </p>
        <p>H13: If the hook uses a possessive apostrophe after the qualifying article, use {{<a href="/wiki/Template:%60" title="Template:`">`</a>}} or {{<a href="/wiki/Template:%60s" title="Template:`s">`s</a>}} to keep the bold text and the apostrophe distinct e.g. "... that <b><a href="/wiki/John" class="mw-disambig" title="John">John</a></b><span class="nowrap">'s</span> house (etc)?" If the article is in italics (e.g. a ship's name), use the slightly different templates {{<a href="/wiki/Template:%27" title="Template:'">'</a>}} or {{<a href="/wiki/Template:%27s" title="Template:'s">'s</a>}} e.g. "... that <i><b><a href="/wiki/HMS_Hood" title="HMS Hood">HMS Hood</a></b></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> anchor (etc)?" </p> 
        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hook_format">Hook format</span></h3> 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
        <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
         <span id="WP:DYKIN"></span>
        </div>
        <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
         <div class="module-shortcutlist">
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:DYKIN&amp;redirect=no">WP:DYKIN</a></span></li>
         </ul>
        </div> 
        <p>The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hook</a> format should resemble hooks on the Main Page. Specifically: </p>
        <p>I1: Entries should start with an <a href="/wiki/Ellipsis" title="Ellipsis">ellipsis</a> of exactly three <a href="/wiki/Full_stop" title="Full stop">full stops</a> (periods) (not the ellipsis character …) and a space. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ELLIPSIS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ELLIPSIS">WP:ELLIPSIS</a>. </p>
        <p>I2: The hook (or in the unusual case of multiple sentences, only the hook's first sentence) should end with one question mark. That is because the implied "Did you know ..." grammatically makes the first sentence, and only the first sentence, into a <a href="/wiki/Question" title="Question">question</a> that needs a question mark. </p>
        <p>I3: Don't write "... That". That is, don't capitalize the first word after the ellipsis (usually "that") unless it's a <a href="/wiki/Proper_noun" class="mw-redirect" title="Proper noun">proper noun</a>. </p>
        <p>I4: The hook must link to a qualifying new <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article</a>. </p>
        <p>I5: The title of the new article must be in <b>bold</b>. </p>
        <p>I6: If your article title appears <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PIPE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PIPE">unpiped</a> in the hook, don't capitalize the first word unless it's part of a <a href="/wiki/Proper_noun" class="mw-redirect" title="Proper noun">proper noun</a>, even though that word is always capitalized in the article title. </p>
        <p>I7: No <a href="/w/index.php?title=Redlinks&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Redlinks (page does not exist)">redlinks</a> in the hook. </p>
        <p>I8: No space before the question mark. No period before the question mark. </p>
        <p>I9: No <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:External_links" title="Wikipedia:External links">external links</a> in the hook. </p> 
        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hook_length">Hook length</span></h3> 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
        <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
         <span id="WP:DYKQN"></span>

        </div> 
        <p>Q1: The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hook</a> itself should be <b>concise</b> (fewer than about 200 <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#characters" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">characters</a>, including spaces and the question mark, but excluding the "..." or any "(pictured)"). While 200 is an outside limit, hooks slightly under 200 characters may still be rejected at the discretion of the selecting reviewers and administrators. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Multiple_articles" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Multiple articles">Multiple articles are an exception.</a> You can count the 200 characters manually. Otherwise, select the text as it displays to the public (not from the edit screen which contains <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikitext" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Wikitext">wikitext</a>), and then <a href="/wiki/Cut,_copy_and_paste#Copy_and_paste" class="mw-redirect" title="Cut, copy and paste">copy and paste</a> it into a blank document using a free website like <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.javascriptkit.com/script/script2/charcount.shtml">this</a>, or using an external <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a> <a href="/wiki/Computer_program" title="Computer program">program</a> like <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Word" title="Microsoft Word">Microsoft Word</a> that has a character-counting feature. Don't use the character count on the <a href="/wiki/T:TDYK" class="mw-redirect" title="T:TDYK">suggestion page</a> history screen, which includes signatures and other comments. </p>

        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Objections_to_your_nomination">Objections to your nomination</span></h3> 
        <p><span id="A1">A1</span>: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">Hooks</a> are subject without notice to copyediting as they move to the <a href="/wiki/Main_Page" title="Main Page">Main Page</a>. The nature of the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#DYK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">DYK</a> process makes it impractical to consult users over every such edit. Also, watch your nomination's subpage to ensure that no issues have been raised about your hook, because if you do not respond to issues raised your hook may not be featured at all. </p> 
        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Patience">Patience</span></h3> 
        <p><span id="A2">A2</span>: If there is no response, please bear with us: we won't reject the hook unless someone has raised an unsatisfied objection, even if it has reached the <a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Did_you_know#Older_nominations" title="Template talk:Did you know">Template talk:Did you know#Older nominations</a> section, <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia_talk:Did_you_know&amp;diff=270207912&amp;oldid=270191056">according to this</a>. Or if the hook has been <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#approval" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">approved</a>, we are especially unlikely to delete the hook until it goes on to a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#preparation_areas" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">preparation area</a> for the Main Page. Other hooks listed in the same section as your hook are also waiting, and scrolling to the bottom of the page gives you an idea of how much longer to wait, as described at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Lore#Daily_headings_on_the_suggestions_page" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Lore">Daily headings on the suggestions page</a>. </p>
        <p><span id="A3">A3</span>: If you can't find the hook you submitted on <a href="/wiki/T:TDYK" class="mw-redirect" title="T:TDYK">the suggestions page</a>, in most cases it means your article has been approved and is in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#queue" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">queues</a> or preparation areas for display on the Main Page. You can check whether your hook has been moved to the queue by reviewing the <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Queue" title="Template:Did you know/Queue">queue listings</a>. You can also check the Main Page, and check the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Recent_additions" title="Wikipedia:Recent additions">archives</a> (where it goes after being on the Main Page). </p>
        <p><span id="A4">A4</span>: If your hook is not in any of the queues or preparation areas, not already on the Main Page, and not in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Recent_additions" title="Wikipedia:Recent additions">archives</a>, it has probably been rejected. Nominations can be rejected if the hook is very old and has unresolved issues for which any discussion has gone stale. If you think your hook has been unfairly rejected, you can query its rejection on the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:DYK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia talk:DYK">discussion page</a>, but as a general rule rejected hooks will only be restored in exceptional circumstances. So be sure to satisfy any objections to your nomination before that happens. </p>

        <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Proofreading">Proofreading</span></h2> 
        <p>This page is about proofreading <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hooks</a>, although proofreading <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">articles</a> is also helpful. Only a few thousand people will look at a Did You Know article during its twelve hours of fame – but almost a million people will load the Main Page during that time, including the hooks, although most people don't really look at the hooks. Source <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://stats.grok.se/">http://stats.grok.se/</a> . One Main Page misspelling or comma can change the world more than you realize. </p>

The rest of this page is mostly about proofreading here at Template talk:Did you know, but you can also proofread the following:</li>

        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Proofreading_Template_talk:Did_you_know">Proofreading Template talk:Did you know</span></h3> 
        <p>If you find proofreading mistakes at Template talk:Did you know, first decide if your change is big, little, or in between. In general, changing a couple words without changing the intended meaning is a little change. If an entry sounds as if English isn't the author's native language, then changing several words can be considered "little". For a little change, just change it; nobody really wants to know that you added a space after their three dots. For an in-between change, change it and then explain what you changed. For a big change, suggest a rewritten hook using an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#ALT,_ALT1,_ALT2_etc." class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">ALT</a>. Look through the rest of the page to see how ALT's are formatted. </p>
        <p>Remember to check ALT hooks in addition the the hook at the top, some of which occur in the middle of a paragraph full of comments. Just because an ALT isn't formalized as an ALT, doesn't mean someone can't copy it to a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#preparation_areas" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">preparation area</a> without noticing typos. </p>
        <p>Hook proofreaders who are familiar with the rules of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Nomination" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Nomination">Wikipedia:Did you know/Nomination</a> can ensure compliance with those rules. <a href="/wiki/User:Shubinator/DYKcheck" title="User:Shubinator/DYKcheck">User:Shubinator/DYKcheck</a> automates some of that process. </p> 
        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Specific_errors_to_look_for">Specific errors to look for</span></h3> 
        <p>Of course you should fix or question anything that's wrong, but here are some specific errors you can look for. Most of them are from our nomination rules, linked above. The search for many of these problems should be automated.         <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Search_strings">Search strings</span></h4> 
        <p>Some routine proofreading errors can be found by searching the whole page for the following <a href="/wiki/String_(computer_science)" title="String (computer science)">strings</a>: </p> 
        <ul>
         <li>"..t" to find a missing space after the three dots, which are called an <a href="/wiki/Ellipsis" title="Ellipsis">ellipsis</a>. Add the space. Note this trick only works if the hook begins with "that", "the" or some other word beginning with "t".</li> 
         <li>"&nbsp;?" to find a space before the question mark at the end of the first sentence (remove the space)</li> 
         <li>"...." to find an ellipsis with more than three dots (remove all but three)</li> 
         <li>"}.. " (that's a curly bracket and two periods) to find an ellipsis with only two dots (it needs three)</li> 
         <li>"'.. " is a variation of "*.. " if <b>AltN</b> is used</li> 
         <li>"}that" to find an ellipsis that is missing altogether</li> 
         <li>"} that", "'that", "' that" are variations of "}that"</li> 
         <li>"pictured) " (note the space) to find (pictured) or (object pictured) unitalicized. Our convention is to <i>italicize</i> that word or words when there is an image.</li> 
         <li>"'')" (single quote, single quote, parenthesis) is a sign of <i>italicizing</i> "pictured" but forgetting to <i>italicize</i> the parentheses, according to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKJN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKJN">J7</a></li>
        </ul> 
        <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Errors_typical_of_Did_You_Know">Errors typical of Did You Know</span></h4> 
        <ul>
         <li>the link to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article</a> should be <b>bold</b> (sometimes the article link is missing altogether)</li> 
         <li>the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hook</a> should end in a question mark (in case of a multi-sentence hook, the first sentence must end in a question mark, and <a href="/wiki/User:Shubinator/DYKcheck" title="User:Shubinator/DYKcheck">User:Shubinator/DYKcheck</a> can't automatically check the total hook length)</li> 
         <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Hook_length" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Hook length">hook length</a>. If it's over the limit of <i>about</i> 200 characters including spaces, I link to <a href="/wiki/User:Art_LaPella/Long_hook" title="User:Art LaPella/Long hook">User:Art LaPella/Long hook</a>, combined with {{<a href="/wiki/Template:DYK%3Fno" title="Template:DYK?no">subst:DYK?no</a>}} (which shows as <a href="/wiki/File:Symbol_possible_vote.svg" class="image"><img alt="Symbol possible vote.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Symbol_possible_vote.svg/18px-Symbol_possible_vote.svg.png" decoding="async" width="18" height="19" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Symbol_possible_vote.svg/27px-Symbol_possible_vote.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Symbol_possible_vote.svg/36px-Symbol_possible_vote.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="185"></a>), to explain this problem. With practice, you can recognize a hook that's too long by how many lines it occupies on your screen, after mentally excluding wikitext that isn't counted.</li> 
         <li>Don't capitalize the first word of the article title, just because it's capitalized in the title (unless it's a <a href="/wiki/Proper_noun" class="mw-redirect" title="Proper noun">proper noun</a>)</li> 
         <li>Excluding <a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Did_you_know" title="Template talk:Did you know">Template talk:Did you know</a>, the word <i>(pictured)</i> should be just after what is actually pictured – especially after the picture has been changed. Therefore, there should be one and only one word <i>(pictured)</i> in the entire list.</li> 
         <li>Duplicate "that", duplicate ellipsis, duplicate question mark ... "that that" at a preparation area or beyond is especially common.</li> 
         <li>Also, if you know all these rules, you can be alert to answer routine questions about those rules.</li>
        </ul> 
        <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Errors_you_would_proofread_on_any_page">Errors you would proofread on any page</span></h4> 
        <p>Remember, almost a million people will at least load this stuff, so routine proofreading is more important than on just any page. </p> 
        <ul>
         <li>spelling</li> 
         <li>grammar</li> 
         <li>capitalization</li> 
         <li>apostrophes</li> 
         <li><a href="/wiki/MOS:NUM" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:NUM">MOS:NUM</a></li> 
         <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DASH" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DASH">WP:DASH</a></li> 
         <li>commas after phrases like "Cleveland, Ohio", "Paris, France", and "June 5, 2009" (see <a href="/wiki/Comma" title="Comma">comma</a> article), and around nonrestrictive <a href="/wiki/Appositive_phrase" class="mw-redirect" title="Appositive phrase">appositive phrases</a></li> 
         <li>correcting links to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disambiguation" title="Wikipedia:Disambiguation">disambiguation pages</a> according to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disambiguation#Links_to_disambiguation_pages" title="Wikipedia:Disambiguation">this</a>. <a href="/wiki/User:Splarka/dabfinder.js" title="User:Splarka/dabfinder.js">User:Splarka/dabfinder.js</a> helps find them. Another way to find short pages, which are usually disambiguation pages, is to click "preferences", "Misc", and then change the "stub link" number.</li> 
         <li><i>italicize</i> books, court cases, ships etc. as described at <a href="/wiki/MOS:TITLE" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:TITLE">MOS:TITLE</a></li> 
         <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikilink" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Wikilink">Wikilink</a> unfamiliar words</li> 
         <li>correct a missing space between a word and the wikilink symbols "[[" for the next word, or between "]]" and the next word</li>
        </ul> 
       </div>

        <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Update_process">Update process</span></h2> 
        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Approval">Approval</span></h3> 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
        <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
         <span id="WP:DYKBN"></span>
        </div>
        <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
         <div class="module-shortcutlist">
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:DYKBN&amp;redirect=no">WP:DYKBN</a></span></li>
         </ul>
        </div> 
        <p><b>First-time reviewers may find the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Reviewing_guide" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Reviewing guide">DYK reviewing guide</a> helpful.</b> </p>
        <p>B1:Approval is verifying that all the rules of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Article" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Article">articles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Nomination" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Nomination">nominations</a> have been followed, while recognizing that no list of rules can cover everything. The nominations to approve are found at <a href="/wiki/T:TDYK" class="mw-redirect" title="T:TDYK">T:TDYK</a>. <a href="/wiki/User:Shubinator/DYKcheck" title="User:Shubinator/DYKcheck">DYKcheck</a> is intended to automate some of this verifying. </p>
        <p>B2: You don't have to be an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrator" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Administrator">administrator</a> or a Did You Know regular to approve or disapprove a nomination. Of course the judgments of regulars are less likely to be challenged. </p>
        <p>B3: You are not allowed to approve your own <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hook</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article</a>. </p>
        <p>B4: At <a href="/wiki/T:TDYK" class="mw-redirect" title="T:TDYK">the suggestions page</a>, what do "length, date, and ref verified" and its synonyms mean? Roughly speaking, "Length" (synonyms "size", "article length") means <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Article_length" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Article length">article length</a>; "date" (synonym "history") means <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/New_article" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/New article">new article</a>, and "ref" (synonyms "reference", "source") means <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Citation" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Citation">citations</a>. "AGF" or "good faith" (referring to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Assume_good_faith" title="Wikipedia:Assume good faith">Wikipedia:Assume good faith</a>) means that the approver didn't read the reference (it wasn't on the Internet, or it wasn't in English, or a fee was required). </p> 
        <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbols">Symbols</span></h4> 
        <p>B5: If you want to confirm that an article is ready to be placed on a later update, or that there is an issue with the article or hook, you may use the following symbols (optional) to point the issues out. The first two, and the last one, are almost always used when finally verifying or rejecting a hook, because these are what DYK volunteers look for when they are looking for hooks to prepare a new update, or clearing out rejected hooks. </p>
        <p><br> Within the context of Wikipedia's "Did you know..." (DYK) project, <b>reviewing</b> refers specifically to the process by which a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Nomination" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Nomination">nominated hook</a> and the associated article(s) are evaluated, improved, and eventually either rejected as irreparably unusable or approved. This page is intended as a guide to aid editors in the reviewing process. Keep in mind that, in the end, <i>Did You Know approval is a subjective process. No amount of studying rules, almost-rules and precedents will guarantee approval, nor will violating any rule guarantee disapproval.</i> (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKAR" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKAR">D13</a>) </p>

        <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pick_a_nomination_to_review">Pick a nomination to review</span></h4> 
        <p>Nominations are listed at <a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Did_you_know" title="Template talk:Did you know">Template talk:Did you know</a>. On that page, the nominations are generally arranged in chronological order, with the oldest nominations at the top of the page. It is best if you start with one of the older unreviewed nominations. </p> 
        <h4><span id="Review_the_article.28s.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Review_the_article(s)">Review the article(s)</span></h4> 
        <p>To qualify for DYK, an article needs to meet several special criteria, in addition to being checked for normal encyclopedic issues. The fact that an article has been accepted as a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Good_article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Good article">Good article</a> should not be considered an assumption that the article meets these criteria. </p> 
        <div>
         <ul>
          <li>Check that within the past seven days, the article either: 
           <ol>
            <li>was created (ie a new article);</li> 
            <li>had its <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#prose" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">readable prose</a> expanded at least fivefold;</li> 
            <li>was listed as a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:GA">good article</a>.</li>
           </ol> <p>The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/DYKcheck" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/DYKcheck">DYKcheck tool</a> is helpful in evaluating this, or if you want to figure it out yourself, detailed instructions are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Fivefold_day_explanation" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Fivefold day explanation">here</a>. Older articles or expansions <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Not_exactly" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Not exactly">can be allowed</a>. </p> 
           <ul>
            <li>If a new article incorporates text copied from another Wikipedia article, then it must be expanded fivefold as if the copied text had been a pre-existing article.</li> 
            <li>Fivefold expansion means at least five times as much prose as the previously existing article, no matter how bad it was (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyvio" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Copyvio">copyvios</a> are <i>the only</i> exception), no matter whether any of it was kept, and no matter if it was <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AFD">up for deletion</a>. This may be a bad surprise to nominators, but we don't have enough time and volunteers to reach consensus on the quality of each previous article.</li>
           </ul> <span></span></li>
          <li>Check that the article hasn't been featured on the Main Page's <i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ITN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ITN">In the news</a></i> section and hasn't previously appeared as a "qualifying article" in an earlier DYK. (Articles that have been only <i>linked</i> from ITN or DYK, without being the qualifying article, linked and bolded, are eligible.)</li>
          <li>Check that the article is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Article_length" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Article length">long enough</a>. Articles must contain at least 1,500 characters of readable prose. 
           <ul>
            <li>In addition to at least 1,500 characters of readable prose, the article must not be a stub. This requires a judgement call, since there is no mechanical stub definition (see the <a href="/wiki/User:Grutness/Croughton-London_rule_of_stubs" title="User:Grutness/Croughton-London rule of stubs">Croughton-London rule</a>). If an article is, in fact, a stub, you should temporarily reject the nomination; if the article is not a stub, ensure that it is correctly marked as a non-stub, by removing any stub template(s) in the article, and changing any talk-page assessments to start-class or higher.</li>
           </ul> <span></span></li>
          <li>Check that the article contains <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Citation" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Citation">appropriate citations</a>. 
           <ul>
            <li>The hook fact(s) must be stated in the article, and must be immediately followed by an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Inline_citation" title="Wikipedia:Inline citation">inline citation</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RS">reliable source</a>. This rule applies even when a citation would not be required for the purposes of the article.</li> 
            <li>The article in general should use inline cited sources. A rule of thumb for DYK is a minimum of one citation per paragraph, possibly excluding the introduction, plot summaries, and paragraphs which summarize information that's cited elsewhere.</li> 
            <li>Sources must be properly labelled in a References (or similar section), not as "External links".</li> 
            <li>References may not have bare URLs such as <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://example.com">[1]</a> or <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://example.com">http://example.com</a></li> 
            <li>Any direct quotation must be marked as such (generally using quotation marks for short quotes and blockquote for longer quotes) and cited to a reliable source.</li>
           </ul> <span></span></li>
          <li>Do not assume that any criteria are met based on the fact that the article is a good article.</li>
          <li>If the article is entirely or substantially sourced to offline, foreign-language or paywalled sources, verify the basic facts, or at the very least, the existence of the article subject.</li>
          <li>Check the article to make sure there are no dispute templates. Any such issues need to be resolved before the article is used for DYK. Also, check the recent edit history to make sure that there wasn't a dispute template that was removed without fixing the problem. A list can be found at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DISPUTETAG" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DISPUTETAG">WP:DISPUTETAG</a>. An orphan tag is not a dispute tag, and not a validation for rejecting or holding up a nomination.</li>
          <li>If the article includes information about living individuals, make sure it does not violate Wikipedia's policy on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLP">biographies of living people</a>. This applies even if the article subject is not a living person.</li>
          <li>Check that the article does not contain plagiarism or close paraphrasing. The <a class="external text" href="https://tools.wmflabs.org/copyvios/">Earwig tool</a> can be helpful for this.</li>
          <li>Consider whether the article deals with the subject in a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPOV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPOV">neutral</a> manner.</li>
          <li>DYK nominations for articles at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AfD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AfD">WP:AfD</a> should be held pending the outcome of the deletion discussion. If the article is retained, the DYK nomination can proceed, and of course, if deleted, the DYK nomination must be rejected.</li>
         </ul>
        </div> 
        <dl>
         <dt>
          Special occasion hold requests
         </dt> 
         <dd></dd>
        </dl> 
        <ul>
         <li>
          <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
           Articles intended to be held for special occasion dates should be nominated within seven days of creation, start of expansion, or promotion to Good Article status. The nomination should be made between at least one week prior to the occasion date, to allow time for reviews and promotions through the prep and queue sets, but not more than six weeks in advance. The proposed occasion must be deemed sufficiently special by reviewers. The timeline limitations, including the six week maximum, may be waived by consensus, if a request is made at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:DYK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia talk:DYK">WT:DYK</a>, but requests are not always successful. Discussion clarifying the hold criteria can be found here: <a class="external autonumber" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Did_you_know/Archive_161#Revision_3">[2]</a>; discussion setting the six week limit can be found here: <a class="external autonumber" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Did_you_know/Archive_65#Rhacophorus_vampyrus">[3]</a>.
          </div></li> 
         <li>
          <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
           April Fools' Day hooks are exempted from the timeline limit; see <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:April_Fool%27s_Main_Page/Did_You_Know" title="Wikipedia:April Fool's Main Page/Did You Know">Wikipedia:April Fool's Main Page/Did You Know</a></b>.

        <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Review_the_hook">Review the hook</span></h4> 
        <p>Assuming that you have a qualifying article, it's now time to review all the hooks, including any ALT hooks that have been suggested. </p> 
        <ul>
         <li>Check that the hook is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Hook_format" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Hook format">properly formatted</a>. If there are formatting problems, you can probably <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SOFIXIT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SOFIXIT">fix them yourself</a>.</li> 
         <li>Check that the hook is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Hook_length" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Hook length">short enough</a>. If it's just a bit too long and you can shorten it with a minor change, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SOFIXIT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SOFIXIT">do it yourself</a>. If shortening the hook would require a more significant change, note the problem. You can also suggest an alternate (ALT) hook that is shorter. Just remember that you shouldn't then approve your own ALT hook.</li> 
         <li>Consider whether there might be <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPOV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPOV">neutrality</a> problems. If there is a problem, consider suggesting a more neutral ALT hook.</li> 
         <li>Consider very carefully whether the hook puts undue emphasis on a negative aspect of a living individual. Err on the side of caution, and when in doubt, suggest an ALT hook.</li> 
         <li>Consider whether the hook is "hooky". Try to avoid hooks that take the form of "... that X is Y?" Interesting hooks will often include one or more of the following: 
          <ul>
           <li>An unlikely juxtaposition. (Example: ... that <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> <a href="/wiki/Safety_(American_football_position)" class="mw-redirect" title="Safety (American football position)">safety</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Jordan_Kovacs" title="Jordan Kovacs">Jordan Kovacs</a></b> went from being a <a href="/wiki/Walk-on" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Walk-on">walk-on</a> to being the second leading <a href="/wiki/Tackle_(football_move)" title="Tackle (football move)">tackler</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Big_Ten_Conference" title="Big Ten Conference">Big Ten Conference</a>?)</li> 
           <li>An unusual nickname. (Example: ... <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_blues" title="Country blues">country blues</a> <a href="/wiki/Singer" class="mw-redirect" title="Singer">singer</a> <b><a href="/wiki/Lottie_Kimbrough" title="Lottie Kimbrough">Lottie Kimbrough</a></b> was nicknamed "the Kansas City Butterball"?)</li> 
           <li>A superlative or record. (Example: ... that with a leg-span of 30&nbsp;centimetres (12&nbsp;inches), the <b><a href="/wiki/Giant_huntsman_spider" title="Giant huntsman spider">giant huntsman</a></b> is one of the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Spider" title="Spider">spiders</a>?)</li> 
           <li>A tease, giving only part of the relevant information. (Example: ... that the discovery of geometrical body <b><a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6mb%C3%B6c" title="Gömböc">Gömböc</a></b> in 2006 helped understanding the body shape of <a href="/wiki/Turtle" title="Turtle">turtles</a>?)</li>
          </ul></li> 
         <li>Review <i>all</i> proposed hooks for the article(s). If you are unable to approve all hooks, be clear about which is/are acceptable and which is/are not. Consider striking through (<s>like this</s>) any rejected hooks to make it clear that they should not be used.</li>
        </ul> 
        <h5><span class="mw-headline" id="If_there_is_an_image">If there is an image</span></h5> 
        <p>If there is an image to go with the hook, you will need to do a little extra checking on it. </p> 
        <ul>
         <li>Make sure the image is free of any copyright restrictions. Fair-use images are not permitted for DYK.</li> 
         <li>Check that the image appears in the article.</li> 
         <li>Ensure the image has an alt text useful to those with visual impairments, if necessary, per <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ALTTEXT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ALTTEXT">WP:ALTTEXT</a>.</li> 
         <li>Consider the relevance of the image to the article and to the hook.</li> 
         <li>Consider the quality of the image, and its clarity at 100 by 100 pixels, the size at which DYK images appear on the Main Page.</li>
        </ul> 
        <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Finishing_the_review">Finishing the review</span></h4> 
        <p>After evaluating both the hook and the nominated article, check the nominator has reviewed another DYK nomination under the <a href="/wiki/Quid_pro_quo" title="Quid pro quo">quid pro quo</a> (QPQ) rules (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKSG#H4" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKSG">WP:DYKSG#H4</a>), and make sure that review adequately covers all of the DYK criteria just as yours will do. A QPQ review is not a requirement if its nominator has fewer than five DYK credits (whether or not self-nominated). If this is a multi-article nomination, then H4 rules apply. If in doubt about the number of previous nominations, look for edits giving DYK credit on the nominator's talk page (possibly using <a class="external text" href="http://tools.wmflabs.org/usersearch/">DYKUpdateBot</a>). Note that not all nominations are credited in this way and the bot also credits DYK creations and expansions, not just nominations. If in doubt, ask as part of your review process. </p>
        <p>Type your review in the section for that nomination. You should begin your review with one of the five DYK review icons. This allows the nominator and other editors to more quickly understand your review decision, including the severity of any problems. It is also used by the bot to keep the tally of how many hooks have been passed. After posting the icon, indicate all aspects of the article that you have reviewed; your comment should look something like the following: </p> 
        <blockquote>
         <p><code>Article length and age are fine, no copyvio or plagiarism concerns, reliable sources are used. But the hook needs to be shortened.</code></p>
        </blockquote> 
        <p>Be sure to give a thorough explanation of any problems or concerns you have, since several other editors may comment on the nomination before you return. </p>

        <p>If the outcome of your review is anything other than an approval (<a href="/wiki/File:Symbol_confirmed.svg" class="image"><img alt="Symbol confirmed.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Symbol_confirmed.svg/16px-Symbol_confirmed.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Symbol_confirmed.svg/24px-Symbol_confirmed.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Symbol_confirmed.svg/32px-Symbol_confirmed.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="185"></a> or <a href="/wiki/File:Symbol_voting_keep.svg" class="image"><img alt="Symbol voting keep.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Symbol_voting_keep.svg/16px-Symbol_voting_keep.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Symbol_voting_keep.svg/24px-Symbol_voting_keep.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Symbol_voting_keep.svg/32px-Symbol_voting_keep.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="185"></a>), please consider notifying the article nominator(s); you can do this with a personal message on their talk page or by placing {{<a href="/wiki/Template:DYKproblem" title="Template:DYKproblem">subst:DYKproblem</a>|<i>Article</i>|header=yes|sig=yes}} there, replacing <i>Article</i> with the title of the nominated article. This will automatically create a new talk page section and will automatically append your signature, so there is no need to do either of those. </p>
        <p>The icon <a href="/wiki/File:Symbol_redirect_vote_4.svg" class="image"><img alt="Symbol redirect vote 4.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Symbol_redirect_vote_4.svg/16px-Symbol_redirect_vote_4.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Symbol_redirect_vote_4.svg/24px-Symbol_redirect_vote_4.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Symbol_redirect_vote_4.svg/32px-Symbol_redirect_vote_4.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="185"></a> — coded as {{<a href="/wiki/Template:DYK%3Fagain" title="Template:DYK?again">subst:DYK?again</a>}} — may be used by creators and nominators to indicate that a nomination that previously had a problem is ready to be reviewed again after changes were made to resolve the issues identified. </p>
        <p>An article cannot be officially promoted until a reviewer has given his/ her approval (<a href="/wiki/File:Symbol_confirmed.svg" class="image"><img alt="Symbol confirmed.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Symbol_confirmed.svg/16px-Symbol_confirmed.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Symbol_confirmed.svg/24px-Symbol_confirmed.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Symbol_confirmed.svg/32px-Symbol_confirmed.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="185"></a> or <a href="/wiki/File:Symbol_voting_keep.svg" class="image"><img alt="Symbol voting keep.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Symbol_voting_keep.svg/16px-Symbol_voting_keep.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Symbol_voting_keep.svg/24px-Symbol_voting_keep.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Symbol_voting_keep.svg/32px-Symbol_voting_keep.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="185"></a>) to at least <i>one</i> of the article's hooks. Nominators are encouraged to work with reviewers to come up with hooks that meet the standards of the DYK process, and new alternate hooks can be proposed by anyone (nominator, reviewer, other third party) in an effort to produce at least one viable hook. Once a reviewer has conducted a thorough review of the nomination and given his/ her approval by placing the requisite symbol on the discussion page along with a statement indicating which hooks are ready, and if no other reviewer subsequently disagrees with this assessment, an uninvolved editor will soon review the discussion and likely close it and promote the article. </p>
        <p>If the article does not qualify for DYK (<a href="/wiki/File:Symbol_delete_vote.svg" class="image"><img alt="Symbol delete vote.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Symbol_delete_vote.svg/16px-Symbol_delete_vote.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Symbol_delete_vote.svg/24px-Symbol_delete_vote.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Symbol_delete_vote.svg/32px-Symbol_delete_vote.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="185"></a>) for some technical reason or if the participants cannot agree on at least one viable hook, the discussion will eventually be closed by an uninvolved editor and the article will not be promoted. </p> 
        <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Resources">Resources</span></h4> 
        <ul>
         <li><a class="mw-selflink selflink">Wikipedia:Did you know/Onepage</a> lists current DYK rules all in one place. It comprises many subpages <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Transclusion" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Transclusion">transcluded</a> onto a single page, so if you need to refer another editor to a specific item, it may be helpful to link to the specific subpage rather than the full listing.</li> 
         <li><a href="/wiki/Template:DYKrule" title="Template:DYKrule">Template:DYKrule</a> is a handy template for linking directly to a specific rule.</li> 
         <li><a href="/wiki/User:Shubinator/DYKcheck" title="User:Shubinator/DYKcheck">DYKcheck</a> is a JavaScript tool created and maintained by <a href="/wiki/User:Shubinator" title="User:Shubinator">Shubinator</a> that many editors find helpful in checking an article for DYK eligibility.</li> 
         <li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://toolserver.org/~dcoetzee/duplicationdetector/">Duplication Detector</a> is a tool to help check for plagiarism/copyright infringements.</li> 
         <li>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:DYK_checklist" title="Template:DYK checklist">DYK checklist</a>}} is an easy-to-use checklist that helps with reviewing DYK nominations.</li>
        </ul>

        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation_areas">Preparation areas</span></h3> 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
        <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
         <span id="WP:DYKNN"></span>
        </div>
        <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
         <div class="module-shortcutlist">
          <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
         </div>
         <ul>
          <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:DYKNN&amp;redirect=no">WP:DYKNN</a></span></li>
         </ul>
        </div> 
        <p>The instructions below are guidelines about content to keep in mind when preparing the prep areas. For technical instructions about how to move a hook to one of the prep areas, see <a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Did_you_know/Approved#How_to_promote_an_accepted_hook" title="Template talk:Did you know/Approved">Template talk:Did you know/Approved#How to promote an accepted hook</a>. </p> 
        <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Rules_of_thumb_for_preparing_updates">Rules of thumb for preparing updates</span></h4> 
        <dl>
         <dt>
          N1
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          Users are encouraged to help out by preparing <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#update" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">updates</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Queue#Prep_areas" title="Template:Did you know/Queue">seven prep areas</a>. You don't have to be an administrator. Note that promoting your own <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">articles</a> is generally discouraged, and promoting your own articles before they have been independently <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#verify" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">verified</a> is disallowed. When possible, it is also best to avoid promoting the same article that you reviewed, nominated, or created. (It is almost invariably possible to avoid this conflict of interest, so eschew such promotions. Ask for assistance at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:DYK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia talk:DYK">WT:DYK</a> if you've run into a seemingly intractable situation.)
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N2
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          Choose <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#approval" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">approved</a> <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hooks</a> (<a href="/wiki/File:Symbol_confirmed.svg" class="image"><img alt="Symbol confirmed.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Symbol_confirmed.svg/18px-Symbol_confirmed.svg.png" decoding="async" width="18" height="19" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Symbol_confirmed.svg/27px-Symbol_confirmed.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Symbol_confirmed.svg/36px-Symbol_confirmed.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="185"></a> or <a href="/wiki/File:Pictogram_voting_keep.svg" class="image"><img alt="Pictogram voting keep.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Pictogram_voting_keep.svg/18px-Pictogram_voting_keep.svg.png" decoding="async" width="18" height="18" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Pictogram_voting_keep.svg/27px-Pictogram_voting_keep.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Pictogram_voting_keep.svg/36px-Pictogram_voting_keep.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="250" data-file-height="250"></a>). These will be on the <a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Did_you_know/Approved" title="Template talk:Did you know/Approved">approved nominations page</a>, including the Special occasions section on the bottom of that page.
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N3
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          The accepted length of an update is a fixed number that changes on occasion, usually six, seven or eight hooks (currently eight). This is not an absolute rule but it is the currently accepted standard length for an update, depending on page balance, so the items selected fit with whatever else is on the Main Page at that time. Check by using the links on the prep page you're working on. For example, for <a href="/wiki/T:DYK/P1" class="mw-redirect" title="T:DYK/P1">Prep 1</a>: "See how this template appears on both <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Main_Page_alternative_(Next_DYK)&amp;action=purge">today's Main Page</a> and <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Main_Page_alternative_(Tomorrow_and_Next_DYK)&amp;action=purge">tomorrow's Main Page</a>." to see if the DYK template balances the rest of the main page layout.
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N4
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          Make sure to choose a varied selection – don't choose half a dozen people hooks, for example, or a bunch of hooks about one particular country or topic. Variety is the spice of life. (However, see the following clause for an important qualification).
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N5
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          Because of the preponderance of submissions on US topics and biography hooks, it is usually appropriate to have roughly 50% of hooks in a given update on both US and biography topics, but no more than half. That is to say, in an eight-hook update you should have roughly four hooks per update on US topics, and four on biography. These are not mutually exclusive, for example if you have two US bio hooks that would count as both two US hooks and two bio hooks. Note that "roughly 50%" means just that – this is not an absolute; you can have less of either if there are not many currently available such hooks to choose from on the Suggestions page. Note however that as a general rule you should never have <i>more</i> than 50% of hooks on US, biography or any other topic, except when doing so is unavoidable.
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N6
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          Also, mix your hooks up. Try to avoid having two hooks of the same general type next to one another in the update (for example, two US hooks or two bio hooks together). Putting several US hooks next to one another in an update makes the update look US-centric; the impact is greatly reduced if you interleave the US hooks with hooks about different countries. In the same spirit, try to avoid putting two bio hooks together, or two hooks on any other subject.
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N7
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          Try to avoid putting inappropriate hooks next to one another. For example, don't put a sad hook next to a funny one; it looks incongruous and jerks the reader uncomfortably from one emotion to another.
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N8
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          Hooks on the <a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Did_you_know/Approved" title="Template talk:Did you know/Approved">approved nominations page</a> that include images often get verified first. Users sometimes then just go and grab a bunch of the nearest verified hooks for the preparation areas, which can often include several of these verified picture hooks. Not every submitted picture can be featured in the picture slot of course, but since only one picture can be featured per update, try to leave the <i>good</i> picture hooks behind for another update if you possibly can.
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N9
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          Consider picking at least one funny or quirky hook if there is one available and putting it in the last (bottom) slot of the update. Just as serious news programs end on an upbeat note to bring viewers back next time, ending on an upbeat or quirky note rounds an update off nicely and encourages readers to come back next time for more.
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N10
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          Don't be afraid to ruthlessly trim hooks of extraneous information and clauses. A lot of people who submit hooks tend to overestimate the amount of information that is required, but the end result is a hook that has too much information and is difficult to process. We don't want our readers to work hard, we want to make reading the DYK section as accessible and enjoyable an experience as possible! In general, the shorter and punchier the hook, the more impact it has. As it says on the Suggestions page, the 200-character limit is an outside limit not a recommended length—the ideal length is probably no more than about 150–160 chars. Note however that some hooks cannot be reduced in length without losing essential information, so don't assume that <i>every</i> hook that is 200 characters long requires trimming.
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N11
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          Please disambiguate linked words in the suggestion. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disambiguation#Links_to_disambiguation_pages" title="Wikipedia:Disambiguation">Here is the Wikipedia guideline.</a>
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N12
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          Make sure to include the article name, date, nominator, and creator under the "Credits" section to allow others to return it if a dispute arises.
         </dd> 
         <dt>
          N13
         </dt> 
         <dd>
          It is the promoter's responsibility to make sure all review issues have been resolved, that the hook is verified by sourcing within the article. The promoter acts as a secondary verification that the nomination was reviewed properly. The article should not have any problem templates on it at the time of promotion.
         </dd>
        </dl>

        <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lore">Lore</span></h2> 
        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="How_a_DYK_suggestion_makes_its_way_to_the_Main_Page">How a DYK suggestion makes its way to the Main Page</span></h3> 
        <p>A DYK suggestion goes through five steps from <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#nominate" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">nomination</a> through appearing in the Did you know section on the <a href="/wiki/Main_Page" title="Main Page">Main Page</a> to removal from the Main Page. </p> 
        <ol>
         <li>First, an editor <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Submission" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Submission">nominates an article</a> by creating the appropriate subpage and transcluding it on <a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Did_you_know" title="Template talk:Did you know">Template talk:Did you know</a>. Discussions about individual suggestions also appear on the subpage, such as suggested improvements of the proposed DYK text (the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#hook" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">hook</a>), or comments about the eligibility of the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#article" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">article</a> under the requirements (see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Article" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Article">WP:Did you know/Article</a>).</li> 
         <li>If the suggested DYK meets the requirements, any uninvolved editor may add the suggestion to a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKTN#preparation_areas" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKTN">DYK preparation page</a> and close the nomination subpage. In practice, to ensure that all suggestions are given fair consideration, the oldest suggestions listed on the suggestions page are selected first, to ensure that they don't go stale before they are chosen. DYK entries listed on a preparation area are not final, and may be edited or rejected by any other editor. If they are rejected, the nomination subpage discussion is reopened.</li> 
         <li>When there are about eight hooks (the prescribed number may change every few weeks) at a preparation area, an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ADMIN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ADMIN">administrator</a> moves the page on to one of the protected <a href="/wiki/T:DYK/Q" class="mw-redirect" title="T:DYK/Q">queues</a>. The administrator moving the suggestions to the queue may edit or reject any DYK entries at their <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators#Administrator_conduct" title="Wikipedia:Administrators">discretion</a>.</li> 
         <li>Approximately every 12 hours (other frequencies may be specified), <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKADMINBOT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKADMINBOT">WP:DYKADMINBOT</a> moves a page from one of the queue files onto the Main Page. So DYK entries appear on the Main Page for about twelve hours, with about two updates per day. DYK entries listed on the Main Page are not final. Non-admins may report errors to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ERRORS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ERRORS">Main Page errors page</a> so they can be dealt with. Admins can amend, edit, replace, and remove entries in the DYK template while it appears on the Main Page. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators#Reinstating_a_reverted_action_.28.22Wheel_warring.22.29" title="Wikipedia:Administrators">Wheel warring</a> prohibits one admin from undoing one another's administrative actions. In particular, wheel warring prohibits one admin from reinserting a DYK hook onto the Main Page that was removed by another admin. In one example, aggressive wheel warring over a Main Page DYK hook led to <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Administrators%27_noticeboard/Incidents&amp;diff=prev&amp;oldid=228022930">desysopping by Jimbo Wales</a>.</li> 
         <li>The bot <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DYKA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DYKA">archives</a> old DYK entries that are removed from the Main Page, and gives credits (notices on nominators' talk pages).</li>
        </ol> 
        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Errors">Errors</span></h3> 
        <p>Notification of DYK errors regarding what is currently on the main page may be posted at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ERRORS#Errors_in_the_current_Did_you_know..." class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ERRORS">the <i>Errors in the current </i>Did you know...<i> section of WP:ERRORS</i></a>. DYK errors on the main page may be addressed by an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ADMIN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ADMIN">admin</a> through changes to the <a href="/wiki/T:DYK" class="mw-redirect" title="T:DYK">DYK template.</a> </p> 
        <dl>
         <dt>
          Admins
         </dt> 
         <dd></dd>
        </dl> 
        <ul>
         <li>If a factual error is reported when the hooks are on the front page, try to replace the hook with another fact from the article, rather than just removing it.</li> 
         <li>In the case it has to be removed, try to replace it with another hook from the suggestions page.</li> 
         <li>If it is the first hook and hence has an associated picture, you <i>must</i> replace it with another hook with a picture.</li>
        </ul>

        <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable relarticle mainarticle selfreference noprint">
         This section is <a href="/wiki/Help:Transclusion" title="Help:Transclusion">transcluded</a> from <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Supplementary_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Supplementary guidelines">Wikipedia:Did you know/Supplementary guidelines</a>. <span class="plainlinks" style="font-style: normal"><span style="font-size:85%;">(<a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Supplementary_guidelines&amp;action=edit">edit</a> | <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Supplementary_guidelines&amp;action=history">history</a>)</span></span>

        <p>These rules are listed elsewhere, but they are often overlooked at Did You Know: </p> 
        <ul>
         <li>There must be a space after the <a href="/wiki/Ellipsis" title="Ellipsis">ellipsis</a>.</li> 
         <li>The link to your article should be in <b>bold</b> (e.g., '''[[Manx cat]]''').</li> 
         <li>The hook should end with a question mark.</li> 
         <li>For a hook with an accompanying picture, the string <i>(pictured)</i> is all in italics, <i>including</i> the parentheses.</li> 
         <li>For titles or words with dashes see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DASH" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DASH">WP:DASH</a>.</li> 
         <li>For hooks containing numbers see <a href="/wiki/MOS:NUM#Numbers_as_figures_or_words" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:NUM">MOS:NUM#Numbers as figures or words</a>.</li> 
         <li>Make sure your article title conforms with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style_(titles)" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style (titles)">Wikipedia:Manual of Style (titles)</a></li> 
         <li>A 'new' article is no more than seven days old. This does not include articles split from older articles, although an article sufficiently expanded from a section of an older article can be a fivefold expansion. The word "fork" is sometimes used to mean <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Splitting" title="Wikipedia:Splitting">Wikipedia:Splitting</a>.</li> 
         <li>Fix redirects in hooks&nbsp;– see <a href="/wiki/MOS:DYKPIPE" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:DYKPIPE">MOS:DYKPIPE</a>.</li>

        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Instruction_creep">Instruction creep</span></h3> 
        <p>Did You Know can be criticized for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Instruction_creep" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Instruction creep">instruction creep</a>. But it is important to distinguish instruction creep from <i>documentation</i> of that instruction creep. Instruction creep is bad because it takes longer and longer to understand all those instructions before you can actually do anything. But documentation of instruction creep eases the process of trying to learn all the unwritten rules before you can do anything. A ten-pound law book is bad, but a thousand-pound library of precedents one needs in order to guess what the unwritten laws are, would be worse. Did You Know nominations are approved by different reviewers, and there isn't an easy way to get them all to behave predictably. </p>
        <p>For instance, when determining if a Did You Know article is long enough or expanded enough, we don't count the whole article. "Did You Know defines 'prose' to exclude infoboxes, categories, references, lists, tables, block quotes, headers, images and captions, the "See also" section if any, Table of Contents, edit buttons, "Citation needed" and similar superscripted text, and reference link numbers like [6]." Wouldn't it be easier to just count the whole article, and use the length Wikipedia provides on each page's history page? That would allow shorter articles, so let's say the 1,500 character limit is raised to about 2,500, or to whatever number would eliminate lowering the bar as a reason to count characters the hard way. That simplification hasn't been done, because it has been argued that a short article can be padded to reach the minimum, just by adding categories and such. But prose can also be padded (<a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Template_talk:Did_you_know&amp;diff=269691211&amp;oldid=269691093">example</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Poetic_justice" title="Poetic justice">poetic justice</a> for anyone who disagrees would be to fill his user talk page with 100 different ways to tediously repeat that point. So it's unclear that complicated character counting rules are worth all the extra effort of computing and debating them. </p>
        <p>But imagine how much worse that computing and debating effort was before the process was documented. If this simplification is desirable, it will happen when everyone can agree to follow a new, simpler rule, not by ignoring the existing rules so that no one but regulars can know what they are, or requiring weeks of studying <a href="/wiki/T:TDYK" class="mw-redirect" title="T:TDYK">the suggestions page</a> to get any idea if a submission would be accepted or not. </p>
        <p>These rules aren't intended to cover everything, and we even have a rule than says so: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/You_knew_this" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/You knew this">R6</a>. </p>
        <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTBUREAUCRACY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTBUREAUCRACY">WP:NOTBUREAUCRACY</a> says "Written rules do not themselves set accepted practice, but rather document already existing community consensus ... " </p>
        <p>In conclusion, the only thing worse than a million written rules, is a million unwritten rules. </p> 
        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Daily_headings_on_the_suggestions_page">Daily headings on the suggestions page</span></h3> 
        <p><a href="/wiki/T:TDYK" class="mw-redirect" title="T:TDYK">T:TDYK</a> has daily headings like "Articles created/expanded on February 18", with nominations as subheadings. We don't have an automated system to maintain the daily headings yet. So as <a href="/wiki/Universal_Time" title="Universal Time">Universal Time</a> midnight approaches, someone should add a heading for the next day, near the top. <a href="/wiki/T:TDYK#Older_nominations" class="mw-redirect" title="T:TDYK">T:TDYK#Older nominations</a> should be moved down a day, so it will always be seven days old. And if it's below the "Older nominations" header, the last daily heading near the bottom of the page can be removed when it contains no more nominations, except for nominations with unsatisfied objections. </p>

        <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_Did_You_Know">History of Did You Know</span></h3> 
        <p>DYK made its first Main Page appearance on <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Template:Did_you_know&amp;oldid=2500457">February 22, 2004</a>. The article, <a href="/wiki/Pencil_sharpener" title="Pencil sharpener">pencil sharpener</a>, was developed by <a href="/wiki/User:Raul654" title="User:Raul654">Raul654</a> who had been an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Editors" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Editors">editor</a> with Wikipedia for about six months at that time and now is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Bureaucrats" title="Wikipedia:Bureaucrats">bureaucrat</a>. An April 2004 <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://web.archive.org/web/20040403171050/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Main_Page">screen shot</a> shows DYK located in the space now occupied by In the News. Credit recognition for article creators started on <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Template:UpdatedDYK&amp;diff=prev&amp;oldid=8361278">November 24, 2004</a>, DYK began placing DYK notifications on article talk pages on <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Template:Dyktalk&amp;diff=prev&amp;oldid=35006381">January 13, 2006</a>, and nominators started receiving credit on <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Template:UpdatedDYKNom&amp;diff=prev&amp;oldid=52932552">May 13, 2006.</a> </p>

       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="article">article</span></h4> 
       <p>At <a href="#Did_You_Know">Did You Know</a>, "article" means the article being introduced on the <a href="/wiki/Main_Page" title="Main Page">Main Page</a> by a <a href="#hook">hook</a>. For instance, if the hook is "... that <b><a href="/wiki/Jon_Olav_Alstad" title="Jon Olav Alstad">Jon Olav Alstad</a></b> was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Norwegian Parliament">Norwegian Parliament</a> at the age of 25?", then it introduces the article <a href="/wiki/Jon_Olav_Alstad" title="Jon Olav Alstad">Jon Olav Alstad</a> (not <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Norwegian Parliament">Norwegian Parliament</a>, which isn't in bold print). Rules for the article are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Article" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Article">here</a>. </p>

       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="characters">characters</span></h4> 
       <p>A <a href="/wiki/Character_(computing)" title="Character (computing)">character</a> is a letter, or a space between letters, or a punctuation mark, or anything else made with one keystroke (or one shifted keystroke). The next sentence is an example of how to count characters. This sentence has 32 characters. Count 4 for the four letters in "This", 1 for the space between "This" and "sentence", 8 for the 8 letters in "sentence", 1 for the space after "sentence", 3 for "has", 1 for the next space, 2 for the "3" and the "2" in "32", 1 for the space, 10 for "characters", and 1 for the period. 4 + 1 + 8 + 1 + 3 + 1 + 2 + 1 + 10 + 1 = 32. </p>
       <p>There are occasional debates on whether Wikipedia should count <a href="#article">article</a> size using "characters" or "bytes". Better terminology for that debate would be whether to use <a href="#prose">prose</a> or the article's history page, which counts the whole article. For ordinary text, one character occupies one byte, and only one byte, so the character count and the byte count are the same. </p>

See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/DYKcheck" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/DYKcheck">WP:Did you know/DYKcheck</a> and <a href="/wiki/User:Shubinator/DYKcheck" title="User:Shubinator/DYKcheck">User:Shubinator/DYKcheck</a>. </p>

See <a href="/wiki/Template:DYKproblem" title="Template:DYKproblem">Template:DYKproblem</a>. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="DYKsug">DYKsug</span></h4>

       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="expansion">expansion</span></h4> 
       <p>at Did You Know means to expand an existing <a href="#article">article</a>, that is, to add text and make it longer, hopefully expanding it enough to make it qualify as a new article. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="fair_use">fair use</span></h4> 
       <p>See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Fair_use" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Fair use">Wikipedia:Fair use</a> </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="fivefold">fivefold</span></h4> 
       <p>means five times. For instance, if an article has 1000 bytes of <a href="#prose">prose</a> (not the whole article, see the "prose" entry), it must be expanded to 5000 bytes of prose (not the whole article) to be considered new, and thus eligible for Did You Know. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="good_article">good article</span></h4> 
       <p>A good article (GA) is one that has been reviewed via the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GAN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:GAN">good article nomination</a> process and been listed as a GA. New GAs are considered to be eligible for Did You Know; it is as if they were newly created on the day they became a good article, so they must be <a href="#nominate">nominated</a> as quickly as a newly created or newly expanded article. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="good_faith">good faith</span></h4> 
       <p>See <a href="#AGF">AGF</a>. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="hook_2">hook</span></h4> 
       <p>An example of a hook is "... that <b><a href="/wiki/Jon_Olav_Alstad" title="Jon Olav Alstad">Jon Olav Alstad</a></b> was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Norwegian Parliament">Norwegian Parliament</a> at the age of 25?" The Did You Know section on the <a href="/wiki/Main_Page" title="Main Page">Main Page</a> contains about eight hooks, usually starting with "... that" and ending with "?". They are called "hooks" because they are intended to hook the reader into reading the associated <a href="#article">article</a>. Rules for the hook are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/Hook" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/Hook">here</a>. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="IGF">IGF</span></h4>

       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="image">image</span></h4>

       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="negative">negative</span></h4> 
       <p>At Did You Know, "negative" means criticism in a hook, as in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Biography_of_Living_Persons" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Biography of Living Persons">Wikipedia:Biography of Living Persons</a> policy. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="NewDYKnom">NewDYKnom</span></h4> 
       <p><span class="anchor" id="NewDYKnomination"></span> is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Template" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Template">template</a> used to format a <a href="#nominate">nomination</a>. See {{<a href="/wiki/Template:NewDYKnom" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:NewDYKnom">NewDYKnom</a>}}. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="nominate">nominate</span></h4> 
       <p><span class="anchor" id="nom"></span> <span class="anchor" id="nomination"></span> To suggest or propose. At <a href="#Did_You_Know">Did You Know</a>, "nominate" means to suggest a <a href="#hook">hook</a> and its associated <a href="#article">article</a>. "Nomination" can mean the hook, the article, or both. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="offline">offline</span></h4> 
       <p>at Did You Know means a reference that you can't get online, that is, on the Internet, usually because the source is a book or similar library resource. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="preparation_areas_2">preparation areas</span></h4> 
       <p>are <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_1" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 1">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 1</a>, <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_2" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 2">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_3" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 3">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 3</a>, <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_4" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 4">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 4</a>, <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_5" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 5">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 5</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Preparation_area_6" title="Template:Did you know/Preparation area 6">Template:Did you know/Preparation area 6</a>. <a href="#approval">Approved</a> <a href="#hook">hooks</a> are moved from <a href="#T:TDYK">T:TDYK</a> to a preparation area and organized into a group of usually about eight. From there, an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrator" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Administrator">administrator</a> will eventually move each preparation area page into one of the <a href="#queue">queues</a>, after which <a href="#DYKADMINBOT">DYKADMINBOT</a> will move it to the <a href="/wiki/Main_Page" title="Main Page">Main Page</a>. See "update". </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="prose">prose</span></h4> 
       <p>At <a href="#Did_You_Know">Did You Know</a>, only <a href="#characters">characters</a> of "prose" are counted when measuring the length of an <a href="#article">article</a>, whether it's to meet the 1,500 character minimum, or to calculate whether an article has been expanded <a href="#fivefold">fivefold</a>. Characters of prose are counted by the <a href="/wiki/User:Shubinator/DYKcheck" title="User:Shubinator/DYKcheck">User:Shubinator/DYKcheck</a> <a href="/wiki/Scripting_language" title="Scripting language">script</a>, which excludes infoboxes, categories, references, lists, tables, block quotes, headers, images and captions, the "See also" section if any, Table of Contents, edit buttons, "citation needed" and similar superscripted text, and reference link numbers like [6]. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Prosesizebytes.js">Prosesizebytes.js</span></h4> 
       <p>is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Scripts" title="Wikipedia:Scripts">script</a> that measures the <a href="#characters">characters</a> in the <a href="#prose">prose</a> portion of the <a href="#article">article</a>. See <a href="/wiki/User_talk:Dr_pda/prosesizebytes.js" title="User talk:Dr pda/prosesizebytes.js">User talk:Dr pda/prosesizebytes.js</a>. <a href="#DYKcheck">DYKcheck</a> is now preferred to prosesizebytes. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Prosesize.js">Prosesize.js</span></h4> 
       <p>is an older form of <a href="#Prosesizebytes.js">#Prosesizebytes.js</a>. <a href="#DYKcheck">DYKcheck</a> is now preferred to prosesize.js and to prosesizebytes.js. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="queue">queue</span></h4> 
       <p>is what Americans call a "line", as in "line up and wait your turn". At <a href="#Did_You_Know">Did You Know</a>, it means <a href="/wiki/Template:Did_you_know/Queue" title="Template:Did you know/Queue">Template:Did you know/Queue</a>, where Did You Know pages wait for their twelve-hour turn to go onto the <a href="/wiki/Main_Page" title="Main Page">Main Page</a>. There is room for six <a href="#update">updates</a> in the queue. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="reliable">reliable</span></h4> 
       <p>means <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources">Wikipedia:Reliable sources</a>. The word is often used in a way that sounds as if we are loosely calling people liars, but all it really means is that a source is on the list of what we consider to be reliable enough. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="run-on">run-on</span></h4> 
       <p>is described at <a href="/wiki/Run-on_sentence" class="mw-redirect" title="Run-on sentence">run-on sentence</a>. It does NOT mean a rambling sentence that runs on and on! If you don't understand the grammatical distinction in the <a href="/wiki/Run-on_sentence" class="mw-redirect" title="Run-on sentence">run-on sentence</a> article, then please do not use the term "run-on". </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="self-nom">self-nom</span></h4> 
       <p>is a <a href="#nominate">nomination</a> of a <a href="#hook">hook</a> and an <a href="#article">article</a> you wrote yourself. This is permitted and encouraged. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="T:TDYK">T:TDYK</span></h4> 
       <p>is both the shortcut to <a href="/wiki/Template_talk:Did_you_know" title="Template talk:Did you know">Template talk:Did you know</a> (the DYK suggestions page) and a shorthand way of referring to it. </p>

       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="update">update</span></h4> 
       <p>A single set of approximately eight <a href="#hook">hooks</a> to be displayed on the Main Page for about twelve hours. Each update is individually prepared in a <a href="#preparation_areas">preparation area</a> and moved into the <a href="#queue">queues</a> by an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrator" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Administrator">administrator</a>, then onto the Main Page by <a href="#DYKADMINBOT">DYKADMINBOT</a>. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="user_subpage">user subpage</span></h4> 
       <p>See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_subpage" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:User subpage">Wikipedia:User subpage</a>. You may write your article on a user subpage, so you will have time to study the Did You Know process before your article is disqualified for being too old. The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Did_you_know/New_article" title="Wikipedia:Did you know/New article">seven days (and the exception)</a> don't start until you move the <a href="#article">article</a> into <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Main_namespace" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Main namespace">Wikipedia:main namespace</a>. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="verify">verify</span></h4> 
       <p>See <a href="#approval">approval</a>. </p> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="x">x</span></h4> 
       <p>as in 5x or 1.3x, is a way of expressing how nearly an <a href="#article">article</a> has reached <a href="#fivefold">fivefold</a> <a href="#expansion">expansion</a>. From 2000 bytes of <a href="#prose">prose</a> to 3000 bytes is a 1.5x expansion. It would have to be expanded to 10,000 bytes of <a href="#prose">prose</a> (not the whole article, just prose) to be 5x and qualify as new. </p>
