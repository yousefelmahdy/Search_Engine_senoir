      <p>The <b>project namespace</b> or <b>Wikipedia namespace</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Namespace" title="Wikipedia:Namespace">namespace</a> consisting of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ADMINP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ADMINP">administration pages</a> with information or discussion about <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a>. Pages in this namespace will always have the prefix <b>Wikipedia:</b>. They can also be reached by alias <b>WP:</b> or the standard (for any <a href="/wiki/MediaWiki" title="MediaWiki">MediaWiki</a> site) prefix <b>Project:</b>. Its namespace number is four (4). </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">Terminology</span></h2> 
      <ul>
       <li>Project pages or Wikipedia pages are pages in the Wikipedia and Wikipedia talk namespaces. WikiProject pages, which are included in the Wikipedia namespace, are usually referred to as WikiProject pages to distinguish them from project pages as a whole.</li> 
       <li>Meta – Some people refer to pages in the project namespace as <i>meta pages</i>. However, this can be confusing, because <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Meta" title="Wikipedia:Meta">Meta</a> is actually the name of the site dedicated to all <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Projects_of_Wikimedia" title="Wikipedia:Projects of Wikimedia">Wikimedia projects</a> at <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/" class="extiw" title="m:">meta.wikimedia.org</a>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Policies_and_guidelines#Not_part_of_the_encyclopedia" title="Wikipedia:Policies and guidelines">Wikipedia:Policies and guidelines §&nbsp;Not part of the encyclopedia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Be_bold#Wikipedia_namespace" title="Wikipedia:Be bold">Wikipedia:Be bold §&nbsp;Wikipedia namespace</a>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_pages#What_may_I_not_have_in_my_user_pages?" title="Wikipedia:User pages">Wikipedia:User pages §&nbsp;What may I not have in my user pages?</a>
      </div> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:PRJC"></span><span id="WP:PRJSD"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PRJC&amp;redirect=no">WP:PRJC</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PRJSD&amp;redirect=no">WP:PRJSD</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Pages within the "Project namespace" themselves are not part of the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_is_an_article%3F" title="Wikipedia:What is an article?">encyclopedia proper</a>. These <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ADMINP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ADMINP">administrative pages</a> are intended for use by editors or by automated tools for the organization and governance of the encyclopedia. Consequently, they do not generally need to conform to the same <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Core_content_policies" title="Wikipedia:Core content policies">content protocols</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:MOS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:MOS"> style conventions</a> as articles. Nevertheless, these pages, as with all pages, should be <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Accessibility" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Accessibility">accessible</a> and <i>must</i> comply with Wikipedia's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_policies#Conduct" title="Wikipedia:List of policies">conduct</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_policies#Legal" title="Wikipedia:List of policies">legal</a> policies. </p>
      <p>The project namespace is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOT#WEBHOST" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOT">not a free web host</a> and should not be used as a long-term archive to host pages that look like articles. Articles in the project namespace under construction may be moved to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Drafts" title="Wikipedia:Drafts">draft namespace</a> or <a href="/wiki/Help:Userspace_draft" title="Help:Userspace draft">Userspace draft</a> allowing time for their development and feedback before being moved to Wikipedia's mainspace. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:STALEDRAFT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:STALEDRAFT">Stalled or abandoned drafts</a> and pages in the project namespace that violate <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_Wikipedia_is_not#Community" title="Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not">policies applicable to non-content pages</a> may be subject to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:MFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:MFD">deletion</a>. Editors may not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyright_violations" title="Wikipedia:Copyright violations">violate copyrights</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:HARASS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:HARASS">harass</a> anywhere on Wikipedia. Under the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Criteria_for_speedy_deletion" title="Wikipedia:Criteria for speedy deletion">criteria for speedy deletion</a>, these types of pages are subject to expeditious deletion. See <a href="#Deletion_of_project_pages">below</a> for more information. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pages_within_project_namespace">Pages within project namespace</span></h2> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="PAGES"></span> </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">

      <p>The project namespace pages are organized according to their function within the overall project schema. This includes Wikipedia official policies and guidelines, process pages, discussion pages, optional essays, maintenance pages, informative pages, and historical pages. </p>
      <p>For lists of pages in the project namespace, see: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Directories_and_indexes" title="Wikipedia:Directories and indexes">Wikipedia:Directories and indexes</a> – a handy list of Wikipedia's directories and indexes.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Department_directory" title="Wikipedia:Department directory">Wikipedia:Department directory</a> – a list of the different administrative divisions of Wikipedia.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Editor%27s_index_to_Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia:Editor's index to Wikipedia">Wikipedia:Editor's index to Wikipedia</a> – an enormous list of the Wikipedia community, intended to help find anything not in the encyclopedia itself.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>A main gateway for the project namespace is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Community_portal" title="Wikipedia:Community portal">Community portal</a>. The Community Portal includes categorized links to the more commonly used pages related to the project. A link to the Community Portal page is available in the <a href="/wiki/MediaWiki:Sidebar" title="MediaWiki:Sidebar">sidebar</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Policies_and_guidelines_pages">Policies and guidelines pages<span class="anchor" id="Policies_and_guidelines"></span></span></h3>

      <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Policies_and_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Policies and guidelines">Policies and guidelines</a> pages describe Wikipedia's best practice and clarify principles that are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">widely accepted by the community</a> and have been through the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PROPOSAL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PROPOSAL">Wikipedia review process</a>. These pages are marked with the {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Policy" title="Template:Policy">policy</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Guideline" title="Template:Guideline">guideline</a>}} or {{<a href="/wiki/Template:MoS_guideline" title="Template:MoS guideline">MoS guideline</a>}} template. For summaries of key policies, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_policies" title="Wikipedia:List of policies">List of policies</a>. For summaries of key guidelines, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:List of guidelines">List of guidelines</a>. For summaries of guidelines about Wikipedia's house style, see the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Contents" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Contents">Manual of Style contents</a> page. For information on editing policies or guidelines see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Editing_policy#Edits_to_policies_and_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Editing policy">Edits to policies and guidelines</a>. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_policies" title="Category:Wikipedia policies">Category:Wikipedia policies</a> – contains important rules that are widely accepted and procedures for important processes such as deletion; there are relatively few of those.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_guidelines" title="Category:Wikipedia guidelines">Category:Wikipedia guidelines</a> – contains consensual rules-of-thumb that are not strict, but are considered by most editors to be useful most of the time.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_Manual_of_Style" title="Category:Wikipedia Manual of Style">Category:Wikipedia Manual of Style</a> – contains style guidelines widely accepted among editors.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Information_and_discussions">Information and discussions</span></h3> 
      <p>Many pages in the Wikipedia namespace have nothing to do with rules or implementation of those rules, and thus do not belong in the above categories. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:The_difference_between_policies,_guidelines_and_essays" title="Wikipedia:The difference between policies, guidelines and essays">Wikipedia:The difference between policies, guidelines and essays</a> for more information. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Discussion_and_noticeboard_pages">Discussion and noticeboard pages<span class="anchor" id="Discussion_and_noticeboard_pages"></span></span></h4> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">

      <p>Some pages are designed for discussions, such as the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Village_pump" title="Wikipedia:Village pump">Village pumps</a>. Others serve as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Noticeboards" title="Wikipedia:Noticeboards">noticeboards</a> to draw attention to discussions taking place elsewhere, such as the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Centralized_discussions" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Centralized discussions">centralized discussions</a> page and various <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RFC">requests for comments</a> pages. While many of these pages operate like talk pages, some of them have posting rules (at the top of the page) which may be administratively enforced, and they also have their own talk pages, usually about the management of the noticeboard or other process in question. For a listing of ongoing discussions and current requests, see the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Dashboard" title="Wikipedia:Dashboard">dashboard</a>. </p>

      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="How-to_and_information_pages">How-to and information pages<span class="anchor" id="Wikipedia_how-to_and_information_pages"></span></span></h4> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">

      <p>Informative and instructional pages are typically edited by the community; while <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Local_consensus" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Local consensus">not policies or guidelines themselves</a>, they are intended to supplement or clarify Wikipedia guidelines, policies, or other Wikipedia processes and practices that are communal norms. Where <a href="#Essay_pages">essay pages</a> offer advice or opinions through viewpoints, information pages should supplement or clarify technical or factual information about Wikipedia in an impartial way. In comparison to policies and guidelines, information pages, like essay pages, have a limited status, and can reflect varying levels of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CONSENSUS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CONSENSUS">consensus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CONACHIEVE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CONACHIEVE">vetting</a>. These pages are typically marked with the {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Information_page" title="Template:Information page">Information page</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Wikipedia_how-to" title="Template:Wikipedia how-to">Wikipedia how-to</a>}}, or {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Supplement" title="Template:Supplement">Supplement</a>}}<sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_a"><a class="external autonumber" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Project_namespace#endnote_a">[1]</a></sup> template. </p>
      <p>There is a large amount of overlap between the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Help_namespace" title="Wikipedia:Help namespace">Help namespace</a> (which provides mainly technical information) and the Wikipedia namespace (which provides mainly procedural information and interpretation). For this reason, <a href="/wiki/Help:Redirect" title="Help:Redirect">redirects</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Hatnote" title="Wikipedia:Hatnote">hatnotes</a> are often set up between these two namespaces. See <a href="/wiki/Help:About_help_pages" title="Help:About help pages">Help:About help pages</a> for more information. For a listing of how-to and information pages, see the <a href="/wiki/Help:Directory" title="Help:Directory">help directory</a>. </p>

      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Essay_pages">Essay pages</span></h4> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:ESSAYPAGES"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:ESSAYPAGES&amp;redirect=no">WP:ESSAYPAGES</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Essays about Wikipedia may be written by anyone; some represent widespread norms, others only represent minority viewpoints. Essays can be long monologues or short theses, serious or funny, informative or opinionated. Essays like information pages have no official status, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CONLEVEL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CONLEVEL">do not speak for the Wikipedia community</a> as they may be created and edited without overall community oversight. Essays are typically marked with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Template_messages/Wikipedia_namespace#Essays" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Template messages/Wikipedia namespace">one of the various</a> {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Essay" title="Template:Essay">essay</a>}} templates. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Essays" title="Wikipedia:Essays">Wikipedia:Essays</a> for more information. For a listing of essays, see the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Essay_directory" title="Wikipedia:Essay directory">essay directory</a>. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_essays" title="Category:Wikipedia essays">Category:Wikipedia essays</a></li>
      </ul> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="WikiProject_pages">WikiProject pages</span></h4> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:PROPAGES"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PROPAGES&amp;redirect=no">WP:PROPAGES</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Pages of a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">WikiProject</a> are the central place for editor collaboration and organization on a particular topic area. Many WikiProjects compose "<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ADVICEPAGE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ADVICEPAGE">advice essays</a>" about how to apply Wikipedia's policies and guidelines to their specific subject area. Pages with <code>Wikipedia:WikiProject</code> prefix form a WikiProject pseudo-namespace. These pages are typically marked with the {{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_status" title="Template:WikiProject status">WikiProject status</a>}} or {{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_advice" title="Template:WikiProject advice">WikiProject advice</a>}} template. For a listing of projects, see the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Directory" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Directory">directory of WikiProjects</a>. For additional resources, or if you have any questions, please visit the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council">WikiProject Council</a>. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_pages">Historical pages</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:HISPAGES"></span>
      </div>

        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:HISPAGES&amp;redirect=no">WP:HISPAGES</a></span></li>

      <p>A historical page or process is one which is no longer in use, or is no longer relevant or consensus has changed about its content. They are kept as a record of past Wikipedia processes to give context to historical discussions and to inform future discussions on similar topics. These pages are typically marked with the {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Historical" title="Template:Historical">historical</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Superseded" title="Template:Superseded">superseded</a>}} or {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Dormant" title="Template:Dormant">dormant</a>}} template. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:HISTORICAL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:HISTORICAL">HISTORICAL</a> for more details. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creating_new_project_pages">Creating new project pages</span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_writing_redundant_essays" title="Wikipedia:Avoid writing redundant essays">Wikipedia:Avoid writing redundant essays</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Content_forking/Internal#Policy_forks" title="Wikipedia:Content forking/Internal">Wikipedia:Content forking/Internal §&nbsp;Policy forks</a>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_pages#What_may_I_have_in_my_user_pages?" title="Wikipedia:User pages">Wikipedia:User pages §&nbsp;What may I have in my user pages?</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Drafts#Creating_and_editing_drafts" title="Wikipedia:Drafts">Wikipedia:Drafts §&nbsp;Creating and editing drafts</a>
      </div> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:PRJCRE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PRJCRE&amp;redirect=no">WP:PRJCRE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Essays and information pages may be established by writing them and adding {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Essay" title="Template:Essay">Essay</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Information_page" title="Template:Information page">Information page</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Wikipedia_how-to" title="Template:Wikipedia how-to">Wikipedia how-to</a>}}, or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Template_messages/Wikipedia_namespace" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Template messages/Wikipedia namespace">similar templates</a> to the page. Essays and information pages in the "Wikipedia namespace" should not be used to create an alternative rule set. Creation of new guideline and policy pages require discussion and a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Local_consensus" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Local consensus">high level of consensus</a> from the entire community before publication (see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PROPOSAL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PROPOSAL">WP:PROPOSAL</a> for procedural details). For general recommendations about the creation and improvement of existing guideline and policy pages, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Policy_writing_is_hard" title="Wikipedia:Policy writing is hard">Wikipedia:Policy writing is hard</a>. </p>
      <p>The project namespace contains many pages and a lot of information. Try to avoid creating new pages unnecessarily. Before creating a new page, you should consider looking through the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Editor%27s_index_to_Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia:Editor's index to Wikipedia">topical index</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Essay_directory" title="Wikipedia:Essay directory">essay directory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Help:Directory" title="Help:Directory">help directory</a> to see if the material belongs at a page that already exists. It is strongly recommended that you propose a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PJ" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PJ">WikiProject</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Proposals" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Proposals">proposal page</a>, if there are enough willing editors to participate in the project. Already existing WikiProjects can be found in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Directory" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Directory">directory of WikiProjects</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deletion_of_project_pages">Deletion of project pages<span class="anchor" id="Deletion_of_project_namespace_pages"></span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main page: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Miscellany_for_deletion" title="Wikipedia:Miscellany for deletion">Wikipedia:Miscellany for deletion</a>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_pages#Deletion_of_user_pages" title="Wikipedia:User pages">Wikipedia:User pages §&nbsp;Deletion of user pages</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Drafts#Deleting_a_draft" title="Wikipedia:Drafts">Wikipedia:Drafts §&nbsp;Deleting a draft</a>
      </div> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:PRJDEL"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PRJDEL&amp;redirect=no">WP:PRJDEL</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>The usual deletion process is to post a request at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:MFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:MFD">miscellany for deletion</a> to have project pages <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Deletion_policy" title="Wikipedia:Deletion policy">deleted</a>. Make sure that a notice of the request is posted to the talk page of the affected project. If the project is under a parent WikiProject, a notice should be posted there as well. However, if editing can improve the page, this should be done rather than deleting the page. Disputes over page content are usually not dealt with by deleting the page, except in severe cases. The content issues should be discussed at the relevant talk page, and other methods of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Resolving_disputes" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Resolving disputes">dispute resolution</a> should be used first, such as listing on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requests_for_comments" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Requests for comments">"requests for comments"</a> for further input. Essays, information pages, and other informal pages that are only supported by a small minority of the community may be moved to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Userfication" title="Wikipedia:Userfication">primary author's userspace</a> over deletion. The Wikipedia community has historically tolerated a wide range of Wikipedia related subjects and viewpoints on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_pages" title="Wikipedia:User pages">user pages</a>. </p>
      <p>Policies, guidelines and process pages should not be nominated for deletion, as such nominations will probably be considered <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Do_not_disrupt_Wikipedia_to_illustrate_a_point" title="Wikipedia:Do not disrupt Wikipedia to illustrate a point">disruptive</a>, and the ensuing discussions <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Speedy_keep" title="Wikipedia:Speedy keep">closed early</a>. The {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Disputed_tag" title="Template:Disputed tag">Disputed tag</a>}} template is typically used for claims that an essay, WikiProject advice or information page was recently assigned guideline or policy status without <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PROPOSAL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PROPOSAL">proper or sufficient consensus</a> being established. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Local_consensus" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Local consensus">WP:Local consensus</a> for more details. </p>
      <p>It is generally preferable that <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Inactive_WikiProjects" title="Wikipedia:Inactive WikiProjects">inactive WikiProjects</a> not be deleted, but instead be marked as <span class="nowrap">{{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_status" title="Template:WikiProject status">WikiProject status</a>|inactive}}</span>, or redirected to a relevant WikiProject, or changed to a task force of a parent WikiProject, unless the WikiProject was incompletely created or is entirely undesirable. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:INACTIVEWP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:INACTIVEWP">WP:INACTIVEWP</a> for more details. </p>
      <p>If a project page clearly satisfies a "general" <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CSD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CSD">speedy deletion criterion</a>, it should be tagged with the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GCSD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:GCSD">appropriate template</a>. For issues only affecting specific revisions on a page (where other page versions are fine) <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CFRD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CFRD">revision delete</a> is usually more appropriate. To request revision-deletion for copyright violations, see {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Copyvio-revdel" title="Template:Copyvio-revdel">Copyvio-revdel</a>}} for details. </p>
      <p>For a complete listing of past and current project namespace deletion requests at MfD, see <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Special%3APrefixIndex&amp;prefix=Miscellany+for+deletion%2FWikipedia&amp;namespace=4">all MfD pages with Wikipedia prefix</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Namespace_history">Namespace history</span></h2> 
      <p>The Wikipedia namespace began after the January 2002 release of the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Phase_II_software" title="Wikipedia:Phase II software">Phase II software</a>. Prior to that, an index of related pages was maintained at <b><a href="https://nostalgia.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia_utilities" class="extiw" title="nost:Wikipedia utilities">Wikipedia utilities</a></b>, which remained for some time afterwards at <b>Wikipedia:Utilities</b>. This list of pages in the project namespace has been replaced by the pages as described <a href="#Pages_within_project_namespace">above</a>. </p>
