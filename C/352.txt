      <p><b><a href="/wiki/Trademark" title="Trademark">Trademarks</a></b> include words and short phrases used by legal entities to identify themselves and their products and services. Often, these names are written in several ways with variations in capitalization, punctuation, and formatting. The advice in this page also applies to names and phrases used to identify individuals, movements, groups, forums, projects, events, and other non-commercial entities and their output. </p>
      <p>When deciding how to format a trademark, editors should examine styles already in use by <i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:IS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:IS">independent</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RS">reliable sources</a>. From among those, choose the style that most closely resembles standard English – regardless of the preference of the trademark owner. Do not invent new styles that are not used by independent reliable sources. This practice helps ensure consistency in language and avoids drawing undue attention to some subjects rather than others. Listed below are more specific recommendations for frequently occurring nonstandard formats. </p>
      <p>This guideline (in its entirety) applies to all trademarks, all <a href="/wiki/Service_mark" title="Service mark">service marks</a>, all <a href="/wiki/Trade_name" title="Trade name">business names</a>, and all other names of business entities. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General_rules">General rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Trademarks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: General rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="MOS:TMRULES"></span><span id="MOS:CAMELCASE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:TMRULES&amp;redirect=no">MOS:TMRULES</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:CAMELCASE&amp;redirect=no">MOS:CAMELCASE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <ul>
       <li>Capitalize trademarks, being proper names. For details, follow the same style as for titles of published works <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1015928993">.mw-parser-output div.crossreference{padding-left:0}</style><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable crossreference">(See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Capital_letters#Composition_titles" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Capital letters">Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Capital letters §&nbsp;Composition titles</a>).</span> 
        <ul>
         <li><i>use:</i> <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Nintendo</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Illumos" title="Illumos"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Illumos</span></a></li> 
         <li><i>avoid:</i> <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">nintendo</span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">illumos</span></li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>Don't expect readers to know, based on trademarks or brand names, what item is being discussed. For example: 
        <ul>
         <li><i>use:</i> <span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Police in Miami confiscated 25 stolen <a href="/wiki/Rolex" title="Rolex">Rolex</a> watches.</span></li> 
         <li><i>avoid:</i> <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">Police in Miami confiscated 25 stolen Rolexes.</span></li> 
         <li><i>however:</i> <span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Police in Miami confiscated 25 stolen <a href="/wiki/Apple_Watch" title="Apple Watch">Apple Watches</a>.</span> (This capitalization is appropriate because the product type is <em>included</em> in the formal name of the product.)</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>Follow standard English <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Text_formatting" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Text formatting">text formatting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Capital_letters" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Capital letters">capitalization</a> practices, even if the trademark owner considers nonstandard formatting "official", as long as this is a style already in widespread use, rather than inventing a new one: 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1015928993"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable crossreference selfref">(But see exception below under <a href="#Trademarks_that_begin_with_a_lowercase_letter">§&nbsp;Trademarks that begin with a lowercase letter</a>.)</span> 
        <ul>
         <li><i>use:</i> <a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;"><i>Time</i></span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Kiss</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Asus" title="Asus"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Asus</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Sony_Mobile" title="Sony Mobile"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Sony Mobile</span></a>. (Capitalize <a href="/wiki/IKEA" title="IKEA"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">IKEA</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">IBM</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Abbreviations#Acronyms" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Abbreviations">as acronyms/initialisms</a>.)</li> 
         <li><i>avoid:</i> <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;"><i>TIME</i></span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">KISS</span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">ASUS</span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">SONY Mobile</span></li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>Using all-caps is preferred if the letters are pronounced individually, even if they don't (or no longer) stand for anything. For instance, use <span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">SAT</span> for the testing system (formerly the Scholastic Assessment Test) and <span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">KFC</span> for the fast-food restaurant (formerly Kentucky Fried Chicken). Using all-lowercase letters may likewise be acceptable if it is done universally by sources, such as with the webcomic <i><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">xkcd</span></i>.</li> 
       <li><span class="anchor" id="®"></span>Do not use the ™ and ® symbols, or similar, in either article text or citations, unless unavoidably necessary for context. 
        <ul>
         <li><i>use:</i> <a href="/wiki/LittleBigPlanet" title="LittleBigPlanet"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;"><i>LittleBigPlanet</i></span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Realtor" class="mw-redirect" title="Realtor"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Realtor</span></a></li> 
         <li><i>avoid:</i> <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;"><i>LittleBigPlanet™</i></span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">REALTOR®</span></li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>Avoid using special characters that are not pronounced, are included purely for decoration, or simply substitute for English words or letters (e.g., "?" used for "love", "!" used for "i") or for normal punctuation, unless a significant majority of reliable sources that are independent of the subject consistently include the special character in the subject's name. Similarly, avoid special stylization, such as superscripting or boldface, in an attempt to emulate a trademark. 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1015928993"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable crossreference">(See also <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Article_titles#Special_characters" title="Wikipedia:Article titles">Wikipedia:Article titles §&nbsp;Special characters</a>.)</span> 
        <ul>
         <li><i>use:</i> <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s" title="Macy's"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Macy's</span></a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Skate_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Skate (magazine) (page does not exist)"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;"><i>Skate</i></span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Tail_(wine)" title="Yellow Tail (wine)"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Yellow Tail</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Seven_(film)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Seven (film)"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;"><i>Seven</i></span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Alien_3" title="Alien 3"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;"><i>Alien 3</i></span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Toys_%22R%22_Us" title="Toys &quot;R&quot; Us"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Toys "R" Us</span></a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[a]</a></sup></li> 
         <li><i>avoid:</i> <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">macy<sup>?</sup>s</span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;"><i>skate.</i></span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">[ yellow tail ]</span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;"><i>Se7en</i></span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;"><i>Alien<sup>3</sup></i></span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">Toys ? Us</span></li> 
         <li>When a name is almost never written except in a particular stylized form, use that form on Wikipedia: <a href="/wiki/Deadmau5" title="Deadmau5"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Deadmau5</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/3M" title="3M"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">3M</span></a>, <i><a href="/wiki/2_Fast_2_Furious" title="2 Fast 2 Furious"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">2 Fast 2 Furious</span></a></i> 
          <ul>
           <li>An exception to the above is to never include periods, exclamation marks, questions marks, or other punctuation, as including these makes the article difficult to read. Therefore, use <a href="/wiki/Fun_(band)" title="Fun (band)"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Fun</span></a> and not <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;"><i>fun.</i></span> when referring to the band.</li>
          </ul></li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>Trademarks in "<a href="/wiki/CamelCase" class="mw-redirect" title="CamelCase">CamelCase</a>" are a judgment call; the style may be used where it reflects general usage and makes the trademark more readable. 
        <ul>
         <li><a href="/wiki/Oxycodone" title="Oxycodone"><span class="example example-neutral" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: initial;">OxyContin</span></a> or <span class="example example-neutral" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: initial;">Oxycontin</span> &nbsp;– <i>editor's choice</i></li> 
         <li><i>however:</i> <a href="/wiki/PlayStation" title="PlayStation"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">PlayStation</span></a> only (camelcase preferred because <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">Playstation</span> is not widely-used.)</li> 
         <li>Initial lowercase in certain trademarks almost never written any other way, such as <a href="/wiki/IPhone" title="IPhone"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">iPhone</span></a> and <a href="/wiki/EBay" title="EBay"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">eBay</span></a>, are accepted on Wikipedia, though for page naming there are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Page_name#Technical_restrictions_and_limitations" title="Wikipedia:Page name">technical limitations</a> and methods of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DISPLAYTITLE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DISPLAYTITLE">displaying the title appropriately</a>.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>Do not "correct" the spelling, punctuation, diacritics, or grammar of trademarks to be different from anything found in reliable sources—the name should be <i>recognizable</i> as referring to the topic. 
        <ul>
         <li><i>correct:</i> <a href="/wiki/Craigslist" title="Craigslist"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Craigslist</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Uber" title="Uber"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Uber</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Players_Association" title="National Basketball Players Association"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">National Basketball Players Association</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Tumblr" title="Tumblr"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Tumblr</span></a></li> 
         <li><i>wrong:</i> <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">Craig's List</span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">Über</span> or <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">Ueber</span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">National Basketball Players' Association</span>, <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">Tumbler</span></li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><span class="anchor" id="TMTHE"></span>Do not capitalize the word <i>the</i> in a trademark 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1015928993"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable crossreference">(see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Capital_letters#Institutions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Capital letters">WP:Manual of Style/Capital letters §&nbsp;Institutions</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Capital_letters#Capitalization_of_The" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Capital letters">§&nbsp;Capitalization of The</a>)</span> regardless how the name is styled in logos and the like, except at the beginning of a sentence.<sup id="cite_ref-Sentence_case_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sentence_case-2">[b]</a></sup> Titles of published works <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Titles#Indefinite_and_definite_articles" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Titles"><em>do</em> have an initial <i>The</i> capitalized</a>; bands and the like <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Music#Names_(definite_article)" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Music">do not</a>. Rarely, an exception may apply. but only when consistently treated this way in most reliable sources (e.g. <a href="/wiki/The_The" title="The The"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">The The</span></a>); when expanding an acronym that starts with <i>T</i> for <i>The</i> (as in <a href="/wiki/The_International_Cat_Association" title="The International Cat Association"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">The International Cat Association</span></a><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;"> (TICA)</span>); and when the name of a publisher begins with the name of a publication with an initial <i>The</i> (thus <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times_Company" title="The New York Times Company"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">The New York Times Company</span></a>, per <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">The New York Times</span></a></i>).</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span id="Mergers.2C_partnerships.2C_and_other_combined_names"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mergers,_partnerships,_and_other_combined_names">Mergers, partnerships, and other combined names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Trademarks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mergers, partnerships, and other combined names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The names of merged companies, partnerships, consolidated divisions, and merged product lines vary by organization, and there are <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=List_of_largest_law_firms_by_revenue&amp;oldid=676865589">many styles</a></span>. Beware assumptions about how such names are constructed and what they mean; a complex real example is <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley_Dean_Witter_Discover_%26_Co." class="mw-redirect" title="Morgan Stanley Dean Witter Discover &amp; Co."><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Morgan Stanley Dean Witter Discover &amp; Co.</span></a>, which resulted from a merger of two corporations, while its name, built from parts of those of previous entities that were themselves the results of mergers, consists of two last names, a first and last name, a company name, and an abbreviation. </p>
      <p>The ampersand (<code>&amp;</code>) is frequently used in trademarks (e.g. <a href="/wiki/AT%26T" title="AT&amp;T"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">AT&amp;T</span></a>), and the plus symbol (<code>+</code>) occasionally (as in <a href="/wiki/Springer_Science%2BBusiness_Media" title="Springer Science+Business Media"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Springer Science+Business Media</span></a>), as substitutes for the word "and". A long-standing trend has been to drop the word entirely (along with commas sometimes) in long, multi-party business names, especially after mergers or the addition of a partner (for example, <a href="/wiki/Harcourt_(publisher)" title="Harcourt (publisher)"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Harcourt, Brace &amp; Company</span></a> became <span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Harcourt Brace Jovanovich</span>, later part of <a href="/wiki/Houghton_Mifflin_Harcourt" title="Houghton Mifflin Harcourt"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Houghton Mifflin Harcourt</span></a>). </p>
      <p>More recently, some have even taken to removing spaces and using camelcase (e.g. <a href="/wiki/DaimlerChrysler" class="mw-redirect" title="DaimlerChrysler"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">DaimlerChrysler</span></a>), sometimes unpredictably (as in <a href="/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase" title="JPMorgan Chase"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">JPMorgan Chase</span></a>). </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Leave compressed names as-is: Do not add "and", a symbol for it, commas, or spaces to such names (e.g. "<span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">Houghton, Mifflin &amp; Harcourt</span>") where independent, reliable sources do not consistently use them, and do not remove them (e.g. "<span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">HoughtonMifflinHarcourt</span>") if they are consistently used.</li> 
       <li>The compressed form is not always the one to use: As with the other considerations above, if reliable sources overwhelmingly favor a particular spelling and punctuation, use it in Wikipedia, but do not simply attempt to mimic graphical marketing materials: <a href="/wiki/Gulf_and_Western_Industries" title="Gulf and Western Industries">Gulf and Western Industries</a> is the proper corporation name (Gulf and Western for short), not the <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">Gulf+Western</span> of their logo; while more concise, it is less recognizable and less common.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>If in doubt about a modern company, their website's small print, contact page, or legal disclaimers (privacy policy, etc.) may provide the official company name, and online searches of corporation registrations and of trademarks can also be used for this purpose. (Note, however, that Wikipedia article titles are usually given <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Article_titles#Use_commonly_recognizable_names" title="Wikipedia:Article titles">the most common name</a> in reliable sources, which <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Official_names" title="Wikipedia:Official names">might not be the official name</a>.) </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trademarks_that_begin_with_a_lowercase_letter">Trademarks that begin with a lowercase letter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Trademarks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Trademarks that begin with a lowercase letter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="MOS:TMLOWER"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:TMLOWER&amp;redirect=no">MOS:TMLOWER</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Capital_letters" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Capital letters">Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Capital letters</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Trademarks that officially begin with a lowercase letter raise several problems because they break the normal capitalization rules of English that proper names are written with initial capital letters wherever they occur in a sentence. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>With the exception that immediately follows, trademarks promoted without any capitals are capitalized like any other: 
        <ul>
         <li><i>use:</i> <span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">I found a <i><a href="/wiki/Thirtysomething_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Thirtysomething (TV series)">Thirtysomething</a></i> DVD and a pair of <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> shoes while browsing <a href="/wiki/Craigslist" title="Craigslist">Craigslist</a>.</span></li> 
         <li><i>avoid:</i> <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">I found a <i>thirtysomething</i> DVD and a pair of adidas shoes while browsing craigslist.</span></li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>The exception is trademarks that begin with a one-letter lowercase prefix pronounced as a separate letter. These are often not capitalized if the second letter is capitalized, but should otherwise follow normal capitalization rules: 
        <ul>
         <li><i>use:</i> <span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">He said that <a href="/wiki/EBay" title="EBay">eBay</a> is where he bought his <a href="/wiki/IPod" title="IPod">iPod</a>.</span></li> 
         <li><i>avoid:</i> <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">He said that EBay is where he bought his IPod.</span></li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>In such a case, rephrase to avoid beginning sentences with lowercase trademarks: 
        <ul>
         <li><i>use:</i> <span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;"><i>He bought his iPod on eBay.</i></span></li> 
         <li><i>avoid:</i> <span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">eBay is where he bought his iPod.</span></li>
        </ul></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Not all trademarks with a pronunciation that could have fit this pattern actually do so, and should not be re-styled to conform to it (use <a href="/wiki/NEdit" title="NEdit"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">NEdit</span></a> not "<span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">nEdit</span>", <a href="/wiki/E-Trade" title="E-Trade"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">E-Trade</span></a> not "<span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">eTrade</span>"; <a href="/wiki/Xbox" title="Xbox"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">Xbox</span></a>, not "<span class="example deprecated-content example-bad" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #8B0000;">xBox</span>"). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Indicating_stylizations">Indicating stylizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Trademarks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Indicating stylizations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="MOS:TMSTYLE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MOS:TMSTYLE&amp;redirect=no">MOS:TMSTYLE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>In the article about a trademark, it is conventional to give the normal English spelling in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Lead_section" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Lead section">lead section</a>, followed by a note, such as "(stylized as ...)" (or "(stylised as ...)" depending on the article's <a href="/wiki/MOS:ENGVAR" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:ENGVAR">variety of English</a>), with the stylized version (which may include simple stylization, like capitalization changes, decorative characters, or superscripting, but not colorization, attempts to emulate font choices, or other elaborate effects),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[c]</a></sup> then resume using an alternative that follows the usual rules of spelling and punctuation, for the remainder of the article. In other articles that mention the subject, use only the normal English spelling, not the stylization. </p>
      <p>However, if the title of the article is the stylized version of the name (e.g. <a href="/wiki/IPod" title="IPod"><span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">iPod</span></a>), it should be given in the boldfaced title recapitulation at the beginning of the lead (i.e., without a "stylized as" note), and used throughout the text (and, in most cases, in other articles that mention it). The lead may also have a note (e.g., "sometimes also written ...") indicating the unstylized version if it is also commonly attested in reliable sources, especially if any confusion could result from its absence. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_of_graphic_logos">Use of graphic logos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Trademarks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Use of graphic logos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Non-free_content" title="Wikipedia:Non-free content">Wikipedia:Non-free content</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Logos" title="Wikipedia:Logos">Wikipedia:Logos</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Icons" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Icons">Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Icons</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Product logos and corporate logos, such as the stylized rendition of the word <i>Dell</i> used by <a href="/wiki/Dell_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Dell Inc.">Dell Inc.</a>, whether copyrighted or not, may be used once in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:INFOBOX" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:INFOBOX">infobox</a> or corner of articles about the related product, service, company, or entity. </p>
      <p>Although many companies claim copyright over their logos, the use of the logo in an encyclopedia article may be considered <a href="/wiki/Fair_use" title="Fair use">fair use</a>. Please <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Untagged_images" title="Wikipedia:Untagged images">tag</a> logo images with {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Non-free_logo" title="Template:Non-free logo">non-free logo</a>}}. Some logos are <a href="/wiki/Free_content" title="Free content">free content</a> because they are in the <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">public domain</a> or are under a free license: for example, logos consisting of short text may <a href="/wiki/Threshold_of_originality" title="Threshold of originality">not be eligible</a> for copyright protection, and old logos that were published without a copyright notice have likely fallen into the public domain. When this is definitely the case, the {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Trademark" title="Template:Trademark">trademark</a>}} tag may be used instead. However, when in doubt, err on the side of caution per non-free content policy by assuming that the logo is copyrighted. </p>
      <p>Note that non-free logos should only be used in the infoboxes of the primary article(s) to which they are affiliated; i.e. a company logo may be used in the article about that company, but not in a separate article about one of the company's products. </p>
      <p>Distinguish clearly between the trademark and the company name when, as with Dell, it is customary to do so. Company names should normally be given in the most common form in English; only specify <i>International Business Machines Corporation</i> to state that that <i>is</i> the legal name, otherwise call it <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a>, as our sources do. </p>

         <li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><i>Toys "R" Us</i> has quotation marks around the <i>R</i> because it is treated this way consistently in reliable sources (probably because the company does this itself in running text, despite that punctuation not being in their graphical logo). This example should <em>not</em> be taken as an instruction to add quotation marks to symbol-for-word substitutions in other proper names, e.g. the film title <i><a href="/wiki/2_Fast_2_Furious" title="2 Fast 2 Furious">2 Fast 2 Furious</a></i>.</span> </li> 
         <li id="cite_note-Sentence_case-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-Sentence_case_2-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Wikipedia uses <a href="/wiki/Sentence_case" class="mw-redirect" title="Sentence case">sentence case</a> for sentences, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Article_titles" title="Wikipedia:Article titles">article titles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Layout" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Layout">section titles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Tables" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Tables">table headers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Images" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Images">image captions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Lists" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Lists">list entries</a> (in most cases), and entries in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Infobox" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Infobox">infoboxes</a> and similar templates, among other things. Any instructions in MoS about the start of a sentence apply to items using sentence case.</span> </li> 
         <li id="cite_note-3"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-3">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">An exception to the "elaborate effects" rule is made at the articles on the <a href="/wiki/TeX" title="TeX">TeX</a> and <a href="/wiki/LaTeX" title="LaTeX">LaTeX</a> text formatting systems, because the more detailed stylizations represent the actual treatment in reliable sources, and also serve to illustrate what these electronic typesetting systems do.</span> </li> 
        </ol>
