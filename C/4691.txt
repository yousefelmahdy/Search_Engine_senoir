        <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="About_me">About me</span></h2> 
        <p>Oshwah is my username. I began contributing to Wikipedia in 2007. After a few years of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:VAND" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:VAND">vandal fighting</a>, discussing <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AFD">AFDs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPP">patrolling new pages</a>, and assisting new editors, I took an extended leave of absence in 2011 to focus on my education (you'll notice a large gap in my contributions - software engineering and applied mathematics isn't easy). After graduating from college and becoming established in my Software Engineering career, I returned to Wikipedia to resume where I left off. </p>
        <p>If you're not on IRC, you'll typically run into me through <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:HG" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:HG">Huggle</a> or on the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AIV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AIV">AIV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RFPP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RFPP">RFPP</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:UAA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:UAA">UAA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ACC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ACC">ACC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SPI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SPI">SPI</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ANI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ANI">ANI</a>, or other noticeboards on Wikipedia. You'll also see me active in various places where vandalism, trolling, and abuse is taking place. I've been <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RCP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RCP">patrolling recent changes</a> and removing vandalism and disruption for over 10 years now. I started in 2007 by performing each revert and leaving warnings completely manually. I then progressed to using <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TW" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TW">Twinkle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:LUPIN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:LUPIN">Lupin's tool</a> to make the process of locating bad faith edits and reverting them much easier. After the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ROLLBACK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ROLLBACK">rollback user right</a> became unbundled from the admin toolset in 2008, I expanded my involvement after being one of the first editors to have it granted to an account. Many years later, I now direct, manage, recruit, and participate in a significant amount of of areas and projects on Wikipedia. </p>
        <p>People often ask me why I spend so much time on Wikipedia. The answer is simple: <i>I enjoy it</i>. <i>Everyone</i> on Wikipedia should be volunteering in places and projects that they <i>enjoy</i>, and volunteering their time and energy on Wikipedia because they <i>enjoy doing so</i>. Otherwise, what's the point?&nbsp;:-) </p> 
        <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geek_stuff">Geek stuff</span></h2> 
        <p>I started using a computer when I was 5 years old - a <a href="/wiki/Compaq" title="Compaq">Compaq</a> computer with a 200MHz <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a> <a href="/wiki/Pentium" title="Pentium">Pentium</a> processor, 48MB of <a href="/wiki/Synchronous_dynamic_random-access_memory" title="Synchronous dynamic random-access memory">SDRAM</a>, and two <a href="/wiki/USB" title="USB">USB</a> ports on the back. It may seem standard to today's average computer user, but the fact that the computer provided <a href="/wiki/Synchronous_dynamic_random-access_memory" title="Synchronous dynamic random-access memory">SDRAM</a>, <a href="/wiki/USB" title="USB">USB</a> output, and the fact that it included a sound card and an internal <a href="/wiki/56_kbit/s_modem" class="mw-redirect" title="56 kbit/s modem">56K modem</a> set my parents back ~$2500 at the time. I used it to play the many old computer games that it came with, such as <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Rebel_Assault_II:_The_Hidden_Empire" title="Star Wars: Rebel Assault II: The Hidden Empire">Star Wars: Rebel Assault II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Dark_Forces" title="Star Wars: Dark Forces">Star Wars: Dark Forces</a>, <a href="/wiki/StarCraft" title="StarCraft">StarCraft</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park:_Chaos_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Jurassic Park: Chaos Island">Jurassic Park: Chaos Island</a>. At age 7, I was proficient enough to teach my parents (mostly my dad) how to install programs, write documents, print, access the internet, and fix the many stupid things that he would do to break his Operating System. I built my first computer at 10 years old: a <a href="/wiki/Slot_1" title="Slot 1">Slot 1</a> <a href="/wiki/Pentium_II" title="Pentium II">Pentium II</a> Processor at 400 MHz, 128 MB of <a href="/wiki/Synchronous_dynamic_random-access_memory" title="Synchronous dynamic random-access memory">SDRAM</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Voodoo3" title="Voodoo3">Voodoo3</a> 2000 GPU, and an <a href="/wiki/Universal_Abit" title="Universal Abit">ABIT</a> (BH6 Model, I think?) motherboard given to me by my uncle. It wasn't the hottest computer on the planet, but building it myself was a huge achievement. I was a computer tech assistant for two years at my Middle School and for four years at my High School. I learned to handle helpdesk tasks, work with switches and networking equipment, address network and IP Addressing issues, perform server administration tasks (domain controllers and active directory), and I did <i>a lot</i> of computer repair. I put enough time into learning all things computer-related, that I was mostly self-taught in many <a href="/wiki/Information_Technology" class="mw-redirect" title="Information Technology">Information Technology</a> skills and concepts by the time I entered college. </p>
