      <p>Decisions on Wikipedia are primarily made by <b><a href="/wiki/Consensus" class="mw-redirect" title="Consensus">consensus</a></b>, which is accepted as the best method to achieve Wikipedia's goals, i.e., the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Five_pillars" title="Wikipedia:Five pillars">five pillars</a>. <em>Consensus</em> on Wikipedia does not mean unanimity (which is ideal but not always achievable), nor is it the result of a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Polling_is_not_a_substitute_for_discussion" title="Wikipedia:Polling is not a substitute for discussion">vote</a>. Decision making and reaching consensus involve an effort to incorporate all editors' legitimate concerns, while respecting Wikipedia's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Policies_and_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Policies and guidelines">policies and guidelines</a>. </p>
      <p>This policy describes how consensus is understood on Wikipedia, how to determine whether it has been achieved (and how to proceed if it has not), and describes exceptions to the principle that all decisions are made by consensus. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Achieving_consensus">Achieving consensus</span></h2> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:CONACHIEVE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:CONACHIEVE&amp;redirect=no">WP:CONACHIEVE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Editors usually reach consensus as a natural process. After one changes a page, others who read it can choose whether or not to further edit. When editors do not reach agreement by editing, discussion on the associated <a href="/wiki/Help:Using_talk_pages" class="mw-redirect" title="Help:Using talk pages">talk pages</a> continues the process toward consensus. </p>
      <p>A consensus decision takes into account all of the proper concerns raised. Ideally, it arrives with an absence of objections, but often we must settle for as wide an agreement as can be reached. When there is no wide agreement, consensus-building involves adapting the proposal to bring in dissenters without losing those who accepted the initial proposal. </p>

      <p>Consensus is a normal and usually implicit and invisible process across Wikipedia. Any edit that is not disputed or reverted by another editor <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Silence_and_consensus" title="Wikipedia:Silence and consensus">can be assumed to have consensus</a>. Should that edit later be revised by another editor without dispute, it can be assumed that a new consensus has been reached. In this way, the encyclopedia is gradually added to and improved over time. </p>
      <p>All edits should be explained (unless the reason for them is obvious)—either by clear <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_summary" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Edit summary">edit summaries</a> indicating the reason why the change was made, or by discussion on the associated talk page. Substantive, informative edit summaries indicate what issues need to be addressed in subsequent efforts to reach consensus. Edit summaries are especially important when <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reverting" title="Wikipedia:Reverting">reverting</a> another editor's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Assume_good_faith" title="Wikipedia:Assume good faith">good faith</a> work. Repeated reversions are contrary to Wikipedia policy under <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_warring" title="Wikipedia:Edit warring">Edit warring</a>, except for specific policy-based material (such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLP">WP:BLP</a> exceptions) and for reversions of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vandalism" title="Wikipedia:Vandalism">vandalism</a>. </p>
      <p>Except in cases affected by content <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Policies_and_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Policies and guidelines">policies or guidelines</a>, most disputes over content may be resolved through minor changes rather than taking an all-or-nothing position. Often, a simple rewording will satisfy all editors' concerns. Whether changes come through editing or through discussion, the encyclopedia is best improved through collaboration and consensus, not through combat and capitulation. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Be_bold" title="Wikipedia:Be bold">Be bold</a>, but not rash. In most cases, the first thing to try is an edit to the page, and sometimes making such an edit will resolve a dispute. Use clear edit summaries that explain the purpose of the edit. If the edit is reverted, try making a compromise edit that addresses the other editors' concerns. Edit summaries are useful, but do not try to discuss disputes across multiple edit summaries; that is generally viewed as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_warring" title="Wikipedia:Edit warring">edit warring</a> and may incur sanctions. If an edit is reverted and further edits seem likely to meet the same fate, create a new section on the associated talk page to discuss the issue. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Through_discussion">Through discussion</span></h3> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="Through_discussion-Gradually_improving"></span>
       <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855"></p>
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:DISCUSSCONSENSUS"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:DISCUSSCONSENSUS&amp;redirect=no">WP:DISCUSSCONSENSUS</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BOLD,_revert,_discuss_cycle" title="Wikipedia:BOLD, revert, discuss cycle">Wikipedia:BOLD, revert, discuss cycle</a>
      </div> 
      <p>When agreement cannot be reached through editing alone, the consensus-forming process becomes more explicit: editors open a section on the associated <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Talk_page_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Talk page guidelines">talk page</a> and try to work out the dispute through discussion, using <em>reasons</em> based in policy, sources, and common sense; they can also suggest alternative solutions or compromises that may satisfy all concerns. The result might be an agreement that does not satisfy anyone completely, but that all recognize as a reasonable solution. Consensus is an ongoing process on Wikipedia; it is often better to accept a less-than-perfect compromise—with the understanding that the page is gradually improving—than to try to fight to implement a particular preferred version immediately. The quality of articles with combative editors is, as a rule, far lower than that of articles where editors take a longer view. </p>
      <p>When editors have a particularly difficult time reaching a consensus, several processes are available for consensus-building (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Third_opinion" title="Wikipedia:Third opinion">third opinions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DRN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DRN">dispute resolution noticeboard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requests_for_comment" title="Wikipedia:Requests for comment">requests for comment</a>), and even more extreme processes that will take authoritative steps to end the dispute (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators%27_noticeboard/Incidents" title="Wikipedia:Administrators' noticeboard/Incidents">administrator intervention</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration">arbitration</a>). Keep in mind, however, that administrators are primarily concerned with policy and editor behavior and will not decide content issues authoritatively. They may block editors for behaviors that interfere with the consensus process (such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_warring" title="Wikipedia:Edit warring">edit-warring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Sock_puppetry" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Sock puppetry">abuse of multiple accounts</a>, or a lack of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Civility" title="Wikipedia:Civility">civility</a>). They may also make decisions about whether edits are or are not allowable under policy, but will not usually go beyond such actions. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Consensus-building">Consensus-building</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Dispute_resolution" title="Wikipedia:Dispute resolution">Wikipedia:Dispute resolution</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Editors who maintain a neutral, detached, and civil attitude can usually reach consensus on an article through the process described above. They may still occasionally find themselves at an impasse, either because they cannot find rational grounds to settle a dispute or because one or both sides of the discussion become emotionally or ideologically invested in <em>winning</em> an argument. What follows are suggestions for resolving intractable disputes, along with descriptions of several formal and informal processes that may help. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="In_talk_pages">In talk pages</span></h4> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:TALKDONTREVERT"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:TALKDONTREVERT&amp;redirect=no">WP:TALKDONTREVERT</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Talk_page_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Talk page guidelines">Wikipedia:Talk page guidelines</a>
      </div> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="talk"></span><span class="anchor" id="Talk"></span><span class="anchor" id="TALK"></span> In determining consensus, consider the quality of the arguments, the history of how they came about, the objections of those who disagree, and existing policies and guidelines. The quality of an argument is more important than whether it represents a minority or a majority view. The arguments "I just don't like it" and "I just like it" usually carry no weight whatsoever. </p>
      <p>Limit article talk page discussions to discussion of sources, article focus, and policy. If an edit is challenged, or is likely to be challenged, editors should use talk pages to explain why an addition, change, or removal improves the article, and hence the encyclopedia. Consensus can be assumed if no editors object to a change. Editors who ignore talk page discussions yet continue to edit in or revert disputed material, or who <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:STONEWALL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:STONEWALL">stonewall</a> discussions, may be guilty of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disruptive_editing" title="Wikipedia:Disruptive editing">disruptive editing</a> and incur sanctions. Consensus cannot always be assumed simply because editors stop responding to talk page discussions in which they have already participated. </p>
      <p>The goal of a consensus-building discussion is to resolve disputes in a way that reflects Wikipedia's goals and policies while angering as few contributors as possible. Contributors with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Competence_is_required" title="Wikipedia:Competence is required">good social skills</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Negotiation" title="Wikipedia:Negotiation">good negotiation skills</a> are more likely to be successful than those who are less than civil to others. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="By_soliciting_outside_opinions">By soliciting outside opinions</span></h4> 
      <p>When talk page discussions fail—generally because two editors (or two groups of editors) simply cannot see eye to eye on an issue—Wikipedia has several established processes to attract outside editors to offer opinions. This is often useful to break simple, good-faith deadlocks, because uninvolved editors can bring in fresh perspectives, and can help involved editors see middle ground that they cannot see for themselves. The main resources for this are as follows: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Third_Opinion" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Third Opinion">Third opinion</a> (3O)
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        A neutral third party will give non-binding advice on the dispute. Reserved for cases where exactly two editors are in dispute.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Noticeboards" title="Wikipedia:Noticeboards">Noticeboards</a>
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        Most policy and guideline pages, and many <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">wikiprojects</a>, have noticeboards for interested editors. Posting a neutrally worded notice of the dispute on applicable noticeboards will make the dispute more visible to other editors who may have worthwhile opinions.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Dispute_resolution_noticeboard" title="Wikipedia:Dispute resolution noticeboard">Dispute resolution noticeboard</a> (DRN)
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        For disputes involving more than two parties, moderators help the parties come to consensus by suggesting analysis, critiques, compromises, or mediation, but generally limited to simple disputes which can quickly be resolved.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requests_for_comment" title="Wikipedia:Requests for comment">Requests for comment</a> (RfC)
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        Placement of a formal neutrally worded notice on the article talk page inviting others to participate which is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Transclusion" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Transclusion">transcluded</a> onto RfC noticeboards.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Village_pump" title="Wikipedia:Village pump">Village pump</a>
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        Neutrally worded notification of a dispute here also may bring in additional editors who may help.
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>Many of these discussions will involve <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Polling_is_not_a_substitute_for_discussion" title="Wikipedia:Polling is not a substitute for discussion">polls</a> of one sort or another; but as consensus is determined by the quality of arguments (not by a simple counted majority), polls should be regarded as structured discussions rather than <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:!VOTE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:!VOTE">voting</a>. Responses indicating individual explanations of positions using Wikipedia policies and guidelines are given the highest weight. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_or_community_intervention">Administrative or community intervention</span></h4> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:CONADMIN"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:CONADMIN&amp;redirect=no">WP:CONADMIN</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>In some cases, disputes are personal or ideological rather than mere disagreements about content, and these may require the intervention of administrators or the community as a whole. Sysops will not rule on content, but may intervene to enforce policy (such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Biographies_of_living_persons" title="Wikipedia:Biographies of living persons">WP:Biographies of living persons</a>) or to impose sanctions on editors who are disrupting the consensus process. Sometimes merely asking for an administrator's attention on a talk page will suffice; as a rule, sysops have large numbers of pages watchlisted, and there is a likelihood that someone will see it and respond. However, there are established resources for working with intransigent editors, as follows: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dt>
        Noticeboards
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        As noted previously, policy pages generally have noticeboards, and many administrators watch them.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators%27_noticeboard/Incidents" title="Wikipedia:Administrators' noticeboard/Incidents">Administrators' noticeboard of incidents</a> and general <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators%27_noticeboard" title="Wikipedia:Administrators' noticeboard">Administrators' noticeboard</a>
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        These are noticeboards for administrators. They are high-volume noticeboards and should be used sparingly. Use AN for issues that need eyes but may not need immediate action; use ANI for more pressing issues. Do not use either except at need.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requests_for_arbitration" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Requests for arbitration">Requests for arbitration</a>
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        The final step for intractable disputes. The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee">Arbitration Committee</a> (ArbCom) may rule on almost any behaviorial or policy-interpretation aspect of a dispute, and has broad powers in its decisions. ArbCom does not settle <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Dispute_resolution#Resolving_content_disputes" title="Wikipedia:Dispute resolution">content disputes</a> or change policy.
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pitfalls_and_errors">Pitfalls and errors</span></h4> 
      <p>The following are common mistakes made by editors when trying to build consensus: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>Off-wiki discussions.</b> Consensus is reached through on-wiki discussion or by editing. Discussions elsewhere are not taken into account. In some cases, such off-wiki communication may generate suspicion and mistrust.</li> 
       <li><b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Canvassing" title="Wikipedia:Canvassing">Canvassing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Sock_puppetry" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Sock puppetry">sock puppetry</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Sock_puppetry#Meatpuppetry" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Sock puppetry">meat puppetry</a>.</b> Any effort to gather participants to a community discussion that has the effect of biasing that discussion is unacceptable. While it is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Canvassing#Friendly_notices" title="Wikipedia:Canvassing">fine</a>—even encouraged—to invite people into a discussion to obtain new insights and arguments, it is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Canvassing#Inappropriate_notification" title="Wikipedia:Canvassing">not acceptable</a> to invite only people favorable to a particular point of view, or to invite people in a way that will prejudice their opinions on the matter. Using an alternative persona ("sock puppet", or "sock") to influence consensus is absolutely forbidden. Neutral, informative messages to Wikipedia <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Noticeboards" title="Wikipedia:Noticeboards">noticeboards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">wikiprojects</a>, or editors are permitted; but actions that could reasonably be interpreted as an attempt to "stuff the ballot box" or otherwise compromise the consensus-building process are considered disruptive.</li> 
       <li><b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Tendentious_editing" title="Wikipedia:Tendentious editing">Tendentious editing</a>.</b> The continuous, aggressive pursuit of an editorial goal is considered disruptive, and should be avoided. Editors should <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disruptive_editing#Failure_or_refusal_to_&quot;get_the_point&quot;" title="Wikipedia:Disruptive editing">listen</a>, respond, and cooperate to build a better article. Editors who refuse to allow any consensus except the one they insist on, and who <a href="/wiki/Filibuster" title="Filibuster">filibuster</a> indefinitely to attain that goal, risk damaging the consensus process.</li>
      </ul>

It does not help develop consensus to try different forums in the hope of finding one where you get the answer you <em>want</em>. (This is also known as "asking the other parent".) Queries placed on noticeboards and talk pages should be phrased as neutrally as possible, in order to get uninvolved and neutral additional opinions. Where multiple issues do exist, then the raising of the individual issues on the correct pages may be reasonable, but in that case it is normally best to give links to show where else you have raised the question.</li>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Determining_consensus">Determining consensus</span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Closing_discussions" title="Wikipedia:Closing discussions">Wikipedia:Closing discussions</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Consensus is ascertained by the quality of the arguments given on the various sides of an issue, as viewed through the lens of Wikipedia policy. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Levels_of_consensus">Levels of consensus</span></h3> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="Level_of_consensus"></span> </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:CONLEVEL"></span><span id="WP:CONLIMITED"></span><span id="WP:LOCALCONSENSUS"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:CONLEVEL&amp;redirect=no">WP:CONLEVEL</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:CONLIMITED&amp;redirect=no">WP:CONLIMITED</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:LOCALCONSENSUS&amp;redirect=no">WP:LOCALCONSENSUS</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee">Arbitration Committee</a><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration/Requests/Case/Infoboxes#Levels_of_consensus" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration/Requests/Case/Infoboxes">statement of principles on levels of consensus</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Consensus among a limited group of editors, at one place and time, cannot override community consensus on a wider scale. For instance, unless they can convince the broader community that such action is right, participants in a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">WikiProject</a> cannot decide that some generally accepted <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:POLICY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:POLICY">policy or guideline</a> does not apply to articles within its scope. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Guide#Advice_pages" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide">WikiProject advice pages</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Project_namespace#How-to_and_information_pages" title="Wikipedia:Project namespace">how-to and information pages</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Template_documentation" title="Wikipedia:Template documentation">template documentation pages</a> have not formally been approved by the community through the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PROPOSAL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PROPOSAL">policy and guideline proposal process</a>, thus have no more status than an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Essays" title="Wikipedia:Essays">essay</a>. </p>
      <p>Wikipedia has a standard of participation and consensus for changes to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Policies_and_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Policies and guidelines">policies and guidelines</a>. Their stability and consistency are important to the community. Accordingly, editors often propose substantive changes on the talk page first to permit discussion before implementing the change. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Be_bold#Wikipedia_namespace" title="Wikipedia:Be bold">Bold</a> changes are rarely welcome on policy pages. Improvements to policy are best made slowly and conservatively, with active efforts to seek out input and agreement from others. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="No_consensus">No consensus</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:NOCON"></span><span id="WP:NOCONSENSUS"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:NOCON&amp;redirect=no">WP:NOCON</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:NOCONSENSUS&amp;redirect=no">WP:NOCONSENSUS</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Discussions sometimes result in no consensus to take or not take an action. What happens next depends on the context: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Deletion" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Deletion">deletion discussions</a>, a lack of consensus normally results in the article, page, image, or other content being kept. 
        <ul>
         <li>However, in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Redirects_for_discussion" title="Wikipedia:Redirects for discussion">Redirects for discussion</a>, no consensus closes may <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Redirects_for_discussion/Common_outcomes#&quot;No_consensus&quot;_retargets" title="Wikipedia:Redirects for discussion/Common outcomes">still lead to a redirect being retargeted</a>.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>In discussions of proposals to add, modify, or remove material in articles, a lack of consensus commonly results in retaining the version of the article as it was prior to the proposal or bold edit. However, for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Contentious" title="Wikipedia:Contentious">contentious</a> matters related to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLP#Restoring_deleted_content" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLP">living people</a>, a lack of consensus often results in the removal of the contentious matter, regardless of whether the proposal was to add, modify, or remove it.</li> 
       <li>When <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators" title="Wikipedia:Administrators">actions by administrators</a> are contested and the discussion results in no consensus either for the action or for reverting the action, the action is normally reverted.</li> 
       <li>In disputes over <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:External_links" title="Wikipedia:External links">external links</a>, disputed links are removed unless and until there is a consensus to include them.</li> 
       <li>In <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Article_titles" title="Wikipedia:Article titles">article title</a> discussions (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TITLECHANGES" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TITLECHANGES">WP:TITLECHANGES</a>), the policy gives a default action for a no-consensus result:</li>
      </ul> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <ul>
         <li>"If it has never been stable, or it has been unstable for a long time, and no consensus can be reached on what the title should be, default to the title used by the first major contributor after the article ceased to be a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Stub" title="Wikipedia:Stub">stub</a>."</li>
        </ul>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Consensus_can_change">Consensus can change</span></h2> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:CCC"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:CCC&amp;redirect=no">WP:CCC</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Editors may propose a change to current consensus, especially to raise previously unconsidered arguments or circumstances. On the other hand, proposing to change a recently established consensus can be disruptive. </p>
      <p>Editors may propose a consensus change by <a href="#Through_discussion">discussion</a> or <a href="#Through_editing">editing</a>. That said, in most cases, an editor who knows a proposed change will modify a matter resolved by past discussion should propose that change by discussion. Editors who revert a change proposed by an edit should generally avoid terse explanations (such as "against consensus") which provide little guidance to the proposing editor (or, if you do use such terse explanations, it is helpful to <em>also</em> include a link to the discussion where the consensus was formed). </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Decisions_not_subject_to_consensus_of_editors">Decisions not subject to consensus of editors</span></h2> 
      <p>Certain policies and decisions made by the Wikimedia Foundation ("WMF"), its officers, and the <a href="/wiki/Arbitration_Committee" title="Arbitration Committee">Arbitration Committee</a> of Wikipedia are outside the purview of editor consensus. This does not constitute an exhaustive list as much as a reminder that the decisions taken under this project apply only to the workings of the self-governing community of English Wikipedia. </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:CONEXCEPT"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:CONEXCEPT&amp;redirect=no">WP:CONEXCEPT</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <ul>
       <li>The WMF has legal control over, and liability for, Wikipedia. Decisions, rulings, and acts of the WMF Board and its duly appointed designees take precedence over, and preempt, consensus. A consensus among editors that any such decision, ruling, or act violates <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Policies">Wikimedia Foundation policies</a></span> may be communicated to the WMF in writing.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Office_actions" title="Wikipedia:Office actions">Office actions</a> are not permitted to be reversed by editors except by prior explicit office permission.</li> 
       <li>The English Wikipedia Arbitration Committee may issue binding decisions, within its <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration/Policy#Scope_and_responsibilities" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration/Policy">scope and responsibilities</a>, that override consensus. The committee has a noticeboard, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration/Requests/Amendment" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration/Requests/Amendment">Wikipedia:Arbitration/Requests/Amendment</a>, for requests that such decisions be amended, and may amend such decisions at any time.</li> 
       <li>Some matters that may seem subject to the consensus of the community at the English-language Wikipedia (<samp>en.wikipedia.org</samp>) are in a separate domain. In particular, the community of <a href="/wiki/MediaWiki" title="MediaWiki">MediaWiki</a> software developers, including both paid Wikimedia Foundation staff and volunteers, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikimedia_sister_projects" title="Wikipedia:Wikimedia sister projects">the sister wikis</a>, are largely separate entities. These independent, co-equal communities operate however they deem necessary or appropriate, such as adding, removing, or changing software features <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1015928993">.mw-parser-output div.crossreference{padding-left:0}</style><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable crossreference">(see <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Limits_to_configuration_changes" class="extiw" title="meta:Limits to configuration changes">meta:Limits to configuration changes</a>)</span>, or accepting or rejecting some contributions, even if their actions are not endorsed by editors here.</li>
      </ul>
