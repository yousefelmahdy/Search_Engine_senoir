          </table> <p><b>Numismatics</b> (ancient <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <b><span lang="grc" title="Ancient Greek (to 1453)-language text">???????????</span></b>) is the scientific study of <a href="/wiki/Money" title="Money">money</a> and its <a href="/wiki/History_of_money" title="History of money">history</a> in all its varied forms. While numismatists are often characterized as studying <a href="/wiki/Coin" title="Coin">coins</a>, the discipline also includes the study of <a href="/wiki/Banknotes" class="mw-redirect" title="Banknotes">banknotes</a> (<a href="/wiki/Notaphily" title="Notaphily">notaphily</a>), stock certificates (<a href="/wiki/Scripophily" title="Scripophily">scripophily</a>), <a href="/wiki/Medal" title="Medal">medals</a>, medallions, and <a href="/wiki/Token_coin" title="Token coin">token coins</a> (also referred to as <a href="/wiki/Exonumia" title="Exonumia">exonumia</a>). </p> <style                   <th style="padding:2px;"> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scope"><span style="color:darkred">Scope</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:WikiProject_Numismatics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Scope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> </th>
                 </tr> 
                 <tr> 
                  <td style="padding:2px 5px 5px;">This <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">WikiProject</a> aims to create articles relating to all kinds of coins, tokens, bills and modern forms of money, and to standardize the content and format of data in those articles. In addition, this WikiProject aims to provide some degree of damage control for poorly written or extraneous numismatic articles. <p>To create consensus on what should be included, provide factual information on all the world's past and present payment systems and create a consistent look and style for each type of page: Coins, paper money, tokens, medals and other forms of money. </p> </td>
