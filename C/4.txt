         <td id="mp-left" class="MainPageBG mp-bordered"> <h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2> 
          <div id="mp-tfa">
           <div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;"> 
            <div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 144px;"> <a href="/wiki/File:Laguna_del_Maule_-_NASA_Earth_Observatory.jpg" class="image" title="False-colour view of Laguna del Maule"><img alt="False-colour view of Laguna del Maule" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Laguna_del_Maule_-_NASA_Earth_Observatory.jpg/144px-Laguna_del_Maule_-_NASA_Earth_Observatory.jpg" decoding="async" width="144" height="137" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Laguna_del_Maule_-_NASA_Earth_Observatory.jpg/216px-Laguna_del_Maule_-_NASA_Earth_Observatory.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Laguna_del_Maule_-_NASA_Earth_Observatory.jpg/288px-Laguna_del_Maule_-_NASA_Earth_Observatory.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1281" data-file-height="1218"></a>
             <div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">
              False-colour view of Laguna del Maule

           <p><b><a href="/wiki/Laguna_del_Maule_(volcano)" title="Laguna del Maule (volcano)">Laguna del Maule</a></b> is a <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_field" title="Volcanic field">volcanic field</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> mountain range of Chile. The volcanic field covers an area of 500 square kilometres (190&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi) and features at least 130 <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanic vents</a>. The field gets its name from <a href="/wiki/Laguna_del_Maule" title="Laguna del Maule">Laguna del Maule</a>, a lake surrounded by <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_cone" title="Volcanic cone">cones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lava_dome" title="Lava dome">lava domes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lava_dome#Lava_coulées" title="Lava dome">lava coulees</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lava" title="Lava">lava flows</a>. The field's volcanic activity began 1.5&nbsp;million years ago during the <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a>. Three major <a href="/wiki/Caldera" title="Caldera">caldera</a>-forming eruptions took place in the volcanic field prior to the <a href="/wiki/Last_Glacial_Period" title="Last Glacial Period">last glacial period</a>. Volcanic activity has increased during the postglacial era. In pre-Columbian times, the field was a regionally important source of <a href="/wiki/Obsidian" title="Obsidian">obsidian</a>. Today <a href="/wiki/Geothermal_gradient" title="Geothermal gradient">geothermal</a> phenomena occur at Laguna del Maule. Between 2004 and 2007, <a href="/wiki/Bradyseism" title="Bradyseism">ground inflation</a> began in the volcanic field, indicating the intrusion of a <a href="/wiki/Sill_(geology)" title="Sill (geology)">sill</a> beneath it. The rate of inflation is faster than those measured on other inflating volcanoes, which has created concern about the potential for impending large-scale eruptive activity. (<b><a href="/wiki/Laguna_del_Maule_(volcano)" title="Laguna del Maule (volcano)">Full&nbsp;article...</a></b>) </p>
