        <div class="glue-expansion-panel-toggle" data-glue-expansion-panel-toggle-for="panel-6108679796162560"> 
         <h3 class="glue-expansion-panel__button-header"> How much does Advanced Protection cost? </h3> 
         <svg alt="Expand" viewbox="0 0 24 24" role="img" aria-hidden="true" class="glue-icon glue-expansion-panel__button-arrow"> <path d="M5.41 7.59L4 9l8 8 8-8-1.41-1.41L12 14.17"></path> 
         </svg> 
        </div> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel-content" id="panel-6108679796162560"> 
         <div class="markdown-block"> 
          <p>The Advanced Protection Program is a free service. However, you may need to <a href="https://store.google.com/us/product/titan_security_key" target="_blank">purchase a security key</a> if you don’t have one.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="glue-expansion-panel"> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel-toggle" data-glue-expansion-panel-toggle-for="panel-5347520944013312"> 
         <h3 class="glue-expansion-panel__button-header"> Do I need any special hardware or software? </h3> 
         <svg alt="Expand" viewbox="0 0 24 24" role="img" aria-hidden="true" class="glue-icon glue-expansion-panel__button-arrow"> <path d="M5.41 7.59L4 9l8 8 8-8-1.41-1.41L12 14.17"></path> 
         </svg> 
        </div> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel-content" id="panel-5347520944013312"> 
         <div class="markdown-block"> 
          <p>Yes, you should have two security keys, one of which can be your phone’s built-in key. You’ll need to have at least one security key with you when you enroll.</p> 
          <p>If your phone is running Android 7 or later, or iOS 10.0 or later with the <a href="https://apps.apple.com/us/app/google-smart-lock/id1152066360" target="_blank">Google Smart Lock</a> app, you can use your phone’s built-in security key as your primary key. There’s no extra cost for using it. You should also purchase an external physical key as a backup. </p> 
          <p>If your phone can’t be used as a security key, you will need to purchase a physical key before you enroll in Advanced Protection. You should still order a second physical security key as your backup.</p> 
          <p>The price of security keys varies by model. You can purchase <a href="https://store.google.com/us/product/titan_security_key" target="_blank">Google’s Titan Security Key</a> or any key that supports the FIDO open standard. You’ll also have an opportunity to view recommended keys during enrollment.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="glue-expansion-panel"> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel-toggle" data-glue-expansion-panel-toggle-for="panel-4817247823200256">

         <svg alt="Expand" viewbox="0 0 24 24" role="img" aria-hidden="true" class="glue-icon glue-expansion-panel__button-arrow"> <path d="M5.41 7.59L4 9l8 8 8-8-1.41-1.41L12 14.17"></path> 
         </svg> 
        </div> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel-content" id="panel-4817247823200256"> 
         <div class="markdown-block"> 
          <p>Yes. Add an <a href="https://myaccount.google.com/recovery/email" target="_blank">email</a> and <a href="https://myaccount.google.com/signinoptions/rescuephone" target="_blank">phone number</a> to your Google Account, so it’s easy to recover if you get locked out. </p> 
          <p>iPhone users </p>
          <ul> 
           <li>Make sure you have the <a href="https://apps.apple.com/us/app/google-smart-lock/id1152066360" target="_blank">Google Smart Lock</a> app installed. You’ll need this during enrollment. </li> 
           <li>Turn on <a href="https://accounts.google.com/signin/v2/challenge/pwd?continue=https%3A%2F%2Fmyaccount.google.com%2Fu%2F1%2Fsigninoptions%2Ftwo-step-verification%3Frapt%3DAEjHL4O3VAyXq9uZpqFzUwPD70z7jXL4rooB5EKDp27E0H_tIFNTt51jRv6lafL2FWEdzuJQj8ws3xjvBQl7bnEMl4NOs8UXyg&amp;service=accountsettings&amp;osid=1&amp;rart=ANgoxcchGuNoK6GRp1ya_0kcH7ME-MMuMVP37hdrsEBbsGrQJSyqBr9I5e3lrHUAsZjGGnsKIvDtbgqAmiNFYce_5Z4XtMI8iw&amp;TL=AM3QAYZYdTRvASRP4xre969zWChiD6AcJP8Gb6enWFCJuybGKBEGD06lAZtyj2q3&amp;flowName=GlifWebSignIn&amp;cid=1&amp;flowEntry=ServiceLogin" target="_blank">2-Step Verification</a> in your Google Account.</li> 
          </ul>

         <svg alt="Expand" viewbox="0 0 24 24" role="img" aria-hidden="true" class="glue-icon glue-expansion-panel__button-arrow"> <path d="M5.41 7.59L4 9l8 8 8-8-1.41-1.41L12 14.17"></path> 
         </svg> 
        </div> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel-content" id="panel-5693054502305792"> 
         <div class="markdown-block"> 
          <p>Most of your day-to-day account activity will not feel different. </p>
          <ul> 
           <li> The biggest change will be that one of your security keys will be required when you sign in to your account on a new device (or sign in on your phone after signing out). The most convenient security key is the one built into your phone. </li> 
           <li> You may find you receive more alerts or warnings before downloading a file or installing an app. Strong protections against malware are built into all Google products, such as Google Play Store and Google Chrome, but Advanced Protection performs even more stringent checks. </li> 
           <li> A number of account security features that were optional will be automatically turned on and kept on for your account. </li> 
          </ul>

      <h3 class="glue-headline glue-headline--headline-3 glue-mod-font-weight-medium"> Using security keys </h3> 
     </div>

   </section>
   <section class="_expansion-panel-4 fade-in fade-in-init glue-page expandable-panel glue-mod-spacer--top
    glue-mod-spacer--bottom"> 
    <div class="glue-grid"> 
     <div class="glue-grid__col glue-grid__col--span-1"></div> 
     <div class="glue-grid__col glue-grid__col--span-10"> 
      <div class="glue-expansion-panels" data-glue-expansion-panels-key="_expansion-panel-4"> 
       <div class="glue-expansion-panels__header"> 
        <h2 class="glue-eyebrow"></h2> <button class="glue-expansion-panels-toggle-all
              glue-button glue-button--low-emphasis glue-button--icon-right"> <span class="glue-expansion-panels__toggle-text
                glue-expansion-panels__toggle-text--expanded"> Expand all </span> <span class="glue-expansion-panels__toggle-text
                glue-expansion-panels__toggle-text--collapsed"> Collapse all </span> 
         <svg alt="Expand all" id="expand-all" viewbox="0 0 24 24" role="img" aria-hidden="true" class="glue-icon"> <path d="M16.59 9.41L18 8l-6-6-6 6 1.41 1.41L12 4.83l4.59 4.58zM12
                  19.17l-4.59-4.58L6 16l6 6 6-6-1.41-1.41L12 19.17z"></path> 
         </svg> </button> 
       </div> 
       <div class="glue-expansion-panel"> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel-toggle" data-glue-expansion-panel-toggle-for="panel-6045911197941760"> 
         <h3 class="glue-expansion-panel__button-header"> What’s a security key and how do I get one? How many do I need? </h3> 
         <svg alt="Expand" viewbox="0 0 24 24" role="img" aria-hidden="true" class="glue-icon glue-expansion-panel__button-arrow"> <path d="M5.41 7.59L4 9l8 8 8-8-1.41-1.41L12 14.17"></path> 
         </svg> 
        </div> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel-content" id="panel-6045911197941760"> 
         <div class="markdown-block"> 
          <p>A security key can be built into your phone, or can be an external physical key, just as you would have for your house or car. Whether it’s your phone or an external physical key, you have an object in your possession that a hacker isn’t likely to have, even if they know your password. </p> 
          <p>Advanced Protection requires a security key when you sign in to your account on a new device (or on your phone, if you sign out and then back in again). Accounts that require keys are much safer from attacks than accounts that only require a password.</p> 
          <p>You’ll need at least one security key to enroll in Advanced Protection, and we strongly recommend adding a second key as a backup during, or very soon after enrollment.</p> 
          <p><b>The security key built into your phone</b></p> 
          <p>The most convenient security key is the one built into your phone — software that allows your phone to act as a security key. We recommend this as your primary account key. </p> 
          <p>This built-in key can detect when the device you’re signing in on is nearby, which means it’s likely that you’re the one signing in. Unauthorized users might be able to get your password, but are much less likely to have your phone nearby when they try to sign in to your account from another device. </p> 
          <p><b>External physical keys</b></p> 
          <p>Your second, backup key will be an external one. This key will keep you from getting locked out of your account if you lose your phone (or other primary key).</p> 
          <p>There are many kinds of external keys. You might plug a USB key into your phone to sign in, keep a Bluetooth key nearby, or hold an NFC key close to your phone when you sign in.</p> 
          <p>You can purchase <a href="https://store.google.com/product/titan_security_key" target="_blank">Google’s Titan Security Key</a> or any key that supports the FIDO open standard. You’ll also have an opportunity to view recommended keys during enrollment. </p> 
          <p>If you don’t have a phone running Android 7 or later, or iOS 10.0 or later, you won’t be able to use your phone as one of your security keys. You’ll need to purchase an external key as your primary key in order to enroll. You should still order a second one as your backup.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="glue-expansion-panel"> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel-toggle" data-glue-expansion-panel-toggle-for="panel-6057506670116864"> 
         <h3 class="glue-expansion-panel__button-header"> Will I need to keep my key with me all the time? What if I lose it? </h3> 
         <svg alt="Expand" viewbox="0 0 24 24" role="img" aria-hidden="true" class="glue-icon glue-expansion-panel__button-arrow"> <path d="M5.41 7.59L4 9l8 8 8-8-1.41-1.41L12 14.17"></path> 
         </svg> 
        </div> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel-content" id="panel-6057506670116864"> 
         <div class="markdown-block"> 
          <p>You’ll need your security key for signing in to your <a href="http://account.google.com" target="_blank">Google Account</a> on a new device, or in case you sign out of your account on your phone and then need to sign back in. This may not happen often, but you’ll want your key handy when it does. Remember to take it with you when you travel.</p> 
          <p>If you lose your key and are still signed in on one of your devices, visit account.google.com to add or replace a key. Otherwise, <a href="https://accounts.google.com/signin/recovery" target="_blank">submit a request to recover your account</a>. Google may take a few days to verify it’s you and restore your access.</p> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
       </div>
