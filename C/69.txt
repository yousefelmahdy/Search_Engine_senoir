      <p><b>Requests for adminship</b> (<b>RfA</b>) is the process by which the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedians" title="Wikipedia:Wikipedians">Wikipedia community</a> decides who will become <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators" title="Wikipedia:Administrators">administrators</a> (also known as admins or sysops), who are users with access to additional technical features that aid in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Maintenance" title="Wikipedia:Maintenance">maintenance</a>. Users can either submit their own requests for adminship (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SELFNOM" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SELFNOM">self-nomination</a>) or may be nominated by other users. Please be familiar with the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators%27_reading_list" title="Wikipedia:Administrators' reading list">administrators' reading list</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators%27_how-to_guide" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Administrators' how-to guide">how-to guide</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Guide_to_requests_for_adminship" title="Wikipedia:Guide to requests for adminship">guide to requests for adminship</a> before submitting your request. Also, consider <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requests_for_adminship/Optional_RfA_candidate_poll" title="Wikipedia:Requests for adminship/Optional RfA candidate poll">asking the community</a> about your chances of passing an RfA. </p>
      <p>This page also hosts <b><a href="#About_RfB">requests for bureaucratship</a></b> (<b>RfB</b>), where new <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Bureaucrats" title="Wikipedia:Bureaucrats">bureaucrats</a> are selected. </p>
      <p>If you are new to participating in a request for adminship, or are not sure how to gauge the candidate, then kindly go through <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Advice_for_RfA_voters" title="Wikipedia:Advice for RfA voters">this mini guide for RfA voters</a> before you participate. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="About_administrators">About administrators</span></h2> 
      <p>The additional features granted to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators" title="Wikipedia:Administrators">administrators</a> are considered to require a high level of trust from the community. While administrative actions are <a href="/wiki/Special:Log" title="Special:Log">publicly logged</a> and can be reverted by other administrators just as other edits can be, the actions of administrators involve features that can affect the entire site. Among other functions, administrators are responsible for blocking users from editing, controlling page protection, and deleting pages. However, they are not the final arbiters in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Dispute_resolution" title="Wikipedia:Dispute resolution">content disputes</a> and do not have special powers to decide on content matters, except to enforce the community consensus and the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration">Arbitration Commitee</a> rulings by protecting or deleting pages and applying sanctions to users. </p>

      <p>The community grants administrator access to trusted users, so nominees should have been on Wikipedia long enough for people to determine whether they are trustworthy. Administrators are held to high standards of conduct because other editors often turn to them for help and advice, and because they have access to tools that can have a negative impact on users or content if carelessly applied. </p> 
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1023754711">.mw-parser-output .vanchor>:target~.vanchor-text{background-color:#b1d2ff}</style><span class="vanchor"><span id="Nomination_standards"></span><span class="vanchor-text">Nomination standards</span></span>
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        The only formal prerequisite for adminship is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Why_create_an_account%3F" title="Wikipedia:Why create an account?">having an account</a> on Wikipedia. However, the RfA page is restricted to editing from non <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_access_levels#Extendedconfirmed" title="Wikipedia:User access levels">extended confirmed</a> users, so editors without an extended confirmed account may have their RfA subpage transcluded by someone who is. This is due to the community deeming that editors without the requisite experience (500 edits and 30 days of experience) are generally unlikely to succeed at gaining adminship.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The community looks for a variety of factors in candidates and discussion can be intense. For examples of what the community is looking for, you could review some <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Successful_requests_for_adminship" title="Wikipedia:Successful requests for adminship">successful</a> and some <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Unsuccessful_adminship_candidacies_(Chronological)" title="Wikipedia:Unsuccessful adminship candidacies (Chronological)">unsuccessful</a> RfAs, or start a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ORCP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ORCP">RfA candidate poll</a>.
       </dd> 
       <dd>
        If you are unsure about nominating yourself or another user for adminship, you may first wish to consult a few editors you respect to get an idea of what the community might think of your request. There is also a list of editors <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Request_an_RfA_nomination" title="Wikipedia:Request an RfA nomination">willing to consider</a> nominating you. Editors interested in becoming administrators might explore <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Adopt-a-user" title="Wikipedia:Adopt-a-user">adoption</a> by a more experienced user to gain experience. They may also add themselves to <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_administrator_hopefuls" title="Category:Wikipedia administrator hopefuls">Category:Wikipedia administrator hopefuls</a>; a list of names and some additional information are automatically maintained at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_administrator_hopefuls" title="Wikipedia:List of administrator hopefuls">Wikipedia:List of administrator hopefuls</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Guide_to_requests_for_adminship" title="Wikipedia:Guide to requests for adminship">RfA guide</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Miniguide_to_requests_for_adminship" title="Wikipedia:Miniguide to requests for adminship">miniguide</a> might be helpful, while <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Advice_for_RfA_candidates" title="Wikipedia:Advice for RfA candidates">Advice for RfA candidates</a> will let you evaluate whether or not you are ready to be an admin.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1023754711"><span class="vanchor"><span id="Nominations"></span><span class="vanchor-text">Nominations</span></span>
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        To nominate either yourself or another user for adminship, follow <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requests_for_adminship/Nominate" title="Wikipedia:Requests for adminship/Nominate">these instructions</a>.</b> If you wish to nominate someone else, check with them <u>before</u> making the nomination page. Nominations may only be added by the candidate or after the candidate has signed the acceptance of the nomination.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        <span class="anchor" id="Canvassing"></span>Notice of RfA
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        Some candidates display the <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:RfX-notice" title="Template:RfX-notice">RfX-notice</a>}}</code> on their userpages. Also, per <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:2015_administrator_election_reform/Phase_II/RfC" title="Wikipedia:2015 administrator election reform/Phase II/RfC">community consensus</a>, RfAs are to be advertised on <a href="/wiki/MediaWiki:Watchlist-messages" title="MediaWiki:Watchlist-messages">MediaWiki:Watchlist-messages</a> and <a href="/wiki/Template:Centralized_discussion" title="Template:Centralized discussion">Template:Centralized discussion</a>. The watchlist notice will only be visible to you if your user interface language is set to (plain) <code>en</code>.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1023754711"><span class="vanchor"><span id="Expressing_opinions"></span><span class="vanchor-text">Expressing opinions</span></span>
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        All Wikipedians—including those without an account or not logged in ("anons")—are welcome to comment and ask questions in an RfA but numerical (#) "votes" in the Support, Oppose, and Neutral sections may only be placed by editors while logged in to their account. 
        <br>If you are relatively new to contributing to Wikipedia, or if you have not yet participated on many RfAs, please consider first reading "<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Advice_for_RfA_voters" title="Wikipedia:Advice for RfA voters">Advice for RfA voters</a>".
       </dd> 
       <dd>
        There is a limit of two questions per editor, with <i>relevant</i> follow-ups permitted. The two-question limit cannot be circumvented by asking questions that require multiple answers (e.g. asking the candidate what they would do in each of five scenarios). The candidate may respond to the comments of others. Certain comments may be discounted if there are suspicions of fraud; these may be the contributions of very new editors, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Sock_puppetry" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Sock puppetry">sockpuppets</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Sock_puppetry#Meatpuppetry" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Sock puppetry">meatpuppets</a>. Please explain your opinion by including a short explanation of your reasoning. Your input (positive or negative) will carry more weight if supported by evidence.
       </dd> 
       <dd>
        To add a comment, click the "Voice your opinion" link for the candidate. <b>Always <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Civility" title="Wikipedia:Civility">be respectful</a></b> towards others in your comments. Constructive criticism will help the candidate make proper adjustments and possibly fare better in a future RfA attempt. However, bureaucrats have been authorized by the community to clerk at RfA, so they may appropriately deal with comments and/or !votes which they deem to be inappropriate. You may wish to review <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arguments_to_avoid_in_adminship_discussions" title="Wikipedia:Arguments to avoid in adminship discussions">arguments to avoid in adminship discussions</a>. <span class="anchor" id="irrelevant"></span>Irrelevant questions may be removed or ignored, so please stay on topic.
       </dd> 
       <dd>
        The RfA process attracts many Wikipedians and some may routinely oppose many or most requests; other editors routinely support many or most requests. Although the community currently endorses the right of every Wikipedian with an account to participate, one-sided approaches to RfA voting have been labeled as "<a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/What_is_a_troll%3F" class="extiw" title="m:What is a troll?">trolling</a>" by some. Before commenting, or responding to comments, in an RfA (especially Oppose comments with uncommon rationales or which feel like "baiting") consider whether others are likely to treat it as influential, and whether RfA is an appropriate forum for your point. Try hard not to fan the fire. Remember, the bureaucrats who close discussions have considerable experience and give more weight to constructive comments than unproductive ones.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        <span class="anchor" id="Decision_process"></span>Discussion, decision, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Bureaucrats#Promotions_and_RfX_closures" title="Wikipedia:Bureaucrats">closing procedures</a>
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        Most nominations will remain active for a minimum of seven days from the time the nomination is posted on this page, during which users give their opinions, ask questions, and make comments. This discussion process is not a vote (it is sometimes referred to as a !vote, using the computer science <a href="/wiki/Negation" title="Negation">negation</a> symbol). At the end of the discussion period, a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Bureaucrats" title="Wikipedia:Bureaucrats">bureaucrat</a> will review the discussion to see whether there is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a> for promotion.
       </dd> 
       <dd>
        Consensus at RfA is not determined by surpassing a numerical threshold, but by the strength of rationales presented. In practice, most RfAs above 75% support pass. In December 2015 the community <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:2015_administrator_election_reform/Phase_II/RfC#C1:_Expand_discretionary_range_to_65%" title="Wikipedia:2015 administrator election reform/Phase II/RfC">determined</a> that <i>in general</i>, RfAs that finish between 65 and 75% support are subject to the discretion of bureaucrats (so, therefore, almost all RfAs below 65% will fail). However, it must be noted that a request for adminship is first and foremost, a <b>consensus-building process</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In calculating an RfA's percentage, only numbered Support and Oppose comments are considered. Neutral comments are ignored for calculating an RfA's percentage, but they (and other relevant information) are considered for determining consensus by the closing bureaucrat. In nominations where consensus is unclear, detailed explanations behind Support or Oppose comments will have more impact than positions with no explanations or simple comments such as "yep" and "no way".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
       </dd> 
       <dd>
        A nomination may be closed as successful only by bureaucrats. In exceptional circumstances, bureaucrats may extend RfAs beyond seven days or restart the nomination to make consensus clearer. They may also close nominations early if success is unlikely and leaving the application open has no likely benefit, and the candidate may withdraw their application at any time for any reason. If uncontroversial, any user in good standing can close a request that has no chance of passing in accordance with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Snowball_clause" title="Wikipedia:Snowball clause">WP:SNOW</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Not_now" title="Wikipedia:Not now">WP:NOTNOW</a>. RfAs with not even the slightest chance to pass per <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTNOW" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTNOW">WP:NOTNOW</a> can be tagged and deleted under <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CSD#G6" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CSD">WP:CSD#G6</a>. Do not close any requests that you have taken part in, or those that have even a slim chance of passing, unless you are the candidate and you are withdrawing your application. In the case of vandalism, improper formatting, or a declined or withdrawn nomination, non-bureaucrats may also delist a nomination. A list of procedures to close an RfA may be found at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Bureaucrats#Promotions_and_RfX_closures" title="Wikipedia:Bureaucrats">WP:Bureaucrats</a>.
       </dd> 
       <dd>
        If your nomination fails, then please wait for a reasonable period of time before renominating yourself or accepting another nomination. Some candidates have tried again and succeeded within three months, but many editors prefer to wait considerably longer before reapplying.
       </dd>

Trialpears has distinguished themselves as a highly competent technical editor, running a <a href="/wiki/User:PearBOT" title="User:PearBOT">bot</a> and with numerous contributions to project templates. You can see they also know how to create content that is useful for our readers with their work at <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_Human_Development_Index" title="List of countries by Human Development Index">List of countries by Human Development Index</a>. As good as all that is, what has stood out to me the most is their ability to keep a cool head even during contentious discussions and their willingness to be helpful to new and experienced editors. All of this combines into someone who is ready to help close and implement discussions at the understaffed <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Templates_for_discussion" title="Wikipedia:Templates for discussion">Wikipedia:Templates for discussion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Categories_for_discussion" title="Wikipedia:Categories for discussion">Wikipedia:Categories for discussion</a>. I hope you will join me in supporting them. <a href="/wiki/User:Barkeep49" title="User:Barkeep49">Barkeep49</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Barkeep49" title="User talk:Barkeep49">talk</a>) 00:57, 4 June 2021 (UTC) </p>

      <p>I came across Trialpears through the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TFD">WP:TFD</a> process, where they steadily built up a reputation as someone who knows how to read consensus of a discussion. They are also technically proficient, from implementing complex template mergers to writing and running <a href="/wiki/User:PearBOT" title="User:PearBOT">PearBOT</a> for more automated processes. As indicated by Barkeep49, I will often see Trialpears as a calm voice in the storm of TFD, giving well-thought-out opinions about the suitability (or lack thereof) of a template that has been nominated for deletion. This also indicates a solid ability to recognize the times when it is more important to join in the conversation with your opinion than it is to "push the buttons" and perform administrative tasks (i.e. I see them joining discussions just as often as closing them). As someone who gained the mop working in the more back-water areas like TFD and bot requests, I am happy to see another editor rise up and show interest and skill in these areas. <a href="/wiki/User:Primefac" title="User:Primefac">Primefac</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Primefac" title="User talk:Primefac">talk</a>) 11:02, 4 June 2021 (UTC) </p>

      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Questions_for_the_candidate">Questions for the candidate</span></h4> 
      <p>Dear candidate, thank you for offering to serve Wikipedia as an administrator. Please answer these questions to provide guidance for participants: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <b>1.</b> What administrative work do you intend to take part in? 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <b>A:</b> I have long been a regular at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TFD">templates for discussion</a> and expect much of my administrative work to be in this area. Apart from the obvious task of closing discussions, this also includes implementing TfD decisions. That may involve deletion, updating transclusions on fully protected pages, and protecting <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:HRT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:HRT">high-risk templates</a>. I also help out with other technical work. Sometimes more niche parts of the toolset can come in handy then, such as moving pages using <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AWB" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AWB">AutoWikiBrowser</a>, noratelimit, and editing the MediaWiki namespace.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          Another task that I hope to get more involved in is closing <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CFD">categories for discussions</a>. This is something I already do from time to time when the backlog gets really bad. However, since non-admins don't have access to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Categories_for_discussion/Working" title="Wikipedia:Categories for discussion/Working">Wikipedia:Categories for discussion/Working</a> and the associated bot, the process is a lot slower and more cumbersome than if I were an admin. With the bit, I would be able to focus on the part I find enjoyable: Determining consensus and not menial bot work.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          I also intend to help out with other technical requests (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:HISTMERGE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:HISTMERGE">history merging</a>) but I have no idea what are things I will enjoy doing regularly and what I will just try once or twice.
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <b>2.</b> What are your best contributions to Wikipedia, and why? 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <b>A:</b> My featured list candidate <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_Human_Development_Index" title="List of countries by Human Development Index">List of countries by Human Development Index</a> is the thing I'm the proudest of. Giving lots of people an accurate, well-developed, and well-presented article on such an important topic is something I take a lot of pride in.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          I'm also quite fond of the hundreds of thousands of short descriptions I've generated (using bots and templates) for biographies, schools, drugs, awards, and many other topics. This is a not-insignificant fraction of all our articles which is quite insane to think about.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          The thing I've spent the most time and effort with, though, is helping TfD run smoothly by closing hundreds of discussions and implementing many of our larger decisions. One worth mentioning would be the {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Aircraft_specs" title="Template:Aircraft specs">Aircraft specs</a>}} mergers which required a gargantuan multi-year effort, but it was well worth it since it added proper unit conversions, fixed broken parameters, and made the specifications follow our manual of style. For a few hundred articles, it even involved complete rewrites, data verification, or unhiding of incorrectly formatted specifications. I've also been a driving force for simplifying and improving our archiving procedures with it now, for example, being possible to get a great archive box by just putting {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Archives" title="Template:Archives">Archives</a>}} on a talk page without configuration by automatically choosing parameters based on various factors.
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <b>3.</b> Have you been in any conflicts over editing in the past or have other users caused you stress? How have you dealt with it and how will you deal with it in the future? 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <b>A:</b> As for everyone involved in as many discussions as I've been, there have been lots of fierce debates, but almost always these have been about a single template or technical detail with no stress or conflict involved.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          There have however been a couple of times where editing has gotten stressful. The one that immediately comes to mind was back in 2019 where I had many unpleasant interactions with a good faith IP editor involving {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Infobox_settlement" title="Template:Infobox settlement">Infobox settlement</a>}} wrappers.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          In cases like that, I take a step back, remember that there are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:MASTODON" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:MASTODON">no angry mastodons</a>, and give any reply a few hours consideration time before publishing it. That usually results in a well-formulated response that deescalates the situation. I also try to focus on the substance of any comments instead of incivilities or off-topic matters. In the case of a not-so-civil TfD comment, a reply may look something like: "I didn't consider that case, thanks for bringing it up! I would handle that as follows: ..." I think that's a lot more likely to result in fruitful discussion than a quick quip.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          I also find it useful to remember that we all are working towards a common goal of improving the encyclopedia. If someone donates lots of their free time I can be fairly certain they have their heart in the right place, even if I may not agree with them or they express their thoughts in an aggressive way.
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </dd>
      </dl>

Not following any of the points laid out in these sections (as well as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:INVOLVED" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:INVOLVED">WP:INVOLVED</a>) would betray the community's trust and open up for severe issues such as poor actions going unchallenged/unresolved or a group of admins having undue influence over an area with concerns not being addressed or met with incivilities. --<a href="/wiki/User:Trialpears" title="User:Trialpears">Trialpears</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Trialpears" title="User talk:Trialpears">talk</a>) 16:57, 5 June 2021 (UTC)

        <b>5.</b> How would you deal with personal attacks, vandalism or disruptive editing 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <b>A:</b> I expect to only rarely deal with this kind of issues and only in a portion of cases actually use any admin buttons as I'm not especially experienced dealing with personal attacks, vandalism or disruptive editing.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          If the issue is very serious and unambiguous such as porn-image vandalism, major ongoing vandalism sprees or other <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Zero_tolerance" title="Wikipedia:Zero tolerance">zero tolerance</a> behavior I would issue a block with a suitable duration considering possible collateral and IP changes for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLOCKIP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLOCKIP">IP blocks</a>. For other vandalism I may issue a block after making sure they were properly warned. In not so clear cut cases I would not issue blocks. Instead I would try to communicate with or warn the editor and then, if it's clear that doesn't resolve the issue, report the editor to a relevant noticeboard such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AIV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AIV">AIV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ANI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ANI">ANI</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AN3" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AN3">AN3</a> depending on issue.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          Blocks, warnings and discussion is of course not the only tools for dealing with disruption with page protection, edit filters and blacklists also being good choices in certain situations. If the case for protection is particularly clear I may implement a conservative page protection, but in many cases I would probably hand it over to our experienced admins at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RFPP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RFPP">RFPP</a>. Same goes with other areas I am not familiar with.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          It is also important not to act when <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:INVOLVED" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:INVOLVED">involved</a> which probably is most often relevant for personal attacks. If I were the subject of a personal attack I would deal with the situation as I outlined in question 3.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          My response may of course change with time and experience, but that is how I would handle it currently. --<a href="/wiki/User:Trialpears" title="User:Trialpears">Trialpears</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Trialpears" title="User talk:Trialpears">talk</a>) 19:33, 5 June 2021 (UTC)
         </dd>

        <b>6.</b> As a technical editor, would you consider helping to implement fully-protected edit requests, particularly on high-use templates? 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <b>A:</b> I would, implementing template-protected edit requests is already something I do at times and I expect I would do the same for fully-protected edit requests at technical pages. There are two main things that should be ensured before any such edit is made: That the change is technically sound (usually using <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TESTCASES" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TESTCASES">testcases</a>) and that there is consensus for the change. In many cases this may involve waiting to allow for additional input or advertising the discussion at relevant discussion pages. --<a href="/wiki/User:Trialpears" title="User:Trialpears">Trialpears</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Trialpears" title="User talk:Trialpears">talk</a>) 19:41, 5 June 2021 (UTC)
         </dd>

       <dd>
        <b>7.</b> A template editor makes a change to a template-protected template with tens of thousands of transclusions. You disagree with the change, and believe it needs discussion, so you revert it. The template editor reinstates it without discussing. What do you do? (you believe said change is misguided enough that it remaining on thousands of pages is a serious problem - but it's <i>not</i> vandalism) 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <b>A:</b> Given that I reverted it I would need to have had a good cause for it per <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TPEDISPUTE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TPEDISPUTE">WP:TPEDISPUTE</a>, presumably something like the change breaking a significant number of transclusions or otherwise having a large impact on pages. The first thing I would do in this situation is making sure they haven't tried communicating through edit summaries, on another talk pages or something else. Seeing a template editor behave like this would be very out of the norm with it being one of the most vetted user rights. Assuming they haven't made any attempt at communication I would write a message on the talk page (with a ping) explaining why I believed this edit to be harmful and that they had violated <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TPEDISPUTE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TPEDISPUTE">WP:TPEDISPUTE</a> since 
          <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r989825325">.mw-parser-output .inline-quote-talk{font-family:Georgia,"DejaVu Serif",serif;color:#008560;quotes:none}.mw-parser-output .inline-quote-talk-italic{font-family:inherit;font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output .inline-quote-talk-marks{quotes:"\"""\""}</style><q class="inline-quote-talk">When a template editor's edit is reversed by a peer, the edit (or a similar one) must not be reinstated by the original or another template editor without clear discussion leading to a consensus decision</q> which isn't the case here. I would also explain that if they reinstate again without any discussion I would take away the template editor bit for the time being citing <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TPEREVOKE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TPEREVOKE">WP:TPEREVOKE</a> criteria 1 as a second edit would make 
          <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r989825325"><q class="inline-quote-talk">a pattern of performing obviously controversial edits to protected templates without first determining consensus.</q>.
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          If it comes to this point the account being compromised wouldn't be out of the question and otherwise the editor had clearly acted in a way that is unacceptable for a template editor. Both possibilities carry a significant risk of disruption to the encyclopedia. I would also report the incident at the administrators noticeboard, both to make sure my behavior was appropriate and to see if other interventions are necessary. I very much hope to never have to do something like this. --<a href="/wiki/User:Trialpears" title="User:Trialpears">Trialpears</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Trialpears" title="User talk:Trialpears">talk</a>) 20:07, 5 June 2021 (UTC)
         </dd>

      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Nomination_2">Nomination</span></h4> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/User:Vami_IV" title="User:Vami IV">Vami&nbsp;IV</a>&nbsp;(<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Vami_IV" title="User talk:Vami IV">talk</a>&nbsp;<b>·</b> <a href="/wiki/Special:Contributions/Vami_IV" title="Special:Contributions/Vami IV">contribs</a>)&nbsp;– As an editor with over six years of activity across mainspace and the back end alike, Vami IV is long overdue for a nomination for adminship. His content work is prolific: Vami is responsible for creating 242 articles, 164 of which are biographies of women under the auspices of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Women_in_Red" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Women in Red">Women In Red</a>. He has expanded numerous others with well-researched and encyclopedic content, particularly stubs for the long-running <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:The_50,000_Destubbing_Challenge" title="Wikipedia:The 50,000 Destubbing Challenge">50,000 Destubbing Challenge</a>. </p>
      <p>On the back end, Vami is a born collaborator who works well with others, an essential skill for any admin. His <a href="/wiki/User:GA_bot/Stats" title="User:GA bot/Stats">229 GA reviews</a>, many nominations of others for Editor of the Week, efforts to educate users interested in helping out at the highly technical <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CCI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CCI">contributor copyright investigations</a> area, and work as coordinator of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GERMANY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:GERMANY">WikiProject Germany</a> all bear witness to this. I hope you'll agree with me that Vami will be a strong addition to the admin corps. ?<a href="/wiki/User:Premeditated_Chaos" title="User:Premeditated Chaos">PMC</a>? <a href="/wiki/User_talk:Premeditated_Chaos" title="User talk:Premeditated Chaos">(talk)</a> 03:54, 31 May 2021 (UTC) </p>

      <p>I am absolutely delighted to introduce Vami IV to the community as a candidate for adminship. Vami has been with us for almost six and a half years, and in that time has produced over 80,000 contributions. They have been continually active since 2016 and are a fantastic contributer, working on articles up to FA class, such as <a href="/wiki/Fort_Concho" title="Fort Concho">Fort Concho</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ludwigsburg_Palace" title="Ludwigsburg Palace">Ludwigsburg Palace</a> with 25,000 edits to mainspace. Their skills in content creation is exemplified by having a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TC">Triple Crown</a>, and won the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:EOTW" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:EOTW">Editor of the week</a> award in 2019. </p>
      <p>However, Vami is much more than just a content creator. They work in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CCI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CCI">WP:CCI</a>, somewhere where the toolset is incredibly helpful, for revision deletion as well as handling blocks. I very much trust Vami with handling themselves with decorum in discussions, and have no qualms with them having the toolset. I hope you’ll agree that Vami IV is a fantastic editor that would benefit from the toolset.         <i>Candidate, please indicate acceptance of the nomination here:</i> I accept these nominations, and thank their authors for writing them. So I reaffirm this: I do not see adminship as something owed to experienced editors, but something a suitable, experienced, and motivated editor owes to the project. –<a href="/wiki/User:Vami_IV" title="User:Vami IV"><span style="background:crimson; color:white; padding:2px;">?Vami</span></a><a href="/wiki/User_talk:Vami_IV" title="User talk:Vami IV"><span style="background:#e2e2e2; color:crimson; padding:2px;">_IV†?</span></a> 04:45, 31 May 2021 (UTC)

      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Questions_for_the_candidate_2">Questions for the candidate</span></h4> 
      <p>Dear candidate, thank you for offering to serve Wikipedia as an administrator. Please answer these questions to provide guidance for participants: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <b>1.</b> What administrative work do you intend to take part in? 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <b>A:</b> I intend to be a copyright admin, investigating and cleaning up copyright violations. I understand that there is also a deficit of admins at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AIV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AIV">WP:AIV</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PERM" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PERM">WP:PERM</a>, but my focus, especially while I get my sea legs, will be copyright cleanup.
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <b>2.</b> What are your best contributions to Wikipedia, and why? 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <b>A:</b> The easy answer would be the two Featured Articles, five Good Articles, and Did You Knows that earned me my <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CROWN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CROWN">Triple Crowns</a>. There's also my participation in Women in Red, the contests run by Encyclopaedius, or my GAN reviews. But my answer is the article <a href="/wiki/Hololive_Production" title="Hololive Production">Hololive Production</a>. I ordinarily wouldn't be proud of a C-class. As experienced editors know, a C-class is a job half done. But I am proud of the story behind it. A handful of editors, and a community of volunteer fan translators built that article from scratch, from Japanese-language media. It still has problems, and has changed a lot since I started pursuing other projects in December 2020, but that collaboration on- and off-wiki – giving readers a new, more collaborative and accurate sense of this project – is my best work.
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <b>3.</b> Have you been in any conflicts over editing in the past or have other users caused you stress? How have you dealt with it and how will you deal with it in the future? 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <b>A:</b> Of course. Always because I made a mistake, because I was gung ho about something. To be honest, it was people like me for whom <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BRD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BRD">WP:BRD</a> was written. How I've resolved disputes (and my goofs), is exactly that formula: be bold, get reverted, and then work it out. Take <a href="/wiki/User_talk:Vami_IV/Archive3#Roman_Catholic_church_categories" title="User talk:Vami IV/Archive3">this example from back in 2018</a>. As an admin however, I will be more cautious before doing <a href="/wiki/Special:Diff/1026034889" title="Special:Diff/1026034889">something like this again</a>.
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </dd>
      </dl>

Multi-part questions disguised as one question, with the intention of evading the limit, are disallowed. Follow-up questions relevant to questions you have already asked are allowed.</span> </p>

      <p><b>Requests for bureaucratship</b> (<b>RfB</b>) is the process by which the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedians" title="Wikipedia:Wikipedians">Wikipedia community</a> decides who will become <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Bureaucrats" title="Wikipedia:Bureaucrats">bureaucrats</a>. Bureaucrats can make other users administrators or bureaucrats, based on community decisions reached here, and remove administrator rights in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Bureaucrats#Removal_of_permissions" title="Wikipedia:Bureaucrats">limited circumstances</a>. They also oversee local <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Changing_username" title="Wikipedia:Changing username">change usernames</a> venues in conjunction with the team of <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/global_renamers" class="extiw" title="meta:global renamers">global renamers</a> and can grant or remove <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BOT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BOT">bot status</a> on an account. </p>
      <p>The process for bureaucrats is similar to that for adminship above; however the expectation for promotion to bureaucratship is significantly higher than for admin, requiring a clearer consensus. In general, the threshold for consensus is somewhere around 85%. Bureaucrats are expected to determine consensus in difficult cases and be ready to explain their decisions. </p>
      <p>Create a new RfB page as you would for an RfA, and insert <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:RfB" title="Template:RfB">subst:RfB</a>|User=<var style="padding-right: 1px;">Username</var>|Description=<var style="padding-right: 1px;">Your description of the candidate.</var> ~~~~}}</code> into it, then answer the questions. New bureaucrats are recorded at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Successful_bureaucratship_candidacies" title="Wikipedia:Successful bureaucratship candidacies">Wikipedia:Successful bureaucratship candidacies</a>. Failed nominations are at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Unsuccessful_bureaucratship_candidacies" title="Wikipedia:Unsuccessful bureaucratship candidacies">Wikipedia:Unsuccessful bureaucratship candidacies</a>. </p>
      <p>At minimum, study what is expected of a bureaucrat by reading discussions at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Requests_for_adminship" title="Wikipedia talk:Requests for adminship">Wikipedia talk:Requests for adminship</a> including the recent archives, <i>before</i> seeking this position. </p>
      <p>While <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Canvassing" title="Wikipedia:Canvassing">canvassing</a> for support is often viewed negatively by the community, some users find it helpful to place the neutrally worded <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:RfX-notice" title="Template:RfX-notice">RfX-notice</a>|b}}</code> on their userpages – this is generally not seen as canvassing. Like requests for adminship, requests for bureaucratship are advertised on the watchlist and on <a href="/wiki/Template:Centralized_discussion" title="Template:Centralized discussion">Template:Centralized discussion</a>. </p>
      <p><em>Please add new requests at the top of the section immediately below this line.</em> </p>
