       <figure class="article-figure" data-progressive-image role="img" aria-label="Location access prompt for the Weather app on iPhone"></figure> 
      </div> 
      <div class="typography-subpage-article-intro article-intro"> 
       <p>Settings have been carefully designed to put you in control of your data. You can adjust what information is shared, where you share it, and when it is backed up. And starting with iOS&nbsp;14.5 and iPadOS&nbsp;14.5, you’re more in control of how apps use your personal data to track&nbsp;you.</p> <a href="/privacy/docs/A_Day_in_the_Life_of_Your_Data.pdf" data-analytics-title="view a day in the life of your data" aria-label="View A Day in the Life of Your Data (PDF)" class="icon-wrapper article-icon-cta"><span class="icon-copy">View A Day in the Life of Your Data&nbsp;(PDF)</span></a> 
      </div>

       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Privacy information on the App&nbsp;Store</h3> 
       <p>Starting in December 2020, product pages on the App&nbsp;Store feature a new section that provides developers’ self?reported summaries of some of their privacy practices in a simple, easy?to?read label. This shows how developers are collecting and using your data, including information like your location, browsing history, and contacts. This is part of ongoing work to increase transparency and control over your data, and Apple will continue to update this feature and work with developers to ensure that users can make informed&nbsp;choices.</p> 
       <div class="article-links-wrapper"> <a href="https://support.apple.com/kb/HT211970" data-analytics-title="Learn more about privacy labels on the App Store" aria-label="Learn more about privacy labels on the App Store" class="icon-wrapper article-link" data-analytics-exit-link><span class="icon-copy">Learn more about privacy labels on the App&nbsp;Store</span><span class="icon icon-after more"></span></a> <a href="/privacy/labels/" data-analytics-title="see how apps from Apple handle your data" aria-label="see how apps from Apple handle your data" class="icon-wrapper article-link"><span class="icon-copy">See how apps from Apple handle your&nbsp;data</span><span class="icon icon-after more"></span></a> 
       </div> 
      </section> 
      <section class="article-item"> 
       <figure class="article-item-image item2-image hardware-image" role="img" aria-label="App tracking controls and transparency screen on iPhone">

       <div class="violator-container"> <span class="violator violator-reduced">New</span> 
       </div> 
       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">App&nbsp;Tracking&nbsp;Transparency</h3> 
       <p>Your devices carry the story of your life. We believe you should&nbsp;have a choice in&nbsp;how apps track and share your data with other companies for advertising or with data&nbsp;brokers.</p> 
       <p>Starting with iOS&nbsp;14.5 and iPadOS&nbsp;14.5, apps are required to ask your permission when they want to track you across apps and websites owned by other companies. You’ll be able to change your preference for any app or&nbsp;prevent&nbsp;apps from asking for permission entirely in&nbsp;Settings.</p> 
       <div class="article-links-wrapper"> 
       </div> 
      </section> 
      <section class="article-item"> 
       <figure class="article-item-image item3-image hardware-image" role="img" aria-label="Location access prompt for Calendar on iPhone"> 
        <div class="screen"></div> 
        <div class="hardware"></div> 
       </figure> 
       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Third-party apps and&nbsp;permissions</h3> 
       <p>Apple gives you transparency and control over the data you share with apps. Apps may request access to things such as your location, contacts, calendars, or photos. You’ll receive a prompt with an explanation the first time a third-party app wants to use this data, so you can make an informed decision about granting permission. Even if you grant access once, you can always change it later in Settings. Starting in early 2021, iOS&nbsp;14 and iPadOS&nbsp;14 will also require developers to get your permission before tracking you or your device across apps and websites owned by other companies for targeted ads or ad measurement purposes, or to share your&nbsp;data with data&nbsp;brokers.</p> 
       <p>No app can access the microphone or camera without your permission. In iOS 14 and iPadOS&nbsp;14, when an app uses the microphone or camera, your device displays an indicator to let you know they are being used –– whether you are in the app, in another app, or on the Home Screen. And Control Center shows you when an app has recently used the microphone or camera. In iOS and iPadOS, access to the camera is disabled for an app when it is in the&nbsp;background.</p> 
       <p>Sometimes apps need to know what other devices are on your local network, like when you’re trying to connect to a smart TV or printer. Starting with iOS&nbsp;14 and iPadOS&nbsp;14, apps need to ask your permission before scanning your local&nbsp;network.</p> 
       <p>iOS&nbsp;14 and iPadOS&nbsp;14 also let you know when an app accesses your Clipboard, so you can confirm that it receives access to only the items you&nbsp;expect.</p>

       </div>

       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Data and privacy information</h3> 
       <p>Data and privacy information screens make it easy to understand how Apple will use your personal information before you sign in or start using new features. When you see the Data and Privacy icon, you’ll find helpful information on what personal data may be shared and how it will be used to improve your experience.</p> 
       <div class="article-links-wrapper"> 
       </div> 
      </section> 
      <section class="article-item">

       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Location data</h3> 
       <p>Sometimes it’s useful for your device to know your location, like when you’re setting up meetings in Calendar or getting directions. Location&nbsp;Services on your device uses a combination of GPS, Bluetooth, and crowd-sourced Wi-Fi hotspots and mobile towers to figure out where you are. Apple gives you control over the collection and use of this location data on all your devices. With iOS&nbsp;14, iPadOS&nbsp;14, and watchOS&nbsp;7, you can choose whether apps have access to your approximate location — an area of about 10 square&nbsp;miles — rather than your precise location. Location Services is not on by default. You can enable it when you first set up your device, and you can always turn it off if you change your&nbsp;mind.</p> 
       <div class="article-links-wrapper"> <a href="/privacy/docs/Location_Services_White_Paper_Nov_2019.pdf" data-analytics-title="view the location services privacy white paper" class="icon-wrapper article-icon-cta"><span class="icon-copy">View the Location Services Privacy white paper (PDF)</span></a>

      </section>

       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Data and Privacy page</h3> 
       <p>To give you more control over your personal information, we provide a set of dedicated privacy management tools on your Data and Privacy page. These tools give you the ability to get a copy of your data, request a correction to your data, deactivate your account, or delete your account.</p> 
       <div class="article-links-wrapper"> <a href="https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT208501" data-analytics-title="learn more about taking control of your data" aria-label="learn more about taking control of your data" class="icon-wrapper article-link" data-analytics-exit-link><span class="icon-copy">Learn more about taking control of your data</span><span class="icon icon-after more"></span></a> <a href="https://privacy.apple.com/" data-analytics-title="visit your data and privacy page" aria-label="visit your data and privacy page" class="icon-wrapper article-link" data-analytics-exit-link><span class="icon-copy">Visit your Data and Privacy page</span><span class="icon icon-after more"></span></a>

      <section class="article-item"> 
       <figure class="article-item-image item7-image hardware-image" role="img" aria-label="Analytics and Improvements menu in Settings on iPhone">

       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Analytics</h3> 
       <p>If you choose to opt in, your iOS and iPadOS devices can collect analytics about your device and any paired Apple&nbsp;Watch and send it to Apple for analysis. This analysis helps Apple improve products and reduce problems like apps crashing. The collected information does not identify you personally and can be sent to Apple only with your explicit consent. Analytics may include details about hardware and operating system specifications, performance statistics, and data about how you use your devices and applications. When it’s collected, personal data is either not logged at all, removed from reports before they’re sent to Apple, or protected by techniques such as Differential Privacy.</p> 
       <p>The information we gather using Differential Privacy helps us improve our services without compromising individual privacy. For example, this technology improves QuickType and emoji suggestions, as well as Lookup Hints in Notes.</p> 
       <p>We identify commonly used data types in the Health app and web domains in Safari that cause performance issues. This information allows us to work with developers to improve your experience without revealing anything about your individual&nbsp;behavior.</p> 
       <p>If you give your explicit consent to share iCloud Analytics, Apple can improve Siri and other intelligent features by analyzing how you use iCloud data from your account, such as text snippets from email messages. Analysis happens only after the data has gone through privacy-enhancing techniques like Differential Privacy so that it cannot be associated with you or your account.</p> 
       <div class="article-links-wrapper"> 
       </div> 
      </section>

       <figure class="article-item-image item8-image hardware-image" role="img" aria-label="Advertising menu in Settings on iPhone">

       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Advertising</h3> 
       <p>Apple is committed to delivering advertising in a way that respects your privacy. Apple?delivered ads may appear on the App&nbsp;Store, Apple&nbsp;News, and Stocks. The Apple advertising platform does not track you, nor does it buy or share your personal information with other companies. Your Apple&nbsp;Pay transactions, Health app data, and HomeKit&nbsp;app data are not used by the Apple advertising platform to deliver ads. Your App Store search and download history may be used to to serve you relevant ads. In the Apple&nbsp;News and Stocks&nbsp;apps, ads are served based partly on what you read or follow. This includes the topics and categories of the stories you read and the publications you follow, subscribe to, or enable notifications from. The stories you read are not used to serve targeted ads to you outside these apps. You can view the information Apple uses to deliver relevant ads to you in Settings. You can also turn off Personalized Ads at any time in Settings to stop receiving targeted ads on the App&nbsp;Store, Apple&nbsp;News, and Stocks. Turning off Personalized&nbsp;Ads will limit Apple’s ability to deliver relevant ads to you but may not reduce the number of ads you receive. The Apple advertising platform doesn’t serve ads to children under 13 years old and Managed Apple&nbsp;IDs. In addition, Apple has strong guidelines for apps in the Kids category of the App&nbsp;Store, including prohibiting apps in the category from including third-party analytics or third-party&nbsp;advertising.</p>

       <h2 class="typography-subpage-article-headline article-title">Secure your devices.</h2> 
       <figure class="article-figure" data-progressive-image role="img" aria-label="Apple Watch with Find My Watch screen displayed"></figure> 
      </div> 
      <div class="typography-subpage-article-intro article-intro"> 
       <p>Prevent anyone except you from using your devices and accessing your&nbsp;information.</p> 
      </div> 
     </header> 
     <div class="article-body"> 
      <section class="article-item"> 
       <figure class="article-item-image item1-image hardware-image" role="img" aria-label="Enter Passcode screen on iPhone"> 
        <div class="screen"></div> 
        <div class="hardware"></div>

       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Six-digit passcodes</h3> 
       <p>Setting a passcode is the most important thing you can do to safeguard your device. The passcode on your Touch ID– or Face&nbsp;ID?enabled device is automatically set to six digits, creating a million possible combinations. You can also enable Erase Data in Settings to have your device wipe itself clean after 10 incorrect guesses.</p> 
       <div class="article-links-wrapper"> <a href="https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT204060" data-analytics-title="Learn more about protecting your device with a&nbsp;passcode" aria-label="Learn more about protecting your device with a&nbsp;passcode" class="icon-wrapper article-link" data-analytics-exit-link><span class="icon-copy">Learn more about protecting your device with a&nbsp;passcode</span><span class="icon icon-after more"></span></a> 
       </div> 
      </section> 
      <section class="article-item"> 
       <figure class="article-item-image item2-image hardware-image" role="img" aria-label="Face ID set-up screen on iPhone"> 
        <div class="screen"></div> 
        <div class="hardware"></div> 
       </figure> 
       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Touch&nbsp;ID and Face&nbsp;ID</h3> 
       <p>Touch&nbsp;ID and Face&nbsp;ID provide intuitive and secure authentication with the touch of a finger or a simple glance. Your fingerprint or face data is converted into a mathematical representation that is encrypted and used only by the Secure&nbsp;Enclave in your Mac, iPad, or iPhone. Since fingerprint and face data is so personal, your device takes extraordinary measures to protect it. This data can’t be accessed by the operating system on your device or by any applications running on it. And it is never stored on Apple servers or backed up to iCloud or anywhere else.</p> 
       <div class="article-links-wrapper"> <a href="https://support.apple.com/guide/security/" data-analytics-title="Learn more about Apple Platform Security" aria-label="Learn more about Apple Platform Security" class="icon-wrapper article-link" data-analytics-exit-link><span class="icon-copy">Learn more about Apple Platform&nbsp;Security</span><span class="icon icon-after more"></span></a> 
       </div> 
      </section> 
      <section class="article-item"> 
       <figure class="article-item-image item3-image hardware-image" role="img" aria-label="Apple ID Verification Code Prompt on iPhone"> 
        <div class="screen"></div> 
        <div class="hardware"></div> 
       </figure> 
       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Two-factor authentication</h3> 
       <p>Two-factor authentication is the best way to keep your information safe because it adds a second layer of security to your Apple&nbsp;ID. It’s designed to ensure that even someone who knows your password can’t access your account. When you sign in on a new device or from the web, a verification code will be displayed automatically on all your trusted devices. Enter this code along with your password and you’ll be signed in. Anyone who can’t provide both your password and the verification code will be kept out. Once signed in, you won’t be asked for a verification code on that device again unless you sign out completely, erase the device, or need to change your password for security reasons.</p> 
       <div class="article-links-wrapper"> <a href="https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT204915" data-analytics-title="Learn how to set up two-factor authentication" aria-label="Learn how to set up two-factor authentication" class="icon-wrapper article-link" data-analytics-exit-link><span class="icon-copy">Learn how to set up two-factor authentication</span><span class="icon icon-after more"></span></a> 
       </div> 
      </section> 
      <section class="article-item"> 
       <figure class="article-item-image item4-image offset-image" role="img" aria-label="Find My screen displaying map of various devices on iPad"> 
       </figure> 
       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Find My</h3> 
       <p>Enabling Find My for iPhone, iPad, and Mac helps keep you connected to your device even if it’s lost or stolen, and Find My is built in a way that protects your privacy. It’s enabled automatically when you sign in to iCloud on a new device. You can see where your device is on a map — and where it has been — so you improve your chances of recovering it. If you’re unable to get your device back, you can remotely erase the personal data on it. Apple receives this location information only when you locate your device, turn on Lost Mode, or enable Send Last Location. Location data is only retained for 24 hours so we can display the last known location of your device. Find My also automatically turns on Activation Lock, which requires your Apple&nbsp;ID to be entered before anyone can erase or reactivate your device.</p>

       <p>Keep an eye out for fraudulent attempts to acquire your personal information.</p>

     <div class="article-body"> 
      <section class="article-item"> 
       <figure class="article-item-image item1-image hardware-image" role="img" aria-label="Email saying “Your Apple ID information has been updated” on iPhone">

       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Pay attention to&nbsp;notifications.</h3> 
       <p>When you sign in for the first time on a new device, update your payment method, change your password, or make other changes to your account, Apple notifies you with an email or a push notification. So if you receive a notification from us about a change that you don’t remember making, it could mean that someone has wrongfully accessed your account. If that happens, go to “Manage your Apple&nbsp;ID” to change your password immediately. If you need additional help, contact Apple&nbsp;Support for&nbsp;assistance.</p> 
       <div class="article-links-wrapper"> <a href="https://support.apple.com/kb/HT5624" data-analytics-title="change your apple id password" aria-label="change your apple id password" class="icon-wrapper article-link" data-analytics-exit-link><span class="icon-copy">Change your Apple&nbsp;ID password</span><span class="icon icon-after more"></span></a> <a href="https://support.apple.com/apple-id" data-analytics-title="contact apple id support" aria-label="contact apple id support" class="icon-wrapper article-link" data-analytics-exit-link><span class="icon-copy">Contact Apple&nbsp;ID Support</span><span class="icon icon-after more"></span></a> 
       </div> 
      </section> 
      <section class="article-item"> 
       <figure class="article-item-image item2-image offset-image" role="img" aria-label="iPad displaying an example of a phishing email scam"> 
       </figure> 
       <h3 class="typography-subpage-article-body-subhead article-body-subhead">Beware of phishing.</h3> 
       <p>“Phishing” refers to fraudulent attempts to get personal information from you, such as your Apple&nbsp;ID password or credit card information, usually through an email or text message. The request may appear to be from a legitimate company or individual, but it’s not. Turning on two-factor authentication is the best way to protect yourself against phishing schemes designed to trick you into revealing your Apple&nbsp;ID account credentials. Never provide your password, verification codes, or any other sensitive account information over email or text messages. If you receive what you believe to be a phishing email purporting to be from Apple, send it to <a href="mailto:reportphishing@apple.com" data-analytics-title="report phishing at apple.com" data-analytics-exit-link>reportphishing@apple.com</a>.</p>
