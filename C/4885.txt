      <p>Some <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedians" title="Wikipedia:Wikipedians">Wikipedians</a> have formed a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">project</a> to better organize information in articles related to <b><a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a></b>. This page and its subpages contain their suggestions; it is hoped that this project will help to focus the efforts of other Wikipedians. If you would like to help, please inquire on the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:WikiProject_Nova_Scotia" title="Wikipedia talk:WikiProject Nova Scotia">talk page</a> and see the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:To-do_list" title="Wikipedia:To-do list">to-do list</a> there. </p>
      <p><big><big><b>The current focus of development is the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Breton_Regional_Municipality" title="Cape Breton Regional Municipality">Cape Breton Regional Municipality</a>.</b></big></big> </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Goals">Goals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:WikiProject_Nova_Scotia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Goals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>This <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">WikiProject</a>'s main goals are: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>Concise and detailed articles about every notable subject relating to <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>.</li> 
       <li>Optimal topics navigation, including full coverage in Wikipedia's navigation subsystems (<a href="/wiki/Portal:Contents/Outlines" class="mw-redirect" title="Portal:Contents/Outlines">outlines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portal:Contents/Lists" class="mw-redirect" title="Portal:Contents/Lists">lists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portal:Contents/Categories" class="mw-redirect" title="Portal:Contents/Categories">categories</a>, etc.), and relevant links between Nova Scotia articles, including pertinent side bars and navigation boxes.</li> 
       <li>High quality and comprehensive coverage for all counties, towns, and incorporated municipalities in Nova Scotia, as well as related sub or specialty pages.</li> 
       <li>Nova Scotia articles of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_content" title="Wikipedia:Featured content">Featured Article</a> (FA) status.</li>
      </ol> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tips">Tips</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:WikiProject_Nova_Scotia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tips">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p><b>To get an article to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_articles" title="Wikipedia:Featured articles">featured article</a> status, make sure to follow these steps to ensure all articles we submit to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_article_candidates" title="Wikipedia:Featured article candidates">Featured article candidates</a> meet the FA criteria. All of these apply to almost any good article, so keep them in mind when editing even outside of Nova Scotia articles. The suggestions are:</b> </p> 
      <table cellspacing="0" cellpadding="4"> 
       <tbody>
        <tr> 
         <td bgcolor="#ffffcc"><font face="verdana,arial"><b>I</b>.</font> </td> 
         <td><b>Write a good lead.</b> Be sure to write a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Lead" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Lead">lead</a> that concisely summarizes the entire article into one or two paragraphs which make sense to someone who may know nothing about the subject. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td bgcolor="#ffff99"><font face="verdana,arial"><b>II</b>.</font> </td> 
         <td><b>Use good spelling and grammar.</b> This is a very important aspect of an article. Many browsers support spell checking. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td bgcolor="#ffcc66"><font face="verdana,arial"><b>III</b>.</font> </td> 
         <td><b>Use footnotes.</b> Take advantage of the footnote ability Wikipedia has, instead of including html links inside the context include them as footnotes. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Footnotes" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Footnotes">Wikipedia:Footnotes</a> on how to use them. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td bgcolor="orange"><font face="verdana,arial"><b>IV</b>.</font> </td> 
         <td><b>Use images if possible.</b> Images enhance articles greatly, but only use them when they are necessary, and ensure that their copyright status has been specified and we are allowed to use it on Wikipedia. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Images" title="Wikipedia:Images">Wikipedia:Images</a> for more information. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td bgcolor="#ff7200"><font face="verdana,arial"><b>V</b>.</font> </td> 
         <td><b>Use references.</b> This is an encyclopedia, so remember to include a ==References== section listing websites, newspapers, articles, books and other sources you used to write the article. An article will not get accepted as a featured article candidate if it is missing references. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">Wikipedia:Verifiability</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Cite_sources" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Cite sources">Wikipedia:Cite_sources</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:References" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:References">Wikipedia:References</a>. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td bgcolor="#d45f00"><font face="verdana,arial"><b>VI</b>.</font> </td> 
         <td><b>Stay on topic.</b> Many articles are criticized for length; sticking to the subject matter helps eliminate this. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td bgcolor="#8b4513"><font face="verdana,arial"><b>VII</b>.</font> </td> 
         <td><b><a href="/wiki/KISS_principle" title="KISS principle">Keep it simple</a>.</b> Remember that the average reader should be able to comprehend the erudition. Although you should use a broad vocabulary of regular, non-technical terms, do not provide such a quantity of locutions as to impel those who aspire to derive serviceable information from the article to consult a dictionary. </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <td bgcolor="#663300"><font face="verdana,arial"><b>VIII</b>.</font> </td> 
         <td><b>Use <a href="/wiki/Common_sense" title="Common sense">common sense</a>.</b> You should know what <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:The_perfect_article" title="Wikipedia:The perfect article">perfect articles</a> look like. They cover everything they should without going on forever. Common sense could have told you almost all of the items mentioned above. Ultimately, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Assume_good_faith" title="Wikipedia:Assume good faith">assume good faith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BB" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BB">be <b>bold</b></a>, and go out there and write some good articles. </td>
        </tr>
       </tbody>
      </table>

            The article meets the <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_article_criteria" title="Wikipedia:Featured article criteria">featured article criteria</a></b>: 
            <p>A <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Featured_articles" title="Wikipedia:Featured articles">featured article</a> exemplifies Wikipedia's very best work and is distinguished by professional standards of writing, presentation, and sourcing. In addition to meeting the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_policies#CONPOL" title="Wikipedia:List of policies">policies regarding content</a> for all Wikipedia articles, it has the following attributes. </p> 
            <ol> 
             <li id="1">It is: 
              <ol style="list-style-type: lower-alpha"> 
               <li id="1A"><b>well-written</b>: its prose is engaging and of a professional standard;</li> 
               <li id="1B"><b>comprehensive</b>: it neglects no major facts or details and places the subject in context;</li> 
               <li id="1C"><b>well-researched</b>: it is a thorough and representative survey of the relevant literature; claims are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verifiable</a> against high-quality <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">reliable sources</a> and are supported by inline citations <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:When_to_cite" title="Wikipedia:When to cite">where appropriate</a>;</li> 
               <li id="1D"><b>neutral</b>: it presents views <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Neutral_point_of_view" title="Wikipedia:Neutral point of view">fairly and without bias</a>;</li> 
               <li id="1E"><b>stable</b>: it is not subject to ongoing <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_warring" title="Wikipedia:Edit warring">edit wars</a> and its content does not change significantly from day to day, except in response to the featured article process; and</li> 
               <li id="1F"><b>compliant with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Copyrights" title="Wikipedia:Copyrights">Wikipedia's copyright policy</a></b> and free of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Plagiarism" title="Wikipedia:Plagiarism">plagiarism</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Close_paraphrasing" title="Wikipedia:Close paraphrasing">too-close paraphrasing</a>.</li> 
              </ol></li> 
             <li id="2">It follows the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style">style guidelines</a>, including the provision of: 
              <ol style="list-style-type: lower-alpha"> 
               <li id="2A"><b>a lead</b> or <a href="/wiki/Lead_paragraph" title="Lead paragraph">lede</a>: a concise <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Lead_section" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Lead section">lead section</a> that summarizes the topic and prepares the reader for the detail in the subsequent sections;</li> 
               <li id="2B"><b>appropriate structure</b>: a substantial but not overwhelming system of hierarchical <a href="/wiki/Help:Section" title="Help:Section">section headings</a>; and</li> 
               <li id="2C"><b>consistent citations</b>: where required by criterion 1c, consistently formatted inline citations using footnotessee <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">citing sources</a> for suggestions on formatting references. Citation templates are not required.</li> 
              </ol></li> 
             <li id="3"><b>Media.</b> It has <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Images" title="Wikipedia:Images">images</a> and other media, where appropriate, with succinct <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Captions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Captions">captions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FAQ/Copyright#Licenses" title="Wikipedia:FAQ/Copyright">acceptable copyright status</a>. Images follow the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Image_use_policy" title="Wikipedia:Image use policy">image use policy</a>. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Non-free_content" title="Wikipedia:Non-free content">Non-free</a> images or media must satisfy the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Non-free_content_criteria" title="Wikipedia:Non-free content criteria">criteria for inclusion of non-free content</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:File_copyright_tags/Non-free" title="Wikipedia:File copyright tags/Non-free">be labeled accordingly</a>.</li> 
             <li id="4"><b>Length.</b> It stays focused on the main topic without going into unnecessary detail and uses <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Summary_style" title="Wikipedia:Summary style">summary style</a>.</li> 
            </ol>

The inclusion of supporting materials should be considered if practical, and the article checked for general compliance with the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style">Manual of Style</a> and related <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_style_guidelines" title="Category:Wikipedia style guidelines">style guidelines</a>.</td>

It may have some gaps or missing elements; need editing for clarity, balance, or flow; or contain policy violations, such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Neutral_point_of_view" title="Wikipedia:Neutral point of view">bias</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">original research</a>. It is most likely that C-Class articles have a reasonable encyclopedic style.

No Start-Class article should be in any danger of being <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Speedy_deletion_criteria#Articles" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Speedy deletion criteria">speedily deleted</a>.

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Articles">Articles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:WikiProject_Nova_Scotia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Articles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><i>See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Canadian_Wikipedians%27_notice_board/Style_guide" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Canadian Wikipedians' notice board/Style guide">Naming conventions</a></i> </p>
      <p>Following Wikipedia standard, article titles should relate to the best-known name in most cases. It has become standard that communities and place names in Canada be followed by the name of the Province, thus <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Regional_Municipality,_Nova_Scotia" class="mw-redirect" title="Halifax Regional Municipality, Nova Scotia">Halifax Regional Municipality, Nova Scotia</a>. Redirects should be created for commonly used nicknames, and for place names without the province following the name, where possible. </p>
      <p><b>Structure</b> </p>
      <p><b>County Pages</b> </p>
      <p>Articles for Nova Scotia Counties need to have the following: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Inclusion or upgrade to NS Counties sidebar template.</li> 
       <li>Removal of county direction finder at bottom of page, in lieu of adjacent county section of template.</li> 
       <li>Inclusion of data from Statistics Canada</li> 
       <li>Check the Stats Canada Webpage for correct household income (most data currently on page is actually average individual income).</li>
      </ul> 
      <p><b>Town/Municipality Pages</b> </p>
      <p>Articles for Nova Scotia Towns/Municipalities need to have the following: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Inclusion or upgrade {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Infobox_Settlement" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:Infobox Settlement">Infobox Settlement</a>}} template.</li> 
       <li>Inclusion of data from Statistics Canada where available.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p><b>Format</b> Here is one suggestion on how to begin: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <b>Sherose Island</b> (4332'00"N, 6535'57"W) is a small <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> island located off the <a href="/wiki/South_Shore_(Nova_Scotia)" class="mw-redirect" title="South Shore (Nova Scotia)">South Shore</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>.
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        The island is located in the Barrington Passage, a small <a href="/wiki/Strait" title="Strait">strait</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shelburne_County,_Nova_Scotia" title="Shelburne County, Nova Scotia">Shelburne County</a> and measures approximately 2 kilometres (1.2&nbsp;mi) long and 0.5 kilometres (0.3&nbsp;mi) wide. It is connected to mainland Nova Scotia by a short <a href="/wiki/Causeway" title="Causeway">causeway</a>.
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>If you are only able to get a few details for a particular article, please add the appropriate stub at the bottom of the page. </p>
