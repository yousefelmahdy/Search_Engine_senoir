      <p><b>Citation Style 1</b> (<b>CS1</b>) is a collection of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">reference citation</a> <a href="/wiki/Help:Template" title="Help:Template">templates</a> that can be modified to create different styles for different referenced materials. Its purpose is to provide a set of default formats for references on Wikipedia. It includes a series of templates that in turn use <a href="/wiki/Module:Citation/CS1" title="Module:Citation/CS1">Module:Citation/CS1</a>. </p>
      <p>The use of CS1 or of templates is not compulsory; per <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CITESTYLE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CITESTYLE">WP:CITESTYLE</a>: </p> 
      <blockquote>
       <p>Wikipedia does not have a single <a href="/wiki/Style_guide" title="Style guide">house style</a>. Editors may choose any option they want; one article need not match what is done in other articles or what is done in professional publications or recommended by academic <a href="/wiki/Style_guide" title="Style guide">style guides</a>. However, citations <i>within a given article</i> should follow a consistent style.</p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CITEVAR" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CITEVAR">WP:CITEVAR</a> additionally states: </p> 
      <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style>
      <blockquote class="templatequote">
       <p>If the article you are editing is already using a particular citation style, you should follow it; if you believe it is inappropriate for the needs of the article, seek consensus for a change on the talk page.</p>
      </blockquote> 
      <p>CS1 uses (in addition to Wikipedia's own <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style">Manual of Style</a>) elements of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chicago_Manual_of_Style" title="The Chicago Manual of Style">The Chicago Manual of Style</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Publication_Manual_of_the_American_Psychological_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association">Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association</a></i>, with significant adaptations. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style">Style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>There are a number of templates that use a name starting with <i>cite</i>; many were developed independently of CS1 and are not compliant with the CS1 style. There are also a number of templates that use one of the general use templates as a meta-template to cite a specific source. </p>
      <p>To be compliant with CS1, a template must: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Use <a href="/wiki/Module:Citation/CS1" title="Module:Citation/CS1">Module:Citation/CS1</a> or one of the templates listed below.</li> 
       <li>Use a period as a punctuation mark to separate fields and end the citation.</li> 
       <li>Use a semicolon as a punctuation mark to separate authors and editors.</li> 
       <li>Format the title of longer works in italics.</li> 
       <li>Format the title of short works such as chapters in quotes.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Templates">Templates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Templates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="General_use">General use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: General use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The following is a list of templates that implement Citation Style 1 for one or more types of citations but are not restricted to any specific source. </p> 
      <table class="wikitable" style="max-width:55em;"> 
       <tbody>
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row" style="background:#9F9;vertical-align:middle;text-align:center;" class="table-yes">Yes </th> 
         <td>Indicates that the corresponding tool or gadget can generate the template anytime user wishes. However, this does not mean that the tool or gadget fully supports all parameters or provides an interface for them. In fact, none of the mentioned items do so. </td>
        </tr>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Specific_source">Specific source</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Specific source">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>There are a number of templates that are CS1 compliant, because they use a CS1 template as a base, but are tied to a specific source; these are listed in <a href="/wiki/Category:Citation_Style_1_specific-source_templates" title="Category:Citation Style 1 specific-source templates">Category:Citation Style 1 specific-source templates</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="How_the_templates_work">How the templates work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: How the templates work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>CS1 uses a series of templates that provide a consistent output. The main difference is in parameters optimized for the subject. For example, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_book" title="Template:Cite book">cite book</a>}} has fields for title and chapter, whereas {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_journal" title="Template:Cite journal">cite journal</a>}} has fields for journal and title. </p>
      <p>This help page uses the names most commonly used across the templates series; see each template's documentation for details. </p>
      <p>CS1 templates present a citation generally as: </p> 
      <ul>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Authors">Authors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Authors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>An author may be cited using separate parameters for the author's surname and given name by using <code class="nowrap" style="">|last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|first=</code> respectively. If a cited source has multiple authors, subsequent authors can be listed in the citation using <code class="nowrap" style="">|last2=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|first2=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|last3=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|first3=</code>, etc.<sup id="cite_ref-NB_Unlimited_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NB_Unlimited-1">[Note 1]</a></sup> For symmetry with the other numbered parameters, <code class="nowrap" style="">|last1=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|first1=</code> are available as well, as shown in the following example: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <code>{{cite book |last1=Hawking |first1=Stephen |last2=Hawking |first2=Lucy |title=George's Secret Key to the Universe}}</code>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r999302996"><cite id="CITEREFHawkingHawking" class="citation book cs1">Hawking, Stephen; Hawking, Lucy. <i>George's Secret Key to the Universe</i>.</cite><span title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Abook&amp;rft.genre=book&amp;rft.btitle=George%27s+Secret+Key+to+the+Universe&amp;rft.aulast=Hawking&amp;rft.aufirst=Stephen&amp;rft.au=Hawking%2C+Lucy&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3AHelp%3ACitation+Style+1" class="Z3988"></span>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>For symmetry with similar parameters for editors and other contributors (discussed further below), longer parameter forms are also available for authors: <code class="nowrap" style="">|author-last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|author-first=</code>, as well as numbered variants like <code class="nowrap" style="">|author-last<var style="padding-right: 1px;">n</var>=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|author-first<var style="padding-right: 1px;">n</var>=</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|author<var style="padding-right: 1px;">n</var>-last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|author<var style="padding-right: 1px;">n</var>-first=</code> (with <var style="padding-right: 1px;">n</var> referring to this author's number in the list). Because the shorthand parameters might erroneously have been used also for editors and other types of contributors by some Wikipedians in the past, please make sure that the parameters actually refer to authors when expanding <code class="nowrap" style="">|last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|first=</code> parameters to their longer equivalents (equivalent parameters for editors etc. exist as well, see <a href="#Editors">below</a>). </p>
      <p>If a cited source has a large number of authors, one can limit the number of authors displayed when the citation is published by using the <code class="nowrap" style="">|display-authors=</code> parameter as described in detail in the <a href="/wiki/Help:Citation_Style_1#Display_options" title="Help:Citation Style 1">Display options</a> section of this help page. </p>
      <p>If a cited author is notable and the author has a Wikipedia article, the author's name can be linked with <code class="nowrap" style="">|author-link=</code>.<sup id="cite_ref-NB_Prefix_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NB_Prefix-2">[Note 2]</a></sup> If a citation includes multiple notable authors, one may use <code class="nowrap" style="">|author-link<var style="padding-right: 1px;">n</var>=</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|author<var style="padding-right: 1px;">n</var>-link=</code>, etc. This method is used because the <code class="nowrap" style="">|last=</code>- and <code class="nowrap" style="">|first=</code>-type parameters do not allow wikilinking. However, <code class="nowrap" style="">|author-link=</code> cannot be used to link to an external website; the external link will not render correctly.<sup id="cite_ref-NB_Prefix_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NB_Prefix-2">[Note 2]</a></sup> Below is an example of a wikilinked author credit: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <code>{{cite book |author-last1=Hawking |author-first1=Stephen |author-link1=Stephen Hawking |author-last2=Hawking |author-first2=Lucy |title=George's Secret Key to the Universe}}</code>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r999302996"><cite id="CITEREFHawkingHawking" class="citation book cs1"><a href="/wiki/Stephen_Hawking" title="Stephen Hawking">Hawking, Stephen</a>; Hawking, Lucy. <i>George's Secret Key to the Universe</i>.</cite><span title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Abook&amp;rft.genre=book&amp;rft.btitle=George%27s+Secret+Key+to+the+Universe&amp;rft.aulast=Hawking&amp;rft.aufirst=Stephen&amp;rft.au=Hawking%2C+Lucy&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3AHelp%3ACitation+Style+1" class="Z3988"></span>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>When an author is cited, the date of the cited work is displayed after the author's name, as shown in the example below: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <code>{{cite book |author-last1=Hawking |author-first1=Stephen |author-link1=Stephen Hawking |author-last2=Hawking |author-first2=Lucy |title=George's Secret Key to the Universe |date=2007}}</code>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r999302996"><cite id="CITEREFHawkingHawking2007" class="citation book cs1"><a href="/wiki/Stephen_Hawking" title="Stephen Hawking">Hawking, Stephen</a>; Hawking, Lucy (2007). <i>George's Secret Key to the Universe</i>.</cite><span title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Abook&amp;rft.genre=book&amp;rft.btitle=George%27s+Secret+Key+to+the+Universe&amp;rft.date=2007&amp;rft.aulast=Hawking&amp;rft.aufirst=Stephen&amp;rft.au=Hawking%2C+Lucy&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3AHelp%3ACitation+Style+1" class="Z3988"></span>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>If no author is cited, the date appears after the title, as shown in the example below: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <code>{{cite book |title=George's Secret Key to the Universe |date=2007}}</code>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r999302996"><cite class="citation book cs1"><i>George's Secret Key to the Universe</i>. 2007.</cite><span title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Abook&amp;rft.genre=book&amp;rft.btitle=George%27s+Secret+Key+to+the+Universe&amp;rft.date=2007&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3AHelp%3ACitation+Style+1" class="Z3988"></span>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>If the cited source does not credit an author, as is common with newswire reports, press releases or company websites use: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <code class="nowrap" style="">|author=&lt;!--Not stated--&gt;</code>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>This HTML comment alerts fact-checking and citation-fixing editors, and potentially bots, that the cited source did not name an author—the author was not overlooked. Without this entry editors and bots would waste time researching cited sources for a non-existent author credit. </p>
      <p>When using <code class="nowrap" style="">|author=</code> avoid citations like <code>{{cite news |work=Weekday Times |author=''Weekday Times'' editors |title=...}}</code>, unless the article is on a field in which the majority of professional journals covering that field use such a citation style. </p>
      <p>Editors should use an <code class="nowrap" style="">|author=</code> organizational citation when the cited source, such as a committee report, specifically names an official body or a sub-unit of the publisher as the collective author of the work, e.g. <code class="nowrap" style="">|author=Commission on Headphone Safety</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|author=Rules Sub-committee</code>. Do not use <code class="nowrap" style="">|author=</code> to assert what you think was probably the collective author when the source itself does not specifically specify a collective author; doing so is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOR" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOR">original research</a> and falsification of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:V" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:V">source verifiability</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RS">reliability</a>. </p>
      <p><code class="nowrap" style="">|author=</code> should never hold the name of more than one author. Separate individual authors into enumerated individual <code class="nowrap" style="">|author<var style="padding-right: 1px;">n</var>=</code> parameters. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Editors">Editors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Editors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>An editor may be cited using separate parameters for the editor's last and first name. A single or first editor would use <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor-last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor-first=</code>; subsequent editors would use <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor2-last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor2-first=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor3-last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor3-first=</code>, etc. </p>
      <p>If an editor has a Wikipedia article, you may wikilink to that Wikipedia article using <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor-link=</code>.<sup id="cite_ref-NB_Prefix_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NB_Prefix-2">[Note 2]</a></sup> If a cited work has multiple editors, you may use <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor2-link=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor3-link=</code>, etc. to wikilink to each editor's Wikipedia article. This method is used because <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor-last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor-first=</code> do not allow wikilinking. <code class="nowrap" style="">|editor-link=</code> cannot be used to link to an external website. </p>
      <p>If a cited source has a large number of editors, one can limit the number of editors displayed when the citation is published using the <code class="nowrap" style="">|display-editors=</code> parameter as described in detail in the <a href="/wiki/Help:Citation_Style_1#Display_options" title="Help:Citation Style 1">Display options</a> section of this help page. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Translators">Translators</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Translators">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>A translator may be cited using separate parameters for the translator's last and first name. A single or first translator would use <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator-last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator-first=</code>; subsequent translators would use <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator2-last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator2-first=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator3-last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator3-first=</code>, etc. </p>
      <p>If a translator has a Wikipedia article, you may wikilink to that Wikipedia article using <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator-link=</code>.<sup id="cite_ref-NB_Prefix_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NB_Prefix-2">[Note 2]</a></sup> If a cited work has multiple translators, you may use <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator2-link=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator3-link=</code>, etc. to wikilink to each translator's Wikipedia article. This method is used because <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator-last=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator-first=</code> do not allow wikilinking. <code class="nowrap" style="">|translator-link=</code> cannot be used to link to an external website. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Others">Others</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Others">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>others</b>: This parameter is used to credit contributors other than an author or an editor, such as an illustrator. You should include both the type of contribution and the name of the contributor, for example <code class="nowrap" style="">|others=Illustrated by John Smith</code>.<sup id="cite_ref-NB_Prefix_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NB_Prefix-2">[Note 2]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dates">Dates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Dates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>Dates are indicated by these parameters: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>date</b>: Full date of publication edition being referenced, in the same format as other dates in citations in the same article. Must not be wikilinked. 
        <ul>
         <li>or: <b>year</b>: Year of publication edition being referenced. Discouraged in favor of <b>date</b>, except in the rare case that <em>all</em> of the following conditions are met: 
          <ol>
           <li>the publication-date format in the template is YYYY-MM-DD</li> 
           <li>the citation requires a <code><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_templates_and_reference_anchors" title="Wikipedia:Citation templates and reference anchors">CITEREF</a></code> <a href="/wiki/Template:Sfn#More_than_one_work_in_a_year" title="Template:Sfn">disambiguator</a></li>
          </ol></li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><b>orig-date</b>: Original publication date or year, for display (in square brackets) after the <b>date</b> (or <b>year</b>). For clarity, please supply specifics, for instance <code>|orig-date=first published 1859</code> or <code>|orig-date=composed 1904</code>. This parameter displays only if there is a value for <b>date</b> (or <b>year</b>). Alias: <b>origyear</b>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>When a source does not have a publication date, use <code class="nowrap" style="">|date=n.d.</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|date=nd</code> </p>
      <p>Acceptable date formats are shown in the "Acceptable date formats" table of the <a href="/wiki/Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Dates,_months_and_years" class="mw-redirect" title="Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">Manual of Style/Dates and numbers §&nbsp;Dates, months and years</a>. Further points: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Prescriptions about date formats only apply when the date is expressed in terms of Julian or Gregorian dates, or which use one of the seasons (spring, summer, autumn or fall, winter). Sources are at liberty to use other ways of expressing dates, such as "spring/summer" or a date in a religious calendar; editors should report the date as expressed by the source. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SEASON" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SEASON">Although the seasons are not normally capitalized</a>, they are capitalized when used as dates in CS1 templates, and the capitalization of the season stated by the source may be altered to follow this convention. In cases where the date as expressed in the source is not compatible with the template software, the citation should be created without using a template.</li> 
       <li>Do not wikilink.</li> 
       <li>Access and archive dates in references should be in either the format used for <a href="/wiki/MOS:DATEUNIFY" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:DATEUNIFY">publication dates</a>, or YYYY-MM-DD.</li>
      </ul>

      <h4><span id="Date_range.2C_multiple_sources_in_same_year"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_range,_multiple_sources_in_same_year">Date range, multiple sources in same year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Date range, multiple sources in same year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>If dates are used, the year range is 100 to present without era indication (AD, BC, CE, BCE). In the case where the same author has written more than one work in the same year, a lower-case letter may be appended to the year in the date parameter (<span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">|date=July 4, 1997b</span>) or the year parameter (<span class="example" style="font-family: Georgia, 'DejaVu Serif', serif; color: #006400;">|year=1997b</span>). </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Auto-formatting_citation_template_dates">Auto-formatting citation template dates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Auto-formatting citation template dates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p> <a class="mw-selflink selflink">Citation Style 1</a> and <a href="/wiki/Help:Citation_Style_2" title="Help:Citation Style 2">2</a> templates automatically render dates (<code class="nowrap" style="">|date=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|access-date=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|archive-date=</code>, etc.) in the style specified by the article's <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Use_dmy_dates" title="Template:Use dmy dates">use dmy dates</a>}}</code> or <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Use_mdy_dates" title="Template:Use mdy dates">use mdy dates</a>}}</code> template. Because it is allowed by <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:MOSDATES" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:MOSDATES">WP:MOSDATES</a>, the CS1/CS2 templates can automatically format dates in a variety of styles. Editors may choose how CS1/CS2 templates render dates by the use of <code class="nowrap" style="">|cs1-dates=&lt;<var style="padding-right: 1px;">keyword</var>&gt;</code> in the article's <span class="nowrap"><code>{{use xxx dates}}</code></span> template. </p> 
      <table class="wikitable"> 
       <caption>
        Acceptable <code>|cs1-dates=</code> keywords 
       </caption> 
       <tbody>
        <tr> 
         <th scope="col">Keyword </th> 
         <th scope="col">Definition </th>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row"><code>l</code> </th> 
         <td rowspan="2">long-form publication and access- / archive-dates;<br>this is the default case when <code class="nowrap" style="border: none; background-color: inherit; color: inherit;">|cs1-dates=</code> is omitted or empty </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row"><code>ll</code> </th>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row"><code>ls</code> </th> 
         <td>long-form publication dates; abbreviated access- / archive-dates </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row"><code>ly</code> </th> 
         <td>long-form publication dates; year-initial numeric access- / archive-dates (ymd) </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row"><code>s</code> </th> 
         <td rowspan="2">abbreviated publication and access- / archive-dates </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row"><code>ss</code> </th>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row"><code>sy</code> </th> 
         <td>abbreviated publication dates; year-initial numeric access- / archive-dates (ymd) </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row"><code>y</code> </th> 
         <td rowspan="2">year-initial numeric publication, access- and archive-dates (ymd);<br>cs1|2 cannot reformat Month YYYY, Season YYYY, date-ranges of any form, or <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a> dates into ymd format </td>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row"><code>yy</code> </th>
        </tr>
       </tbody>
      </table> 
      <p>Example: to have the CS1/CS2 templates in an article render their publication dates in the long form (fully spelled-out month names) with access-/archive-dates rendered in short form (abbreviated month names), write: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Use_dmy_dates" title="Template:Use dmy dates">use dmy dates</a>|date=June 2021|cs1-dates=ls}}</code>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>This documentation page has <code>{{use dmy dates|date=June 2021|cs1-dates=y}}</code> at the top of this section so this cs1 template will render with ymd dates: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <code>{{cite web |title=Example Webpage |url=//example.com |website=Example |date=31 October 2017 |access-date=Dec 5, 2017}}</code> 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r999302996"><cite class="citation web cs1"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//example.com">"Example Webpage"</a>. <i>Example</i>. 2017-10-31<span class="reference-accessdate">. Retrieved <span class="nowrap">2017-12-05</span></span>.</cite><span title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Ajournal&amp;rft.genre=unknown&amp;rft.jtitle=Example&amp;rft.atitle=Example+Webpage&amp;rft.date=2017-10-31&amp;rft_id=%2F%2Fexample.com&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3AHelp%3ACitation+Style+1" class="Z3988"></span>
         </dd>
        </dl>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>This global setting may be overridden in individual CS1/CS2 templates by use of <code class="nowrap" style="">|df=</code>; abbreviated date forms are not supported by <code class="nowrap" style="">|df=</code>. </p>
      <p><i lang="la" title="Latin-language text">Nota bene</i>: CS1/CS2 auto-date formatting does not apply when previewing an article section that does not contain a <span class="nowrap"><code>{{use xxx dates}}</code></span> template. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles_and_chapters">Titles and chapters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Titles and chapters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>title</b>: The title of the cited source. Titles are displayed in italics, except for short works such as a <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_press_release" title="Template:Cite press release">cite press release</a>}}</code> and cited articles in <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_news" title="Template:Cite news">cite news</a>}}</code>, <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_journal" title="Template:Cite journal">cite journal</a>}}</code>, <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_magazine" title="Template:Cite magazine">cite magazine</a>}}</code>, <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_web" title="Template:Cite web">cite web</a>}}</code>, <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_conference" title="Template:Cite conference">cite conference</a>}}</code>, and <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_podcast" title="Template:Cite podcast">cite podcast</a>}}</code>, where the title is shown in quotation marks. Use title case unless the cited source covers a scientific, legal or other technical topic and sentence case is the predominant style in journals on that topic. Use either <a href="/wiki/MOS:CT" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:CT">title case</a> or sentence case consistently throughout the article. Do not omit a leading "The" from the title. Subtitles are typically separated from titles with ": " though " – " is also used. <a href="/wiki/MOS:TM" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:TM">As with trademarks</a>, Wikipedia <em>does not</em> attempt to emulate any stylistic flourishes used by the cited source's publisher, such as ALL-CAPS, all-lower-case, <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r920966791">.mw-parser-output span.smallcaps{font-variant:small-caps}.mw-parser-output span.smallcaps-smaller{font-size:85%}</style><span class="smallcaps">Small Caps</span>, etc.; use either standard title case or sentence case consistently. If the cited source is itself notable and has a Wikipedia article, the title can be wikilinked. Wikilinking the title will make it impossible to use the "url" parameter to link to an external copy of the cited source, so only do this when citing works that do not need to be externally linked. A link to the actual source is preferred to a link to a Wikipedia article about the source.</li> 
       <li><b>script-title</b>: Languages that do not use a Latin-based alphabet, Arabic, Chinese, Cyrillic, Greek, Hebrew, Japanese, Korean, etc., should not be italicized and may also read <a href="/wiki/Right-to-left" class="mw-redirect" title="Right-to-left">right-to-left</a> (RTL). To include titles in these languages, use <b>script-title</b>. Titles in <b>script-title</b> are wrapped in special HTML markup to isolate RTL script from adjacent left-to-right text. Part of that special markup is a language attribute that browsers can use to assist in the proper display of the script. Editors must add a prefix to the script that will identify the language. The prefix is one of the <a href="/wiki/Help:Citation_Style_1#|script-<param>=_language_codes" title="Help:Citation Style 1">supported language codes</a> followed by a colon: <code class="nowrap" style="">|script-title=ar:<bdi>???????</bdi></code>. Unrecognized codes are ignored and will display in the rendered citation.</li> 
       <li><b>trans-title</b>: If the cited source is in a foreign language, an English translation of the title can be given here. This field will be displayed in square brackets after the title and will be linked to <code class="nowrap" style="">|url=</code> if used.</li> 
       <li><b>chapter</b>: The title of the cited chapter from the source, written in full. Displayed in quotes before the title. For websites arranged in sections the "at" parameter serves a similar function: <code class="nowrap" style="">|at=Featured News</code></li> 
       <li><b>script-chapter</b>: Languages that do not use a Latin-based alphabet, Arabic, Chinese, Cyrillic, Greek, Hebrew, Japanese, Korean, etc., may possibly read <a href="/wiki/Right-to-left" class="mw-redirect" title="Right-to-left">right-to-left</a> (RTL). To include chapter titles in these languages, use <b>script-chapter</b>. Chapter titles in <b>script-chapter</b> are wrapped in special HTML markup to isolate RTL script from adjacent left-to-right text. Part of that special markup is a language attribute that browsers can use to assist in the proper display of the script. Editors must add a prefix to the script that will identify the language. The prefix is one of the <a href="/wiki/Help:Citation_Style_1#|script-<param>=_language_codes" title="Help:Citation Style 1">supported language codes</a> followed by a colon: <code class="nowrap" style="">|script-chapter=ar:<bdi>???????</bdi></code>. Unrecognized codes are ignored and will display in the rendered citation. This field will be displayed following the transliterated title.</li> 
       <li><b>trans-chapter</b>: If the cited source is in a foreign language, an English translation of the cited chapter title can be given here. This field will be displayed in square brackets within the quotation marks which enclose the chapter field.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Type">Type</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Type">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>type</b>: Specifies the type of work cited. Appears in parentheses immediately after the title. Some templates use a default that can be overridden; example: {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_press_release" title="Template:Cite press release">cite press release</a>}} will show "(Press release)" by default. Other useful values are: Review, Systemic review, Report, Abstract, Meta-analysis, Original article, Oral history, Email, Website, Text, Document. Alias: <b>medium</b>, in {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_AV_media" title="Template:Cite AV media">cite AV media</a>}}, where typical values are: Motion picture, Moving image, Television production, Videotape, DVD, Blu-ray, Trailer, CD, Radio broadcast, Podcast, Sound.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Language">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li><b id="csdoc_language">language</b>: The language (or a comma-separated list of the languages) in which the source is written, as either the <a href="/wiki/ISO_639" title="ISO 639">ISO 639</a> language code (preferred) or the full language name. Examples: <code class="nowrap" style="">|language=ru</code>; <code class="nowrap" style="">|lang=fr, pt-br</code>; <code class="nowrap" style="">|lang=Russian</code>; <code class="nowrap" style="">|language=French, Portuguese</code>. <a href="/wiki/Template:Citation_Style_documentation/language/doc" title="Template:Citation Style documentation/language/doc">See the list of supported codes and names</a>. Do not use templates or wikilinks. Displays in parentheses with "in" before the language name or names. When the only source language is English, no language is displayed in the citation. The use of languages recognized by the citation module adds the page to the appropriate subcategory of <a href="/wiki/Category:CS1_foreign_language_sources" title="Category:CS1 foreign language sources">Category:CS1 foreign language sources</a>. Because cs1|2 templates are often copied from en.wiki to other wikis, the use of language codes is preferred so that language names render in the correct language and form: espagnol at a French-language wiki instead of the English word "Spanish". Aliases: <b>lang</b></li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_and_publisher">Work and publisher</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Work and publisher">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CITEHOW" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CITEHOW">WP:CITEHOW</a>
      </div> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>work</b>: Used by some templates such as {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_web" title="Template:Cite web">cite web</a>}} (where it is aliased to <b>website</b>), {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_news" title="Template:Cite news">cite news</a>}} (aliased to <b>newspaper</b>), {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_magazine" title="Template:Cite magazine">cite magazine</a>}} (aliased to <b>magazine</b>), {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_journal" title="Template:Cite journal">cite journal</a>}} (aliased to <b>journal</b>), and others where the citation is usually to a specific item (given in the "title" parameter) found in a larger work (this "work" parameter), most commonly an article in a website or print periodical, or an episode in a TV series. <em>Do not confuse this with the "publisher" parameter</em>, which is for the publishing company. If the work is notable and has an article in Wikipedia, it should be wiki-linked at first appearance in citations in the article. If the "title" is already linked-to externally, do not externally link to the "work". If the work title as given by the site/publication would be exactly or substantially the same as the name of the publisher, do not use the "publisher" parameter (see below for more detail).</li>
      </ul> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        On websites, in most cases "work" is the name of the website (as usually given in the logo/banner area of the site, and/or appearing in the <code class="nowrap" style="">&lt;title&gt;</code> of the homepage, which may appear as the page title in your browser tab, depending on browser). Do not append ".com" or the like if the site's actual title does not include it (thus <code class="nowrap" style="">|work=[[Salon (magazine)|Salon]]</code>, not <code>Salon.com</code>). If no clear title can be identified, or the title explicitly is the domain name, then use the site's domain name. Do not falsify the work's name by adding descriptive verbiage like "Website of [Publisher]" or "[Publisher]'s Homepage". Capitalize for reading clarity, and omit "www.", e.g. convert "www.veterinaryresourcesuk.com" to "VeterinaryResourcesUK.com".
       </dd> 
       <dd>
        Many journals use highly abbreviated titles when citing other journals (e.g. <i>J Am Vet Med</i> for <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_of_the_American_Veterinary_Medical_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association">Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association</a></i>) because specialists in the field the journal covers usually already know what these abbreviations mean. Our readers usually do not, so these abbreviations should always be expanded.
       </dd> 
       <dd>
        If the titled item being cited is part of some other larger work, as in a book in a series, a special issue of a periodical, or a sub-site at a domain (e.g., you are citing the law school's section of a university's website system), it is usually better to use the name of that more specific work than just that of the entire larger work. Various citation templates provide separate fields for such information, e.g. <code class="nowrap" style="">|chapter=</code><code class="nowrap" style="">|title=</code><code class="nowrap" style="">|volume=</code><code class="nowrap" style="">|series=</code> in <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_book" title="Template:Cite book">Cite book</a>}}</code>. If the nature of the work and its relation to the site, book, or other context in which it is found is complicated or confusing, simply explain the situation after the citation template and before the <code class="nowrap">&lt;/ref&gt;</code> that closes the citation.
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>publisher</b>: the name of the organization that actually published the source. The field should not include the corporate designation such as "Ltd" or "Inc.", unless some ambiguity would result or the organization is usually known with that designation even in everyday use (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple Inc.</a>, which otherwise might be confused with <a href="/wiki/Apple_Records" title="Apple Records">Apple Records</a> and other publishers). "Publisher", "Publishing" and "Publications" can be abbreviated "Pubr.", "Pubg." and "Pubs." respectively, but some templates in this series include a period (full-stop) immediately after this parameter, so the period may have to be omitted; check the output if you abbreviate here. They are usually safe to omit, but are usefully included where the publisher's name might be confusing without it. This is most often the case when the publisher's name is something like "Joshua Martin Publications", which without the designation might be mistaken for a co-author/editor. A leading "The" can generally be omitted, again unless confusion might result (e.g., for <a href="/wiki/The_International_Cat_Association" title="The International Cat Association">The International Cat Association</a>, "The" is part of their official acronym, TICA). If the publisher is notable and has an article independent of the "work", the "publisher" parameter can include a wiki-link to that article, but should never externally link to the publisher's website. Whether the publisher needs to be included depends to an extent on the type of work and sometimes on its recognizability. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">WP:Citing sources</a>, and most off-Wikipedia citation guides, suggest that it should be used for books (even famous ones), but not necessarily other works. The "publisher" parameter should not be included for widely-known mainstream news sources, for major academic journals, or where it would be the same or mostly the same as the work. For example, the "publisher" parameter should be omitted in these examples: 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <code class="nowrap" style="">|work=[[Amazon.com]]</code><code class="nowrap" style="color: #8B0000;">|publisher=Amazon Inc.</code>
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          <code class="nowrap" style="">|newspaper=The Aberdeen Times</code><code class="nowrap" style="color: #8B0000;">|publisher=The Aberdeen Times</code>
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          <code class="nowrap" style="">|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]</code><code class="nowrap" style="color: #8B0000;">|publisher=The New York Times Company</code>
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          <code class="nowrap" style="">|newspaper=[[USA Today]]</code><code class="nowrap" style="color: #8B0000;">|publisher=[[Gannett Company]]</code>
         </dd> 
         <dd>
          <code class="nowrap" style="">|journal=[[Nature (journal)|Nature]]</code><code class="nowrap" style="color: #8B0000;">|publisher=[[Nature Research]]</code>
         </dd>
        </dl></li>
      </ul> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        If the work is self-published, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SELFPUB" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SELFPUB">this is a very important fact</a> about <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RS">potential reliability of the source</a>, and needs to be specified; no consensus exists for the exact value of <code class="nowrap" style="">|publisher=</code> in such a case, but some printed style guides suggest "author", while many Wikipedia editors have used "self-published" for increased clarity. When an exhaustive attempt to discover the name of the publisher (try <a href="/wiki/Whois" class="mw-redirect" title="Whois">whois</a> for websites, and <a href="/wiki/WorldCat" title="WorldCat">WorldCat</a> for books, etc.) fails, use <code class="nowrap" style="">|publisher=&lt;!--Unspecified by source.--&gt;</code> to explicitly indicate that this was checked, so other editors do not waste time duplicating your fruitless efforts. Do not guess at the publisher when this information is not clear. See next entry for co-published works and how to specify multiple publishers and their locations.
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>location</b> (alias <code class="nowrap" style="">|publication-place=</code>): Geographical place of publication (<em>not</em> where you found your copy, nor location of material in the source). This is usually <var style="padding-right: 1px;">City, Country</var>, or <var style="padding-right: 1px;">City, US State</var>. The city name by itself can be used for world-recognized cities like New York, London (except in articles about <a href="/wiki/London,_Ontario" title="London, Ontario">Canadian</a> topics), Paris, Tokyo. Simply having a unique name does not mean it is globally recognizable; e.g., many people do not know where <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> is, especially if they are old enough that it was called <a href="/wiki/Bombay" class="mw-redirect" title="Bombay">Bombay</a> for much of their lives. If in doubt, be more not less specific, since "Toronto, Canada" and "San Francisco, California" do not actually hurt anything. Be more specific when a <var style="padding-right: 1px;">City, Country</var> would be ambiguous, e.g. <code class="nowrap" style="">|location=Hanley, Staffordshire, UK</code>, versus <code class="nowrap" style="">|location=Hanley, Worcestershire, UK</code>. Do not use sub-national postal abbreviations ("DE", "Wilts", etc.), per <a href="/wiki/MOS:POSTABBR" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:POSTABBR">MOS:POSTABBR</a>. The location parameter should be omitted when it is implied by the name of the work, e.g. <i>The Sydney Morning Herald</i>. The location parameter should be used when the location is part of a common appellation of but not the actual title of a periodical. For example, the newspaper sometimes called the <i>New York Daily News</i> is actually <a href="/wiki/Daily_News_(New_York)" class="mw-redirect" title="Daily News (New York)"><i>Daily News</i></a> and can be entered with <code class="nowrap" style="">|newspaper=Daily News</code> <code class="nowrap" style="">|location=New York</code>, which yields: <i>Daily News</i>. New York. A similar example is <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> of London (contrast <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>). Per <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SAYWHEREYOUGOTIT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SAYWHEREYOUGOTIT">WP:SAYWHEREYOUGOTIT</a>, provide the location of the edition you are using (e.g., if a publisher has operations in both New York and London and you have the London-published edition, use <code class="nowrap" style="">|location=London</code>, even if the publisher's corporate HQ is in New York). If your edition specifically gives multiple locations, this can be indicated with, e.g., <code class="nowrap" style="">|location=New York / London</code>. This same technique can be used for co-published works, e.g.: <code class="nowrap" style="">|location=Oakbrook Terrace, Illinois / Los Angeles</code><code class="nowrap" style="">|publisher=[[Council of Science Editors]] / [[Loyola Marymount University]] Press</code>; just get them in corresponding order in both parameters. The templates do <em>not</em> have separate <code class="nowrap" style="color: #8B0000;">|location1=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="color: #8B0000;">|publisher1=</code>, etc., parameters. For historical publications that are still worth citing and still findable (e.g. via book digitizers – see the "via" parameter below), do not confuse the <em>printer</em> (printing press owner-operator) with the <em>publisher</em> (organization or person that sponsored the work); an edition may have a printer's name in larger type than the publisher, but for citation purposes we care about the publisher. If the distinction cannot be determined for certain in a particular case, list both. While some off-site publishers lean toward omitting publishing locations, they can serve more than purely bibliographical purposes on Wikipedia (e.g. an overabundance of material published in one place in an article about another place may reveal an editorial bias).</li> 
       <li><b>publication-date</b>: Date of publication when different from the date the work was written. Displays only if date or year are defined and only if different, else publication-date is used and displayed as date. Use the same format as other dates in the article; do not wikilink. Follows publisher; if work is not defined, then publication-date is preceded by "published" and enclosed in parenthesis.</li> 
       <li><b>via</b> (optional): Name of the content deliverer (when they are <em>not</em> the publisher). "via" is not a replacement for "publisher", but provides additional detail. It may be used when the content deliverer presents the source in a format other than the original, or when the URL provided does not make clear the identity of the deliverer, or as suggested in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:The_Wikipedia_Library" title="Wikipedia:The Wikipedia Library">WP:The Wikipedia Library</a>, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Credo_accounts/Citations" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Credo accounts/Citations">WP:Credo accounts/Citations</a>. See also <a href="#Registration_or_subscription_required">§&nbsp;Registration or subscription required</a>. Typical uses of this parameter are identification of a book-scanning and -databasing project such as those provided by the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Archive" title="Internet Archive">Internet Archive</a>, <a href="/wiki/Project_Gutenberg" title="Project Gutenberg">Project Gutenberg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Google_Books" title="Google Books">Google Books</a>; journal indexing and search services through which we commonly find academic articles, e.g. <a href="/wiki/PubMed_Central" title="PubMed Central">PubMed Central</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paperity" title="Paperity">Paperity</a>, and <a href="/wiki/JSTOR" title="JSTOR">JSTOR</a>; and other aggregators or indexers of previously-published content, such as <a href="/wiki/Dictionary.com" title="Dictionary.com">Dictionary.com</a>. Use via only when a standard identifier cannot be used (see <a href="#Identifiers">§&nbsp;Identifiers</a>). Example: <code>{{Cite dictionary |entry=skeptic |entry-url=https://www.dictionary.com/browse/skeptic?s=t#collins-section |title=[[Collins English Dictionary]] |edition=Complete &amp; Unabridged Digital |date=2012 |location=London |publisher=[[HarperCollins]] |via=[[Dictionary.com]] |access-date=4 June 2021}}</code></li>
      </ul>

      <p>An editor may use any <u>one</u> of the following parameters in a given citation to refer to the specific page(s) or place in a cited source that contains the information that supports the article text. If more than one of the following parameters are used in the same citation, the error message <span style="color:red">Extra <code>|pages=</code> or <code>|at=</code> (</span><a href="/wiki/Help:CS1_errors#extra_pages" title="Help:CS1 errors">help</a><span style="color:red">)</span> will display in the published citation. When more than one of the following parameters is used in error, <code class="nowrap" style="">|page=</code> overrides both <code class="nowrap" style="">|pages=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|at=</code>; <code class="nowrap" style="">|pages=</code> overrides <code class="nowrap" style="">|at=</code>. To resolve the error, remove extra parameters of this type until only one remains in the affected citation. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>page</b>: page in the cited source containing the information that supports the article text, for example <code class="nowrap" style="">|page=52</code> . 
        <ul>
         <li><span class="anchor" id="pagehyphen"></span><b>Note:</b> For a hyphenated page, use <code class="nowrap" style="">|page=12{{hyphen}}34</code>. This will not only properly display a hyphen, but also reduce the likelihood that an editor/bot will convert this to <code class="nowrap" style="">|pages=12{{endash}}34</code> by mistake.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><span class="anchor" id="csdoc_pages"></span><b>pages</b>: pages in the cited source containing the information that supports the article text. Separate page ranges with an <a href="/wiki/En_dash#En_dash" class="mw-redirect" title="En dash">en dash</a>: – , for example <code class="nowrap" style="">|pages=236–239</code> . Separate non-sequential pages with a comma, for example <code class="nowrap" style="">|pages=157, 159</code> . The form <code class="nowrap" style="">|pages=461, 466–467</code> is used when you are citing both non-contiguous and sequential pages. 
        <ul>
         <li><b>Note:</b> CS1 citations do not record the total number of pages in a cited source; do not use this parameter for that purpose.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><b>at</b>: place in the cited source containing the information that supports the article text when a page number is not given, is inappropriate or is insufficient. Common examples include column or col., paragraph or para. and section or sec. Depending on the source being cited, other indicators may be appropriate to use with the <code class="nowrap" style="">|at=</code> parameter including, but not limited to, track, hours, minutes and seconds, act, scene, canto, book, part, folio, stanza, back cover, liner notes, indicia, colophon, dust jacket, etc. Examples include <code class="nowrap" style="">|at=Column 2</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|at=Paragraph 5</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|at=Back cover</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|at=Act III, Scene 2</code>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>If the same source is reused with different pages, separate citations must be created. A way around this problem is to use a short citation <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Sfn" title="Template:Sfn">sfn</a>}}</code>, or <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Rp" title="Template:Rp">rp</a>}}</code> to provide linked page number citations. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Edition_identifiers">Edition identifiers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Edition identifiers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>edition</b>: Identifies the particular edition of a cited source when the source has more than one edition, such as "2nd", "Revised", etc. Note that this parameter automatically displays " ed." after your entry. For example, <code class="nowrap" style="">|edition=Revised third</code> displays as: Revised third ed. The edition parameter can be omitted if there is no content difference between two editions of the same work; e.g. if a book was identically published in the UK and the US except, for example, ISBN number and cover art, it is not necessary to indicate either "UK" or "US" edition; or if citing minute:seconds of a film available in both a regular edition and a "Special Limited Collector's Edition", if the running time and cut are the same, it is not necessary to cite the particular edition.</li> 
       <li><b>series</b>: When the source is part of a series, such as a book series or a journal where the issue numbering has restarted. For journal series, <code class="nowrap" style="">|series=</code> should be limited to <code class="mw-highlight mw-highlight-lang-text mw-content-ltr" id="" style="" dir="ltr">Original/New Series</code>, <code class="mw-highlight mw-highlight-lang-text mw-content-ltr" id="" style="" dir="ltr">First/Second/Third/... Series</code>, or similar.<sup id="cite_ref-c17_14.126_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-c17_14.126-4">[Note 3]</a></sup></li> 
       <li><span class="anchor" id="csdoc_volume"></span><b>volume</b>: For a source published in several volumes. This field is displayed in bold after the title and series parameters. An alternative is to include the volume information in the title parameter after the main title.</li> 
       <li><span class="anchor" id="csdoc_issue"></span><b>issue</b>: When the source is part of a series that is published periodically. Alias: <b>number</b>. Please choose either <code class="nowrap" style="">|issue=</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|number=</code> depending on what nomenclature is actually used in a publication.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="External_links">External links</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: External links">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>url</b>: Adds a link to the title</li> 
       <li><b>chapter-url</b>: Adds a link to the chapter.</li> 
       <li><b>format</b>: Format of the document at its URL (e.g., PDF, XLS, etc.). Do not specify for HTML as it is implied as the default. Do not use this parameter for completely different purposes, with annotations such as "fee required" or "reprint"; its intent is to notify readers what the file format is (e.g. in case some browsers might have trouble with it, or some might prefer to save the link to disk rather than open it in the browser). Access restrictions can be shown using the appropriate <code class="nowrap" style="">|url-access=</code> parameter, see <a href="#Registration_or_subscription_required">Registration or subscription required</a>. Other notes about reprintings, etc., should be placed after the template, and before <code class="nowrap" style="">&lt;/ref&gt;</code>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h4><span id="Using_.7Cformat.3D"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Using_|format=">Using |format=</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Using |format=">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>When MediaWiki encounters an external link URL with a '.pdf' or '.PDF' <a href="/wiki/Filename_extension" title="Filename extension">extension</a>, it renders the external link with a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//example.com/document.pdf">PDF icon</a> in place of the usual <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//example.com/document">external-link icon</a>. To make rendered cs1|2 citations that link to PDF documents somewhat more accessible, cs1|2 automatically adds a parenthetical PDF annotation so that those readers using screen-reader technology can know the type of the linked file. This is imperfect because some on-line sources redirect .pdf URLs to .html landing pages (this is common for PDF documents behind paywalls or registration barriers). Because the parenthetical PDF annotation happens automatically, editors are not required to set <code class="nowrap" style="">|format=PDF</code>, though doing so causes no harm. The <code class="nowrap" style="">|format=PDF</code> parameter may be deleted as part of a more substantial edit but editors should consider that many cs1|2 templates are copied from en.Wikipedia to other-language Wikipedias when articles here are translated to that other language. Do not assume that other-language Wikipedias use up-to-date cs1|2 templates; many do not so removing <code class="nowrap" style="">|format=PDF</code> here can affect readers/translators at other Wikipedias. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_sources">Online sources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Online sources">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main pages: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:External_links" title="Wikipedia:External links">Wikipedia:External links</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">Wikipedia:Citing sources</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Links to sources are regarded as conveniences and are not required, except when citing Web-only sources. There are many digital libraries with works that may be used as sources. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Links should be to full versions of the source.</li> 
       <li>Online sources that require payment or subscription may be included per <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">Wikipedia:Verifiability</a>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Do not link to: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Sites that do not have permission to republish the work or which otherwise violate copyright.</li> 
       <li>Commercial sites such as Amazon, unless no alternative exists.</li> 
       <li>Reviews of the work.</li> 
       <li>Very short extracts such as Google Books snippet view where there is not enough context to verify the content, unless the entire work is also freely available there. (See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BOOKLINKS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BOOKLINKS">WP:BOOKLINKS</a>)</li>
      </ul> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Link_formats">Link formats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Link formats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>Links should be kept as simple as possible. For example, when performing a search for a Google Book, the link for <i>Monty Python and Philosophy</i> would look like: </p>

        <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://books.google.com/books?id=NPDgD546-doC&amp;pg=PP1#v=onepage&amp;q&amp;f=false">https://books.google.com/books?id=NPDgD546-doC&amp;pg=PP1#v=onepage&amp;q&amp;f=false</a>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>But can be trimmed to: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://books.google.com/?id=NPDgD546-doC&amp;pg=PP1#v=onepage&amp;q&amp;f=false">https://books.google.com/?id=NPDgD546-doC&amp;pg=PP1#v=onepage&amp;q&amp;f=false</a>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>or: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://books.google.com/?id=NPDgD546-doC&amp;printsec=frontcover">https://books.google.com/?id=NPDgD546-doC&amp;printsec=frontcover</a>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>or: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://books.google.com/?id=NPDgD546-doC">https://books.google.com/?id=NPDgD546-doC</a> (if Google Books does not provide the cover page).
       </dd>
      </dl>

      <p>A direct link to a specific page may be used if supported by the host. For example, the link to page 172 of <i>Monty Python and Philosophy</i> on Google Books: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://books.google.com/?id=NPDgD546-doC&amp;pg=PA172">https://books.google.com/?id=NPDgD546-doC&amp;pg=PA172</a>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>like so: </p> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <code>|page=[https://books.google.com/?id=wPQelKFNA5MC&amp;pg=PA172 172]</code>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_characters">Special characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Special characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>URLs must begin with a supported <a href="/wiki/URI_scheme" class="mw-redirect" title="URI scheme">URI scheme</a>. <code>http://</code> and <code>https://</code> will be supported by all browsers; however, <code>ftp://</code>, <code>gopher://</code>, <code>irc://</code>, <code>ircs://</code>, <code>mailto:</code> and <code>news:</code> may require a plug-in or an external application and should normally be avoided. <a href="/wiki/IPv6" title="IPv6">IPv6</a> host-names are currently not supported. </p>
      <p>If URLs in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_templates#Examples" title="Wikipedia:Citation templates">citation template parameters</a> contain certain characters, then they will not display and link correctly. Those characters need to be <a href="/wiki/Percent-encoded" class="mw-redirect" title="Percent-encoded">percent-encoded</a>. For example, a space must be replaced by <code>%20</code>. To encode the URL, replace the following characters with: </p> 
      <table class="wikitable"> 
       <tbody>
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row">Character </th> 
         <th scope="col">space </th> 
         <th scope="col">" </th> 
         <th scope="col">' </th> 
         <th scope="col">&lt; </th> 
         <th scope="col">&gt; </th> 
         <th scope="col">[ </th> 
         <th scope="col">] </th> 
         <th scope="col">{ </th> 
         <th scope="col">| </th> 
         <th scope="col">} </th>
        </tr> 
        <tr> 
         <th scope="row">Encoding </th> 
         <td>%20</td> 
         <td>%22</td> 
         <td>%27</td> 
         <td>%3C</td> 
         <td>%3E</td> 
         <td>%5B</td> 
         <td>%5D</td> 
         <td>%7B</td> 
         <td>%7C</td> 
         <td>%7D </td>
        </tr>
       </tbody>
      </table> 
      <p>Single apostrophes do not need to be encoded; however, unencoded multiples will be parsed as italic or bold markup. Single curly closing braces also do not need to be encoded; however, an unencoded pair will be parsed as the double closing braces for the template transclusion. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Access_date">Access date</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Access date">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>access-date</b>: The full date when the content pointed to by <b>url</b> was last verified to support the text in the article; do not wikilink; requires <b>url</b>; use the same format as other access and archive dates in the article's citations. It is <i>not required for linked documents that do not change.</i> For example, <b>access-date</b> is not required for links to copies of published research papers accessed via DOI or a published book, but should be used for links to news articles on commercial websites (these can change from time to time, even if they are also published in a physical medium). Note that <b>access-date</b> is the date that the URL was checked to not just be working, but to support the assertion being cited (which the current version of the page may not do). Can be <a href="/wiki/Help:Citation_Style_1/accessdate" title="Help:Citation Style 1/accessdate">hidden or styled</a> by registered editors. Alias: <b>accessdate</b>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Web_archives">Web archives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Web archives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot">Wikipedia:Link rot</a>
      </div> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>archive-url</b>. Alias: <b>archiveurl</b>.</li> 
       <li><b>archive-date</b>. Alias: <b>archivedate</b>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>The original link may become unavailable. When an archived version is located, the original URL is retained and <code class="nowrap" style="">|archive-url=</code> is added with a link to an archived copy of a web page, usually from services like WebCite and the Internet Archive. <code class="nowrap" style="">|archive-date=</code> must be added to show the date the page was archived, not the date the link was added. When <code class="nowrap" style="">|archive-url=</code> is used, <code class="nowrap" style="">|url=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|archive-date=</code> are required, else an error will show. When an archived link is used, the citation displays with the title linked to the archive and the original link at the end: 
       <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r999302996"><cite class="citation book cs1"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130501102912/http://books.google.com/books?id=wPQelKFNA5MC"><i>Monty Python and Philosophy</i></a>. Archived from <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://books.google.com/books?id=wPQelKFNA5MC">the original</a> on 2013-05-01.</cite><span title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Abook&amp;rft.genre=book&amp;rft.btitle=Monty+Python+and+Philosophy&amp;rft_id=https%3A%2F%2Fbooks.google.com%2Fbooks%3Fid%3DwPQelKFNA5MC&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3AHelp%3ACitation+Style+1" class="Z3988"></span> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>url-status</b>: To change the order with the title retaining the original link and the archive linked at the end, set <code class="nowrap" style="">|url-status=live</code>:</li>
      </ul> 
      <dl>
       <dd>
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r999302996"><cite class="citation book cs1"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://books.google.com/books?id=wPQelKFNA5MC"><i>Monty Python and Philosophy</i></a>. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130501102912/http://books.google.com/books?id=wPQelKFNA5MC">Archived</a> from the original on 2013-05-01.</cite><span title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Abook&amp;rft.genre=book&amp;rft.btitle=Monty+Python+and+Philosophy&amp;rft_id=https%3A%2F%2Fbooks.google.com%2Fbooks%3Fid%3DwPQelKFNA5MC&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3AHelp%3ACitation+Style+1" class="Z3988"></span>
       </dd>
      </dl> 
      <p>When the original URL has been usurped for the purposes of spam, advertising, or is otherwise unsuitable, setting <code class="nowrap" style="">|url-status=unfit</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|url-status=usurped</code> suppresses display of the original URL (but <code class="nowrap" style="">|url=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|archive-url=</code> are still required). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Identifiers">Identifiers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Identifiers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>The following identifiers create links and are designed to accept a single value. Using multiple values or other text will break the link and/or invalidate the identifier. In general, the parameters should include only the variable part of the identifier, e.g. <code>|rfc=822</code> or <code>|pmc=345678</code>. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b id="csdoc_arxiv">arxiv</b>: <a href="/wiki/ArXiv" title="ArXiv">arXiv</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://arxiv.org/help/arxiv_identifier">identifier</a>; for example: <code>|arxiv=hep-th/9205027</code> (before April 2007) or <code>|arxiv=0706.0001</code> (April 2007 – December 2014) or <code>|arxiv=1501.00001</code> (since January 2015). Do not include extraneous file extensions like ".pdf" or ".html". Aliases: <b>eprint</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_asin">asin</b>: <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Standard_Identification_Number" title="Amazon Standard Identification Number">Amazon Standard Identification Number</a>; if first character of <b id="">asin</b> value is a digit, use <b id="">isbn</b>. Because this link favours one specific distributor, include it only if standard identifiers are not available. Example <code>|asin=B00005N5PF</code>. Aliases: <b>ASIN</b>. 
        <ul>
         <li><b id="csdoc_asin-tld">asin-tld</b>: ASIN top-level domain for Amazon sites other than the US; valid values: <code>ae</code>, <code>au</code>, <code>br</code>, <code>ca</code>, <code>cn</code>, <code>de</code>, <code>es</code>, <code>fr</code>, <code>in</code>, <code>it</code>, <code>jp</code>, <code>mx</code>, <code>nl</code>, <code>pl</code>, <code>sa</code>, <code>se</code>, <code>sg</code>, <code>tr</code>, <code>uk</code>. Aliases: none.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_bibcode">bibcode</b>: <a href="/wiki/Bibcode" title="Bibcode">bibcode</a>; used by a number of astronomical data systems; for example: <code>1974AJ.....79..819H</code>. Aliases: none.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_biorxiv">biorxiv</b>: <a href="/wiki/BioRxiv" title="BioRxiv">bioRxiv</a> id, as in the entire DOI (e.g. <code>10.1101/078733</code> for <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://biorxiv.org/content/early/2016/10/01/078733">http://biorxiv.org/content/early/2016/10/01/078733</a> or <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://doi.org/10.1101/078733">https://doi.org/10.1101/078733</a>; <code>10.1101/2020.07.24.220400</code> for <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://doi.org/10.1101/2020.07.24.220400">https://doi.org/10.1101/2020.07.24.220400</a>). Aliases: none.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_citeseerx">citeseerx</b>: <a href="/wiki/CiteSeerX" title="CiteSeerX">CiteSeerX</a> id, a string of digits and dots found in a CiteSeerX URL (e.g. <code>10.1.1.176.341</code> for <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://citeseerx.ist.psu.edu/viewdoc/summary?doi=10.1.1.176.341">http://citeseerx.ist.psu.edu/viewdoc/summary?doi=10.1.1.176.341</a>). Aliases: none.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_doi">doi</b>: <a href="/wiki/Digital_object_identifier" title="Digital object identifier">Digital object identifier</a>; for example: <code>10.1038/news070508-7</code>. It is checked to ensure it begins with (<code class="mw-highlight mw-highlight-lang-text mw-content-ltr" id="" style="" dir="ltr">10.</code>). Aliases: <b>DOI</b>. 
        <ul>
         <li>Supports accept-this-as-written markup to indicate <i>valid</i> DOIs using a non-standard format, see <a href="#ATAWM">below</a>.</li> 
         <li><b id="csdoc_doi_brokendate">doi-broken-date</b>: Date a valid DOI was found to be non-working/inactive at <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://doi.org">https://doi.org</a>. Use the same format as other dates in the article. Aliases: none.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_eissn">eissn</b>: <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Serial_Number" title="International Standard Serial Number">International Standard Serial Number</a> for the electronic media of a serial publication; eight characters may be split into two groups of four using a <a href="/wiki/Hyphen" title="Hyphen">hyphen</a>, but not an <a href="/wiki/Dash#En_dash" title="Dash">en dash</a> or a space; example <code>|eissn=1557-2986</code>. Aliases: <b>EISSN</b>. 
        <ul>
         <li>Supports accept-this-as-written markup to indicate <i>valid</i> eISSNs using a non-standard format, see <a href="#ATAWM">below</a>.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_hdl">hdl</b>: <a href="/wiki/Handle_System" title="Handle System">Handle System</a> identifier for digital objects and other resources on the Internet; example <code>|hdl=20.1000/100</code>. Aliases: <b>HDL</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_isbn">isbn</b>: <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Book_Number" title="International Standard Book Number">International Standard Book Number</a>; for example: <code>978-0-8126-9593-9</code>. <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1015928993">.mw-parser-output div.crossreference{padding-left:0}</style><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable crossreference">(See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ISBN" title="Wikipedia:ISBN">Wikipedia:ISBN</a> and <a href="/wiki/ISBN#Overview" class="mw-redirect" title="ISBN">ISBN §&nbsp;Overview</a>.)</span> Hyphens in the ISBN are optional, but preferred. Use the ISBN actually printed on or in the book. Use the 13-digit ISBN – beginning with 978 or 979 – when it is available. If only a 10-digit ISBN is printed on or in the book, use it. ISBNs can be found on the page with the publisher's information – usually the back of the title page – or beneath the barcode as a number beginning with 978 or 979 (barcodes beginning with any other numbers are not ISBNs). For sources with the older 9-digit SBN system, use <b id="">sbn</b>. Do not convert a 10-digit ISBN to 13-digit by just adding the 978 prefix; the last digit is a calculated check digit and just making changes to the numbers will make the ISBN invalid. This parameter should hold only the ISBN without <em>any</em> additional characters. It is checked for length, invalid characters – anything other than numbers, spaces, and hyphens, with "X" permitted as the last character in a 10-digit ISBN – and the proper check digit. Aliases: <b>ISBN</b>, <b>isbn13</b>, <b>ISBN13</b>. 
        <ul>
         <li>Supports accept-this-as-written markup to indicate <i>valid</i> ISBNs using a non-standard format, see <a href="#ATAWM">below</a>.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_ismn">ismn</b>: <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Music_Number" title="International Standard Music Number">International Standard Music Number</a>; for example: <code>979-0-9016791-7-7</code>. <a href="/wiki/Hyphens" class="mw-redirect" title="Hyphens">Hyphens</a> or spaces in the ISMN are optional. Use the ISMN actually printed on or in the work. This parameter should hold only the ISMN without <i>any</i> additional characters. It is checked for length, invalid characters – anything other than numbers, spaces, and hyphens – and the proper check digit. Aliases: <b>ISMN</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_issn">issn</b>: <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Serial_Number" title="International Standard Serial Number">International Standard Serial Number</a>; eight characters may be split into two groups of four using a <a href="/wiki/Hyphen" title="Hyphen">hyphen</a>, but not an <a href="/wiki/Dash#En_dash" title="Dash">en dash</a> or a space; example <code>|issn=2049-3630</code>. Aliases: <b>ISSN</b>. 
        <ul>
         <li>Supports accept-this-as-written markup to indicate <i>valid</i> ISSNs using a non-standard format, see <a href="#ATAWM">below</a>.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_jfm">jfm</b>: <i lang="de" title="German-language text"><a href="/wiki/Jahrbuch_%C3%BCber_die_Fortschritte_der_Mathematik" class="mw-redirect" title="Jahrbuch über die Fortschritte der Mathematik">Jahrbuch über die Fortschritte der Mathematik</a></i>; do not include "JFM" in the value; example <code>|jfm=53.0144.01</code>. Aliases: <b>JFM</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_jstor">jstor</b>: <a href="/wiki/JSTOR" title="JSTOR">JSTOR</a> reference number; for example: <code>|jstor=3793107</code>. Aliases: <b>JSTOR</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_lccn">lccn</b>: <a href="/wiki/Library_of_Congress_Control_Number" title="Library of Congress Control Number">Library of Congress Control Number</a>. When present, alphabetic prefix characters are to be lower case and without a space; example <code>|lccn=79-57364</code> or <code>|lccn=2004042477</code> or <code>|lccn=e09001178</code>. Aliases: <b>LCCN</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_mr">mr</b>: <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_Reviews" title="Mathematical Reviews">Mathematical Reviews</a>; example <code>|mr=630583</code>. Aliases: <b>MR</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_oclc">oclc</b>: <a href="/wiki/OCLC_Number" class="mw-redirect" title="OCLC Number">OCLC Number</a> for looking up publications in the <a href="/wiki/WorldCat" title="WorldCat">WorldCat</a> <a href="/wiki/Union_catalog" title="Union catalog">union catalog</a>; example <code>|oclc=9355469</code>. Aliases: <b>OCLC</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_ol">ol</b>: <a href="/wiki/Open_Library" title="Open Library">Open Library</a> identifier; do not include "OL" in the value; example <code>|ol=7030731M</code>. Aliases: <b>OL</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_osti">osti</b>: <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Scientific_and_Technical_Information" title="Office of Scientific and Technical Information">Office of Scientific and Technical Information</a>; example <code>|osti=4367507</code>. Aliases: <b>OSTI</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_pmc">pmc</b>: <a href="/wiki/PubMed_Central" title="PubMed Central">PubMed Central</a>; use article number for <a href="/wiki/Open_repository" class="mw-redirect" title="Open repository">open repository</a> full-text of a journal article, e.g. <code>|pmc=345678</code>. Do not include "PMC" in the value. 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1015928993"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable crossreference">See also the <b id="">pmid</b> parameter, below; these are two different identifiers.</span>. Aliases: <b>PMC</b>. 
        <ul>
         <li><b id="csdoc_Embargo">pmc-embargo-date</b>: Date that <b id="">pmc</b> goes live; if this date is in the future, then <b id="">pmc</b> is not linked until that date. Aliases: none.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_pmid">pmid</b>: <a href="/wiki/PubMed" title="PubMed">PubMed</a>; use unique identifier; example <code>|pmid=17322060</code> 
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1015928993"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable crossreference">See also the <b id="">pmc</b> parameter, above; these are two different identifiers.</span> Aliases: <b>PMID</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_rfc">rfc</b>: <a href="/wiki/Request_for_Comments" title="Request for Comments">Request for Comments</a>; example <code>|rfc=3143</code>. Aliases: <b>RFC</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_sbn">sbn</b>: <a href="/wiki/Standard_Book_Number" class="mw-redirect" title="Standard Book Number">Standard Book Number</a>; example <code>|sbn=356-02201-3</code>. Aliases: <b>SBN</b>. 
        <ul>
         <li>Supports accept-this-as-written markup to indicate <i>valid</i> SBNs using a non-standard format, see <a href="#ATAWM">below</a>.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_ssrn">ssrn</b>: <a href="/wiki/Social_Science_Research_Network" title="Social Science Research Network">Social Science Research Network</a>; example <code>|ssrn=1900856</code>. Aliases: <b>SSRN</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_s2cid">s2cid</b>: <a href="/wiki/Semantic_Scholar" title="Semantic Scholar">Semantic Scholar</a> corpus ID; example <code>|s2cid=37220927</code>. Aliases: <b>S2CID</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_zbl">zbl</b>: <span lang="de" style="font-style: normal;" title="German-language text"><a href="/wiki/Zentralblatt_MATH" title="Zentralblatt MATH">Zentralblatt MATH</a></span>; example <code>|zbl=0472.53010</code> For zbMATH search results like <code>JFM 35.0387.02</code> use <code>|jfm=35.0387.02</code>. Aliases: <b>ZBL</b>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="ATAWM"></span>In very rare cases, <i>valid</i> identifiers (f.e., as actually printed on publications) do not follow their defined standard format or use non-conforming checksums, which would typically cause an error message to be shown. Do not alter them to match a different checksum. In order to suppress the error message, some identifiers (<code class="nowrap" style="">|doi=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|eissn=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|isbn=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|issn=</code>, and <code class="nowrap" style="">|sbn=</code>) support a special <a href="/wiki/Help:Citation_Style_1#Accept-this-as-written_markup" title="Help:Citation Style 1">accept-this-as-written markup</a> which can be applied to disable the error-checking (as <code class="nowrap" style="">|&lt;<var style="padding-right: 1px;">param</var>&gt;=((&lt;<var style="padding-right: 1px;">value</var>&gt;))</code>). If the problem is down to a mere typographical error in a third-party source, correct the identifier value instead of overriding the error message. </p>
      <p>For some identifiers, it is possible to specify the <a href="/wiki/Help:Citation_Style_1#Registration_or_subscription_required" title="Help:Citation Style 1">access status</a> using the corresponding <code class="nowrap" style="">|&lt;<var style="padding-right: 1px;">param</var>&gt;-access=</code> parameter. </p>
      <p>For {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Cite_journal" title="Template:Cite journal">cite journal</a>}}, some identifiers (specifying free resources) will automatically be linked to the title when <code class="nowrap" style="">|url=</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|title-link=</code> are not used to specify a different link target. This behaviour can be overridden by one out of a number of special keywords for <code class="nowrap" style="">|title-link=</code> to manually select a specific source (<code class="nowrap" style="">|title-link=pmc</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|title-link=doi</code>) for auto-linking or to disable the feature (<code class="nowrap" style="">|title-link=none</code>). </p>
      <p>It is not necessary to specify a URL to a link <i>identical</i> to a link also produced by an identifier. The <code class="nowrap" style="">|url=</code> parameter (or <code class="nowrap" style="">|title-link=</code>) can then be used for providing a direct deep link to the corresponding document or a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#Convenience_links" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">convenience link</a> to a resource that would not otherwise be obviously accessible. </p>
      <p>A custom identifier can be specified through </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b id="csdoc_id">id</b>: A <a href="/wiki/Unique_identifier" title="Unique identifier">unique identifier</a>, used where none of the specialized identifiers are applicable; wikilink or use a template as applicable. For example, <code class="nowrap" style="">|id=NCJ 122967</code> will append "NCJ 122967" at the end of the citation. You can use templates such as <code class="nowrap" style="">|id={{NCJ|122967}}</code> to append <a href="/wiki/NCJ_(identifier)" class="mw-redirect" title="NCJ (identifier)">NCJ</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.ncjrs.gov/App/Publications/abstract.aspx?ID=122967">122967</a> instead.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Registration_or_subscription_required">Registration or subscription required</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Registration or subscription required">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><span id="csdoc_access_level"></span> Citations of online sources that require registration or a subscription are acceptable in Wikipedia as documented in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PAYWALL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PAYWALL">Verifiability § Access to sources</a>. As a courtesy to readers and other editors, editors should signal restrictions on access to material provided via the external links included in a citation. These levels describe requirements or constraints related to accessing and viewing the cited material; they are not intended to indicate the ability to reuse, or the copyright status, of the material, since that status is not relevant to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:V" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:V">verifying claims in articles</a>. </p>
      <p>Four access levels can be used: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="#Access_indicator_for_named_identifiers">access indicator for named identifiers</a>: 
        <ul>
         <li><img alt="Freely accessible" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Lock-green.svg/9px-Lock-green.svg.png" decoding="async" title="Freely accessible" width="9" height="14" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Lock-green.svg/14px-Lock-green.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Lock-green.svg/18px-Lock-green.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="813"> <code>free</code>: the source is free to read for anyone</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><a href="#Access_indicators_for_url-holding_parameters">access indicators for url-holding parameters</a>: 
        <ul>
         <li><img alt="Free registration required" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Lock-gray-alt-2.svg/9px-Lock-gray-alt-2.svg.png" decoding="async" title="Free registration required" width="9" height="14" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Lock-gray-alt-2.svg/14px-Lock-gray-alt-2.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Lock-gray-alt-2.svg/18px-Lock-gray-alt-2.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="813"> <code>registration</code>: a free registration is required to access the source</li> 
         <li><img alt="Free access subject to limited trial, subscription normally required" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Lock-gray-alt-2.svg/9px-Lock-gray-alt-2.svg.png" decoding="async" title="Free access subject to limited trial, subscription normally required" width="9" height="14" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Lock-gray-alt-2.svg/14px-Lock-gray-alt-2.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Lock-gray-alt-2.svg/18px-Lock-gray-alt-2.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="813"> <code>limited</code>: there are other constraints (such as a cap on daily views) to freely access this source</li> 
         <li><img alt="Paid subscription required" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Lock-red-alt-2.svg/9px-Lock-red-alt-2.svg.png" decoding="async" title="Paid subscription required" width="9" height="14" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Lock-red-alt-2.svg/14px-Lock-red-alt-2.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Lock-red-alt-2.svg/18px-Lock-red-alt-2.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="597" data-file-height="947"> <code>subscription</code>: the source is only accessible via a paid subscription ("<a href="/wiki/Paywall" title="Paywall">paywall</a>")</li>
        </ul></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>As there are often multiple external links with different access levels in the same citation, each value is attributed to a specific external link. </p> 
      <h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Access_indicators_for_url-holding_parameters">Access indicators for url-holding parameters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Template:Citation_Style_documentation/registration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="Edit section: ">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5> 
      <p>Online sources linked by <code class="nowrap" style="">|url=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|article-url=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|chapter-url=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|contribution-url=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|entry-url=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|map-url=</code>, and <code class="nowrap" style="">|section-url=</code> are presumed to be free-to-read.<sup id="cite_ref-NotRef_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NotRef-5">[2]</a></sup> When they are not free-to-read, editors should mark those sources with the matching access-indicator parameter so that an appropriate icon is included in the rendered citation. Because the sources linked by these url-holding parameters are presumed to be free-to-read, they may not be marked as <code>free</code>. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quote">Quote</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Quote">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li><b id="csdoc_quote">quote</b>: Relevant text quoted from the source. Displays enclosed in quotes. When supplied, the citation terminator (a period by default) is suppressed, so the quote must include terminating punctuation. If <b>script-quote</b> is defined, use <b>quote</b> to hold a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ROMAN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ROMAN">Romanization</a> (if available) of the text in <b>script-quote</b>. 
        <ul>
         <li><b>script-quote</b>: Original quotation for languages that do not use a Latin-based script (Arabic, Chinese, Cyrillic, Greek, Hebrew, Japanese, Korean, etc.); not italicized, follows italicized Romanization defined in <b>quote</b> (if available). Alias: none. Must be prefixed with one of the <a href="/wiki/Help:Citation_Style_1#|script-<param>=_language_codes" title="Help:Citation Style 1">supported language codes</a> to help browsers properly display the script: 
          <dl>
           <dd>
            <code>... |quote=T?ky? taw? |script-quote=ja:????? |trans-quote=Tokyo Tower ...</code>
           </dd>
          </dl></li> 
         <li><b>trans-quote</b>: English translation of the quotation if the source quoted is in a foreign language. Displays in square brackets. Alias: none.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li><b>quote-page</b>: The number of a single page quoted in <code class="nowrap" style="">|quote=</code>. Use either <code class="nowrap" style="">|quote-page=</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|quote-pages=</code>, but not both. Should be a subset of the page(s) specified in <code class="nowrap" style="">|page=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|pages=</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|at=</code>. Displays preceded by <code class="mw-highlight mw-highlight-lang-text mw-content-ltr" id="" style="" dir="ltr">p.</code> unless <code class="nowrap" style="">|no-pp=yes</code>. If hyphenated, use {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Hyphen" title="Template:Hyphen">hyphen</a>}} to indicate this is intentional (e.g. <code class="nowrap" style="">|quote-page=3{{hyphen}}12</code>). Alias: none.</li> 
       <li>OR: <b>quote-pages</b>: A list or range of pages quoted in <code class="nowrap" style="">|quote=</code>. Use either <code class="nowrap" style="">|quote-page=</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|quote-pages=</code>, but not both. Should be a subset of the pages specified in <code class="nowrap" style="">|pages=</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|at=</code>. Separate using an en dash (–); separate non-sequential pages with a comma (,). Displays preceded by <code class="mw-highlight mw-highlight-lang-text mw-content-ltr" id="" style="" dir="ltr">pp.</code> unless <code class="nowrap" style="">|no-pp=yes</code> is defined. Hyphens are automatically converted to en dashes; if hyphens are appropriate because individual page numbers contain hyphens, for example: pp. 3-1–3-15, use <a href="/wiki/Help:Citation_Style_1#Accept-this-as-written_markup" title="Help:Citation Style 1">double parentheses</a> to tell the template to display the value of <code class="nowrap" style="">|quote-pages=</code> without processing it, and use {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Hyphen" title="Template:Hyphen">hyphen</a>}} to indicate to editors that a hyphen is really intended: <code class="nowrap" style="">|quote-pages=((3{{hyphen}}1{{ndash}}3{{hyphen}}15))</code>. Alias: none.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anchors">Anchors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Anchors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

The ID is created from up to four author last names and the year, of the format <code>CITEREF<i>lastname(s)year</i></code>. </p>

The <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Harvid" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:Harvid">harvid</a>}}</code> template may be used here to create an ID for the Harv- and sfn-family templates. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Display_options">Display options</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Display options">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>These features are not often used, but can customize the display for use with other styles. </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><span id="csdoc_display"></span><b id="csdoc_mode">mode</b>: Sets element separator, default terminal punctuation, and certain capitalization according to the value provided. For <code class="nowrap" style="">|mode=cs1</code>, element separator and terminal punctuation is a period (<code>.</code>); where appropriate, initial letters of certain words are capitalized ('Retrieved...'). For <code class="nowrap" style="">|mode=cs2</code>, element separator is a comma (<code>,</code>); terminal punctuation is omitted; where appropriate, initial letters of certain words are not capitalized ('retrieved...'). To override default terminal punctuation use <b>postscript</b>.</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_author-mask">author-mask</b>:</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_contributor-mask">contributor-mask</b>:</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_editor-mask">editor-mask</b>:</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_interviewer-mask">interviewer-mask</b>:</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_subject-mask">subject-mask</b>:</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_translator-mask">translator-mask</b>: 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          Replaces the name of the (first) author with <a href="/wiki/Dash#Em_dash" title="Dash">em dashes</a> or text. Set <b>&lt;<var style="padding-right: 1px;">name</var>&gt;-mask</b> to a numeric value <i>n</i> to set the dash <i>n</i> em spaces wide; set <b>&lt;<var style="padding-right: 1px;">name</var>&gt;-mask</b> to a text value to display the text without a trailing author separator; for example, "with". The numeric value 0 is a special case to be used in conjunction with <b>&lt;<var style="padding-right: 1px;">name</var>&gt;-link</b>—in this case, the value of <b>&lt;<var style="padding-right: 1px;">name</var>&gt;-link</b> will be used as (linked) text. In either case, you must still include the values for all names for metadata purposes. Primarily intended for use with bibliographies or bibliography styles where multiple works by a single author are listed sequentially such as <a href="/wiki/Help:Shortened_footnotes" title="Help:Shortened footnotes">shortened footnotes</a>. Do not use in a list generated by <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Reflist" title="Template:Reflist">reflist</a>}}</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">&lt;references /&gt;</code> or similar as there is no control of the order in which references are displayed. Mask parameters can take an enumerator in the name of the parameter (e.g. <code class="nowrap" style="">|author<var style="padding-right: 1px;">n</var>-mask=</code>) to apply the mask to a specific <var style="padding-right: 1px;">name</var>.
         </dd>
        </dl></li>
      </ul> 
      <ul>
       <li><b id="csdoc_display-authors"><span id="csdoc_displayauthors"></span>display-authors</b>:</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_display-contributors"><span id="csdoc_displaycontributors"></span>display-contributors</b>:</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_display-editors"><span id="csdoc_displayeditors"></span>display-editors</b>:</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_display-interviewers"><span id="csdoc_displayinterviewers"></span>display-interviewers</b>:</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_display-subjects"><span id="csdoc_displaysubjects"></span>display-subjects</b>:</li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_display-translators"><span id="csdoc_displaytranslators"></span>display-translators</b>: 
        <dl>
         <dd>
          Controls the number of author or editor names that are displayed when a citation is published. To change the displayed number of names, set <b>display-authors</b> and/or <b>display-editors</b> to the desired number. For example, <code class="nowrap" style="">|display-authors=2</code> will display only the first two authors in a citation (and not affect the display of editors). Likewise, <code class="nowrap" style="">|display-editors=2</code> will display only the first two editors (and all authors). <code class="nowrap" style="">|display-authors=0</code> and <code class="nowrap" style="">|display-editors=0</code> are special cases suppressing the display of all authors or editors including the et al. By default, all authors and editors are displayed. <code class="nowrap" style="">|display-authors=etal</code> displays all authors in the list followed by et al. Aliases: none.
         </dd>
        </dl></li> 
       <li><b id="csdoc_postscript">postscript</b>: Controls the closing punctuation for a citation; defaults to a period (<code>.</code>); for no terminating punctuation, specify <code class="nowrap" style="">|postscript=none</code> – leaving <code class="nowrap" style="">|postscript=</code> empty is the same as omitting it, but is ambiguous. Additional text or templates beyond the terminating punctuation may generate an error message. <code class="nowrap" style="">|postscript=</code> is ignored if <b>quote</b> is defined.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="et_al.">et al.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: et al.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>et al. is the abbreviation of the Latin <i lang="la" title="Latin-language text">et alii</i> ('and others'). It is used to complete a list of authors of a published work, where the complete list is considered overly long. The abbreviation is widely used in English, thus it is not italicized per <a href="/wiki/MOS:FOREIGN" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:FOREIGN">MOS:FOREIGN</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accept-this-as-written_markup">Accept-this-as-written markup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Accept-this-as-written markup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p>There are occasions where <a href="/wiki/Module:Citation/CS1" title="Module:Citation/CS1">Module:Citation/CS1</a> emits error or maintenance messages because of, or makes changes to, the values assigned to a select set of parameters. The markup for this is <code>((&lt;<var style="padding-right: 1px;">value</var>&gt;))</code>, i.e., wrap the entire parameter value in two sets of parentheses.       <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Printing">Printing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: Printing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>When viewing the page, CS1 templates render the URL to the title to create a link; when printing, the URL is printed. <a href="/wiki/Help:External_link_icons" title="Help:External link icons">External link icons</a> are not printed. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Elements_not_included">Elements not included</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Elements not included">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Not all factually accurate pieces of information about a source are used in a Citation Style 1 citation. Examples of information not included: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>The total number of pages in a cited source</li> 
       <li>The name of the library that provided access to an electronic copy of a cited source</li> 
       <li>The name of the library that owns a physical copy of a cited work</li> 
       <li>The library record and/or shelf location of a physical copy of a cited work</li>
      </ul>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Common_issues">Common issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Help:Citation_Style_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=41" title="Edit section: Common issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="Help:CS1PROBS"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Help:CS1PROBS&amp;redirect=no">Help:CS1PROBS</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <dl>
       <dt>
        <code class="nowrap" style="">|access-date=</code> does not show.
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        If <code class="nowrap" style="">|url=</code> is not supplied, then <code class="nowrap" style="">|access-date=</code> does not show; by design.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        The bare URL shows before the title.
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        If the <code class="nowrap" style="">|title=</code> field includes a newline or an invalid character then the link will be malformed; see <a href="#Web_links">Web links</a>.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        The title appears in red.
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        If URL is supplied, then the title cannot be wikilinked.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        The URL is not linked and shows in brackets.
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        The URL must include the <a href="/wiki/URI_scheme" class="mw-redirect" title="URI scheme">URI scheme</a> in order for MediaWiki to recognize it as a link. For example: <code>www.example.org</code> vs. <code><a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://www.example.org">http://www.example.org</a></code>.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        A field is truncated.
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        A pipe (<code>|</code>) in the value will truncate it. Use <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:!" title="Template:!">!</a>}}</code> instead.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        The template markup shows.
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        Double open brackets [[ are used in a field without closing double brackets ]].
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        The author shows in brackets with an external link icon.
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        The use of an URL in <code class="nowrap" style="">|author-link=</code> will break the link; this field is for the name of the Wikipedia article about the author, not a website.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        Multiple author or editor names are defined and one or more does not show
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        The parameters must be used in sequence, i.e. if <code class="nowrap" style="">|last=</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|last1=</code> is not defined, then <code class="nowrap" style="">|last2=</code> will not show. By design.
       </dd> 
       <dt>
        <code class="nowrap" style="">|page=</code>, <code class="nowrap" style="">|pages=</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|at=</code> do not show.
       </dt> 
       <dd>
        These parameters are mutually exclusive, and only one will show; by design.
       </dd>
      </dl>

In 1913–1969, the volume numbering restarted at 1 and went up to 188 (the second series). In 1970, <i>Physical Review</i> split into different parts, <i>Physical Review A</i>, <i>Physical Review B</i>, <i>Physical Review C</i>, and <i>Physical Review D</i>, where volumes again restarted at 1 (the third series). Since there are two publications identified as <i>Physical Review, Volume 1</i>, there is a need to distinguish which is which by use of <code class="nowrap" style="">|series=First Series</code> or <code class="nowrap" style="">|series=Second Series</code>. While <i>Physical Review A</i> is in the third series of the <i>Physical Review</i> <a href="/wiki/Media_franchise" class="mw-redirect" title="Media franchise">media franchise</a>, it is the first series of the publication known as <i>Physical Review A</i>. Since there is no confusion about what <i>Physical Review A, Volume 1</i> could be referring to, there is no need to clarify to which numbering series the journal belong.</p> <p>In particular, note that the <code class="nowrap" style="">|series=</code> parameter is not to be used to distinguish the different parts of a <a href="/wiki/Media_franchises" class="mw-redirect" title="Media franchises">media franchises</a>, like <i><a href="/wiki/Physical_Review_A" title="Physical Review A">Physical Review A</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Acta_Crystallographica_Section_A" class="mw-redirect" title="Acta Crystallographica Section A">Acta Crystallographica Section A</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_of_the_Royal_Statistical_Society,_Series_B" class="mw-redirect" title="Journal of the Royal Statistical Society, Series B">Journal of the Royal Statistical Society, Series B</a></i>.</p></span> </li> 
        </ol>
