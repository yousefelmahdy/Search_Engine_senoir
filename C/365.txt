      <p>Wikipedia's <b>policies and guidelines</b> are developed by the community to describe best practices, clarify principles, resolve conflicts, and otherwise further our goal of creating a free, reliable encyclopedia. There is no need to read any policy or guideline pages to start editing. The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Five_pillars" title="Wikipedia:Five pillars">five pillars</a> are a popular summary of the most pertinent principles. </p>
      <p>Although Wikipedia generally <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_Wikipedia_is_not#Wikipedia_is_not_a_bureaucracy" title="Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not">does not employ hard-and-fast rules</a>, Wikipedia's policy and guideline pages describe its principles and agreed-upon best practices. Policies are standards all users should normally follow, and guidelines are generally meant to be best practices for following those standards in specific contexts. Policies and guidelines should always be applied using reason and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_%22Ignore_all_rules%22_means#Use_common_sense" title="Wikipedia:What &quot;Ignore all rules&quot; means">common sense</a>. </p>
      <p>This policy page specifies the community standards related to the organization, life cycle, maintenance of, and adherence to policies, guidelines, and related pages of the <a href="/wiki/English_Wikipedia" title="English Wikipedia">English Wikipedia</a>. It does not cover other editions of Wikipedia. The full <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_policies" title="Wikipedia:List of policies">list of policies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:List of guidelines">list of guidelines</a> can be found at those respective links. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Derivation">Derivation</span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administration#Human_and_legal_administration" title="Wikipedia:Administration">Wikipedia:Administration §&nbsp;Human and legal administration</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Wikipedia is operated by the not-for-profit <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Wikimedia Foundation">Wikimedia Foundation</a>, which reserves certain legal rights—see <a href="https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Policies" class="extiw" title="foundation:Policies">the Wikimedia Foundation's Policies page</a> for a list of its policies. See also <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Role_of_Jimmy_Wales" title="Wikipedia:Role of Jimmy Wales">Role of Jimmy Wales</a>. Nevertheless, normally Wikipedia is a self-governing project run by its community. Its policies and guidelines are intended to reflect the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a> of the community. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Role">Role</span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:The_difference_between_policies,_guidelines_and_essays" title="Wikipedia:The difference between policies, guidelines and essays">Wikipedia:The difference between policies, guidelines and essays</a>
      </div> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:POLICIES"></span><span id="WP:GUIDES"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:POLICIES&amp;redirect=no">WP:POLICIES</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:GUIDES&amp;redirect=no">WP:GUIDES</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div>
      <p><span class="anchor" id="Policies"></span><span class="anchor" id="policies"></span> </p>
      <p><b>Policies</b> have wide acceptance among editors and describe standards all users should <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Ignore_all_rules" title="Wikipedia:Ignore all rules">normally</a> follow. All policy pages are in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_policies_and_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:List of policies and guidelines">Wikipedia:List of policies and guidelines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_policies" title="Category:Wikipedia policies">Category:Wikipedia policies</a>. For summaries of key policies, see also <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_policies" title="Wikipedia:List of policies">List of policies</a>. </p>
      <p><span class="anchor" id="Guidelines"></span><span class="anchor" id="guides"></span><span class="anchor" id="guide"></span><b>Guidelines</b> are sets of best practices supported by consensus. Editors should attempt to follow guidelines, though they are best treated with common sense, and occasional exceptions may apply. Guideline pages can be found in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_policies_and_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:List of policies and guidelines">Wikipedia:List of policies and guidelines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_guidelines" title="Category:Wikipedia guidelines">Category:Wikipedia guidelines</a>. For summaries of key guidelines, see also <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:List_of_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:List of guidelines">List of guidelines</a>. </p>
      <p><span class="anchor" id="Essays"></span><b>Essays</b> are the opinion or advice of an editor or group of editors for which widespread consensus has not been established. They do not speak for the entire community and may be created and written without approval. Essays the author does not want others to edit, or that contradict widespread consensus, belong in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_pages" title="Wikipedia:User pages">user namespace</a>. <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1015928993">.mw-parser-output div.crossreference{padding-left:0}</style><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable crossreference">(For more information, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Essays" title="Wikipedia:Essays">Wikipedia:Essays</a>.)</span> </p>
      <p>Other <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administration#Data_structure_and_development" title="Wikipedia:Administration">administration pages</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Project_namespace" title="Wikipedia:Project namespace">project namespace</a> include: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Process pages, which facilitate application of the policies and guidelines (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Articles_for_deletion" title="Wikipedia:Articles for deletion">Wikipedia:Articles for deletion</a>),</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">WikiProject</a> pages, including <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_advice_pages" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject advice pages">essays they have written to give advice to other editors</a> about their areas of interest,</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:How_to_pages" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:How to pages">How-to</a> or help pages (also found in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Help_namespace" title="Wikipedia:Help namespace">Help namespace</a>), which provide mainly technical information,</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Information_pages" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Information pages">Information pages</a>, which generally provide factual information,</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Supplemental_pages" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Supplemental pages">Supplements</a> to guidelines and policies, which explain advice in greater detail,</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_discussion" title="Category:Wikipedia discussion">Community discussion pages</a> and <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_noticeboards" title="Category:Wikipedia noticeboards">noticeboards</a> for communication between editors, and</li> 
       <li>Historical pages, which are outdated.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
      </ul> 
      <p>These other pages are not policies or guidelines, although they may contain valuable advice or information. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adherence">Adherence</span></h2> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:ADHERENCE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:ADHERENCE&amp;redirect=no">WP:ADHERENCE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_%22Ignore_all_rules%22_means#Use_common_sense" title="Wikipedia:What &quot;Ignore all rules&quot; means">Use common sense</a> in interpreting and applying policies and guidelines; Rules have <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Ignore_all_rules" title="Wikipedia:Ignore all rules">occasional exceptions</a>. That said, those who violate the spirit of a rule may be reprimanded or sanctioned even if they do not technically break the rule. </p>
      <p>Whether a policy or guideline is an accurate description of best practice is determined through <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a>. </p>
      <p>On discussion pages and in edit summaries, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcuts" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Shortcuts">shortcuts</a> are often used to refer to policies and guidelines. (For example, <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:NOR&amp;redirect=no">WP:NOR</a></span> (no original research), <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:NPOV&amp;redirect=no">WP:NPOV</a></span> (neutral point of view) and <span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:BLP&amp;redirect=no">WP:BLP</a></span> (biographies of living persons)). Similar shortcuts are also used for other types of project page like essays and how-to guides. Thus a shortcut does not necessarily imply the page linked to has policy or guideline status or has been widely accepted by the community. Additionally, <a href="/wiki/Map%E2%80%93territory_relation" title="Map–territory relation">the shortcut is not the policy</a>; the plain-English definition of the page's title or shortcut may be importantly different from the linked page. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Enforcement">Enforcement</span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Enforcement_policies" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Enforcement policies">Wikipedia:Enforcement policies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Active_sanctions" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Active sanctions">Wikipedia:Active sanctions</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_enforcement" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration enforcement">Wikipedia:Arbitration enforcement</a>
      </div> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:ENFORCEMENT"></span><span id="WP:ENFORCE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:ENFORCEMENT&amp;redirect=no">WP:ENFORCEMENT</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:ENFORCE&amp;redirect=no">WP:ENFORCE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Enforcement on Wikipedia is similar to other social interactions. If an editor violates the community standards described in policies and guidelines, other editors can persuade the person to adhere to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CONDUCT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CONDUCT">acceptable norms of conduct</a>, over time resorting to more forceful means, such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators" title="Wikipedia:Administrators">administrator</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Stewards" title="Wikipedia:Stewards">steward</a> actions. In the case of gross violations of community norms, they are likely to resort to more forceful means fairly rapidly. Going against the principles set out on these pages, particularly policy pages, is unlikely to prove acceptable, although it may be possible to convince fellow editors <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Ignore_all_rules" title="Wikipedia:Ignore all rules">an exception ought to be made</a>. This means individual editors (including <i>you</i>) enforce and apply policies and guidelines. </p>
      <p>In cases where it is clear a user is acting against policy (or against a guideline in a way that conflicts with policy), especially if they are doing so intentionally and persistently, that user may be temporarily or indefinitely <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Blocking_policy" title="Wikipedia:Blocking policy">blocked</a> from editing by an administrator. In cases where the general <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Dispute_resolution" title="Wikipedia:Dispute resolution">dispute resolution</a> procedure has been ineffective, the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee">Arbitration Committee</a> has the power to deal with highly disruptive or sensitive situations. </p> 
      <h2><span id=".C2.ADContent"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="­Content"><span class="anchor" id="Content"></span>­Content</span></h2> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_instruction_creep" title="Wikipedia:Avoid instruction creep">Wikipedia:Avoid instruction creep</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Policy and guideline pages should: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><b>Be clear</b>. Avoid esoteric or quasi-legal terms or dumbed-down language. Be plain, direct, unambiguous, and specific. Avoid platitudes and generalities. Even in guidelines, help pages, and other non-policy pages, do not be afraid to tell editors directly they <i>must</i> or <i>should</i> do something.</li> 
       <li><b>Be as concise as possible—but no more concise.</b> Verbosity is not a reliable defense against misinterpretation. Omit needless words. Direct, concise writing may be clearer than rambling examples. Footnotes and links to other pages may be used for further clarification.</li> 
       <li><b>Emphasize the spirit of the rule.</b> Expect editors to use common sense. If the spirit of the rule is clear, say no more.</li> 
       <li><b>Maintain scope and avoid redundancy.</b> Clearly identify the purpose and scope early in the page, as many readers will just look at the beginning. Content should be within the scope of its policy. When the scope of one advice page overlaps with the scope of another, minimize redundancy. When one policy refers to another policy, it should do so briefly, clearly and explicitly.</li> 
       <li><b>Avoid overlinking.</b> Links to policies, guidelines, essays, and articles should be used only when clarification or context is needed. Links to other advice pages may inadvertently or intentionally defer authority to them. Make it clear when links defer, and when they do not.</li> 
       <li><b>Not contradict each other.</b> The community's view cannot simultaneously be "A" and "not A". When apparent discrepancies arise between pages, editors at all the affected pages should discuss how they can most accurately represent the community's current position and correct all the pages to reflect the community's view. This discussion should be on <u>one</u> talk page, with invitations to that page at the talk pages of the various affected pages; otherwise the corrections may still contradict each other.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Not_part_of_the_encyclopedia">Not part of the encyclopedia</span></h2> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:NOTPART"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:NOTPART&amp;redirect=no">WP:NOTPART</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Wikipedia has many policies and guidelines about encyclopedic <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Content_in_Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia:Content in Wikipedia">content</a>. These standards require verifiability, neutrality, respect for living people, and more. </p>
      <p>The policies, guidelines, and process pages themselves are not part of the encyclopedia proper. Consequently, they do not generally need to conform to the same <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Core_content_policies" title="Wikipedia:Core content policies">content standards</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style">style conventions</a> as articles. It is therefore not necessary to provide <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources">reliable sources</a> to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verify</a> Wikipedia's administrative pages, or to phrase Wikipedia procedures or principles in a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Neutral_point_of_view" title="Wikipedia:Neutral point of view">neutral</a> manner, or to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">cite an outside authority</a> in determining Wikipedia's editorial practices. Instead, the content of these pages is controlled by <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">community-wide consensus</a>, and the style should emphasize clarity, directness, and usefulness to other editors.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p>
      <p>These pages do, however, need to comply with Wikipedia's legal and behavioral policies, as well as policies applicable to non-content pages. For example, editors may not violate copyrights anywhere on Wikipedia, and edit warring is prohibited everywhere, not merely in encyclopedia articles. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_cycle">Life cycle</span></h2> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="#Life_cycle"></span> </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:PGLIFE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PGLIFE&amp;redirect=no">WP:PGLIFE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Many of the most well-established policies and guidelines have developed from principles which have been accepted as fundamental since Wikipedia's inception. Others developed as solutions to common problems and disruptive editing. Policy and guideline pages are seldom established without precedent<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and always require strong community support. Policies and guidelines may be established through new proposals, promotion of existing essays or guidelines, and reorganization of existing policies and guidelines through splitting and merging. </p>
      <p>Essays and information pages may be established by writing them and adding {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Essay" title="Template:Essay">essay</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Information_page" title="Template:Information page">Information page</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Wikipedia_how-to" title="Template:Wikipedia how-to">Wikipedia how-to</a>}}, or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Template_index/Wikipedia_namespace" title="Wikipedia:Template index/Wikipedia namespace">a similar template</a> to the page. </p>
      <p>Current policy and guideline proposals can be found in <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_proposals" title="Category:Wikipedia proposals">Category:Wikipedia proposals</a>, and failed proposals can be found in <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_failed_proposals" title="Category:Wikipedia failed proposals">Category:Wikipedia failed proposals</a>. All editors are welcome to comment on these proposals. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposals">Proposals</span></h3> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="proposal"></span> </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:PROPOSAL"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PROPOSAL&amp;redirect=no">WP:PROPOSAL</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:How_to_contribute_to_Wikipedia_guidance" title="Wikipedia:How to contribute to Wikipedia guidance">Wikipedia:How to contribute to Wikipedia guidance</a>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Polling_is_not_a_substitute_for_discussion#Policy_and_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Polling is not a substitute for discussion">Wikipedia:Polling is not a substitute for discussion §&nbsp;Policy and guidelines</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_Wikipedia_is_not#Wikipedia_is_not_a_bureaucracy" title="Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not">Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not §&nbsp;Wikipedia is not a bureaucracy</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Proposals for new guidelines and policies require discussion and a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Local_consensus" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Local consensus">high level of consensus</a> from the entire community for promotion to guideline or policy. Adding the {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Policy" title="Template:Policy">policy</a>}} template to a page without the required consensus does not mean the page is policy, even if the page summarizes or copies policy. Most commonly, a new policy or guideline documents existing practices, rather than proposing a change to what experienced editors already choose to do. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Good_practice_for_proposals">Good practice for proposals</span></h4> 
      <p>One path for proposals is developing them through steps of </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Brainstorming" title="Template:Brainstorming">brainstorming</a>}}</li> 
       <li>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Draft_proposal" title="Template:Draft proposal">draft proposal</a>}}</li> 
       <li>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Proposal" title="Template:Proposal">proposal</a>}}</li> 
       <li>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Policy" title="Template:Policy">policy</a>}} or {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Guideline" title="Template:Guideline">guideline</a>}}</li>
      </ol> 
      <p>The first step is to write the best initial proposal you can. Authors can request early-stage feedback at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Village_pump_(idea_lab)" title="Wikipedia:Village pump (idea lab)">Wikipedia's village pump for idea incubation</a> and from any relevant WikiProjects. Amendments to a proposal can be discussed on its talk page. It is crucial to improve a proposal in response to feedback received from outside editors. Consensus is built through a process of listening to and discussing the proposal with many other editors. </p>
      <p>Once you think the initial proposal is well written, and the issues involved have been sufficiently discussed among early participants to create a proposal that has a solid chance of success with the broader community, start an RfC for your policy or guideline proposal in a new section on the talk page and include the <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Rfc" title="Template:Rfc">rfc</a>|policy}}</code> tag along with a brief, time-stamped explanation of the proposal. Then, if you want, you can provide a detailed explanation of what the page does and why you think it should be a policy or guideline. The {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Proposal" title="Template:Proposal">proposal</a>}} template should be placed at the top of the proposed page; this tag will get the proposal properly categorized. </p>
      <p>The RfC should typically be announced at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Village_pump_(policy)" title="Wikipedia:Village pump (policy)">policy</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Village_pump_(proposals)" title="Wikipedia:Village pump (proposals)">proposals</a> village pumps, and you should notify other potentially interested groups. If your proposal affects a specific content area, then related WikiProjects can be found at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Directory" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Directory">WikiProject directory</a>. If your proposal relates to an existing policy or guideline, then leave a note on the talk page of the related policy or guideline. For example, proposed style guidelines should be announced at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Manual_of_Style" title="Wikipedia talk:Manual of Style">Wikipedia talk:Manual of Style</a>, which is the main guideline for style issues. Try to identify the subcategory of guideline or policy (see {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Subcat_guideline" title="Template:Subcat guideline">subcat guideline</a>}}). Proposals involving contentious subjects or wide-ranging effects should normally be listed on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Centralized_discussion" title="Wikipedia:Centralized discussion">Wikipedia:Centralized discussion</a> for the duration of the RfC. Rarely, a particularly important proposal may be advertised via a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Watchlist_notices" title="Wikipedia:Watchlist notices">watchlist notice</a>; sitenotices (which are displayed to all readers, not just to active editors) are not used for proposals. RfCs for policy and guideline proposals are normally left open for at least a week or sometimes a couple months. </p>
      <p>To avoid later complaints about insufficient notice, it may be helpful to provide a complete list of the groups or pages you used to advertise the proposal on the talk page. Be careful to not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Canvassing" title="Wikipedia:Canvassing">canvass</a> with non-neutral wording. </p>
      <p>Editors should respond to proposals in a way that helps identify and build consensus. Explain your thoughts, ask questions, and raise concerns. Many editors begin their responses with bold-font 'vote' of support or opposition to make evaluation easier. </p>
      <p>Closing a discussion requires careful evaluation of the responses to determine the consensus. This does not require the intervention of an administrator; it may be done by any sufficiently experienced impartial editor, not involved in the discussion, who is familiar with all policies and guidelines related to the proposal. The following points are important in evaluating consensus: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">Consensus</a> for guidelines and policies should be reasonably strong, though unanimity is not required.</li> 
       <li>There must be exposure to the community beyond just the authors of the proposal.</li> 
       <li>Consider the strength of the proposed page: 
        <ul>
         <li>Have major concerns raised during the community discussion been addressed?</li> 
         <li>Does the proposal contradict any existing guidelines or policies?</li> 
         <li>Can the new proposed guideline or policy be merged into an existing one?</li> 
         <li>Is the proposed guideline or policy, or some part of it, redundant with an existing guideline or policy?</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>A proposal's status is not determined by counting votes. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Polling_is_not_a_substitute_for_discussion" title="Wikipedia:Polling is not a substitute for discussion">Polling is not a substitute for discussion</a>, nor is a poll's numerical outcome tantamount to consensus.</li> 
       <li><span class="anchor" id="failed"></span>If consensus for broad community support has not developed after a reasonable time, the proposal has failed. If consensus is neutral or unclear on the issue and unlikely to improve, the proposal has likewise failed.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Discussion may be closed as one of: <b>Promote</b>, <b>No consensus</b>, or <b>Failed</b>. Please leave a short note about the conclusion you came to. Update the proposal to reflect the consensus. Remove the {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Proposal" title="Template:Proposal">Proposal</a>}} template and replace it with another appropriate template, such as {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Subcat_guideline" title="Template:Subcat guideline">Subcat guideline</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Policy" title="Template:Policy">Policy</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Supplement" title="Template:Supplement">Supplement</a>}}, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Essay" title="Template:Essay">Essay</a>}}, or {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Failed_proposal" title="Template:Failed proposal">Failed proposal</a>}}. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Template_index/Wikipedia_namespace" title="Wikipedia:Template index/Wikipedia namespace">Wikipedia namespace templates</a> for a listing of banners. </p>
      <p>If a proposal fails, the failed tag should not usually be removed. It is typically more productive to rewrite a failed proposal from scratch to address problems, or seek consensus to integrate uncontroversial aspects of it into existing pages, than to re-nominate a proposal. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demotion"><span class="anchor" id="historical"></span>Demotion</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:HISTORICAL"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:HISTORICAL&amp;redirect=no">WP:HISTORICAL</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Project_namespace#Deletion_of_project_pages" title="Wikipedia:Project namespace">Wikipedia:Project namespace §&nbsp;Deletion of project pages</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Project_namespace#Historical_pages" title="Wikipedia:Project namespace">Wikipedia:Project namespace §&nbsp;Historical pages</a>
      </div> 
      <p>An accepted policy or guideline may become obsolete because of changes in editorial practice or community standards, may become redundant because of improvements to other pages, or may represent unwarranted <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_instruction_creep" title="Wikipedia:Avoid instruction creep">instruction creep</a>. In such situations editors may propose that a policy be demoted to a guideline, or that a policy or guideline be demoted to a <a href="/wiki/Template:Supplement" title="Template:Supplement">supplement</a>, <a href="/wiki/Template:Information_page" title="Template:Information page">informational page</a>, <a href="/wiki/Template:Essay" title="Template:Essay">essay</a> or <a href="/wiki/Template:Historical" title="Template:Historical">historical</a> page. In certain cases, a policy or guideline may be <a href="/wiki/Template:Superseded" title="Template:Superseded">superseded</a>, in which case the old page is marked and retained for historical interest. </p>
      <p>The process for demotion is similar to promotion. A talk page discussion is typically started, the <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Under_discussion" title="Template:Under discussion">Under discussion</a>|status|<i>Discussion Title</i>}}</code> template is added to the top of the project page, and community input is solicited. After a reasonable amount of time for comments, an independent editor should close the discussion and evaluate the discussion and determine whether a consensus has formed to change the status. </p>
      <p>The {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Disputed_tag" title="Template:Disputed tag">Disputed tag</a>}} template is typically used instead of {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Under_discussion" title="Template:Under discussion">Under discussion</a>}} for claims that a page was recently assigned guideline or policy status without proper or sufficient consensus being established. </p>
      <p>Essays, information pages, and other informal pages that are supported by only a small minority of the community are typically moved to the primary author's userspace. These discussions typically happen on the page's talk page, sometimes with an RfC, but they have at times also been conducted at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Miscellany_for_deletion" title="Wikipedia:Miscellany for deletion">Miscellany for deletion</a> (despite the MFD guidelines explicitly discouraging this practice). Other pages are retained for historical reference and are marked as such. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Content_changes">Content changes</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:PGCHANGE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PGCHANGE&amp;redirect=no">WP:PGCHANGE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Editing_policy#Edits_to_policies_and_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Editing policy">Wikipedia:Editing policy §&nbsp;Edits to policies and guidelines</a>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Policy_writing_is_hard" title="Wikipedia:Policy writing is hard">Wikipedia:Policy writing is hard</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Policies and guidelines can be edited like any other Wikipedia page. It is not strictly necessary to discuss changes or to obtain written documentation of a consensus in advance. However, because policies and guidelines are sensitive and complex, users should take care over any edits, to be sure they are faithfully reflecting the community's view and to be sure they are not accidentally introducing new sources of error or confusion. </p>
      <p>Keep in mind that the purpose of policies and guidelines is to state what most Wikipedians agree upon, and should be phrased to reflect the present <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a> on a subject. Editing a policy/guideline/essay page does not in itself imply an immediate change to accepted practice. It is, naturally, bad practice to recommend a rejected practice on a policy or guideline page. To update best practices, you may change the practice directly (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_%22Ignore_all_rules%22_means" title="Wikipedia:What &quot;Ignore all rules&quot; means">you are permitted to deviate from practice for the purposes of such change</a>) and/or set about building widespread consensus for your change or implementation through discussion. When such a change is accepted, you can then edit the page to reflect the new situation. </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Substantive_changes">Substantive changes</span></h4> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:TALKFIRST"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:TALKFIRST&amp;redirect=no">WP:TALKFIRST</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Be_bold#Wikipedia_namespace" title="Wikipedia:Be bold">Wikipedia:Be bold §&nbsp;Wikipedia namespace</a>
      </div> 
      <p><b>Talk first.</b> Talk page discussion typically precedes substantive changes to policy. Changes may be made if there are no objections or if discussion shows there is consensus for the change. Minor edits to improve formatting, grammar, and clarity may be made at any time. </p>
      <p>If the result of discussions is unclear, then it should be evaluated by an administrator or other independent editor, as in the proposal process. Major changes should also be publicized to the community in general; announcements similar to the proposal process may be appropriate. </p>
      <p>If wider input on a proposed change is desired, it may be useful to mark the section with the tag <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Under_discussion" title="Template:Under discussion">Under discussion</a>|section|talk=<i>Discussion Title</i>}}</code>. (If the proposal relates to a single statement, use <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Under_discussion_inline" title="Template:Under discussion inline">Under discussion inline</a>|<i>Discussion Title</i>}}</code> immediately after it.) <span class="anchor" id="Bold"></span> </p> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:PGBOLD"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PGBOLD&amp;redirect=no">WP:PGBOLD</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p><b>Or be bold.</b> Although most editors find prior discussion, especially at well-developed pages, very helpful, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus#Reaching_consensus_through_editing" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">directly editing these pages is permitted by Wikipedia's policies</a>. Consequently, you should not remove any change <i>solely</i> on the grounds that there was no formal discussion indicating consensus for the change before it was made. Instead, you should give a substantive reason for challenging it and, if one hasn't already been started, open a discussion to identify the community's current views. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Be_bold#Wikipedia_namespace" title="Wikipedia:Be bold">Bold</a> editors of policy and guideline pages are strongly encouraged to follow <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_warring#Other_revert_rules" title="Wikipedia:Edit warring">WP:1RR</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Edit_warring#Other_revert_rules" title="Wikipedia:Edit warring">WP:0RR</a> standards. Editing a policy to support your own argument in an active discussion may be seen as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Gaming_the_system" title="Wikipedia:Gaming the system">gaming the system</a>, especially if you do not disclose your involvement in the argument when making the edits. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conflicts_between_advice_pages">Conflicts between advice pages</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:POLCON"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:POLCON&amp;redirect=no">WP:POLCON</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Content_forking/Internal#Policy_forks" title="Wikipedia:Content forking/Internal">Wikipedia:Content forking/Internal §&nbsp;Policy forks</a>
      </div> 
      <p>If policy and/or guideline pages directly conflict, one or more pages need to be revised to resolve the conflict so all the conflicting pages accurately reflect the community's actual practices and best advice. As a temporary measure during that resolution process, if a guideline appears to conflict with a policy, editors may assume the policy takes precedence. </p>
      <p>More commonly, advice pages do not directly conflict, but provide multiple options. For example, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources">Wikipedia:Reliable sources</a> says newspaper articles are generally considered to be reliable sources, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Identifying_reliable_sources_(medicine)" title="Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources (medicine)">Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources (medicine)</a> recommends against newspaper articles for certain technical purposes. Editors must use their best judgement to decide which advice is most appropriate and relevant to the specific situation at hand. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span></h2> 
      <p>The page names of policies and guidelines usually do not include the words "policy" or "guideline", unless required to distinguish the page from another. </p>
