          <div class="description mobile-left glue-body">
           <p>Be careful about using public or free Wi-Fi, even if it requires a password. These networks may not be encrypted, so when you connect to a public network, anyone in the vicinity may be able to monitor your internet activity, such as the websites you visit and the information you type into sites.           <h2 class="glue-headline glue-mod-spacer-2-top glue-headline--headline-5 title">Know how scammers might reach you</h2> 
          <div class="text-description glue-mod-spacer-2-bottom"> 
           <p class="glue-caption" aria-label="Scammers can take advantage of goodwill by disguising their scams as legitimate messages. Alongside emails, scammers may also use text messages, automated calls, and malicious websites to exploit you.">Scammers can take advantage of goodwill by disguising their scams as legitimate messages. Alongside emails, scammers may also use text messages, automated calls, and malicious websites to exploit you.</p>

          <h2 class="glue-headline glue-mod-spacer-2-top glue-headline--headline-5 title">Always validate suspicious URLs or links</h2> 
          <div class="text-description glue-mod-spacer-2-bottom"> 
           <p class="glue-caption" aria-label="Phishing is an attempt to trick you into revealing critical personal or financial information, like a password or bank details. It can take many forms, such as a fake login page. To avoid getting phished, never click on questionable links; double-check the URL — by hovering over the link or long-pressing the text on mobile — to make sure the website or app is legitimate; and make sure the URL begins with “https.”">Phishing is an attempt to trick you into revealing critical personal or financial information, like a password or bank details. It can take many forms, such as a fake login page. To avoid getting phished, never click on questionable links; double-check the URL — by hovering over the link or long-pressing the text on mobile — to make sure the website or app is legitimate; and make sure the URL begins with “https.”</p> 
          </div>

          <h2 class="glue-headline glue-mod-spacer-2-top glue-headline--headline-5 title">Watch out for impersonators</h2> 
          <div class="text-description glue-mod-spacer-2-bottom"> 
           <p class="glue-caption" aria-label="Scammers might pose as legitimate organizations like the government or nonprofits. Always proceed with caution when reading messages from someone claiming to be an authoritative resource. If someone you know emails you but the message seems odd, their account may have been hacked. Don’t reply to the message or click any links unless you can confirm the email is legitimate. Look out for things like urgent requests for money,  sob stories about being stranded abroad, or the person claiming their phone was stolen and cannot be called.">Scammers might pose as legitimate organizations like the government or nonprofits. Always proceed with caution when reading messages from someone claiming to be an authoritative resource. If someone you know emails you but the message seems odd, their account may have been hacked. Don’t reply to the message or click any links unless you can confirm the email is legitimate. Look out for things like urgent requests for money, sob stories about being stranded abroad, or the person claiming their phone was stolen and cannot be called.</p> 
          </div>

          <h2 class="glue-headline glue-mod-spacer-2-top glue-headline--headline-5 title">Beware of email scams or requests for personal information</h2> 
          <div class="text-description glue-mod-spacer-2-bottom"> 
           <p class="glue-caption" aria-label="Messages from strangers can be suspect, and even a communication from someone you trust, like your bank, might be an impersonation. Don't reply to suspicious emails, instant messages, or pop-up windows that ask for personal information. Never click suspicious links or enter personal information in questionable forms or surveys. If asked for a nonprofit donation, go directly to the organization's website to donate rather than clicking on the link sent to you.">Messages from strangers can be suspect, and even a communication from someone you trust, like your bank, might be an impersonation. Don't reply to suspicious emails, instant messages, or pop-up windows that ask for personal information. Never click suspicious links or enter personal information in questionable forms or surveys. If asked for a nonprofit donation, go directly to the organization's website to donate rather than clicking on the link sent to you.</p> 
          </div>

         <div class="glue-expansion-panel-toggle" data-glue-expansion-panel-toggle-for="panel-1"> 
          <h3 class="glue-expansion-panel__button-header" aria-label="Know how scammers might reach you">Know how scammers might reach you</h3> 
          <svg alt="" role="img" aria-hidden="true" class="glue-icon glue-expansion-panel__button-arrow"> <use xlink:href="/static-2020/img/glue-icons.svg?cache=de92586#expand-more"></use>

         </div> 
         <div class="glue-expansion-panel-content" data-glue-expansion-panel-initial="expanded" id="panel-1"> 
          <div class="text-description"> 
           <p>Scammers can take advantage of goodwill by disguising their scams as legitimate messages. Alongside emails, scammers may also use text messages, automated calls, and malicious websites to exploit you.</p> 
          </div> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel"> 
         <div class="glue-expansion-panel-toggle" data-glue-expansion-panel-toggle-for="panel-2"> 
          <h3 class="glue-expansion-panel__button-header" aria-label="Always validate suspicious URLs or links">Always validate suspicious URLs or links</h3> 
          <svg alt="" role="img" aria-hidden="true" class="glue-icon glue-expansion-panel__button-arrow"> <use xlink:href="/static-2020/img/glue-icons.svg?cache=de92586#expand-more"></use> 
          </svg> 
         </div> 
         <div class="glue-expansion-panel-content" id="panel-2"> 
          <div class="text-description"> 
           <p>Phishing is an attempt to trick you into revealing critical personal or financial information, like a password or bank details. It can take many forms, such as a fake login page. To avoid getting phished, never click on questionable links; double-check the URL — by hovering over the link or long-pressing the text on mobile — to make sure the website or app is legitimate; and make sure the URL begins with “https.”</p> 
          </div> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel"> 
         <div class="glue-expansion-panel-toggle" data-glue-expansion-panel-toggle-for="panel-3"> 
          <h3 class="glue-expansion-panel__button-header" aria-label="Watch out for impersonators">Watch out for impersonators</h3> 
          <svg alt="" role="img" aria-hidden="true" class="glue-icon glue-expansion-panel__button-arrow"> <use xlink:href="/static-2020/img/glue-icons.svg?cache=de92586#expand-more"></use> 
          </svg> 
         </div> 
         <div class="glue-expansion-panel-content" id="panel-3"> 
          <div class="text-description"> 
           <p>Scammers might pose as legitimate organizations like the government or nonprofits. Always proceed with caution when reading messages from someone claiming to be an authoritative resource. If someone you know emails you but the message seems odd, their account may have been hacked. Don’t reply to the message or click any links unless you can confirm the email is legitimate. Look out for things like urgent requests for money, sob stories about being stranded abroad, or the person claiming their phone was stolen and cannot be called.</p> 
          </div> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="glue-expansion-panel"> 
         <div class="glue-expansion-panel-toggle" data-glue-expansion-panel-toggle-for="panel-4"> 
          <h3 class="glue-expansion-panel__button-header" aria-label="Beware of email scams or requests for personal information">Beware of email scams or requests for personal information</h3> 
          <svg alt="" role="img" aria-hidden="true" class="glue-icon glue-expansion-panel__button-arrow"> <use xlink:href="/static-2020/img/glue-icons.svg?cache=de92586#expand-more"></use> 
          </svg> 
         </div> 
         <div class="glue-expansion-panel-content" id="panel-4"> 
          <div class="text-description"> 
           <p>Messages from strangers can be suspect, and even a communication from someone you trust, like your bank, might be an impersonation. Don't reply to suspicious emails, instant messages, or pop-up windows that ask for personal information. Never click suspicious links or enter personal information in questionable forms or surveys. If asked for a nonprofit donation, go directly to the organization's website to donate rather than clicking on the link sent to you.</p> 
          </div>
