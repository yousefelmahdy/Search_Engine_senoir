      <p>Wikipedia should strive to provide a respectful, transparent, and positive experience for everyone. To support our work and to help maintain a pleasant environment, contributors should behave politely, calmly, and reasonably. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Assume_good_faith" title="Wikipedia:Assume good faith">Assuming good faith</a> is a core principle of Wikipedia. We realize most people want to help the projects, not hurt them. In a heated situation, try posting briefly and less frequently. Your message will still get through to people, and the slower pace will contribute a cooling effect. Try writing a post in your sandbox and reviewing after a few hours before pressing the "save" button in the Mainspace. </p>
      <p>Ask yourself the following questions: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>Do I understand the <b>context and background</b> of the discussion? Have I educated myself before asking others to explain things to me?</li> 
       <li>Do I understand the <b>cultural context</b> within which this person is operating? Am I being sensitive to cultural differences?</li> 
       <li>Could I be misunderstanding the discussion due to <b>language barriers</b>?</li> 
       <li>Do I understand this person’s <b>level of experience</b> with Wikipedia?</li> 
       <li>Am I <b>familiar with Wikipedia's rules and processes</b>, or am I bringing in assumptions from other spaces or contexts?</li> 
       <li>Can my passion for this issue be <b>misread as aggression</b>? Am I speaking in a way that’s likely to encourage discussion, or shut it down?</li> 
       <li>Am I framing my questions in a <b>supportive and constructive way</b>, offering suggestions for improvement rather than criticizing the gaps or problems?</li> 
       <li>Do I remember that <b>most of us are volunteers</b> and need to be appreciated for the time and effort we put into improving these projects?</li>
      </ol> 
      <p>Once you've checked yourself with these questions, take a deep breath, and re-engage with the discussion. If you do not think you can engage calmly and positively, contact a fellow Wikipedian who can help communicate your concerns. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Harassment">Harassment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:Maintaining_a_friendly_space&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Harassment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Wikipedia should not tolerate harassment of contributors in any form. Harassment includes: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Offensive comments related to gender, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, disability, physical appearance, race, ethnicity, political affiliation, or religion;</li> 
       <li>Violence, threats of violence, deliberate intimidation and personal attacks;</li> 
       <li>Stalking, following, or continued one-on-one communications after being asked to cease;</li> 
       <li>Sustained disruption of discussion;</li> 
       <li>Deliberate “outing” of any person’s identity without their consent;</li> 
       <li>Publication of non-harassing private communication.</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>For more information, see Wikipedia's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Harassment" title="Wikipedia:Harassment">official policy on Harassment</a>. </p>
      <p><b>If you are experiencing harassment,</b> you can contact the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee">Arbitration Committee</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Volunteer_Response_Team" title="Wikipedia:Volunteer Response Team">Volunteer Response Team</a> in confidence.
