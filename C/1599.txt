      <p>Disagreements on Wikipedia are normal; editors will frequently disagree with each other, particularly on content decisions. Editors are expected to engage in good faith to resolve their disputes, and must not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BATTLEGROUND" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BATTLEGROUND">personalise disputes</a>. Many disputes can be resolved without external input, through <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BRD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BRD">gradual editing</a>, discussion, and attempts to understand the legitimate objections of others. </p>
      <p>If discussion stalemates, editors may seek outside input to help resolve the dispute. Discussions can be advertised to noticeboards and WikiProjects to receive participation from interested uninvolved editors. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Mediation" title="Wikipedia:Mediation">Mediation</a> can also help in finding a compromise solution. As content decisions on Wikipedia are made through <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a>, the final stage in content dispute resolution is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requests_for_comment" title="Wikipedia:Requests for comment">request for comments</a>, where a discussion is advertised to uninvolved editors to receive broad input on the issue. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Resolving_content_disputes">Resolving content disputes</span></h2> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="Avoiding_conflict"></span> </p> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       "Wikipedia:Discussion" redirects here. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Talk" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Talk">Wikipedia:Talk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_discussion" title="Category:Wikipedia discussion">Category:Wikipedia discussion</a>.
      </div>

      <p>There are many methods on Wikipedia for resolving disputes. Most methods are not formal processes and do not involve third-party intervention. Respond to all disputes or grievances, in the first instance, by approaching the editor or editors concerned and explaining which of their edits you object to and why you object. Use the article talk page or their user talk page to do so; be civil, polite, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Assume_good_faith" title="Wikipedia:Assume good faith">always assume good faith</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Follow_the_normal_protocol">Follow the normal protocol</span></h3> 
      <p>When you find a passage in an article that is biased, inaccurate, or unsourced the best practice is to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FIXTHEPROBLEM" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FIXTHEPROBLEM">improve it if you can</a> rather than deleting salvageable text. For example, if an article appears biased, add balancing material or make the wording more neutral. Include <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">citations</a> for any material you add. If you do not know how to fix a problem, ask for help on the talk page. </p>
      <p>To help other editors understand the reasoning behind your edits, always explain your changes in the <a href="/wiki/Help:Edit_summary" title="Help:Edit summary">edit summary</a>. If an edit is too complex to explain in an edit summary, or the change is contentious, add a section to the talk page that explains your rationale. Be prepared to justify your changes to other editors on the talk page. If you are reverted, continue to explain yourself; do not start an edit war. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Discuss_with_the_other_party"><span id="discuss"></span><span id="Discuss"></span>Discuss with the other party</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">

        </div>
       </div>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">

      <p>Talking to other parties is not a mere formality, but an integral part of writing the encyclopedia. Discussing heatedly or poorly&nbsp;– or not at all&nbsp;– will make other editors less sympathetic to your position, and prevent you from effectively using later stages in dispute resolution. Sustained discussion between the parties, even if not immediately successful, demonstrates your good faith and shows you are trying to reach a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CONSENSUS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CONSENSUS">consensus</a>. Try negotiating a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Truce" title="Wikipedia:Truce">truce</a> or proposing a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Negotiation" title="Wikipedia:Negotiation">compromise</a> through negotiation. </p>
      <p>Do not continue edit warring; once sustained discussion begins, productively participating in it is a priority. Uninvolved editors who are invited to join a dispute will likely be confused and alarmed if there are large numbers of reverts or edits made while discussion is ongoing. </p>
      <p>Talk page discussion is a prerequisite to almost all of Wikipedia's venues of higher dispute resolution. If you wish at any time to request a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:3O" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:3O">Third Opinion (3O)</a>, use the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DRN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DRN">Dispute Resolution Noticeboard (DRN)</a>, or open a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RfArb" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RfArb">request for arbitration</a>, you will be expected to show there has been <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Talk" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Talk">talk page</a> discussion of the dispute. Actual discussion is needed; discussion conducted entirely through <a href="/wiki/Help:Edit_summary" title="Help:Edit summary">edit summaries</a> is inadequate. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RFC">Requests for Comment</a> generally require that at least an effort be made to discuss the matter in question before making the request. </p> 
      <div style="clear:both;"></div> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Focus_on_content">Focus on content</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:FOC"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:FOC&amp;redirect=no">WP:FOC</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Editing_policy" title="Wikipedia:Editing policy">Wikipedia:Editing policy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Talk_page_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Talk page guidelines">Wikipedia:Talk page guidelines</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Focus on article content during discussions, not on editor conduct; comment on content, not the contributor. Wikipedia is written through collaboration, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Assume_good_faith" title="Wikipedia:Assume good faith">assuming that the efforts of others are in good faith</a> is therefore vital. Bringing up conduct during discussions about content creates a distraction to the discussion and may inflame the situation. </p>
      <p>Focusing on content, and not bringing up conduct, can be difficult if it seems other editors are being uncivil or stubborn. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Staying_cool_when_the_editing_gets_hot" title="Wikipedia:Staying cool when the editing gets hot">Stay cool</a>! It is never to your benefit to respond in kind. When it becomes too difficult or exhausting to maintain a civil discussion based on content, you should seriously consider going to an appropriate dispute resolution venue <a href="#Resolving_content_disputes_with_outside_help">detailed below</a>; but at no juncture should you lose your temper. Wikipedia is not like a lot of the Internet: we expect editors to be polite and reasonable at all times. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Disengage">Disengage</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:DISENGAGE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:DISENGAGE&amp;redirect=no">WP:DISENGAGE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Most situations are not actually urgent; <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:There_is_no_deadline" title="Wikipedia:There is no deadline">there are no deadlines</a> on Wikipedia, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:IMPERFECT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:IMPERFECT">perfection is not required</a>. At all stages during discussion, consider whether you should take a break from the dispute. Taking a deep breath and sleeping on it often helps. You can always return to the discussion later, but at least you will return without an inflamed temper. </p>
      <p>Take a long-term view of the situation. You will probably be able to return and carry on editing an article when the previous problems no longer exist and the editor you were in dispute with might themselves move on. The disputed article will continue to evolve, other editors may become interested, and they might have different perspectives if the issue comes up again. Even if your position on the article is not accepted, it might be in the future. </p>
      <p>Disengaging is particularly helpful when in dispute with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_do_not_bite_the_newcomers" title="Wikipedia:Please do not bite the newcomers">new users</a>, as it gives them a chance to familiarise themselves with Wikipedia's policies and culture. As of 5 June 2021, there are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Size_of_Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia:Size of Wikipedia">6,311,222 articles</a> on Wikipedia. Focus your contributions on another article, where you can more easily make constructive edits. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Resolving_content_disputes_with_outside_help">Resolving content disputes with outside help</span></h2> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="Receive_outside_help_for_content_disputes"></span>
       <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855"></p>
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:CONTENTDISPUTE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:CONTENTDISPUTE&amp;redirect=no">WP:CONTENTDISPUTE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Dispute_resolution_requests" title="Wikipedia:Dispute resolution requests">Wikipedia:Dispute resolution requests</a>
      </div> 
      <p>While Noticeboards, Subject-specific help, and Editor assistance may well help in resolving disputes through increasing knowledge and obtaining outside opinions, they are not themselves dispute resolution processes <i>per se</i>. The order in which the following options are listed is <i>not</i> significant and they can, in general, be used in any order, though only one should be used at a time.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The ordering of dispute resolution processes in terms of complexity, experience and volunteer expertise would be: Third Opinion first (if only two editors are involved), then Dispute Resolution Noticeboard, with the Request for Comments option being available at any point in that sequence.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Please note that some Wikipedia processes such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AFD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AFD">Articles for Deletion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RM" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RM">Requested Move</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Articles_for_creation/Reviewing_instructions" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Articles for creation/Reviewing instructions">review of Draft articles</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> have built-in resolution processes and are not ordinarily handled via Third Opinion or the Dispute Resolution Noticeboard. </p>
      <p>Participation in <i>content</i> dispute resolution is voluntary and no one is required to participate if they do not care to do so, but it must be borne in mind that in some forums and processes<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> a discussion may proceed without an editor who chooses not to participate and consensus may be reached without the nonparticipating editor's input. Moreover, there is no <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:POLICY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:POLICY">policy or guideline</a> which prohibits <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SYSOP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SYSOP">administrators</a> or the community from taking an editor's failure or refusal to participate in content dispute resolution into consideration (or manner or degree of participation) as an item of evidence in a discussion about whether an editor's activities have, overall, been in the best interest of the encyclopedia. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Moderated_discussion">Moderated discussion</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Mediation" title="Wikipedia:Mediation">Wikipedia:Mediation</a>
      </div> 
      <p>The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DRN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DRN">Dispute resolution noticeboard</a> (DRN) is the place where editors involved in a content dispute can have a discussion facilitated by uninvolved volunteers, in an attempt to find compromise and resolution to disputes. The volunteers are experienced Wikipedia editors with knowledge in dispute resolution. Disputes are sometimes referred to a more appropriate venue (such as Requests for Comment). </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Third_opinion">Third opinion</span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Third_opinion" title="Wikipedia:Third opinion">Third opinion</a> is an excellent venue for small disputes involving <b>only two</b> editors. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Request_community_input_on_article_content">Request community input on article content</span></h3> 
      <p><span class="anchor" id="RfC"></span><span class="anchor" id="RFC"></span><span class="anchor" id="RfCs"></span><span class="anchor" id="RFCs"></span><span class="anchor" id="Rfc"></span><span class="anchor" id="Rfcs"></span> 
       <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855"></p>
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:DR#RfCs"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:DR&amp;redirect=no#RfCs">WP:DR#RfCs</a></span></li>

      </div> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Rfc#Request_comment_through_talk_pages" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Rfc">Request for comment</a> (RfC) is a process to request community-wide input on article content. RfCs can be used when there is a content-related dispute, or simply to get input from other editors before making a change. To solicit responses from a large number of editors, RfCs can be publicized via <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Noticeboards" title="Wikipedia:Noticeboards">noticeboards</a> or relevant <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">WikiProject</a> talk pages. An RfC bot will also automatically notify the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FRS">feedback request service</a> pool of editors. RfC discussions related to article content take place on article Talk pages. </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subject-specific_help">Subject-specific help</span></h3> 
      <p>Ask at a subject-specific <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">Wikipedia:WikiProject</a> talk page. Usually, such projects are listed on top of the article talk page. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Editor_assistance">Editor assistance</span></h3> 
      <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Editor_assistance" title="Wikipedia:Editor assistance">Editor assistance</a> helps editors find someone experienced to provide one-on-one advice and feedback. While not a required part of dispute resolution, it is designed to help you understand how to clearly and civilly express your views and work toward consensus. You may request an assistant's help at any time, whether you're involved in dispute resolution or not. Assistants can also help you find the best way to resolve your dispute or issue. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Resolving_user_conduct_disputes">Resolving user conduct disputes</span></h2> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:RUCD"></span><span id="WP:CONDUCTDISPUTE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:RUCD&amp;redirect=no">WP:RUCD</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:CONDUCTDISPUTE&amp;redirect=no">WP:CONDUCTDISPUTE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div>
      <p><span class="anchor" id="Request_community_input_on_conduct"></span><span class="anchor" id="Request_community_input_on_suspected_sockpuppets"></span><span class="anchor" id="Request_community_input_on_usernames"></span> </p>
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Tools/User_interaction_investigations" title="Wikipedia:Tools/User interaction investigations">Wikipedia:Tools/User interaction investigations</a>
      </div> 
      <p>The difference between a conduct and a content dispute is that, in a conduct dispute, the actions of a user (such as how an editor edits or the comments the editor makes about other users) is the overriding issue. If there would be no substantive dispute if the editor was not behaving in a disruptive or unprofessional way, then it is a conduct dispute; if the primary issue is that two editors cannot agree on what the content of an article should be, then it is a content dispute. </p>
      <p>If the issue is a conduct dispute (i.e., editor behavior) the first step is to talk with the other editor at their <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:USERTALK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:USERTALK">user talk page</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CIVIL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CIVIL">polite, simple, and direct</a> way. Try to avoid discussing conduct issues on article Talk pages. There are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Template_messages/User_talk_namespace" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Template messages/User talk namespace">several templates</a> you may use to warn editors of conduct issues,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> or you may choose to use your own words to open a discussion on the editor's talk page. In all cases, and even in the face of serious misconduct, please try to act in a professional and polite manner. <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/turn_the_other_cheek" class="extiw" title="wikt:turn the other cheek">Turn the other cheek</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noticeboards_2">Noticeboards</span></h3> 
      <p>If discussion with the editor fails to resolve the issue, you may ask an administrator to evaluate the conduct of the user. You can ask for an administrator's attention at a noticeboard such as the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ANI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ANI">administrators' noticeboard for incidents</a> (ANI). Conduct complaints that fall into certain sub-categories of misconduct have their own administrators' noticeboard; for example, complaints about edit warring should be made at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators%27_noticeboard/Edit_warring" title="Wikipedia:Administrators' noticeboard/Edit warring">edit warring noticeboard</a> (AN3), and requests for enforcing an Arbitration Committee decision at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AE">arbitration enforcement noticeboard</a> (AE). Administrators and the community will look to see if you have tried to resolve the conflict before escalating, and they will look at your behavior as well as the behavior of the other editor or editors. Administrators have wide latitude to use their permissions to stop misconduct and damage to the encyclopedia; for example, an editor who is making personal attacks, and does not stop when you ask them, may be warned by an administrator and subsequently blocked. </p>
      <p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SPI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SPI">Sockpuppet investigations</a> is for evaluating concerns that two users may be <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SOCK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SOCK">sockpuppets</a> (editors who are operating two accounts pretending to be different people, or blocked editors returning under a different account). <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requests_for_comment/User_names" title="Wikipedia:Requests for comment/User names">Requests for comment on usernames</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:UAA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:UAA">usernames for administrator attention (UAA)</a> are the main methods of bringing attention to usernames which may be inappropriate. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sensitive_issues_and_functionary_actions">Sensitive issues and functionary actions</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee">Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CheckUser" title="Wikipedia:CheckUser">Wikipedia:CheckUser</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Oversight" title="Wikipedia:Oversight">Wikipedia:Oversight</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Harassment" title="Wikipedia:Harassment">Wikipedia:Harassment</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Volunteer_Response_Team" title="Wikipedia:Volunteer Response Team">Wikipedia:Volunteer Response Team</a>
      </div> 
      <p>A small number of user conduct grievances involve sensitive or non-public information. These include issues where an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AC">arbitrator</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CHECK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CHECK">checkuser</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SIGHT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SIGHT">oversighter</a> has stated a privacy issue exists in the case, and disputes where there is a concern of a sensitive or private nature. For example: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Non-public details: Grievances where the relevant information and evidence are not accessible to all participants or to the community as a whole. This can also happen due to copyright or privacy reasons, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLP" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLP">BLP</a>, or when the material is on an unsuitable <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:EL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:EL">external link</a>.</li> 
       <li>"Outing" concerns: When discussion may in effect mean "<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OUTING" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:OUTING">outing</a>", for example if there is a concern that a user is editing with a secret conflict of interest and the evidence would tend to identify them.</li> 
       <li>Serious matters: The issue involves legal concerns, harassment, or allegations that are very serious or perhaps defamatory.</li> 
       <li>Advice on divisive and sensitive issues: The issue may potentially be very divisive and advice is needed on how best to handle it (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ADMINSOCK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ADMINSOCK">socking by an administrator</a> is one example).</li>
      </ul> 
      <p>Disputes or issues of this kind should usually be referred to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FUNC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FUNC">functionaries mailing list</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ARBCOM" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ARBCOM">Arbitration Committee</a>. In some cases it may be possible to seek advice from an uninvolved trusted administrator by IRC, email or other private means. Where an action is marked as CheckUser, Oversight, OTRS, or Arbitration Committee, that action should <u>not</u> be reverted without checking beforehand. The presumption is that they have a good reason, and those aware of the reason may need time to recheck, consult, and respond. Sometimes the relevant talk page or other wiki pages will have more details and these are always a good first place to check. </p>
      <p>Such actions, if disputed, should initially be raised (by email if necessary) with the agent or functionary concerned. Where a dispute about CheckUser and Oversighter actions cannot be resolved in this manner, it should be referred to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FUNC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FUNC">functionaries mailing list</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee/Audit" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee/Audit">Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee/Audit</a> where appropriate. Disputes about ArbCom actions should be referred to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AC">Arbitration Committee</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Last_resort:_Arbitration">Last resort: Arbitration</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Main page: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requests_for_arbitration" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Requests for arbitration">Wikipedia:Requests for arbitration</a>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee">Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_policy" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration policy">Wikipedia:Arbitration policy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_guide" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration guide">Wikipedia:Arbitration guide</a>
      </div> 
      <p>If you have taken all other reasonable steps to resolve the dispute, <i>and the dispute is not over the content of an article</i>, you can <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Requests_for_arbitration" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Requests for arbitration">request arbitration</a>. Be prepared to show that you tried to resolve the dispute by other means. Arbitration differs from other forms of dispute resolution in that the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_Committee" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration Committee">Arbitration Committee</a> will consider the case and issue a decision, instead of merely assisting the parties in reaching an agreement. If the issue is decided by arbitration, you will be expected to abide by the result. If the case involves serious user misconduct, arbitration may result in a number of serious consequences up to totally banning someone from editing, as laid out in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arbitration_policy" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Arbitration policy">arbitration policy</a>. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Words_of_caution">Words of caution</span></h2> 
      <p>Dispute resolution is sometimes used by editors to try to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Gaming_the_system" title="Wikipedia:Gaming the system">game the system</a>. This generally backfires badly. Remember that dispute resolution mechanisms are ultimately there to enable editors to collaboratively write an encyclopedia&nbsp;– not to win personal or political battles. </p>
      <p>Under <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CONEXCEPT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CONEXCEPT">Wikipedia:Decisions not subject to consensus of editors</a>, some disputes are resolved in different forums using those forums' methods. </p>
