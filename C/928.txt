      <h1>Apple Privacy Policy</h1> 
      <h3 class="typography-subsection-headline">Updated June 1, 2021</h3> 
      <p class="typography typography-intro large-8 medium-10 small-12 center">Apple’s Privacy Policy describes how Apple collects, uses, and shares your personal data.</p> 
      <p class="typography typography-intro large-8 medium-10 small-12 center">In addition to this Privacy Policy, we provide data and privacy information embedded in our products and certain features that ask to use your personal information. This product-specific information is accompanied by our Data &amp; Privacy Icon.</p> 
      <p class="typography-intro large-8 medium-10 small-12 center"><img src="https://www.apple.com/legal/images/icon_dataprivacy.png"></p> 
      <p class="typography typography-intro large-8 medium-10 small-12 center">You will be given an opportunity to review this product-specific information before using these features.&nbsp;&nbsp;You also can view this information at any time, either in Settings related to those features and/or online at <a href="/legal/privacy/">apple.com/legal/privacy</a>.</p> 
      <p class="typography typography-intro large-8 medium-10 small-12 center">Please take a moment to familiarize yourself with our privacy practices, accessible via the headings below, and <a href="contact/">contact us</a> if you have any questions.</p> 
      <p class="typography-intro large-8 medium-10 small-12 center"><a href="/legal/privacy/pdfs/apple-privacy-policy-en-ww.pdf">Download a copy of this Privacy Policy</a><br> &nbsp;<br> <a class="more" href="/legal/privacy/california/">Your California Privacy Disclosures</a>&nbsp;<br> <a class="more" href="/legal/privacy/apple-health-studies/en-ww">Apple Health Study Apps Privacy Policy</a><br> </p> 
     </div> 
    </section> 
    <section class="section section-transparency-accordion "> 
     <div class="section-content"> 
      <div class="row"> 
       <ul class="accordion-wrapper"> 
        <li class="accordion-item" data-analytics-title="accordion open - What Is Personal Data at Apple?"> 
         <div class="header-wrapper"> 
          <h2 id="accordion-label-d4a39d30eed8ebc5df66347dbcce2bee" class="accordion-headline typography-callout">What Is Personal Data at Apple?</h2> <button class="icon icon-plus" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="accordion-panel-d4a39d30eed8ebc5df66347dbcce2bee" aria-labelledby="accordion-label-d4a39d30eed8ebc5df66347dbcce2bee"></button> 
         </div> 
         <div class="accordion-panel" id="accordion-panel-d4a39d30eed8ebc5df66347dbcce2bee" aria-hidden="true"> 
          <div class="accordion-content"> 
           <p>At Apple, we believe strongly in fundamental privacy rights — and that those fundamental rights should not differ depending on where you live in the world. <strong>That’s why we treat any data that relates to an identified or identifiable individual or that is linked or linkable to them by Apple as “personal data,” no matter where the individual lives.</strong> This means that data that directly identifies you — such as your name — is personal data, and also data that does not directly identify you, but that can reasonably be used to identify you — such as the serial number of your device — is personal data.&nbsp;Aggregated data is considered non?personal data for the purposes of this Privacy Policy.</p> 
           <p>This Privacy Policy covers how <a href="https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/en/affiliated-company/">Apple or an Apple-affiliated company</a> handles personal data whether you interact with us on our websites, through Apple apps (such as Apple Music or Wallet), or in person (including by phone or when visiting our retail stores). Apple may also link to third parties on our services or make third-party apps available for download in our App Store. Apple’s Privacy Policy does not apply to how third parties define personal data or how they use it. We encourage you to read their privacy policies and know your privacy rights before interacting with them.&nbsp;</p> 
          </div> 
         </div> </li> 
        <li class="accordion-item" data-analytics-title="accordion open - Your Privacy Rights at Apple"> 
         <div class="header-wrapper"> 
          <h2 id="accordion-label-ef0788f7889f41fcf01b93a23a7dce46" class="accordion-headline typography-callout">Your Privacy Rights at Apple</h2> <button class="icon icon-plus" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="accordion-panel-ef0788f7889f41fcf01b93a23a7dce46" aria-labelledby="accordion-label-ef0788f7889f41fcf01b93a23a7dce46"></button> 
         </div> 
         <div class="accordion-panel" id="accordion-panel-ef0788f7889f41fcf01b93a23a7dce46" aria-hidden="true"> 
          <div class="accordion-content"> 
           <p>At Apple, we respect your ability to know, access, correct, transfer, restrict the processing of, and delete your personal data. We have provided these rights to our global customer base and if you choose to exercise these privacy rights, you have the right not to be treated in a discriminatory way nor to receive a lesser degree of service from Apple. Apple does not sell your data including as “sale” is defined in Nevada and California.&nbsp;</p> 
           <p><strong>To exercise your privacy rights and choices, visit the Apple Data and Privacy page at </strong><a href="https://privacy.apple.com">privacy.apple.com</a><strong> for Apple or </strong><a href="https://shazam.com/privacy">shazam.com/privacy</a><strong> for Shazam. </strong>To help protect the security of your personal data, you must sign in to your account and your identity will be verified. If the Data and Privacy page is not available in your region, you can make privacy rights requests at <a href="https://apple.com/legal/privacy/contact">apple.com/legal/privacy/contact</a>.&nbsp;</p> 
           <p>There may be situations where we cannot grant your request — for example, if you ask us to delete your transaction data and Apple is legally obligated to keep a record of that transaction to comply with law. We may also decline to grant a request where doing so would undermine our legitimate use of data for anti-fraud and security purposes, such as when you request deletion of an account that is being investigated for security concerns. Other reasons your privacy request may be denied are if it jeopardizes the privacy of others, is frivolous or vexatious, or would be extremely impractical.</p> 
           <p><em>If you live in California,</em> if you cannot access Apple’s Data and Privacy page, you or your authorized agent can make a request at <a href="https://apple.com/legal/privacy/contact">apple.com/legal/privacy/contact</a>&nbsp;or by calling 1-800-275-2273.</p> 
           <p>For more information on exercising your rights, visit <a href="https://support.apple.com/kb/HT208501">support.apple.com/kb/HT208501</a>.&nbsp;</p> 
          </div> 
         </div> </li> 
        <li class="accordion-item" data-analytics-title="accordion open - Personal Data Apple Collects from You"> 
         <div class="header-wrapper"> 
          <h2 id="accordion-label-86bc2cb7c9713fe47c56d23f4fe54658" class="accordion-headline typography-callout">Personal Data Apple Collects from You</h2> <button class="icon icon-plus" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="accordion-panel-86bc2cb7c9713fe47c56d23f4fe54658" aria-labelledby="accordion-label-86bc2cb7c9713fe47c56d23f4fe54658"></button> 
         </div> 
         <div class="accordion-panel" id="accordion-panel-86bc2cb7c9713fe47c56d23f4fe54658" aria-hidden="true"> 
          <div class="accordion-content"> 
           <p>At Apple, we believe that you can have great products and great privacy. This means that we strive to <strong>collect only the personal data that we need</strong>. The personal data Apple collects depends on how you interact with Apple. Descriptions of how Apple handles personal data for certain individual services are available at <a href="https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/data">apple.com/legal/privacy/data</a>.</p> 
           <p>When you create an Apple ID, apply for commercial credit, purchase and/or activate a product or device, download a software update, register for a class at an Apple Store, connect to our services, contact us (including by social media), participate in an online survey, or otherwise interact with Apple, we may collect a variety of information, including:</p> 
           <ul> 
            <li><strong>Account Information. </strong>Your Apple ID and related account details, including email address, devices registered, account status, and age</li> 
            <li><strong>Device Information.</strong> Data from which your device could be identified, such as device serial number, or about your device, such as browser type</li> 
            <li><strong>Contact Information</strong>. Data such as name, email address, physical address, phone number, or other contact information</li>

On your iOS or iPadOS device, you can disable Personalized Ads by going to Settings &gt; Privacy &gt; Apple Advertising and tapping to turn off Personalized Ads. On your Mac, you can disable Personalized Ads by choosing Apple menu &gt; System Preferences &gt; Security &amp; Privacy, clicking Privacy, clicking Advertising, and deselecting Personalized Ads. You may still see ads in the App Store, Apple News, or Stocks based on context like your search query or the channel you are reading.
