         <td class="mbox-text"><b>This page documents an English Wikipedia <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_project_content_guidelines" title="Category:Wikipedia project content guidelines">project content guideline</a>.</b><br>
          <div style="font-size: 90%;">
            It is a generally accepted standard that editors should attempt to follow, though it is best treated with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_%22Ignore_all_rules%22_means#Use_common_sense" title="Wikipedia:What &quot;Ignore all rules&quot; means">common sense</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Ignore_all_rules" title="Wikipedia:Ignore all rules">occasional exceptions</a> may apply. Any substantive edit to this page should reflect <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a>. When in doubt, discuss first on the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:WikiProject_Council/Guide" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia talk:WikiProject Council/Guide">talk page</a>.

      <p>A <b>WikiProject</b> is a group of people who want to work as a team to improve Wikipedia. This <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Policies_and_guidelines" title="Wikipedia:Policies and guidelines">guideline</a> outlines the accepted practices for starting and maintaining WikiProjects, as well as some tips for how to organize WikiProjects effectively. For more detailed instructions on how to start a WikiProject, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Guide/WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide/WikiProject">Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide/WikiProject</a>. </p>

      <p>A <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">WikiProject</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Social_group" title="Social group">group of editors</a> interested in collaborating on a specific topic within Wikipedia. A WikiProject is a group of people, not a set of pages, a subject area, a list of tasks, or a category. The pages of a WikiProject serve as a central place for coordination, discussion, and organization of the group's activities related to the specific topic. WikiProject pages may be used to develop criteria, maintain various collaborative processes, keep track of work that needs to be done, and act as a forum where relevant issues may be discussed. </p>
      <p>A WikiProject may also help build ties between Wikipedians interested in a topic, and the broader community interested in that topic: establishing partnerships, mentoring new Wikipedians, etc. In this respect, the role of a WikiProject may overlap with the role of a <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Wikimedia_chapters" class="extiw" title="meta:Wikimedia chapters">Wikimedia chapter</a>, <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Wikimedia_thematic_organizations" class="extiw" title="meta:Wikimedia thematic organizations">thematic organization</a>, or <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Wikimedia_user_groups" class="extiw" title="meta:Wikimedia user groups">user group</a>. </p>
      <p><strong>A WikiProject is fundamentally a <em>social</em> construct: its success depends on its ability to function as a cohesive group of editors working towards a common goal.</strong> Much of the work that participants do to sustain a successful WikiProject (quality assessment, peer review, coordination of volunteer editors, et al.) can be tedious, unrewarding, and unappreciated. To be effective, a WikiProject must foster an <i><a href="/wiki/Esprit_de_corps" class="mw-redirect" title="Esprit de corps">esprit de corps</a></i> among its participants. When group cohesion is maintained—where, in other words, project participants are willing to share in the less exciting work—a WikiProject can muster the energy and direction to produce excellent articles systematically rather than incidentally. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creating_a_WikiProject">Creating a WikiProject</span></h2> 
      <p>WikiProjects exist in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Project_namespace" title="Wikipedia:Project namespace">project namespace</a> and can technically be started by any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AUTOCONFIRM" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AUTOCONFIRM">auto-confirmed user</a>. However, to avoid the proliferation of unused and underutilized WikiProjects, it is <b>strongly</b> recommended that those interested in starting a WikiProject read the guidance below and propose their project at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Proposals" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Proposals">proposal page</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Before_you_begin">Before you begin</span></h3> 
      <p>Interested in starting a WikiProject? First, check to see if the project (or related projects) already exists. You can browse existing projects at the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Directory" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Directory">manual directory</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Directory" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Directory">automatically curated directory</a> as well as the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://bambots.brucemyers.com/drb/WikiProjectList.html">WikiProject list</a> maintained by <a href="/wiki/User:Bamyers99" title="User:Bamyers99">Bamyers99</a>. Additionally, you can use the searchbox below to text search for existing pages in the project space: </p> 
      <div class="mw-inputbox-centered" style="background-color: white;">
       <form name="searchbox" class="searchbox" action="/wiki/Special:Search">
        <input class="mw-searchInput searchboxInput mw-ui-input mw-ui-input-inline" name="search" type="text" value="" placeholder="e.g. Canada" size="35" dir="ltr">
        <input type="hidden" value="Wikipedia:WikiProject" name="prefix"> 
        <input type="submit" name="fulltext" class="mw-ui-button" value="Search WikiProjects">
        <input type="hidden" value="Search" name="fulltext">
       </form>
      </div> 
      <p>If your project doesn't already exist, it's time to look for editors with similar interests. <b>This is the most important step.</b> You <b>must</b> find people who want to work together on the project with you. The first stop should be to look for projects with similar interests (if any exist). Use the directories above or check the talk pages of related articles for projects with interests that overlap with yours. Post at the talk pages for those similar projects asking if editors are interested in your starting a project focused on your topic. If you can't find other editors with similar interests, consider participating in a current project instead. Single-editor projects tend to have short lives. </p>
      <p>If your project idea fits within another existing project (e.g. you want to start <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:WikiProject_Hockey_Arenas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Hockey Arenas (page does not exist)">Wikipedia:WikiProject Hockey Arenas</a> when <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Hockey" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Hockey">Wikipedia:WikiProject Hockey</a> already exists), and you're concerned coordination on this topic would either overwhelm the existing project's talk page, or be overwhelmed by it, consider organizing as a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Task_force" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Task force">task force</a> under that existing project instead. This would drastically reduce the amount of setup and maintenance required to keep the project pages functioning. </p>
      <p>If you're having trouble finding interested editors, consider that the scope of your proposed project may be too narrow. Wikipedia is a huge place with many draws on editors' attention; finding several long-term editors with a sustained interest in one topic may not be easy. Projects with overly narrow scopes tend to become inactive due to lack of editors, not enough associated encyclopedia pages for sustained effort, or competing with a broader-scoped more popular project that draws the time of interested editors. Broadening the proposed scope may help to bring in more pages and editors; although a scope too broad may fail to interest editors who wish to collaborate on only a subset of your topic. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposing_a_project">Proposing a project</span></h3> 
      <p>If the project doesn't yet exist, but you've found interested editors, it's time to propose your project idea! Go to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Proposals" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Proposals">WikiProject proposals</a> page and search that page and its <a href="/wiki/Help:Archiving_a_talk_page" title="Help:Archiving a talk page">archives</a> to see if your project idea has been proposed before (if it has, be prepared to justify why you feel this time the project will succeed). Follow the instructions on that page to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Proposals#Creating_a_proposal" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Proposals">create a proposal</a>. You'll need to list the pages and <a href="/wiki/Help:Categories" title="Help:Categories">categories</a> that are key to your proposed group, as well as current WikiProjects that relate to those pages. Then interested users will sign-up to support the project (feel free to advertise this at related projects or pages. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Canvassing" title="Wikipedia:Canvassing">Canvassing</a> is not a concern here; however, filling the support roles with new accounts is unlikely to lead to a sustainable project). While there are no hard rules for what constitutes "sufficient" support, projects that are likely to succeed tend to start with at least 6 to 12 active Wikipedians. Once that threshold is reached, the proposal can be considered successful and the project created (see below). If there is insufficient support to start the project after a few months, the proposal will generally be <a href="/wiki/Category:Archived_WikiProject_proposals" title="Category:Archived WikiProject proposals">archived</a> for future reference. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Create_the_WikiProject">Create the WikiProject</span></h3> 
      <p>Creating a WikiProject is technically as easy as starting a page titled <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:WikiProject_Your_Favorite_Topic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Your Favorite Topic (page does not exist)">Wikipedia:WikiProject Your Favorite Topic</a>. However, various tools have been developed to help WikiProjects keep track of pages of interest and to facilitate collaboration on improving the encyclopedia coverage of a topic area. Getting a new project set up utilizing these tools is a slightly complicated process; detailed instructions can be found at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Guide/WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide/WikiProject">Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide/WikiProject</a>. Additionally, you may ask at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:WikiProject_Council" title="Wikipedia talk:WikiProject Council">Wikipedia talk:WikiProject Council</a> where interested experienced editors may be willing to help. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Task_force">Task force</span></h3> 
      <p>Based on discussion at a WikiProject proposal or at a given WikiProject, you may instead wish to start a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Taskforce" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Taskforce">task force</a> under an existing WikiProject. A task force is a group of editors interested in a smaller part of a WikiProject's scope (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:USMIL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:USMIL">United States military history task force</a> is under the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:MILHIST" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:MILHIST">Military history WikiProject</a>). Creating a task force gives those editors space to collaborate on the part of the WikiProject scope they are interested in, without drowning out broader discussion on the WikiProject's talk page. Additionally, task forces tend to have reduced administrative overhead, as they can use the tools and templates already developed for the parent WikiProject. Instructions for how to setup a new task force are at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Guide/Task_forces" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide/Task forces">Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide/Task forces</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General_principles">General principles</span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tagging_pages_with_WikiProject_banners">Tagging pages with WikiProject banners</span></h3> 
      <p>Many WikiProjects use <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Guide/WikiProject#Talk_page_banners" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide/WikiProject">talk page banners</a> to mark certain pages as within the scope of the WikiProject. This helps the WikiProjects to organize their progress improving pages within the project's scope. Additionally, talk page banners may point interested editors towards relevant WikiProjects where they might become involved, or just ask a question about an article. Consequently, pages should only be marked with WikiProject banners for projects that intend to support the tagged pages. Pages of broad interest may fall within the scopes of several projects, and may therefore have several project banners on their talk page (these banners are often collapsed to be less visible with <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProjectBannerShell" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:WikiProjectBannerShell">WikiProjectBannerShell</a>}}</code>). In general, one should not attempt to police which projects are sufficiently relevant to place their banners on a given talk page.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Conversely, projects that place their banners on a talk page have no special <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OWN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:OWN">ownership</a> over that page, and the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CONLEVEL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CONLEVEL">consensus</a> of project participants can be overruled by a broader consensus at a more visible forum. <span class="anchor" id="OWN"></span> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="WikiProjects_define_their_scopes">WikiProjects define their scopes</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:PROJSCOPE"></span><span id="WP:OVERBANNER"></span><span id="WP:SCOPEWAR"></span><span id="WP:PROJGUIDE#OWN"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PROJSCOPE&amp;redirect=no">WP:PROJSCOPE</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:OVERBANNER&amp;redirect=no">WP:OVERBANNER</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:SCOPEWAR&amp;redirect=no">WP:SCOPEWAR</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PROJGUIDE&amp;redirect=no#OWN">WP:PROJGUIDE#OWN</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Many editors place banners on behalf of WikiProjects in which they are not participants. This practice is normally welcomed by WikiProjects as it brings to their attention new and interesting articles. Be judicious in making such placements by carefully reviewing the scope of the project. Information about the project's scope is often available on the WikiProject's main page, and sometimes also on documentation associated with the template. All editors should avoid tagging an article with a disruptive number of WikiProject banners. If an article is only tangentially related to the scope of a WikiProject, then please do not place that project's banner on the article. For example, washing <a href="/wiki/Toy" title="Toy">toys</a> for babies reduces transmission of some diseases, but the banners for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Health_and_fitness" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Health and fitness">WP:WikiProject Health and fitness</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Biology" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Biology">WP:WikiProject Biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Viruses" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Viruses">WP:WikiProject Viruses</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Medicine" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Medicine">WP:WikiProject Medicine</a> do not need to be added to <a href="/wiki/Talk:Toy" title="Talk:Toy">Talk:Toy</a>. If you are uncertain that the placement will be welcomed, leave a note on the project's talk page instead of placing the banner yourself. Note that for projects involved in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:1.0" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:1.0">WP:1.0</a> assessment program (which is most of them), every banner placed is a demand for an assessment according to the project's guidelines. It is more friendly to omit outside WikiProjects that you think will rate the article as low importance relative to their specific field. </p>
      <p>If you place a banner for a WikiProject in which you do not participate, and one of its regular participants removes it, do not re-add the banner without discussion. A WikiProject's participants define the scope of their project (the articles that they <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:VOLUNTEER" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:VOLUNTEER">volunteer</a> to track and support), which includes defining an article as being outside the scope of the project. Similarly, if a WikiProject says that an article is within their scope, do not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:EDITWAR" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:EDITWAR">edit war</a> to remove the banner.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Advice_pages">Advice pages</span></h3> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:PROJPAGE"></span><span id="WP:ADVICEPAGE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:PROJPAGE&amp;redirect=no">WP:PROJPAGE</a></span></li>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:ADVICEPAGE&amp;redirect=no">WP:ADVICEPAGE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       Further information: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Essays" title="Wikipedia:Essays">Wikipedia:Essays</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Policy_writing_is_hard" title="Wikipedia:Policy writing is hard">Wikipedia:Policy writing is hard</a>
      </div> 
      <p>Many large WikiProjects collect advice about how to apply Wikipedia's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:POLICY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:POLICY">policies and guidelines</a> to their specific subject area. This advice, sometimes in a separate <i>advice page</i>, sometimes in a section of the WikiProject's main page, is often excellent, and may helpfully consolidate and explain the specific details of many site-wide policies and guidelines, the application of which to a particular context might otherwise cause confusion among editors. A separate-page example is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Cycling/Notability" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Cycling/Notability">Wikipedia:WikiProject Cycling/Notability</a>. A page-section example is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Bibliographies#Recommended_structure" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Bibliographies">Wikipedia:WikiProject Bibliographies#Recommended structure</a>. </p>
      <p>Editors who are working on such an advice page or section are encouraged to carefully study the main policies, guidelines, and relevant well-accepted general Wikipedia essays. The good advice pages <em>do not conflict</em> with the site-wide pages, and avoid unnecessary duplication of material from them. </p>
      <p>WikiProject advice can best help editors by providing: subject-specific considerations in applying site-wide standards; links to subject-specific templates; a list of information that editors should consider including in a given type of article; relevant examples; and clear explanations (e.g., reasons why editors recommend "this" instead of "that"). Well-written WikiProject advice material also takes into account the fact that most articles are within the scope of multiple WikiProjects, and seeks to avoid conflicting advice, which can lead to unproductive "territorial" disputes between projects, and between projects and editors with WikiProject-unrelated editing concerns. </p>
      <p>However, in a few cases, projects have wrongly used these pages as a means of asserting <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:OWN" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:OWN">ownership over articles</a> within their scope, such as insisting that all articles that interest the project must contain <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Criticism" title="Wikipedia:Criticism">a criticism section</a> or must not contain <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Infoboxes#Using_infoboxes_in_articles" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Infoboxes">an infobox</a>, or that a specific type of article can't be linked in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NAVBOX" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NAVBOX">navigation templates</a>, and that other editors of the article get no say in this because of a "consensus" within the project. An advice page written by several participants of a project is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:LOCALCONSENSUS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:LOCALCONSENSUS">"local consensus" that is no more binding</a> on editors than material written by any single individual editor. Any advice page that has not been formally approved by the community through the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:PROPOSAL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:PROPOSAL">WP:PROPOSAL</a> process has the actual status of an optional <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Essays" title="Wikipedia:Essays">essay</a>. Contents of WikiProject advice pages that contradict widespread consensus belong in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:User_pages" title="Wikipedia:User pages">user namespace</a>. </p>
      <p>Some important site-wide topical guidelines, such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Identifying_reliable_sources_(medicine)" title="Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources (medicine)">Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources (medicine)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Notability_(books)" title="Wikipedia:Notability (books)">Wikipedia:Notability (books)</a>, originally began as advice pages written by WikiProjects. However, after being adopted by the community, they are no longer WikiProject advice pages and have the same status as any other guideline. When this happens, the WikiProject's participants cede any notion of control over the page, and everyone in the community participates equally in further development of the guidelines. Such pages move out from under their original "Wikipedia:WikiProject <var style="padding-right: 1px;">Something</var>/" path. </p>
      <p>The following templates are available to WikiProjects for clarifying the distinction between WikiProject advice and Wikipedia-wide guidelines: </p> 
      <ul>
       <li><code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_advice" title="Template:WikiProject advice">WikiProject advice</a>}}</code> 
        <ul>
         <li><code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_style_advice" title="Template:WikiProject style advice">WikiProject style advice</a>}}</code></li> 
         <li><code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_notability_advice" title="Template:WikiProject notability advice">WikiProject notability advice</a>}}</code></li> 
         <li><code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_content_advice" title="Template:WikiProject content advice">WikiProject content advice</a>}}</code></li>
        </ul></li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Role_of_the_WikiProject_Council">Role of the WikiProject Council</span></h3> 
      <p>There may still arise situations when there is a seemingly intractable disagreement between projects. If that happens, you can ask for advice from the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council">WikiProject Council</a>. This group contains people who have generally shown some ability at working with and in groups. In severe cases, formal <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Dispute_resolution" title="Wikipedia:Dispute resolution">dispute resolution</a> channels are available. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tools_for_WikiProjects">Tools for WikiProjects</span></h2> 
      <p>The community has developed a broad array of tools to help WikiProjects manage the articles that they're interested in. Actively maintained tools are listed below, as well as instructions for how to utilize them: </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recruiting_editors">Recruiting editors</span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li>Make your project visible: 
        <ul>
         <li>add your project to the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Directory" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Directory">manually maintained</a> WikiProject directory (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Directory/WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Directory/WikiProject">instructions</a>), and to a subcategory of <a href="/wiki/Category:WikiProjects" title="Category:WikiProjects">Category:WikiProjects</a> (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Category:WikiProject_Birds" title="Category:WikiProject Birds">Category:WikiProject Birds</a> is in <a href="/wiki/Category:Biology_WikiProjects" title="Category:Biology WikiProjects">Category:Biology WikiProjects</a>) so that it will appear in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Directory" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Directory">Wikipedia:WikiProject Directory</a> (updated by <a href="/wiki/User:Reports_bot" title="User:Reports bot">Reports bot</a>).</li> 
         <li>add the {{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_status" title="Template:WikiProject status">WikiProject status</a>}} template to your project page; with no other parameters, this will add the project to <a href="/wiki/Category:Active_WikiProjects" title="Category:Active WikiProjects">Category:Active WikiProjects</a>.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>Find editors active in your area – For each project in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Directory" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Directory">Wikipedia:WikiProject Directory</a>, <a href="/wiki/User:Reports_bot" title="User:Reports bot">Reports bot</a> makes a list of editors active on those pages (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Directory/Description/WikiProject_Birds" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Directory/Description/WikiProject Birds">WikiProject Birds</a>).</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="General">General</span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li>Article statistics – <a href="/wiki/User:WP_1.0_bot" title="User:WP 1.0 bot">WP 1.0 bot</a> generates tables summarizing article quality and importance statistics. For more information, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Guide/WikiProject#Tracking_progress" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide/WikiProject">here</a>.</li> 
       <li>New pages relevant to your project – <a href="/wiki/User:AlexNewArtBot" title="User:AlexNewArtBot">AlexNewArtBot</a> (now run by <a href="/wiki/User:InceptionBot" title="User:InceptionBot">InceptionBot</a>) searches newly created pages for keywords related to projects, and makes a list of all new pages in the last 14 days that may be of interest to your project (<a href="/wiki/User:AlexNewArtBot/BirdsSearchResult" title="User:AlexNewArtBot/BirdsSearchResult">example</a>). Sign-up instructions are <a href="/wiki/User:AlexNewArtBot#How_to_add_feeds_to_the_new_article_bot" title="User:AlexNewArtBot">here</a>, or you can ask for help at <a href="/wiki/User_talk:Bamyers99" title="User talk:Bamyers99">the bot operator's talk page</a>.</li> 
       <li>Project watchlist – You can make a <a href="/wiki/Help:Watchlist" title="Help:Watchlist">watchlist</a> of all pages tagged with the WikiProject banner by making a list of all WikiProject articles, then use <a href="/wiki/Special:RecentChangesLinked" title="Special:RecentChangesLinked">Special:RecentChangesLinked</a> to list all recent changes to them. <a href="/wiki/User:SQLBot" title="User:SQLBot">User:SQLBot</a> and <a href="/wiki/User:Femto_Bot" title="User:Femto Bot">User:Femto Bot</a> used to maintain WikiProject lists but both are currently down. Lists can still be made manually; Example: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Firearms/Watchlist" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Firearms/Watchlist">WikiProject Firearms/Watchlist</a>.</li> 
       <li>Enable <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AALERTS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AALERTS">Article Alerts</a> to produce an automatically updated list of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Articles_for_deletion" title="Wikipedia:Articles for deletion">WP:Articles for deletion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Proposed_deletions" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Proposed deletions">WP:Proposed deletions</a>, and other time-sensitive news about tagged articles. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AALERTS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AALERTS">WP:AALERTS</a> for details, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Article_alerts/Subscribing" title="Wikipedia:Article alerts/Subscribing">Wikipedia:Article alerts/Subscribing</a> for instructions on how to subscribe.</li> 
       <li><a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Community_Tech/Popular_pages_bot" class="extiw" title="m:Community Tech/Popular pages bot">Popular pages bot</a> can produce a monthly list of the most-visited pages with a WikiProject's banner (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Birds/Popular_pages" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Birds/Popular pages">example</a>). Sign-up instructions are <a href="/wiki/User:Community_Tech_bot/Popular_pages_config.json" title="User:Community Tech bot/Popular pages config.json">here</a>.</li> 
       <li><a href="/wiki/User:Evad37/rater" title="User:Evad37/rater">Evad37's rater script</a> makes it easier to fill out assessments on WikiProject talk banners. Instructions for installing it are <a href="/wiki/User:Evad37/rater" title="User:Evad37/rater">here</a>.</li>
      </ul> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tracking_progress">Tracking progress</span></h3> 
      <ul>
       <li>Tracking maintenance templates on project articles – <a href="/wiki/User:CleanupWorklistBot" title="User:CleanupWorklistBot">CleanupWorklistBot</a> produces a weekly report of all project articles tagged with various maintenance templates (<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://bambots.brucemyers.com/cwb/bycat/Birds.html">example</a>). Instructions for adding a project are <a href="/wiki/User:CleanupWorklistBot" title="User:CleanupWorklistBot">here</a>.</li> 
       <li>Tracking and displaying recognized content – <a href="/wiki/User:JL-Bot" title="User:JL-Bot">JL-Bot</a> can generate a subpage listing the recognized content (Featured Articles, Good Articles, In The News, etc.) of a WikiProject. See <a href="/wiki/User:JL-Bot/Project_content" title="User:JL-Bot/Project content">User:JL-Bot/Project content</a> for details.</li>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Identifying">Identifying</span></h3> 
      <p>Many WikiProjects fall inactive after some time. While inactive projects do no harm to the encyclopedia, it may be beneficial to tag them as inactive, in order to divert interested editors to more active projects on similar topics. Projects are generally considered inactive if the talk page has received nothing other than routine/automated announcements or unanswered queries for a year or more. To verify that a project is inactive, post on its talk page asking if anyone minds marking it as such. If there are no objections, you can add <code>inactive</code> to the <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_status" title="Template:WikiProject status">WikiProject status</a>}}</code> template at the top of the WikiProject page. This will add the project to <a href="/wiki/Category:Inactive_WikiProjects" title="Category:Inactive WikiProjects">Category:Inactive WikiProjects</a> and display a message at the top of the page notifying editors of the project's status. </p>
      <p>Alternatively, a project that has gone inactive because it has served its stated purpose, had an unrealistic scope, or is otherwise unlikely to be ever revived can be marked as "defunct" per the instructions at <a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_status#Usage:_Defunct_projects" title="Template:WikiProject status">Template:WikiProject status#Usage: Defunct projects</a>. This will generate a more discouraging message, suggesting interested editors look for related projects. Sometimes small, inactive projects are simply <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:MERGE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:MERGE">merged</a> into larger, more active projects. This could be a good option if you wish for links to the smaller project to now point to the larger project. If there's still an active community at the smaller project, it could instead be merged as a task force of a larger project. Guidance on that can be found <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Guide/Task_forces#Converting_existing_projects_to_task_forces" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide/Task forces">here</a>. If an inactive project never seems to have grown beyond its founding, you may consider moving it to the founder's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:UFY" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:UFY">userspace</a> or nominating it for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DELETION" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DELETION">deletion</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:MfD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:MfD">MfD</a>. In general, medium or larger projects are marked as defunct rather than deleted to preserve the project's history. For more, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Project_namespace#Deletion_of_project_pages" title="Wikipedia:Project namespace">Wikipedia:Project namespace#Deletion of project pages</a>. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Revival">Revival</span></h3> 
      <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">
       See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedia_Signpost/2011-05-16/WikiProject_report#Back_to_Life:_Reviving_WikiProjects" title="Wikipedia:Wikipedia Signpost/2011-05-16/WikiProject report">Wikipedia:Wikipedia Signpost/2011-05-16/WikiProject report §&nbsp;Back to Life: Reviving WikiProjects</a>
      </div> 
      <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1007196855">
      <div class="module-shortcutanchordiv">
       <span id="WP:REVIVE"></span>
      </div>
      <div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note">
       <div class="module-shortcutlist">
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a>
       </div>
       <ul>
        <li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:REVIVE&amp;redirect=no">WP:REVIVE</a></span></li>
       </ul>
      </div> 
      <p>Any editor may revive an inactive WikiProject by changing the <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_status" title="Template:WikiProject status">WikiProject status</a>}}</code> template parameter to <code>active</code>. The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Guide#Creating_a_WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide">guidance</a> for starting a new WikiProject applies here: WikiProjects are groups of editors; if you can't recruit other editors to the cause, the project will likely become inactive again. If you decide to revive a WikiProject, consider the following tips: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>Recruit other interested editors by posting on the talk page of similar WikiProjects, the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Community_bulletin_board" title="Wikipedia:Community bulletin board">community bulletin board</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedia_Signpost/Newsroom/WikiProject_desk" title="Wikipedia:Wikipedia Signpost/Newsroom/WikiProject desk">Signpost WikiProject desk</a>. Also update the project's status in the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Directory" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Directory">directory</a>.</li> 
       <li>Archive old clutter (clean and simple is better for attracting new participants), use <a href="/wiki/Category:Generic_WikiProject_templates" title="Category:Generic WikiProject templates">generic WikiProject templates</a> appropriately to organise content (e.g., {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Infobox_Wikipedia_WikiProject" title="Template:Infobox Wikipedia WikiProject">Infobox Wikipedia WikiProject</a>}}) and make use of any helpful tools the project hadn't been using (see section above).</li> 
       <li>Provide clear suggestions on what participants can do, using <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:To-do_list" title="Wikipedia:To-do list">to-do lists</a>, {{<a href="/wiki/Template:Tasks" title="Template:Tasks">tasks</a>}} and cleanup listings, and perhaps linking to relevant pages elsewhere. You can use the {{<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_help" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:WikiProject help">WikiProject help</a>}} template, either directly or as inspiration.</li> 
       <li>Create any missing userbox, project banner, or user invite templates. See whether the assessment system for the project banner works, and fix it if not.</li> 
       <li>Notify existing participants of your efforts and invite them to contribute, to make suggestions, or to leave a note on the project's talk page about what they're currently editing.</li> 
       <li>Use automation (see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Council/Guide/WikiProject#Tag_some_talk_pages" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Council/Guide/WikiProject">WikiProject guide</a>) to ensure most if not all appropriate pages are tagged with the project banner, thus promoting the project to those who may be interested. (Don't go overboard with this... in general, don't tag a page not within the project's main category unless you could justify making it the project's Collaboration of the Month.)</li> 
       <li>Provide a <a href="/wiki/Special:RecentChangesLinked" title="Special:RecentChangesLinked">Special:RecentChangesLinked</a> link on the project page, using the project's article category. (For project Wikipedia:WikiProject X, this will generally be <code>Category:X articles, Category:WikiProject X articles,</code> or <code>X work group articles</code>. Try it and see.) This gives an easy way to see recent relevant talk page discussions. <a href="/wiki/Special:RecentChangesLinked/Category:Latin_America_articles" title="Special:RecentChangesLinked/Category:Latin America articles">Example</a>.</li> 
       <li>Seek out collaboration with related projects. Tell them that the project is active, invite them to help, and ask whether there is an article of mutual interest that both groups could collaborate on.</li> 
       <li>Respond promptly to queries and post occasional messages at the WikiProject's talk page to let people know what you're working on and how they can help.</li>
      </ol> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Renaming_a_WikiProject">Renaming a WikiProject</span></h2> 
      <p>Renaming a WikiProject is not as simple as moving the WikiProject page itself. The steps involved would generally include: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>Ensure there is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a> for the rename.</li> 
       <li>Rename the WikiProject page, moving all subpages with it (for best results, seek the assistance from an <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators" title="Wikipedia:Administrators">administrator</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Page_mover" title="Wikipedia:Page mover">page mover</a>).</li> 
       <li>Update the WikiProject template to point to the new WikiProject page, and to have it use a new category that matches the new name.</li> 
       <li>Rename the relevant categories pages (also may require admin or page mover assistance).</li> 
       <li>Ensure assessment data has been moved by querying for the new WikiProject at <a href="/wiki/Special:PageAssessments" title="Special:PageAssessments">Special:PageAssessments</a>. It may take a while for the data to be updated. If done correctly, no pages should be returned under the old name.</li> 
       <li>If the WikiProject uses any of the tools referenced above, you may need to update the WikiProject listings used by those tools to ensure the tools will still work with the new WikiProject name.</li>
      </ol>
